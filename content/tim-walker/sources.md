# Tim Walker — source shortlist

This set follows Walker's fantasy-fashion practice through twelve complete frames from his own public portfolio, from early editorial scale-play to the Bosch-inspired *Garden of Earthly Delights* project. The artist's site identifies Walker's work as lavish, narrative-driven tableaux made with extraordinary sets and props, and identifies the V&A's 2019–20 *Wonderful Things* commission as a major body of related work: https://www.timwalkerphotography.com/. Each file below was retrieved directly from the official public image response, opens as a JPEG, and has a 2500px long edge. They are source originals only; the later build will make web-sized 1600px derivatives for this nonprofit teaching site.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. No open licence or public-domain statement was found for these contemporary images; credit is therefore **Tim Walker / Tim Walker Photography**, with the originating magazine or project named in each portfolio caption. Each is an individual assessment: this book uses a credited, linked, web-sized derivative for transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching; the whole frame is needed to study the photographed set and its spatial relationships; and the use does not substitute for the official portfolio, magazine record, original print, or a licensed reproduction.

## Research context

Walker states that his practice developed through imaginative, fantastical fashion pictures and elaborate narrative shoots; the same official biography describes the 2015 Bosch-inspired commission and the V&A's *Wonderful Things* commission. The official portfolio captions supplied the titles, originating publication/project, and dates used above. V&A's exhibition announcement independently characterizes *Wonderful Things* as the museum's largest exhibition devoted to Walker: https://www.vam.ac.uk/info/tim-walker-wonderful-things.
