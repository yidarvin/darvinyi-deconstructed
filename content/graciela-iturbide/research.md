# Research — Graciela Iturbide

## What the supplied record establishes

The twelve collection renditions documented in [source notes](sources.md) span
1979 to 2006, with several records distinguishing an exposure date from a later
print: *Mujer ángel* (1979, printed later), *Autorretrato con los Seris* (1979,
printed 2017), and *Autorretrato en la casa de Trotsky* (2006, printed 2017).
The records place the set across Juchitán, the Sonoran desert, and later
self-portrait work, and identify SFMOMA as the holding collection.

## Technique and material limits

The supplied records establish author, title, date, collection credit, and in
some cases a later print date. They do not consistently establish camera body,
lens, film stock, aperture, shutter speed, exposure method, development, paper,
or original print process. The normalized JPEGs retain no original-camera EXIF
(one derivative contains an ISO tag, which is not evidence about the historical
exposure). This chapter therefore makes no camera-setting or darkroom claims.

The plates are black-and-white collection renditions. Their measured palette and
tonal values describe these web reproductions, not an original negative or print.
The source notes retain the per-image fair-use rationale, rights notice, record
link, and collection credit.

## Visual through-lines

Across portraits, intimate scenes, painted or devotional pictures, animal forms,
and rough surfaces, the selection repeatedly makes a body share authority with a
second image or object: iguanas, a tattoo, a mural, a framed portrait, a child,
a hammock, a carved lizard, or punctures in a wall. The overlays mark only those
visible relations. Their grids, convergence points, tonal divisions, and
contours are aids to reading the supplied frames, not claims about what the
photographer intended or about conditions outside the frame.
