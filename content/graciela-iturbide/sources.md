# Graciela Iturbide — source shortlist

This twelve-image working set begins with Iturbide’s two widely taught 1979 images and follows the reciprocal structures of her Juchitán, Sonoran, and self-portrait work. Every file was downloaded from the publicly displayed SFMOMA collection rendition linked below, opened successfully, and reduced only to a 1600px-long-edge teaching copy; no crop, retouching, or upscaling was performed. The resulting JPEGs are 1061–1600px on the long edge, exceeding the project’s 640px floor.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. SFMOMA’s individual object records name Graciela Iturbide and carry the notice **© Graciela Iturbide**; collection credit is recorded per work below. Each assessment is individual: this nonprofit book uses a complete but web-sized frame for transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching; the photographs are creative works; the complete frame is necessary to analyze the stated spatial relation; and the credited, record-linked web copy does not replace the museum record, original print, or licensed reproduction.

## Shortlist

### 01 — *Our Lady of the Iguanas, Oaxaca* (1979)

- **Why it matters:** The woman’s frontal silhouette is made monumental by the iguanas’ radiating, asymmetrical crown; the work is a precise lesson in how a dense top-edge cluster can stabilize a human figure.
- **Source:** [SFMOMA object 2014.366](https://www.sfmoma.org/artwork/2014.366/) (public display rendition: `https://d1hhug17qm51in.cloudfront.net/www-media/2026/06/08053148/2014.366_01_P02-web.jpg`); collection credit: Collection SFMOMA, Gift of Byron R. Meyer.

## Research context

SFMOMA’s [artist page](https://www.sfmoma.org/artist/Graciela_Iturbide/) places these works in a broad collection that includes the Juchitán, Sonoran desert, and later self-portrait series. The selected group supplies a coherent teaching progression: iconic silhouette and open desert, then paired and ritual social arrangements, then reflections and thresholds. This public-collection route replaces the earlier failed attempt: all source links and display files responded directly without authentication, paywall, or technical-access circumvention.
