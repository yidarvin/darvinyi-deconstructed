# Diane Arbus — research notes

## Scope and provenance

This chapter studies ten public, web-sized display renditions from the Princeton University Art Museum, listed individually in `sources.md`. Princeton identifies the works as photographs by Diane Arbus and retains the notice `© The Estate of Diane Arbus LLC`. The records establish dates from 1960 to 1971, titles, collection credit, and—in two cases—a later print by Neil Selkirk. They do not license a claim that the display JPEG is an original print or a facsimile of its physical surface.

## What the available record supports

The sequence supports close reading of square and nearly square portrait fields: family grouping, a three-person arrangement, frontal faces, domestic rooms, a wedding portrait, camp portrait, and the late *Untitled* setting. The Princeton record calls *A Jewish Giant at Home with his Parents in the Bronx, New York* one of Arbus's most iconic photographs. The source notes also document two wider canonical works at MoMA and the Metropolitan Museum of Art, but this chapter confines its image analysis to the ten acquired Princeton renditions.

## Technique, printing, and settings

The selected collection records do not provide camera body, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, film stock, negative process, paper, lighting plan, or exposure settings for these individual photographs. The ingested JPEGs contain no EXIF. The chapter can therefore discuss visible frontal address, shallow or open setting, tonal contrast, and the relation of body to surrounding field, but it must not turn those observations into undocumented production facts.

Two source records add limited printing information: Princeton identifies *A Flower Girl at a Wedding, Connecticut* (1964) and *Untitled (27)* (1970–71) as prints by Neil Selkirk. That is a credit for those collection objects, not evidence for a general printing method across this selection.

## Measured display-file observations

The analyzer reports a broad tonal range in the supplied reproductions. *Girl with a baseball glove* has the highest edge density in this group (0.1613), consistent with the grass and dark tree texture around the figure; *Untitled (27)* is much quieter (0.0086), where a single standing body meets an open field. *Blonde Girl with Shiny Lipstick* has the greatest measured tonal spread (standard deviation 0.3806), while *Untitled (27)* is lower (0.2218). The palette representatives are largely near-neutral—such as `#f8f4ec`, `#120d07`, `#d0ccc5`, and `#2d2826`—and describe only these web display files, not the papers or original photographic materials.

## Compositional throughline

Across the ten plates, Arbus's subjects are not isolated from their environments. Chairs, lawn, couch, room divisions, costume, glove, and open field give the portrait a social and spatial pressure. The overlays name those visible relations conservatively: intervals between figures, face-to-mouth movement, body-to-room scale, and the balance of a standing body against empty ground. They do not treat detector artifacts at print borders as photographic horizons or assume an ideal geometry that the pictures do not need.
