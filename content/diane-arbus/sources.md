# Diane Arbus — source shortlist

Arbus's square portraits make the encounter between camera and subject unusually explicit: frontal gaze, shallow space, and a carefully kept surrounding field make posture, costume, gesture, and room structure work together. This working set draws ten publicly displayed collection renditions from the Princeton University Art Museum. Each was retrieved directly from the museum's public IIIF image service, verified as an opening JPEG, and kept to a 1200px-long-edge maximum (or the smaller source maximum). The source records identify the works and display the notice `© The Estate of Diane Arbus LLC`.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. Arbus's photographs remain copyrighted; none is presented as open or public-domain material. The Estate notice is retained below for each work. Each is a distinct, limited assessment: (1) this nonprofit book makes transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching; (2) the photograph is a highly creative work; (3) the complete frame is needed to teach the particular relation of subject, setting, and edge; and (4) the linked, credited, web-sized display copy does not substitute for the museum record, an original print, or a licensed reproduction.

## Research context

The ten acquired works are supported by a wider canonical frame: MoMA's collection records identify *Child with a toy hand grenade in Central Park, N.Y.C.* (1962) and *A Puerto Rican housewife, N.Y.C.* (1964); the Metropolitan Museum of Art holds *Identical twins, Roselle, N.J.* (1966); and Princeton's record calls *A Jewish Giant at Home with his Parents in the Bronx, New York* one of Arbus's most iconic photographs. Those records, together with the ten accessible collection objects above, establish a twelve-work research set that ranges from the direct square portrait to family group, domestic interior, childhood, and late institutional photographs. MoMA: https://www.moma.org/collection/works/57781 and https://www.moma.org/collection/works/89195; Met: https://www.metmuseum.org/art/collection/search/271468; Princeton: https://artmuseum.princeton.edu/art/collections/objects/13410.
