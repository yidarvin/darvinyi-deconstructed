+Curtis made the photographs in this chapter as part of *The North American Indian* (1907–30), an enterprise whose handsome plates are inseparable from its colonial premise. The Library of Congress notes that critics have identified Curtis’s removal of signs of contemporary Indigenous life and its romanticized view of many distinct communities. These overlays therefore make only narrow claims about visible edges, masses, and spacing. They do not turn a constructed picture into neutral ethnography.

The analyzer finds an intentionally restricted tonal world rather than a uniform sepia style: *The vanishing race* is the darkest frame here (mean luminance 0.265), with brown-black #2d251e and #453427 beneath a near-white #fdfdfb highlight; *The scout in winter* is the lightest (0.730), where #dfdfdf snow isolates a nearly black horse. Across the set, Curtis repeatedly makes a figure or group carry a large, simplified field.

## corridors and low horizons

<Plate slug="edward-s-curtis" id="01-vanishing-race-navaho" label="The vanishing race: Navaho, 1904" caption="The printed field, ridge line, and canyon axis narrow the frame around the receding riders." />

In *The vanishing race*, the overlay identifies the centered canyon axis and the upper ridge, not a fabricated vanishing point. The riders remain difficult to separate in the low-key brown image, but that is the device: the canyon and road close around a receding procession. The analyzer’s edge density is only 0.026, so broad dark terrain rather than line detail creates the compression. The title’s “vanishing” language is Curtis’s colonial construction, not a fact the composition proves.

<Plate slug="edward-s-curtis" id="02-three-chiefs-piegan" label="The three chiefs: Piegan, 1900" caption="A prairie horizon and an alternating mounted rhythm turn a group portrait into a wide frieze." />

*The three chiefs* works in the opposite direction. The 0.371 prairie horizon leaves most of the 2:1 frame to sky; a phi grid is the analyzer’s better measured fit (0.585 versus 0.310 for thirds). The central horse anchor and the short mounted-rhythm path keep the three riders connected without pretending they are a symmetrical unit. Its grayscale palette runs from #2c2c2c through #d7d7d7, and mean luminance 0.615 makes the horses read as a dark band below the open sky.

<Plate slug="edward-s-curtis" id="06-scout-in-winter" label="The scout in winter: Crow, 1908" caption="The photographic field and a single horse-and-rider mass hold against an expanse of snow." />

The border in *The scout in winter* matters as a reproduced photographic field, but the ellipse does not mistake it for landscape geometry. Inside it, horse and rider make one dense silhouette while the diagonal snowy slope at left supplies the only counter-motion. Edge density is a very low 0.015; the picture survives on the contrast between #060606 and the pale #dfdfdf–#bdbdbd field. That reduction makes the pose feel suspended, not surveyed.

## enclosure, gesture, and surface

<Plate slug="edward-s-curtis" id="03-apache-mother-child" label="Apache mother and child, 1903" caption="The cradleboard arch and two overlapping enclosures keep mother and child in a compact field." />

The cradleboard’s arch is the organizing line in *Apache mother and child*. The overlay separates the cradleboard from the mother-and-child enclosure so the line does not flatten them into a single decorative shape. A dark brown field (#52281c) gives way to cloth highlights #be9875 and #d4b89a; mean luminance 0.385 and edge density 0.069 explain why face, cloth, and hoop remain readable despite the image’s soft reproduction.

<Plate slug="edward-s-curtis" id="04-apache-reaper" label="The Apache reaper, 1906" caption="A bent body, stubble edge, and lowered working gesture set the image’s direction." />

*The Apache reaper* does not need a distant vista to create depth. The ellipse names the worker’s bent body, while the marked stubble edge crosses the lower field and the gesture runs down into the crop. Its phi grid is a better measured fit than thirds (0.457 versus 0.331), but the actual structure is bodily and local. The #412612 and #673e1f shadows press against #f7d592 cloth; the set’s highest edge density among these labor and portrait plates, 0.061, comes from the wheat and garment rather than an invented horizon.

<Plate slug="edward-s-curtis" id="08-mandan-girls-gathering-berries" label="Mandan girls gathering berries, c. 1908" caption="Two overlapping figures and a marked branch diagonal make reaching into the shrubs the frame’s repeated action." />

In *Mandan girls gathering berries*, a branch diagonal descends through the right side while two ellipses identify the nearer and more distant figures. The marks are deliberately simple: they describe repeated reaching and depth through overlap, not individual intention. Mid-gray #5c5851 and #736c63 mingle with light #c8bfb0; its modest tonal spread (p05 0.318 to p95 0.769) and edge density 0.065 let foliage act as a woven surface around the bodies.

## staged silhouettes and reflection

<Plate slug="edward-s-curtis" id="05-bears-belly" label="Bear’s Belly (Apsáalooke/Crow), 1908" caption="A portrait axis, lit face, and cloak silhouette distinguish the body from the surrounding darkness." />

*Bear’s Belly* makes a frontal portrait from a face held inside a thick cloak contour. The vertical axis is a formal claim about the image’s near-bilateral construction, not an assertion about the sitter. Its palette moves from #1f1d1e and #3c342e to #e3e0d6, and the 0.240 tonal standard deviation is enough to cut the face from the fur without dissolving the heavy mass around it.

<Plate slug="edward-s-curtis" id="07-dancing-eclipsed-moon" label="Dancing to restore an eclipsed moon: Qagyuhl, 1914" caption="A ridge and central group axis support an irregular row of dancer silhouettes." />

The dancing photograph turns people, raised forms, smoke, and slope into a compressed silhouette band. The overlay’s ridge and group-center line give a reader bearings; the zigzag is deliberately a contour of the collective silhouette rather than a claim of orderly choreography. Mean luminance 0.426, a #040404-to-#bababa palette, and edge density 0.018 show how much of the motion is made by broad black forms against sky.

<Plate slug="edward-s-curtis" id="09-at-waters-edge-piegan" label="At the water’s edge: Piegan, 1910" caption="The far bank, right lodge, and curving shore edge pivot the frame between land and reflection." />

*At the water’s edge* is a more exact tonal hinge. The detected far bank sits at y=0.336 with a slight 0.9° rise; above it the lodges sit in pale air, while their inverted forms extend into the water. The right lodge is the measured saliency anchor, and the shore edge prevents the reflection from becoming a merely empty lower half. Thirds is the better grid fit (0.655); #c89675 and #daaf8b keep the water luminous against a darker #513c3d bank.

<Plate slug="edward-s-curtis" id="10-big-mouth-spring" label="Big Mouth Spring, 1910" caption="The face, garment mass, and diagonal handheld branch organize a close portrait against open ground." />

In *Big Mouth Spring*, the overlay avoids the analyzer’s false perspective suggestions and follows the visible face, garment mass, and branch. The bright background leaves the person’s shoulder and dark braid as counterweights, while the branch sends the eye out to the lower right. Mean luminance is 0.663 with a wide p05–p95 span of 0.090–0.937; #e2e2e2, #929292, and #1a1a1a describe a sharp grayscale portrait, not evidence about the sitter beyond the picture’s form.

## Exercises

<ExerciseCard n="01" title="Make a figure carry a field">
Photograph one person or one group against an open tonal field. Make the field at least half the frame. Then add only the contour or horizon that makes the empty space active.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="02" title="Separate a print from a picture">
Use a scan with a visible border, plate mark, or mount. Decide whether that edge changes the composition you want to teach. If it does, label it as a printed field; if it does not, do not let it become a false horizon or vanishing line.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="03" title="Describe without claiming a story">
Make an overlay with three visible relations: a body mass, an edge, and a tonal division. Name only what the marks can support. Remove any label that assumes a motive, identity, or historical condition not visible in the frame.
</ExerciseCard>
