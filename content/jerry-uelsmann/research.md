# Jerry Uelsmann — research note

## Record and period

The source packet records twelve Jerry Uelsmann works made from 1965 to 2012. It draws its display copies from the Jerry Uelsmann Estate portfolio, with collection-record corroboration for the 1966 and 1969 *Untitled* photographs. The sequence spans the period in which Uelsmann made his characteristic impossible spaces from photographs rather than from digitally altered files: figures, trees, rooms, water, and clouds can remain tonally convincing even when their scale or location cannot be.

## Technique and printing

The Estate identifies these as conventional wet-darkroom, archivally toned gelatin silver prints. The source record for the 1966 and 1969 *Untitled* works identifies combination or multiple-negative printing. This is the relevant technical fact for the chapter: the compositions are constructed by joining negatives in the darkroom, not by a single-camera exposure of an existing scene and not by an undocumented digital procedure.

The web reproductions show why that method matters compositionally. Their visible joins are usually not hard cut lines; Uelsmann instead lets shared cloud tones, water bands, architectural perspective, reflected tree trunks, or repeated central axes make incompatible elements feel temporarily continuous. The analysis measurements describe the supplied display copies only. For example, *Voyager* is relatively sparse (edge density 0.050), while the branch-and-figure construction in *Small Woods Where I Met Myself* is much denser (0.225); neither measurement is a claim about an original print's physical surface.

## Camera settings

The supplied source records do not document a camera body, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, film stock, negative format, or exposure setting for any individual work. The ingested files contain no capture EXIF. The chapter therefore does not infer settings from the appearance of these photographs. It confines technical claims to the documented wet-darkroom combination-printing and gelatin-silver-print context.
