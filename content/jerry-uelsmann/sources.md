# Jerry Uelsmann — source shortlist

Jerry Uelsmann made seamless, uncanny scenes by combination-printing several negatives in a conventional wet darkroom. This set follows the recurring compositional problem in that process: a believable horizon, room, body, or tree is made to carry a second, incompatible space without losing tonal continuity. All twelve files were downloaded from the public portfolio published by the Jerry Uelsmann Estate, converted from its web display WebP rendition to JPEG without resizing or retouching, and verified as opening JPEGs. The estate identifies itself as publisher and displays “© 2026 Estate of Jerry N. Uelsmann. All rights reserved”; therefore none is treated as openly licensed.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. The credited rightsholder is the Estate of Jerry N. Uelsmann. Each individual assessment below is for nonprofit, transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching; it acknowledges the photographs’ creative nature, uses the complete frame only because the composited spatial relation is the subject of analysis, and retains a small web-sized, credited copy linked to the rightsholder’s portfolio. Those copies do not replace the original, the estate’s record, or a licensed reproduction.

## Research context

The Estate’s portfolio describes the selected works as conventional wet-darkroom, archivally toned gelatin silver prints, and its sequence makes a useful cross-section of Uelsmann’s practice from 1965 to 2012. Museum records for the 1966 and 1969 *Untitled* prints independently identify the technique as combination or multiple-negative printing, confirming the central compositional concern of this source set: seamless construction, not digital alteration.
