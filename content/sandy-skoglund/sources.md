# Sandy Skoglund — source shortlist

Skoglund constructs rooms, objects, and figures as deliberately artificial environments, then fixes their saturated color, scale, and staging in a complete photographic frame. This twelve-work selection follows the artist's own public archive from the first domestic installations through the later food-material rooms. Every retrieved file is a 1280 × 960px JPEG publicly served from the artist's archive and opens locally; the direct files are web displays, not print masters.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. Each archive page expressly credits the work `© [year] Sandy Skoglund`; no open licence is stated. The artist's public archive is the authoritative display route, and each entry below uses a web-sized, complete-frame copy only for nonprofit criticism, scholarship, and transformative composition teaching. In each case, (1) the purpose is analytical rather than decorative, (2) the work is highly creative, (3) the entire frame is needed to examine the staged spatial relations, and (4) the credited, linked display JPEG does not substitute for an original, edition, or licensed reproduction. The individual notes make the frame-specific teaching purpose explicit.

## Research context

The artist's archive identifies the installations' materials and staging: for example, individually made plaster cats in *Radioactive Cats*, hand-made ceramic goldfish in *Revenge of the Goldfish*, and painted rooms with live models across the early sequence. That first-party description makes the selected full frames especially useful for a chapter on constructed realities: the composition is not discovered in an existing scene, but designed across color, object repetition, setting, and photographic viewpoint.
