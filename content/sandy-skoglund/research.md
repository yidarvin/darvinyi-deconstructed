# Sandy Skoglund — research notes

## Verified working method

The supplied first-party archive describes Skoglund's photographs as complete views of constructed installations: painted rooms, placed objects, and live models. In the early sequence it specifically identifies individually made plaster cats for *Radioactive Cats* and hand-made ceramic goldfish for *Revenge of the Goldfish*. The source set also documents the household-object installations, later material environments, and their dates from 1979 to 1994. The photographs therefore record a scene made for the camera, not an unaltered domestic scene encountered by it. [Source shortlist](sources.md)

## What the record does not establish

The available artist-archive display pages and downloaded 1280 × 960 JPEGs do not state a camera model, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, film stock, ISO/sensitivity, exposure setting, or darkroom/printing process for these twelve works. The files' dimensions identify a web-display rendition, not the original photographic negative, print, or edition. No camera or printing claim should be inferred from the files' modern metadata.

## Source and rights context

All twelve images are credited to Sandy Skoglund on the artist's public archive and are retained as web-sized complete-frame copies under the per-image fair-use rationale recorded in [sources.md](sources.md). The chapter uses them to teach composition—repetition, staged perspective, color zoning, and figure placement—rather than as decorative replacements for editions or licensed reproductions.
