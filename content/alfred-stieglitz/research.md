# Alfred Stieglitz — research

## Record and era

Alfred Stieglitz (1864–1946) is represented here by eleven works dated 1887–1911 in `sources.md`. The selection begins with the social scene *The Last Joke, Bellagio* (1887), passes through the soft-focus city and weather pictures of the 1890s and early 1900s, and reaches the urban and maritime work around 1910–11. The source package deliberately stops before the later *Equivalents* photographs, which are not part of this open source set.

## Objects, reproduction, and process

The supplied files are institutional scans from the Library of Congress, Rijksmuseum, Metropolitan Museum of Art, and Commons records named in `sources.md`. They are not a uniform set of original, uncropped photographic prints: several visibly preserve a mount, a book spread, a printed page, or a color target. Those objects are useful evidence about the reproduction, but their borders and page geometry are not attributed to Stieglitz in the overlays or chapter.

The available records establish the dates, titles, collection routes, and rights basis for the selected works, but do not establish one print process for every particular impression in this build. No cited record or ingested manifest supplies a camera body, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, film stock, or exposure for these images. Those settings are therefore not inferred.
