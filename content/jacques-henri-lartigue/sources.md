# Jacques-Henri Lartigue — source shortlist

Lartigue's earliest photographs make the Belle Époque feel less like a settled historical period than a sequence of experiments in what the small hand-held camera could catch: a body apparently suspended in air, a homemade machine, an animal-shaped patch of speed, or an elegant pedestrian set against a moving city. The selection stays with the 1905–29 childhood, leisure, aviation, racing, and street work specified for this chapter. It draws from the Art Institute of Chicago's public collection display and IIIF delivery service, which identifies each work as by Jacques-Henri Lartigue and marks the records `is_public_domain: false`. Accordingly, every use below is a documented **fair-use** use, not an assertion that the underlying work is free of copyright.

**Rights basis and source method:** Art Institute display files were obtained directly from its public IIIF service, at web size (long edge 843–1,115px as delivered), with no login, circumvention, or alteration. The Art Institute credits Lartigue (and, for the 1972 portfolio prints, printer Jean Yves du Barré); its record for *Grand Prix* gives the notice **© Ministère de la Culture - France/AAJHL**. MoMA's related collection records identify the rights holder as the Association des Amis de Jacques Henri Lartigue, and the Met's Lartigue records use **Photographie J.H. Lartigue © Ministère de la Culture - France/A.A.J.H.L.** That credited rights context is retained for each selection below.

This project uses one credited, linked, web-sized copy for nonprofit criticism, scholarship, and transformative composition teaching; it neither substitutes for a collection record, original print, nor a licensed reproduction, and it does not offer the work as a stand-alone download. ## Research context

The [Getty ULAN record](https://www.getty.edu/vow/ULANFullDisplay?subjectid=500023491) describes Lartigue's early photographs of family leisure, racing, and aviation, including his 1908–10 aviation pictures and the 1912 *Delaye Grand Prix* action image. The [Metropolitan Museum of Art record for *Motor Car on Mountain Road, France*](https://www.metmuseum.org/en/art/collection/search/285025) gives the closely related 1915 work's rights notice and discusses his deliberate high vantage and racing subject. Together with the Art Institute records, these sources support reading the selection as a chapter about motion, apparatus, and the freely constructed frame rather than as a general biography.
