# Jacques-Henri Lartigue — research

Scope: technique, era, printing, and documented camera information for the ten
selected photographs. The collection records cited in `sources.md` establish
the titles, dates, print medium, and, for nine images, a later portfolio-print
history. They do not establish camera settings, so this chapter does not infer
them.

## Era and approach

The selection runs from 1905 to 1929: childhood and family play, aviation,
street life, automobilism, and staged portraiture. The supplied Getty ULAN
record and the Art Institute collection records place these subjects in
Lartigue's early work; the linked Met record for the related 1915 motor-car
photograph confirms his sustained interest in racing and an elevated vantage.
The chapter consequently treats motion and apparatus as visual problems,
rather than treating the pictures as spontaneous snapshots without structure.

## Technique and printing

Art Institute records 47117–47125 identify plates 01–09 as gelatin silver
prints from *A Portfolio of Photographs by Jacques Henri Lartigue* (1972),
printed under Lartigue's supervision by Jean Yves du Barré. The original
exposure dates in this group run from 1905 to 1929. The Art Institute record
for *Bibi, London* (71848) identifies it as a gelatin silver print dated
October 1926; its supplied record does not state a separate print date or
printer. Those distinctions are retained rather than silently collapsing all
ten works into one print history.

## Camera settings

No verified camera model, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, film
stock, or sensitivity is supplied for this selection by the linked collection
records. The well-known optical distortion in *Grand Prix of the Automobile
Club of France* can be discussed as a visible effect, but it is not evidence
for an exact shutter or camera specification. Specific settings would be
invented and are omitted.
