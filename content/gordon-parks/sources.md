# Gordon Parks — source record

## Selected photographs

This opening selection concentrates on Gordon Parks's 1942 Farm Security
Administration/Office of War Information (FSA/OWI) study of Ella Watson and
her Washington, D.C. community.  It deliberately begins with the full frame
of *American Gothic* and then follows the related sequence through labour,
home, transit, street, and congregation; that gives the later chapter both an
iconic single-image argument and a coherent set of compositional variations. The
Library of Congress (LOC) catalog dates these records to August 1942 (except
where the catalog's broader date display is simply 1942).  The local copies
are LOC's public JPEG derivatives: nine are 1024px on the long edge and two
are 640px, the normal acquisition floor. No image has been enlarged or
retouched.

## Rights verification

Every selected item is in LOC's **Farm Security Administration/Office of War
Information Black-and-White Negatives** collection. Each item record says
“No known restrictions” and links LOC's rights statement, which says the
collection's contents are public domain and free to use and reuse. See the
[LOC collection rights and restrictions statement](https://www.loc.gov/rr/print/res/071_fsab.html).
The required credit is: **Library of Congress, Prints & Photographs Division,
Farm Security Administration/Office of War Information Black-and-White
Negatives.**

No fair-use image is used in this source pass.
