# Gordon Parks — research

Scope: technique, era, printing, and recorded camera information for the eleven Library of Congress (LOC) plates in this chapter. The account separates what the catalog records from what can only be seen in a modern scan.

## Era and assignment

These photographs belong to Gordon Parks's August 1942 study of Ella Watson, a Washington, D.C. government charwoman, and to related Washington street and church photographs made that month. The individual LOC records linked in `sources.md` supply the dates, captions, and collection identification. The selection follows Watson from federal workplace to home, then pairs that sequence with the 7th Street and St. Martin's church records; it is not a claim that these eleven frames exhaust Parks's 1942 assignment or his career.

## Technique and printing

LOC identifies every source here as part of the Farm Security Administration/Office of War Information (FSA/OWI) Black-and-White Negatives collection. The image files are contemporary LOC JPEG derivatives of the cataloged records. Several preserve an archival border, caption strip, or negative-edge marking. Those visible features are evidence about the digitized archival object, not a reason to assign a particular paper, enlarger, or historical print finish to Parks.

The source records establish a black-and-white federal-negative collection, but do not, for this set, document a per-frame camera model, lens, negative format, film stock, developer, darkroom workflow, or original print process. The chapter consequently describes tonal structure in the present scans and does not turn it into a claim about a historical print surface.

## Camera settings

No aperture, shutter speed, focal length, exposure value, or filter is recorded in the supplied LOC catalog entries. `manifest.json` contains no camera EXIF; that absence is not filled with a guess. No camera setting is stated in the chapter.

## Provenance and rights

The LOC rights statement says the FSA/OWI Black-and-White Negatives collection is public domain and free to use and reuse. The required credit is Library of Congress, Prints & Photographs Division, FSA/OWI Black-and-White Negatives. All eleven plates are sourced as open/public-domain works; no fair-use image is used.
