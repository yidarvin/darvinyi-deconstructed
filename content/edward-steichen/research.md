# Edward Steichen — research notes

## Scope and evidence

The supplied open source set concentrates on Steichen's early Pictorialist landscapes and portraits (1901–08), with *Brancusi's Studio* (1920) and *Isadora Duncan in the Parthenon, Athens* (1921) representing a later, more object- and architecture-focused turn. The dates, titles, and source routes are documented plate by plate in [sources.md](sources.md).

## Process and printing

The source record identifies *The Pond—Moonlight* as a 1904 negative printed in 1906 and describes it as a color-gum landscape. It identifies *Road into the Valley—Moonrise* as a 1904 negative printed in 1906. For the rest of this set, the supplied records give titles and dates but do not reliably specify a negative process, paper, or print process; the chapter therefore treats visible softness, color, borders, and tonal range as properties of these supplied reproductions unless the source record says otherwise.

## Camera record

No supplied source record or ingested EXIF metadata establishes a camera body, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, film stock, or exposure for these individual photographs. Those settings are deliberately omitted rather than reconstructed from the pictures.

## Compositional evidence from the ingested files

The analyzer finds especially sparse hard-edge structure in *Road into the Valley—Moonrise* (edge density 0.0083) and *The Pond—Moonlight* (0.0134), while the portrait and figure studies retain more local edge activity: *Rodin—Le Penseur* measures 0.0771, *Self-Portrait* 0.0759, and the supplied *Isadora Duncan* file 0.0678. These are measurements of the normalized source images, not claims about original camera settings or the physical prints.
