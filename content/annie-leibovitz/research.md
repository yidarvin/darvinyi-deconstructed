# Annie Leibovitz — research notes

## Scope and provenance

This chapter studies eleven public, web-sized Sotheby's display renditions: *Queen Elizabeth II, Buckingham Palace, London* (2007); *John Lennon and Yoko Ono, New York* (1980); *Whoopi Goldberg, Berkeley, California* (1984); *Meryl Streep, New York City* (1981); *R2-D2, Pinewood Studios, London* (2002); *Keith Haring, New York* (1986); *Cindy Crawford, New York* (1993); *Dan Aykroyd and John Belushi (The Blues Brothers), Hollywood* (1979); *Bruce Springsteen* (1999); *Patti Smith, New Orleans* (1978); and *John Lennon, New York City* (1970). `sources.md` records each source page, credit/rightsholder notice, and image-specific fair-use rationale. The supplied records establish titles, locations where given, and dates; they do not supply a production history for the photographs.

## What the files support

The selection spans direct portraiture, editorial staging, performance, and constructed celebrity image-making. That is a description of this source set, not a claim that eleven pictures define Leibovitz's complete practice. In the display copies, bodies are often organized by an enclosing field: palace furniture around the Queen, a pale floor around Lennon and Ono, a bathtub around Goldberg, the open crate around R2-D2, a mural around Haring, a film-strip edge around Smith, and a printed border around Lennon. These are visible compositional readings, not assertions about the photographer's intention.

## Technique, printing, and camera settings

The local source set does not document camera body, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, film stock, negative format, lighting setup, original print process, paper, print date, or darkroom procedure for any of the eleven photographs. The ingested JPEGs contain no EXIF. The sources identify photographs and public display renditions, but do not establish the technical production settings of those photographs. Discussion in the chapter therefore stays with visible framing, tonal grouping, pose, set, and the supplied reproduction's palette.

## Measured display-file observations

The analyzer reports a broad range across the web files. *Queen Elizabeth II* is the darkest by mean luminance (0.230) and is also the most edge-dense (0.1423), consistent with its many furniture, mirror, and carpet details. *Whoopi Goldberg* is the brightest (0.617) and least edge-dense (0.0128), where the tub gives the image a large nearly continuous light field. *Cindy Crawford* has the greatest tonal standard deviation (0.415), while *R2-D2* (0.353) and *Keith Haring* (0.348) also separate bright and dark strongly. The display-file palettes range from the deep near-black `#131514` in *Bruce Springsteen* to the pale `#fefefe` registered in several high-key pictures; *Patti Smith* alone yields a conspicuous orange `#e17437`. These measurements describe the supplied JPEGs, not original prints.

## Source and access boundary

Every image is used under the documented, image-specific fair-use analysis in `sources.md`: transformative criticism and composition teaching, a complete web-sized frame necessary to discuss the composition, and a credited display rendition that does not substitute for a collectible print or licensed reproduction. No reproduction is presented as open licensed, and no technical access control was bypassed.
