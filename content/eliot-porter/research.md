# Eliot Porter — research notes

## Scope and historical record

This selection spans 1938–1971. *East Penobscot Bay, Maine* (1938) is the one black-and-white counterpoint; the other eleven are color photographs made from 1950 to 1971. The Getty’s exhibition account describes Porter as a photographer who brought color photography into a fine-art and conservation argument from the 1940s into the mid-1970s. It also records his use of Kodachrome after 1938 and his willingness, in general, to adjust the brilliance, contrast, or saturation of transparencies. Those are general practices, not evidence of a particular adjustment in any plate here. See the [Getty exhibition](https://www.getty.edu/art/exhibitions/porter/) and the linked object records in [sources.md](sources.md).

## Print processes

The ten Art Institute of Chicago color selections are catalogued as dye-imbibition prints. Getty catalogues *Eastern Flicker* as a dye-transfer print. Getty catalogues *East Penobscot Bay* as a gelatin-silver print. The chapter therefore uses “dye-imbibition/dye-transfer” only for the color selection as a group, and never treats dye transfer as a capture medium or a uniform print process for every plate.

## Camera and exposure limits

The available object records do not document a camera body, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, film speed, negative format, or a per-image development/print recipe for these twelve works. Getty describes Porter’s general bird-photography setup as a tripod-mounted 4×5 sheet-film camera with synchronized strobe lamps, used for high shutter speed and a small aperture; it is not assigned to either bird image in this chapter. The EXIF attached to the two Getty display files identifies a 2005 Fujifilm FinePix S3Pro digitization, not Porter’s original exposure. Specific settings would be inventions.

## Method for the chapter

The plate commentary concerns the supplied web reproductions. Tonal measurements, palette swatches, and structural suggestions come from the deterministic analysis JSON for each ingested image; named contours and anchors were then checked against rendered overlay proofs. Source attribution, copyright notice, and the individual four-factor fair-use rationale remain in [sources.md](sources.md).
