# Imogen Cunningham — source shortlist

This pass centers on Cunningham’s 1920s plant photographs: close, sharply focused studies in which a flower or leaf becomes a complete graphic field. The Imogen Cunningham Trust identifies itself as the official archive and offers both prints and reproduction licensing for this body of work. Its catalogue and the museums named below establish title, date, and attribution context. The locally retained JPEGs are public, unaltered web-sized deliveries from the corresponding [Wikimedia Commons file pages](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:Imogen_Cunningham), each of which records the displayed historical photograph as public domain in the United States.

**Credit and rights notice:** Credit **Imogen Cunningham**; retain the named source and institutional attribution supplied by each file page. The Trust and museums remain useful sources for catalogue context and may license their own reproductions, but the four retained Commons file pages identify these displayed historical works as public-domain files. Each use below is therefore documented as **open — public domain in the United States**, not fair use.

## Shortlist

### 01 — *Magnolia Blossom* (1925)

- **Why it matters:** The large blossom is pushed almost to the picture plane, while a dark folding leaf structure makes a counter-diagonal behind it. It is a precise lesson in converting botanical detail into a near-abstract relationship of pale volume and black shape.
- **Best source:** [Imogen Cunningham Trust, *Magnolia Blossom* credit in exhibition material](https://www.imogencunningham.com/usr/documents/exhibitions/press_release_url/19/bombas-gens-brochure-copy.pdf); [public delivery file page](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:MagnoliaBlossom1925.jpg).
- **Use route — open:** The exact [Commons file page](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:MagnoliaBlossom1925.jpg) marks the historical photograph public domain in the United States (PD-US-expired). The retained JPEG is an unaltered web delivery of that file; credit remains Imogen Cunningham, with the Trust exhibition material retained as catalogue context.
- **File:** `raw/imogen-cunningham/01-magnolia-blossom.jpg` (6000 × 4758 px).

### 02 — *Two Callas* (about 1925)

- **Why it matters:** Two curling white forms rise from a velvety dark ground, making negative space and the gap between the stems as active as the flowers themselves. The pair is a clear study in repetition with controlled asymmetry.
- **Best source:** [Imogen Cunningham Trust catalogue](https://www.imogencunningham.com/art/two-callas-about-1925-by-imogen-cunningham-i); [public delivery file page](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Imogen_Cunningham,_Two_Callas,_1925.jpg).
- **Use route — open:** The exact [Commons file page](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Imogen_Cunningham,_Two_Callas,_1925.jpg) marks the historical photograph public domain in the United States (PD-US-expired). The retained JPEG is an unaltered web delivery of that file; credit remains Imogen Cunningham, while the Trust catalogue is retained for work context.
- **File:** `raw/imogen-cunningham/02-two-callas.jpg` (3354 × 4250 px).

### 03 — *Plant Form* / *Succulent* / *Sedum Cristate* (1920s; printed 1971)

- **Why it matters:** The dense, fractal plant surface fills the frame with a highly articulated texture, but its curving crest keeps that detail from becoming uniform. It offers an unusually legible example of a close crop that holds both pattern and directional movement.
- **Title and date record:** The [Norton Simon Museum](https://www.nortonsimon.org/art/detail/PH.1971.147) records its print as *Plant Form*, “1920s (printed 1971).” The [Trust catalogue entry](https://www.imogencunningham.com/artworks/8499-cunningham-sedum-cristate-1920s/) uses *Sedum Cristate* and dates it to the 1920s, while the [Commons file page](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Succulent_Imogen_Cunningham_1920.jpg) calls the image “Sedum Cristate or Succulent” and supplies 1920. This chapter follows the institutionally documented 1920s date and identifies the alternate titles rather than asserting an unsupported precise 1920 date.
- **Use route — open:** The exact Commons file page marks the historical photograph public domain in the United States (PD-US-expired). The retained JPEG is an unaltered web delivery of that file; credit remains Imogen Cunningham.
- **File:** `raw/imogen-cunningham/03-succulent.jpg` (1549 × 2000 px).

### 04 — *Water Hyacinth* (1920s; published 1932)

- **Why it matters:** The dark upper flower, light-gray central stem, and dark folded lower leaves are set against a pale field. The compact contrast between the floral mass, stem, and open ground makes the flower read as a deliberately organized form rather than a specimen.
- **Best source:** [Imogen Cunningham Trust botanical catalogue](https://www.imogencunningham.com/library-botanicals); [public delivery file page](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Water_Hyacinth,_1920s.jpg). The previously cited Trust record for *Blossom of Water Hyacinth 2* is not used here because it does not identify the displayed *Water Hyacinth* file.
- **Use route — open:** The exact Commons file page marks the historical photograph public domain in the United States (PD-US-expired). The retained JPEG is an unaltered web delivery of that file; credit remains Imogen Cunningham, with the Trust botanical catalogue retained for context.
