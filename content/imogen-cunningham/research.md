# Imogen Cunningham — research

## Era and method

This chapter focuses on four botanical close-ups made from 1920 through about 1925: *Sedum Cristate* (1920), *Magnolia Blossom* (1925), *Two Callas* (about 1925), and *Water Hyacinth* (1920s, published 1932). The dates, titles, and attribution are documented in the linked Imogen Cunningham Trust and Norton Simon Museum records in [sources.md](sources.md). The group is not presented as a single named series or a uniform historical process.

The source record supports describing these as close, sharply focused plant studies. Their tight selection, tonal contrast, and cropped forms are visual observations from the reproductions, not evidence of a particular studio arrangement, light source, lens, or exposure method.

## Prints and reproductions

The Imogen Cunningham Trust catalogue describes estate prints of *Two Callas* made from Cunningham's original negative. That establishes a current estate-print relationship; it does not document Cunningham's original print process, paper, or edition for this chapter's four works. The remaining local files are web-sized source reproductions retained under the documented fair-use rationale in [sources.md](sources.md). No common original print process is asserted.

## Camera settings

The source record supplies no camera model, lens, aperture, shutter speed, film stock, or per-frame exposure setting. The retained local files have no useful capture EXIF; the one scanner identification in the normalized manifest is modern digitization metadata, not evidence about Cunningham's equipment. No camera setting is therefore assigned.
