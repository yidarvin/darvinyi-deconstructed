# Imogen Cunningham — research

## Era and method

This chapter focuses on four botanical close-ups made in the 1920s through about 1925: *Plant Form* / *Succulent* / *Sedum Cristate* (1920s; printed 1971), *Magnolia Blossom* (1925), *Two Callas* (about 1925), and *Water Hyacinth* (1920s, published 1932). Norton Simon documents *Plant Form* as “1920s (printed 1971)”; the Trust and Commons records use the alternate *Sedum Cristate* and *Succulent* labels. The chapter therefore uses the documented 1920s date and identifies the alternate-title record rather than claiming a precise 1920 date. The group is not presented as a single named series or a uniform historical process.

The source record supports describing these as close, sharply focused plant studies. Their tight selection, tonal contrast, and cropped forms are visual observations from the reproductions, not evidence of a particular studio arrangement, light source, lens, or exposure method.

## Prints and reproductions

The Imogen Cunningham Trust catalogue describes estate prints of *Two Callas* made from Cunningham's original negative. That establishes a current estate-print relationship; it does not document Cunningham's original print process, paper, or edition for this chapter's four works. The retained local files are web-sized deliveries of Commons pages documented as open/public-domain in the United States in [sources.md](sources.md). No common original print process is asserted.

## Camera settings

The source record supplies no camera model, lens, aperture, shutter speed, film stock, or per-frame exposure setting. The retained local files have no useful capture EXIF; the one scanner identification in the normalized manifest is modern digitization metadata, not evidence about Cunningham's equipment. No camera setting is therefore assigned.
