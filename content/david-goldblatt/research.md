# David Goldblatt — research

## Scope and record

This chapter concerns the eleven credited public display renditions collected in *TJ: Johannesburg Photographs 1948–2010*. The source record identifies David Goldblatt as author, supplies titles and dates, and places the photographs between 1963 and 1973. They show Johannesburg, Orlando West, White City, Crown Mines, and Bezuidenhout Park during the apartheid era. The chapter treats the available 700px-scale JPEGs as display renditions: useful evidence for framing, tonal distribution, and spatial relations, but not substitutes for exhibition prints or authoritative print objects.

## Technique and printing

The supplied source record does not identify camera, lens, negative format, film stock, printing paper, developer, or darkroom method for any individual photograph. The black-and-white appearance of the supplied renditions is not enough to establish a particular film, printing process, or print size. No technical claim beyond the visible monochrome rendering is made here.

## Camera settings

No aperture, shutter speed, focal length, exposure index, or lighting setup is recorded in the source notes for these eleven photographs. The ingested files contain no capture EXIF. Accordingly, the chapter discusses only visible photographic decisions—distance, framing, figure scale, and the placement of architectural or retail structures—and does not infer settings from depth of field or tonal range.
