# David Goldblatt — source shortlist

This selection uses the eleven publicly displayed photographs in LensCulture’s *TJ: Johannesburg Photographs 1948–2010* project. The project identifies each work by title, date, and `© David Goldblatt`, and supplies a public 700px-long-edge JPEG rendition. These are compositionally useful, web-sized copies—large enough to read figures, thresholds, and the social spaces Goldblatt builds, but not substitutes for exhibition prints or licensed files.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. The project’s captions identify David Goldblatt as the author and carry `© David Goldblatt`; no contrary open licence is stated. Each assessment below applies the four factors to the specific image: this nonprofit book uses the complete public display frame for transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching; the photographs are creative works; the intact frame is necessary to analyze the relation of people, architecture, and social space; and the credited 700px web copy links to its host page and does not replace the original, a museum record, exhibition print, or licensed reproduction.

## Research context

LensCulture presents this as *TJ: Johannesburg Photographs 1948–2010*, an eleven-image David Goldblatt project. The bounded series gives this sourcing unit an internally consistent group of publicly visible, authoritative display copies. It also matches Goldblatt’s sustained concern with how apartheid-era social relations become visible through ordinary interiors, shops, streets, and institutional thresholds.
