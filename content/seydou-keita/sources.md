# Seydou Keïta — source shortlist

This is an eight-image, institutionally catalogued core drawn from the Art Institute of Chicago’s public collection API and IIIF image service. It reaches the chapter minimum of four with distinct examples of Keïta’s central studio strategies: patterned backdrops, textiles, props of modernity, paired and grouped sitters, and deliberately balanced poses. The Art Institute records credit Seydou Keïta (Malian, 1921–2001); its pages do not provide a specific copyright notice. The registry identifies the Seydou Keïta Estate / CAAC as the collection starting point, so these are retained as credited, public web-display images under the individual fair-use assessments below, not as open-licensed files.

All eight responses were direct public JPEGs from the Art Institute IIIF service, successfully opened after download. They are 843px on the long edge: web-sized, legible for composition study, and not a substitute for a print or licensed reproduction.

## Research context

The Art Institute identifies Keïta’s Bamako studio practice in the 1950s and records the scarcity of original studio prints; its collection holds eight Keïta works. The [Metropolitan Museum’s account of Keïta’s studio practice](https://www.metmuseum.org/exhibitions/listings/2008/african-textiles) further documents his use of props and changing textile backdrops, directly supporting the selected set’s focus on staged modernity and pattern-on-pattern composition. A public search of additional Met collection records located canonical works, but its records explicitly do not offer downloadable image files; no restricted route was pursued because the Art Institute set already supplies eight verified images.
