Seydou Keïta's portraits make the studio a place where sitters compose themselves. A patterned cloth, a crisp shirt, a radio, a handbag, or a chair is never just background or accessory: each gives the body a scale, a direction, and a surface to answer. The pictures are formally exact without feeling mechanically posed. Their calm comes from frontal attention and carefully placed hands; their energy comes from the friction of smooth skin, patterned textiles, and props of modernity. ## the group is a structure

<Plate slug="seydou-keita" id="01-untitled-c1950" label="Untitled, c. 1950" caption="A standing center and two seated figures make a three-person portrait whose dresses, jewelry, and hand positions keep the stable arrangement active." />

The three women in the first portrait are organized as a broad triangle: two seated bodies make the low base, while the standing sitter rises through the middle. The overlay names the three faces and the seated base rather than pretending the floral cloth is a landscape. The analyzer's thirds grid suits this arrangement, and its moderate edge density of 0.0447 helps explain why the faces and jewelry remain legible amid the fabrics. The warm palette ranges from pale #c4b8aa through darker #464039, giving the dark patterned dress at left and the pale central dress distinct weights.

<Plate slug="seydou-keita" id="03-untitled-portrait-c1950" label="Untitled Portrait, c. 1950; printed 2019" caption="Two seated women make a low horizontal stage, joined by one arm and grounded by the checked textile." />

The paired portrait changes the group from a triangle into a low, wide stage. Each sitter occupies an ellipse, but the short line across the linking arm matters most: it turns proximity into a visible relation. The checked blanket does not recede as neutral ground; it repeats the patterned dresses and carries the eye across the full width. With the set's highest edge density, 0.1011, and a p05-to-p95 range of 0.169 to 0.875, this picture can sustain many competing textile marks without losing the two faces.

<Plate slug="seydou-keita" id="04-untitled-1956-57" label="Untitled, 1956–57; printed 1998" caption="Three upright sitters collect around a central floral dress, held against a densely arabesque backdrop." />

The later three-person portrait is more vertical and closer to a mirror arrangement. The measured symmetry axis at x0.50 is a useful description of the group spine, not a claim that every detail is identical. The central floral dress is the pivot between the pale shirts and darker trousers, while the two outer figures lean and stand differently enough to keep the picture from becoming static. Its bright #dddcdb to dark #31302d palette and broad tonal spread make backdrop, shirts, and floral fabric distinct layers.

## props make counterweights

<Plate slug="seydou-keita" id="02-woman-with-radio" label="Untitled (Woman with Radio), c. 1950" caption="A pale robe and raised arm answer a dark radio, turning the prop into a deliberate counterweight." />

The woman with radio occupies a large pale, low-detail field: the analyzer records an edge density of only 0.0108. That restraint gives unusual authority to the dark radio and its marbled support at right. The outlined radio is not a decorative annotation; it identifies the rectangular mass that balances the robe, handbag, and raised hand. The phi grid fits the unequal division, while the palette's #433d36 dark is set against pale #c5b9ac and #b1a698.

<Plate slug="seydou-keita" id="05-untitled-portrait-1953-57" label="Untitled Portrait, 1953–57; printed 2019" caption="A frontal head and folded arms settle on a radio, making the prop a dark base beneath a patterned portrait." />

Here the radio becomes a pedestal. The portrait spine at x0.475 passes close to the face and the radio's central grille, while the folded arms make a shallow horizontal shelf above it. The cloth backdrop, head wrap, sleeves, and necklace introduce repeated curves around that direct frontal gaze. The analyzer reports a deep p05 of 0.114 against p95 0.847, so the radio can hold as a dark foundation without swallowing the sitter's face and patterned clothing.

<Plate slug="seydou-keita" id="08-untitled-undated" label="Untitled, undated" caption="An offset seated man, clasped hands, and wicker chair make diagonals answer the backdrop's checkerboard." />

The seated man is placed to the right of a large checkered backdrop rather than centered against it. The wicker chair's angled contour and his clasped hands pull the eye diagonally into the figure; the checks resist that motion with a rigid grid. The phi grid is the analyzer's best fit, and the relatively low 0.0289 edge density lets the chair, vest, and hat read as large connected shapes rather than texture alone.

## pose is a line of force

<Plate slug="seydou-keita" id="06-untitled-june-2-1955-a" label="Untitled, June 2, 1955" caption="A seated mother holds two children in a compact frontal family triangle, staged by a curtain and repeated dress pattern." />

This family portrait establishes a central axis at x0.463 but refuses a rigid bilateral balance. The mother's seated figure carries the composition; short overlay paths describe her relation to each child, who stand at different heights and distances. Curtain folds frame the group without becoming a false horizon. The tonal range is gentle, from p05 0.275 to p95 0.835, with warm browns from #dbcab7 to #4f453a, so the repeated ovals of the dress become a measured visual rhythm.

<Plate slug="seydou-keita" id="07-untitled-june-2-1955-b" label="Untitled, June 2, 1955" caption="Crossed arms and long crossed legs make the seated pose a relaxed diagonal counterpoint to chair and studio cloth." />

The other June 2 portrait works through crossing rather than frontality. One overlay path follows the arms, another the long leg toward the lower-right shoe; together they make the pose's relaxed diagonal explicit. The striped shirt provides a second, horizontal rhythm across that diagonal. This is one of the least edge-dense images at 0.0160, with a compressed palette from #d9c7b3 to #5e5243, so pose and tonal mass do more work than fine detail.

## Exercises

<ExerciseCard n="01" title="Build a portrait triangle">
Photograph three people using one standing and two seated positions. Before making the picture, decide where the three faces and the low base will fall. Give each person a different surface, plain cloth, pattern, jewelry, or jacket, so the arrangement is stable without becoming uniform.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="02" title="Make a prop carry weight">
Make one seated portrait with an object that has a clear tonal mass: a radio, bag, book, chair, or small table. Place it so it answers the sitter's head or shoulder rather than merely filling an empty corner. Compare a version where the object is incidental with one where it changes the pose.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="03" title="Let pattern and pose disagree">
Use a patterned backdrop or cloth, then pose a sitter so the body's main direction crosses the pattern's strongest rhythm. Keep the face readable and leave enough plain tonal space that the pattern supports the pose instead of consuming it.
</ExerciseCard>
