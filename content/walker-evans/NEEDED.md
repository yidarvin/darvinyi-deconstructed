# Walker Evans — needed / flagged (not cleanly public domain)

Canonical Evans images that fit the frontal / typological / signage signature but are **not** available as U.S.-government public domain. Copyright in Evans's pre-FSA and independent work is held by the Walker Evans Archive at the Metropolitan Museum of Art (Evans d. 1975; life+70 runs to 2045). Listed here for reference; do not ingest without a clean rights basis.

## License Photo Studio, New York (1934)
- **Why it matters:** The signature pre-FSA typographic frontal — a small storefront fronted by no fewer than six stacked hand-lettered signs, shot square-on with the sign plane parallel to the film; the prototype of Evans's flat-signage compositions (picture 80 in *American Photographs*, 1938).
- **Rights:** NOT public domain. Pre-FSA independent work; copyright held by the Walker Evans Archive, Metropolitan Museum of Art. The Met's record is **not** Open Access. Made 1934, so US copyright / life+70 protection persists.
- **Where a good scan lives:** MoMA — https://www.moma.org/collection/works/58957 ; Met — https://www.metmuseum.org/art/collection/search/260642 ; Getty — https://www.getty.edu/art/collection/object/104A5C

## Sidewalk and Shopfront, New Orleans (1935)
- **Why it matters:** A dead-frontal barbershop / shopfront with awning and lettered glass, the classic Evans planar storefront — would strengthen the signage/typography set.
- **Rights:** Not cleanly PD. Made during Evans's 1935 New Orleans trip but **not** present in the LOC FSA/OWI negative file (searched; no FSA negative found); it circulates as an independent print under the Walker Evans Archive / Met. Skip until a verified PD scan surfaces.
- **Where a good scan lives:** MoMA and the Met hold prints (Met Walker Evans Archive); no confirmed CC0 / PD full-res copy located.

## Subway Portraits / "Many Are Called" (1938–1941)
- **Why it matters:** Hidden-camera frontal portraits of subway riders — the ultimate Evans frontal typology of anonymous faces.
- **Rights:** NOT public domain. Made independently (not FSA), first published 1966; copyright held by the Walker Evans Archive, Metropolitan Museum of Art. Flagged so it is not mistaken for FSA-era PD material.
- **Where a good scan lives:** Metropolitan Museum of Art, Walker Evans Archive (not Open Access).
