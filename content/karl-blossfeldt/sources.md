# Karl Blossfeldt — sources

Blossfeldt photographed single plant specimens in extreme close-up against a plain neutral ground, centered and frontal — the ideal overlay test bed. Look for pure natural geometry: bilateral symmetry, logarithmic spirals, radial umbels, fractal branching, and segmented rhythm, isolated from any environmental context.

**Rights basis:** Public domain. Plates from *Urformen der Kunst* (1928) and *Wundergarten der Natur* (1932); author Karl Blossfeldt died in 1932. The 1928 plates are unambiguously PD in the US and (life+70) internationally; the whole specimen set is treated as PD here. Digitized copies below are hosted on Wikimedia Commons, most from the J. Paul Getty Museum's public-domain holdings.

## Shortlist

### 01 — Aristolochia clematitis / Aristolochia stems and leaves (birthwort) (c. 1928)
- **Why it matters:** The flagship Blossfeldt geometry — tightly coiled crozier-like stems and buds reading as near-logarithmic spirals, with a strong central vertical spine.
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Blossfeldt_-_Aristolochia_stems_and_leaves,_84.XM.142.9.jpg
- **Rights:** Public domain (Getty Museum; Blossfeldt d. 1932)
- **File:** raw/karl-blossfeldt/01-aristolochia-clematitis.jpg (6119px)

### 02 — Equisetum hyemale (rough horsetail) (c. 1928, Urformen der Kunst)
- **Why it matters:** Pure segmented vertical rhythm — a jointed stalk of repeating nodes, one specimen dead-center, the clearest possible test of a vertical axis and modular spacing.
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Plantstudie,_Equisetum_hiemale_Urformen_der_Kunst_(serietitel),_RP-F-2008-51-2170-1.jpg
- **Rights:** Public domain (Rijksmuseum, Urformen der Kunst plate; Blossfeldt d. 1932)
- **File:** raw/karl-blossfeldt/02-equisetum-hyemale.jpg (5246px)

### 03 — Adiantum pedatum (northern maidenhair fern) (1928)
- **Why it matters:** Unrolling fiddlehead — a classic logarithmic spiral fern crozier, the textbook Fibonacci curl set against plain ground.
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Karl_Blossfeldt_Adiantum_pedatum_1928.jpg
- **Rights:** Public domain (Blossfeldt d. 1932)
- **File:** raw/karl-blossfeldt/03-adiantum-pedatum.jpg (1206px)

### 04 — Impatiens glandulifera (Himalayan balsam) seed capsules (c. 1928)
- **Why it matters:** Symmetric seed capsules and stalks radiating from a node — bilateral pairs and a clean branching fork, ideal for symmetry-axis overlays.
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Blossfeldt_-_Impatiens_glandulifera,_84.XM.142.4.jpg
- **Rights:** Public domain (Getty Museum; Blossfeldt d. 1932)
- **File:** raw/karl-blossfeldt/04-impatiens-glandulifera.jpg (6218px)

### 05 — Acanthus mollis (bear's breeches) (1928)
- **Why it matters:** Deeply lobed bract with strong bilateral symmetry — the leaf that became the Corinthian capital; a mirror axis and nested lobes.
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Karl_Blossfeldt._Acanthus_mollis,_1928.jpg
- **Rights:** Public domain (Blossfeldt d. 1932)
- **File:** raw/karl-blossfeldt/05-acanthus-mollis.jpg (6220px)

### 06 — Cucurbita (pumpkin/gourd tendrils) (1928)
- **Why it matters:** Coiled climbing tendrils — springlike helical spirals, the purest curvilinear-geometry specimen in the set.
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Karl_Blossfeldt._Cucurbita,_1928.jpg
- **Rights:** Public domain (Blossfeldt d. 1932)
- **File:** raw/karl-blossfeldt/06-cucurbita.jpg (6203px)

### 07 — Dipsacus laciniatus (cut-leaved teasel) (c. 1928)
- **Why it matters:** Spiny seed head with radially arrayed bracts around a domed core — a dense radial/phyllotactic pattern about a single center point.
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Blossfeldt_-_Dipsacus_laciniatus,_84.XM.142.3.jpg
- **Rights:** Public domain (Getty Museum; Blossfeldt d. 1932)
- **File:** raw/karl-blossfeldt/07-dipsacus-laciniatus.jpg (6224px)

### 08 — Blumenbachia hieronymi (Loasaceae) (c. 1928)
- **Why it matters:** Star-shaped seed capsule with helically twisted ribs — radial symmetry plus a superimposed rotational twist, a rich overlay subject.
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Blossfeldt_-_Blumenbachia_hieronymi_(Loasaceae),_84.XM.142.12.jpg
- **Rights:** Public domain (Getty Museum; Blossfeldt d. 1932)
- **File:** raw/karl-blossfeldt/08-blumenbachia-hieronymi.jpg (6192px)

### 09 — Allium ostrowskianum (ornamental garlic) (c. 1928)
- **Why it matters:** Umbel of stalks fanning from one point into a near-spherical head — radial burst geometry from a single anchor node.
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Blossfeldt_-_Allium_ostrowskianum,_Knoblauchpflanze,_84.XM.142.7.jpg
- **Rights:** Public domain (Getty Museum; Blossfeldt d. 1932)
- **File:** raw/karl-blossfeldt/09-allium-ostrowskianum.jpg (6228px)

### 10 — Aesculus parviflora (bottlebrush buckeye) (c. 1928)
- **Why it matters:** Branching bud/twig showing fractal-like bifurcation — repeated forking against plain ground, a clean test of branching-tree overlays.
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Blossfeldt_-_Aesculus_parviflora,_84.XM.142.8.jpg
- **Rights:** Public domain (Getty Museum; Blossfeldt d. 1932)
- **File:** raw/karl-blossfeldt/10-aesculus-parviflora.jpg (6213px)

### 11 — Papaver orientale (Oriental poppy) seed capsule (c. 1928–1932)
- **Why it matters:** Canonical radial seed-head — a ringed crown of stigmatic rays around a single domed center, a textbook rotational/radial-symmetry test about one anchor point.
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Karl_Blossfeldt._Papaver_orientalis,_1928_-_1932.jpg
- **Rights:** Public domain (Blossfeldt d. 1932)
- **File:** raw/karl-blossfeldt/11-papaver-orientale.jpg (6207px)

### 12 — Seseli gummiferum (moon carrot) umbel (c. 1928)
- **Why it matters:** Young compound umbel — a single ribbed stalk forking into a candelabra of radiating rays, each tipped with a bud; branching-plus-radial symmetry about one central vertical axis.
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Blossfeldt_-_Seseli_gummiferum,_84.XM.142.1.jpg
- **Rights:** Public domain (Getty Museum; Blossfeldt d. 1932)
- **File:** raw/karl-blossfeldt/12-seseli-gummiferum.jpg (6223px)
