# Robert Frank — source shortlist

This working set uses twelve direct, public-display JPEG renditions served by the Art Institute of Chicago's IIIF service. They were checked as opening JPEG files; every long edge is 843px or greater, above the project's 640px floor. Eleven images concentrate on Frank's 1955–56 American road-trip work and one early Spanish image establishes the compressed, off-center vocabulary from which *The Americans* emerged.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. The Art Institute's object records identify the works as © Robert Frank and supply the collection credit recorded below. Each entry is a separate four-factor assessment: this nonprofit book makes a transformative use for criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching; the underlying photograph is creative; the complete frame is necessary because the chapter analyzes its spatial and visual relations; and only a credited, record-linked, web-sized display copy is used, which does not substitute for a museum record, original print, or licensed reproduction. The page links to the authoritative collection record and preserves its copyright notice.

## Research context

The National Gallery of Art's *Looking In: Robert Frank's The Americans* describes the 1958/59 book as the product of the artist's 1955–56 Guggenheim-supported journey, drawn from more than 25,000 exposures and edited to 83 photographs: https://www.nga.gov/exhibitions/looking. Its account emphasizes the deliberately immediate, off-kilter manner through which Frank connected cars, diners, politics, race, work, and public ritual. This acquired collection-record set makes those compositional problems directly available at restrained web resolution, while pointing readers to the institutional records for the works and their rights information.
