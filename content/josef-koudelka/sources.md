# Josef Koudelka — source shortlist

Josef Koudelka's early work moves between the close, social pressure of the *Gypsies (Cikáni)* photographs, the public shock of Prague in 1968, and the later, spare allegories of *Exiles*. This selection keeps those bodies in view: the crowded rituals and domestic interiors establish his use of proximity and unstable group space; the Prague pictures make political fracture legible through signs and emptied ground; and *Exiles* opens the frame into displacement, distance, and symbolic landscape. All twelve files are direct, public web-display JPEG renditions from the Art Institute of Chicago's collection records and open successfully at 843px on their long edge.

### 01 — *(Proč lidé umírali [Why did people die])* (August 21/27, 1968)

## Research context

The Art Institute's Josef Koudelka collection search identifies the *Gypsies (Cikáni)* works alongside images from *Exiles* and the August 1968 Prague sequence: https://www.artic.edu/artists/36327/josef-koudelka. The selected set therefore follows the registry's requested Prague–*Gypsies*–*Exiles* arc without treating any single image as a substitute for the others: it develops from early sculpting of crowds and interiors to political surfaces and then to the expansive, unsettled landscapes of exile.
