# Josef Koudelka — research note

## Record and period

The supplied Art Institute of Chicago records place this selection across Koudelka's early work, the Prague sequence of August 1968, and the later *Exiles* photographs. The dates in the source notes run from *Poland* (1958) and *Prague, Czech Republic* (1959), through the *Gypsies (Cikáni)* works of 1962–66, to *Ireland* from *Exiles* (1972). Several records distinguish exposure date from a later print date: the *Gypsies* plates here are listed as printed in 1967, while the two *Exiles* plates are listed as printed in the 1980s. Those dates establish the historical span of this selection; they do not establish a uniform negative or printing process.

## Technique and visual method

The source shortlist supports a description of visible method rather than an unverified equipment history. Koudelka repeatedly makes a social space legible by putting a hard directional structure beside bodies: road against crowd in Jarabina, doorway against three seated or standing men in Kadaň, instruments against faces in Strážnice, and cart harness against a bicyclist in *Czechoslovakia*. Elsewhere he lets a small human presence contend with a large field: the lit route in *Prague*, the child in white in *Utěkač*, the wrapped woman under an open sky in Rakúsy, and cropped legs above a wire tangle in *Poland*.

The composition analyses substantiate that range in the supplied reproductions. The early *Prague* image is the sparsest-looking dark field (mean luminance 0.133; edge density 0.061), while Jarabina is the most edge-dense in this set (0.166), owing to road, crowd, buildings, and the foreground figure. Rakúsy is the lightest and least textural plate (mean 0.668; edge density 0.039), a useful numerical counterpart to its large sky. These are observations about the web reproductions, not measurements of original prints.

## Printing, camera, and settings

The supplied source notes record print dates for selected objects, but do not document a per-image print process, camera model, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, film stock, negative format, paper, or developer. The ingested JPEGs have empty EXIF fields. It would therefore be false to infer settings from grain, contrast, blur, or depth of field. This chapter keeps technical claims to what the records and visible structure support.
