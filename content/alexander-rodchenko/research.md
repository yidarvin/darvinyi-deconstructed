# Alexander Rodchenko — research

Scope: technique, era, printing, and documented camera information for the four
selected works. This note distinguishes collection-record facts from
composition readings made in the chapter.

## Era and approach

The selection spans 1925–34: *Fire Escape (with a Man)* (1925), *Radio
Listener* (1929), *Pioneer with a Bugle* (1930), and *Girl with a Leica*
(1934), as recorded in [the source notes](sources.md). SFMOMA identifies
Rodchenko as a painter, graphic designer, writer, and photographer working at
the forefront of Russia's post-1917 avant-garde. It says he began photography
in 1925 with a camera bought in Paris, and describes the 1925 fire-escape
photograph as an early experiment in an unusual point of view and unstable
perspective. The same record connects his angled perspectives to a program of
learning to see differently. [SFMOMA, *Fire Escape*](https://www.sfmoma.org/artwork/96.191/)

## Technique and printing

The collection records document *Pioneer with a Bugle* (1930) as a gelatin
silver print at MoMA and *Fire Escape* (1925) as a gelatin silver print at
SFMOMA. [MoMA, *Pioneer with a Bugle*](https://www.moma.org/collection/works/56414)
[SFMOMA, *Fire Escape*](https://www.sfmoma.org/artwork/96.191/)
The public display pages used for *Radio Listener* and *Girl with a Leica* do
not, in the project’s recorded source set, establish a print process. This
chapter therefore does not assign either a print medium.

## Camera information

The available record establishes only that Rodchenko had bought a camera in
Paris by the start of his photographic work in 1925. It gives no verified
camera model, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, film stock, or
sensitivity for this selection. The title *Girl with a Leica* describes the
pictured subject's camera; it is not evidence for the camera used to make the
photograph. Specific exposure or equipment claims would be invented and are
omitted.
