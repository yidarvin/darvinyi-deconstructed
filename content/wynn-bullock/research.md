# Wynn Bullock — research note

## Scope and record-bound facts

This chapter uses twelve public, web-sized collection displays, each documented as a fair-use teaching copy in [sources.md](sources.md). The selection runs from *Solarized Nude* (c. 1940s) through the two 1960s *Color Light Abstraction* photographs. The Amon Carter Museum records credit the Bullock Family Photography LLC for the works it holds; the Art Institute of Chicago records its Bullock works as non-public-domain. The individual record links, credits, dates, and per-image four-factor rationales are retained in the source notes.

The available records support discussion of the visible sequence: figures placed in woodland and architectural frames; still life treated as a bilateral field; sea and pilings organized as tonal bands; and later colour works that make streaked light the subject. The dates in the source notes identify the historical span, but they do not by themselves establish an unrecorded production method for any individual image.

## Technique, printing, and settings

The source records assembled for this chapter do not state a camera body, lens, format, film stock, aperture, shutter speed, exposure, development method, paper, print process, or printing date for most individual photographs. The one supplied qualification—*Woman and Thistle* printed before 1971 and *The Pilings* printed before 1970—does not identify a printing process. The ingested display JPEGs contain no EXIF. No camera setting, darkroom procedure, or light source is inferred from apparent grain, contrast, solarisation, or blur.

The chapter may identify visible tonal reversal in *Solarized Nude*, the obscuring pane and cobweb marks in *Nude behind Cobwebbed Window*, and the directional colour in the 1961 and 1964 abstractions. Those are readings of the displayed images, not claims about undocumented technique beyond what is visibly present.

## Measured display-file observations

The analyzer finds two especially active natural screens: *Child in Forest* has edge density 0.2966 and *Child on Forest Road* 0.2924. By contrast, the colour abstractions are sparse in detected edges—0.0329 for #1006 and 0.0290 for 1185—so their force is carried by broad colour currents rather than descriptive detail. The darkest display rendition here is *Burnt Chair* (mean luminance 0.166; p05 0.039; p95 0.518); its palette runs from near-black `#12100f` through gray `#6f6f6e`. *Solarized Nude* has a wider highlight reach (p95 0.941) with `#373536` and `#e4e5de` as representative dark and light values.

For the colour pair, the normalized #1006 file is organized around blue-violet representatives `#19253c`, `#1e2d53`, and `#494466`; 1185 moves from deep teal `#021f2f` to rust `#6f3c33` and `#a35c38`. These measurements describe the supplied web reproductions, never the original prints.
