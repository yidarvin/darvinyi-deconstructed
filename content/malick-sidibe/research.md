# Malick Sidibé — research note

## Scope and documented context

The source packet identifies this eleven-image selection as Malick Sidibé photographs made between 1962 and 1976. The Guardian's captioned display gallery supplies the individual titles and dates; the Fondation Cartier exhibition record supplies the broader context of Sidibé's Bamako youth, dance, party, and studio practice. These records establish a subject and period for the selection, but not a uniform technical history for every negative or print.

## Technique and printing

The supplied source records describe public display renditions and identify the photographer and rights holder, but do not document the negative format, film stock, developer, paper, original printing process, or print date for each of these eleven works. The monochrome source JPEGs are therefore treated as present display derivatives. Their visible tonal structure, framing, flash-like contrast, studio curtains, floors, and open-air settings can be discussed as pictured facts; none is treated as proof of a particular camera or darkroom process.

## Camera settings

No supplied record identifies a camera body, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, sensitivity, lighting setup, or exposure setting for these works. The normalized manifest has no capture EXIF. The chapter does not infer settings from the look of the photographs; it confines its technical observations to measured tonal values and visible compositional relations.

## Rights and source use

Every plate uses the per-image fair-use rationale recorded in `sources.md`: a credited, linked, web-sized display rendition is retained for transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching, and does not substitute for an original print, collection record, or licensed reproduction.
