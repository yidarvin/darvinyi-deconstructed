# Julia Fullerton-Batten — source shortlist

This twelve-image working set concentrates on *Teenage Stories* (2005), a defining early series in which Fullerton-Batten photographs street-cast girls at model-village scale. The artist’s project page identifies every title and explains that the deliberately oversized figures, static direction, and location work turn ordinary suburban actions into an adolescent fantasy world. Each file below was downloaded directly from the artist’s publicly accessible portfolio, opens as a JPEG, preserves the published whole frame, and measures at least 870 × 1100px.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. The artist’s portfolio footer states “Copyright © 2026” and its commented rights notice identifies Julia Fullerton-Batten and reserves reproduction rights; no open licence was offered. Each assessment below is separate: this nonprofit book uses a credited, linked, web-sized copy for transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching; the complete published frame is needed to analyze the photograph’s scale, staging, lighting, and spatial construction; and the copy does not replace the artist’s work, a print, or a licensed reproduction.

## Research context

The artist’s [*Teenage Stories* project page](https://juliafullerton-batten.com/types/teenage-stories-2005/) supplies the image titles and project statement. It explains that the girls were photographed on location in model villages, directed to remain static and unemotional, and staged to visualize adolescent fantasy and vulnerability. The artist’s [biography](https://juliafullerton-batten.com/info/) characterizes her broader practice as cinematic visual storytelling made through staged tableaux, unusual locations, street-cast models, and sophisticated lighting. Together, the selected full-frame web displays provide a compact, teachable account of those compositional methods without substituting for the original works.
