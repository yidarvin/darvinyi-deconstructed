## Required fixes

1. **04-self-portrait-garden.jpg** — chapter.mdx body text (paragraph under "the staged tableau") and the plate caption both claim: "a leading line traces the ladder's diagonal from (0.093, 0.52) up to (0.214, 0.104), a line the analyzer independently confirms: it finds a vanishing point at x equals 0.159, y equals 0.714 built from four converging diagonals, almost exactly where that ladder line points" (caption repeats this as "confirmed by the analyzer's own vanishing point near x0.16, y0.71"). This is not true. Extrapolating the ladder's own detected line (analysis `lines` entry with the identical endpoints, angle -77.94°) through the analyzer's reported vanishing_point y (0.7144) lands at x ≈ 0.037, not x ≈ 0.159 — a 0.12 miss, four times the 0.03 coordinate tolerance. The perpendicular distance from the reported VP (0.1585, 0.7144) to the ladder line is ≈0.148 (14.8% of frame) — clearly not an inlier — while four *other* diagonal lines, all part of the trellis lattice behind Bayard (confirmed visually in the proof, and the overlay's own notes call out "one cell of the lattice"), sit within 0.0–0.07 of the VP and are almost certainly the actual "four converging diagonals" the algorithm used. So the analyzer's vanishing point is real and correctly transcribed as a number, but it is built from the trellis lattice, not the ladder, and the ladder does not point toward it. The prose should either (a) attribute the vanishing point to the trellis lattice's own diagonal bracing rather than the ladder, and describe the ladder's leading line as a separate, hand-picked compositional observation not corroborated by that specific VP, or (b) drop the "independently confirms / almost exactly" framing entirely. The leading_line primitive itself is fine and correctly traces the visible ladder in the photograph — only the prose's causal claim about it needs correcting.
