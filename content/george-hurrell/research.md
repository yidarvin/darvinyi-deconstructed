# George Hurrell — research note

## Scope and records consulted

The local source dossier identifies ten George Hurrell publicity and magazine portraits dated from 1930 through 1942: Conrad Nagel (1930), Jean Harlow (1933), Jean Parker (1935), Joan Crawford (1936), Carole Lombard (c. 1937), Bette Davis and Olivia de Havilland (1938), Rita Hayworth (1942), plus 1930s portraits of Karen Morley and Myrna Loy. The source records are Wikimedia Commons file pages, credited to Hurrell and the named publication or studio collection; each is recorded there as public domain. This chapter uses the locally acquired, normalized display files described in [sources.md](sources.md).

## Visual method and era

These are studio and publicity portraits from the classical Hollywood period, not a continuous record of one sitting or one production process. The chapter’s claims concern visible construction: face, hair, costume, chair, print border, shadow, and the measured tonal relationships in the supplied files. The repeated visual strategy is to make the face legible within a deliberately organized supporting mass: a magazine masthead and lapels for Davis, a tufted chair for Hayworth, a projected shadow for Parker, or a pale printed margin for Morley. Those are readings of the photographs and their composition analyses, not claims about unrecorded direction or intent.

## Process, printing, and camera settings

The supplied source records document titles, dates or date ranges, credits, publication context, and public-domain status. They do not document a camera model, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, film stock, negative format, lighting equipment, or darkroom recipe for these individual photographs. The normalized manifest has no reliable capture EXIF: several web-file timestamps are plainly later digitization metadata, not evidence for historic capture dates or settings. No camera setting, lighting setup, or print process is inferred here from contrast, soft focus, grain, or shadow.

It is supportable to describe the visible lighting effects, pose, printed reproduction, costume, and tonal organization. It is not supportable from this source set to name the equipment or exposure that produced them.
