# Lori Nix / Kathleen Gerber — research note

## What the supplied sources establish

This selection is drawn from *The City* (2005–15): *Museum of Art* and
*Natural History* (2005), *Majestic* (2006), *Library* and *Aquarium* (2007),
*Botanic Garden* and *Laundromat* (2008), *Bar* (2009), *Violin Repair Shop*
(2011), *Circulation Desk* and *Subway* (2012), and *Observatory* (2015). The
artist portfolio supplies those titles, dates, and the public-display files used
here. The Nerman Museum describes the sequence as a post-apocalyptic city in
which nature reclaims built interiors; its later works are co-credited to Lori
Nix and Kathleen Gerber. [Source notes](sources.md)

Nix designs handmade miniature dioramas and Gerber fabricates them. Accounts of
their process describe sets built for a single camera position, with lighting,
object placement, and diffusion adjusted across repeated photographs. Reported
materials include basswood, polymer clay, plaster, styrene, paper pulp, foam,
sawdust, acrylic paint, casting resin, and Plexiglas. The sources describe an
8×10 large-format film/view-camera practice for this method. They do **not**
establish a lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, sensitivity, or an
exposure for any plate in this chapter.

The available print record is work-specific rather than uniform: *Library*
(2007) is catalogued by Hood Museum as a chromogenic color print; Bau-Xi lists
*Botanic Garden* as a chromogenic print in an edition of 15; Spencer lists
*Observatory* (2015) as an inkjet print. Those facts do not justify assigning one
printing process to the entire series. The ingested JPEG metadata identifies some
scanning equipment and dates, not original camera settings or print production.

## Method for this chapter

The chapter treats the miniature construction, single-viewpoint method, and
posthuman civic interiors as documented context. Its overlays make only visible
claims about the supplied whole-frame reproductions: axes, rails, frames, object
clusters, and the apertures through which the rooms admit weather. Tonal and
palette observations come from the deterministic analyses; they are descriptions
of these web-display derivatives, not claims about an original print. Per-image
fair-use rationale, credit, and source links remain in the [source notes](sources.md).
