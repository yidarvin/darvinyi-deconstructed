# Lori Nix — source shortlist

This source set centers on *The City* (2005–15), Nix’s sequence of meticulously built, unpeopled interiors in which vegetation, water, and deterioration reclaim civic spaces. The artist’s public portfolio identifies every title and date below and serves a complete-frame 1500px JPEG. All twelve files were retrieved directly from that publicly visible portfolio and opened successfully; no file is below the 640px long-edge floor.

## Research context

Nerman Museum describes *The City* as post-apocalyptic photographs of libraries, casinos, restaurants, and laundromats made from elaborate, hand-built dioramas, and notes that Nix and Gerber began co-crediting works shortly after its 2016 exhibition: https://www.nermanmuseum.org/exhibitions/2016-02-04-nix-lori-the-city.html. The Museum of Fine Arts, Houston similarly describes Nix’s constructed models as spaces that evoke a post-apocalyptic future: https://emuseum.mfah.org/objects/73678/paradise. The selected sequence preserves the chapter’s central compositional argument: civic interiors begin as ordered, perspective-driven systems and are then destabilized by water, plants, collapse, and absence.
