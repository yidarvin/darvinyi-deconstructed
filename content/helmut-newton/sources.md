# Helmut Newton — source shortlist

Newton’s fashion pictures make editorial assignment into a charged scene: hard flash, a deliberately shallow stage, multiplied selves, and a push-pull between posed control and theatrical action. This twelve-image working set begins with the *Elle*, *Nova*, and *Marie Claire* magazine pictures foregrounded by the Newton Foundation’s *Newton: Fired* exhibition, then extends to a 1983 portrait and a 1996 *US Vogue* image from the Foundation’s permanent loan selection. Each is a direct, publicly served, web-sized JPEG from the official Helmut Newton Foundation exhibition pages and was checked as an opening JPEG. The files are 720px on the short edge (and 714–1082px on the long edge), adequate for composition teaching but not a print substitute.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. The official source’s footer states “© 2026 HELMUT NEWTON FOUNDATION. All rights reserved”; the Foundation preserves and exhibits Newton’s work, and the magazine commissions may also carry publisher or other rights not independently asserted here. Each entry therefore has an individual, deliberately limited four-factor assessment: (1) nonprofit, transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching; (2) a highly creative photographic work; (3) the complete frame is necessary for the stated spatial analysis; and (4) a credited, linked, web-sized display copy cannot replace the Foundation record, an original print, or a licensed reproduction.

## Research context

The Newton Foundation’s *Newton: Fired* description identifies the 1960s–70s editorial sources (*Elle*, *Queen*, *Nova*, and *Marie Claire*) and explains that the *Nova* pictures include intentionally aggressive, staged “naughty girls”; it also situates the later *Big Nudes* as an important endpoint of Newton’s treatment of forceful female figures. The Foundation’s permanent-loan page identifies the collection as Newton’s own donation, preserved by the Stiftung Preußischer Kulturbesitz, and describes its fashion, nude, and portrait holdings. The selected files do not try to reproduce a monograph: they establish a teachable sequence of hard flash, staged agency, mirror recursion, social tableau, and portrait scale from authoritative public exhibition displays.
