verdict: revise

Fresh-eyes review of all 11 plates (proofs, overlays, analysis JSON, sources.md, research.md, chapter.mdx). Overlay geometry is sound throughout — every primitive traces the feature its label names, primitive counts stay in the 3-5 pedagogical range, and no camera settings are fabricated (the Callout is scrupulously honest about the wet-plate record). The problems are all in chapter.mdx's prose: two elapsed-time claims are off by roughly 2x against the dates the chapter itself gives, and three "the analyzer says this is the most/widest/only X in the chapter" superlatives are contradicted by the analyzer numbers printed elsewhere in the very same chapter (in one case the contradiction is explicit — one paragraph says "the one... in the whole chapter" and a later paragraph says "again"). All five required findings below were independently re-derived and confirmed in a second pass, including recomputing every elapsed-time claim by hand and rechecking all 11 palettes for achromaticity.

## Required fixes

1. **01-lincoln-cracked-plate** — "Lincoln sat for this portrait on February 5, 1865, five weeks before the assassination." Lincoln was assassinated April 14, 1865. February 5 to April 14 is 68 days, about 9.7 weeks (roughly two and a half months), not five weeks. Fix the elapsed-time figure (or drop the specific count and say "ten weeks" / "about two and a half months").

2. **09-crowd-second-inauguration** — "At the second inauguration, three weeks before Lincoln's death." The second inauguration was March 4, 1865; the assassination was April 14, 1865 — 41 days, about 5.9 weeks (roughly six weeks), not three. Fix the elapsed-time figure.

3. **03-ruins-richmond-bridge** — "measures the busiest ground of any landscape here, an edge density of 0.079." This is false by the chapter's own analysis JSON: 05-burnside-bridge-antietam has edge_density 0.182 (more than double), 07-dunker-church-dead-antietam has 0.082, 08-execution-scaffold-conspirators has 0.092, and 09-crowd-second-inauguration has 0.107 — all higher than 03's 0.079. This is even confirmed by the chapter's own later paragraph on 05, which states "the busiest ground in the entire chapter" belongs to 05. Drop or correct the superlative; 03 is not the busiest ground in this set even restricted to the bridge/landscape plates.

4. **05-burnside-bridge-antietam** and **08-execution-scaffold-conspirators** — 05's paragraph claims it is "the darkest and widest-ranging print in the set," citing std 0.29; 08's paragraph separately says its own std of 0.34 is "the widest here after Burnside Bridge" (i.e., second to 05). But 0.34 > 0.29 — 08 is actually the widest-ranging plate in the whole chapter (analysis std: 08 = 0.339, 09 = 0.319, 05 = 0.289, third place). "Darkest" is fine (05 does have the lowest mean, 0.43), but the "widest-ranging ... in the set" claim for 05 and the "after Burnside Bridge" ranking for 08 are backwards and contradict each other. Fix both paragraphs to reflect 08 as the widest std, 09 second, 05 third.

5. **08-execution-scaffold-conspirators** and **11-lincoln-mcclellan-tent** — 08's paragraph calls its five-gray palette "the one fully achromatic set in the whole chapter." 11's paragraph later calls its own palette "the palette again fully achromatic" — directly contradicting 08's "the one." Checking every palette in analysis/*.json confirms four plates (01, 08, 09, 11) are fully neutral gray (R=G=B in every swatch), not one. Remove the uniqueness claim in 08, or reword to acknowledge the chapter has several achromatic plates.

## Advisory (non-blocking)

- **02-lewis-powell-conspirator** — "in the prison yard two months after Lincoln's last sitting." Feb 5 to Apr 27, 1865 is closer to 11.5 weeks (~2.7 months). A generous rounding but not the ~2x errors above; leave as editorial judgment call.
- **06-lincoln-mcclellan-antietam** — "the highest symmetry score of any Gardner landscape in this set" (0.916). Depending on how "landscape" is scoped, 08-execution-scaffold-conspirators (0.944) may be higher; the chapter never defines the category boundary, and the margin is small (0.028, near the coordinate tolerance). Worth a wording pass but not blocking.
- **07-dunker-church-dead-antietam** — "twelve converging diagonals, more than any other plate here." 09-crowd-second-inauguration has thirteen (and is correctly credited elsewhere in the chapter as "the most of any plate in the chapter"). Off by one; doesn't change the compositional reading of either image.
- **manifest.json** — images 06, 07, and 10 carry leftover EXIF from digitization scanning equipment (Sinar/Phase One backs, ISO/ExposureTime/DateTimeOriginal fields from ~2010 scans, not the original wet-plate exposures). Chapter.mdx correctly never cites these, so no reader-facing harm, but worth scrubbing from the manifest to avoid a future accidental citation.
