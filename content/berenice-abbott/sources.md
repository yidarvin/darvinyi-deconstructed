# Berenice Abbott — source shortlist

This source pass concentrates on Berenice Abbott’s *Changing New York* work for the Federal Art Project: a deliberately varied group of street canyons, elevated viewpoints, traffic fields, industrial structures, and commercial surfaces. The New York Public Library’s collection describes the project as Abbott’s 1935–38 WPA documentation of New York City. Every selected record is marked by NYPL as believed to be in the public domain in the United States; the corresponding Wikimedia Commons files are additionally labelled public domain / `PD-US-WPA`. The local JPEGs are direct, unaltered public mirrors from those Commons records, acquired on 2026-07-17.

**Open-use basis and credit:** For each image below, the use basis is **open**. NYPL’s rights statement says it believes the item is public domain under United States law and asks, though it does not require, the credit “From The New York Public Library” with a link back to the item. Credit the photographer as **Berenice Abbott** and the holding institution as **The Miriam and Ira D. Wallach Division of Art, Prints and Photographs, The New York Public Library**. The Commons mirror’s explicit public-domain designation is corroborating delivery metadata, not a replacement for NYPL’s record.
