# Berenice Abbott — research note

## Scope and sources

This chapter studies ten photographs from Berenice Abbott's *Changing New York*, made for the Federal Art Project between 1935 and 1938. The source records identify the project as WPA documentation, not as Farm Security Administration work. The selected files are open-use public-domain deliveries: the New York Public Library believes each Federal Art Project image is public domain in the United States, and the linked Wikimedia Commons files are labelled `PD-US-WPA`.

The photographs are credited to Berenice Abbott and held by The Miriam and Ira D. Wallach Division of Art, Prints and Photographs, The New York Public Library. NYPL requests the credit line “From The New York Public Library.” Titles, dates, source records, delivery routes, and image dimensions are recorded in [sources.md](sources.md).

## What the record supports

The source set documents an urban method rather than a single subject: street canyons, commercial façades, elevated traffic views, industrial construction, and advertising surfaces. The ten plates make visible choices of viewpoint, scale, edge direction, repetition, and tonal contrast. The composition analyses measure those features in the ingested scans; the chapter treats them as evidence about the present files, not as a substitute for catalog research.

No source record or retained image metadata identifies Abbott's camera, lens, negative format, film stock, aperture, shutter speed, exposure, or printing process for these plates. The manifest contains no original camera EXIF; the two modern dates retained in download metadata belong to the delivery files, not to Abbott's exposures. The chapter therefore makes no claim about particular settings or process.
