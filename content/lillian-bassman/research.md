# Lillian Bassman — research notes

## Scope and provenance

This compact chapter uses four intact, public gallery display JPEGs from Joseph Bellows Gallery's 2007 Lillian Bassman selected-works exhibition. The records identify work made for *Harper's Bazaar* in Paris and New York between April 1949 and 1954. `sources.md` preserves each direct display link, title, model, location, garment where supplied, publication, date, copyright notice, and image-specific fair-use rationale. ABC News's public Edwynn Houk Gallery slideshow supplies the estate/courtesy attribution for its displayed material; the Metropolitan Museum of Art record for *[Beauty Study]* (ca. 1950, object 933648) gives “© Estate of Lillian Bassman.”

## What the record supports

The four pictures are mid-century fashion-editorial work, not a survey of Bassman's complete career. The selected frames visibly use high-contrast tonal grouping, hats and dresses as enclosing shapes, cropped light, pose, and reflective or glossy passages. Those are formal readings of these reproductions, not claims about Bassman's intention or a universal method: image 01 sets a bright veiled figure between dark presences, 02 makes a giant hat enclose face and hand, 03 joins hat, glove, shoulder, and a diffuse reflection, and 04 unsettles its central figure with adjacent and doubled faces.

## Technique, printing, and camera settings

The consulted local source set does not document a camera body, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, film stock, negative format, lighting setup, original print process or paper, print date, or darkroom procedure for these four photographs. The 535px-long-edge gallery JPEGs contain no EXIF. Visible blur, grain, contrast, and the editorial setting cannot establish a soft-focus process, exposure choice, or printing recipe. The chapter therefore discusses only observable effects in the supplied files. The gallery identifies objects as gelatin silver prints, but that collection metadata does not establish the processing history of these web reproductions.

## Measured display-file observations

The analyzer finds a broad tonal range throughout the supplied grayscale files. Image 01 has the highest mean luminance (0.512) and a high standard deviation (0.376), matching its large bright dress against dark figures. Image 02 is the darkest overall (mean 0.308) and least edge-dense (0.0364), consistent with its almost continuous hat mass. Image 03 is the densest (0.0940), where hat, glove, face, and glossy background make many small transitions. Image 04 sits between them (mean 0.329; edge density 0.0531) with palette representatives from near-black `#1c1c1c` to pale `#e5e5e5`. These values describe the gallery display copies, not the historical prints.

## Source and access boundary

All four files are documented, compositionally essential low-resolution exceptions: 535 × 377, 535 × 395, 535 × 411, and 535 × 405px. They are not upscaled. Each is used under the specific fair-use analysis in `sources.md`: nonprofit transformative criticism and teaching; a complete frame needed for the composition claim; and a credited web-sized display file that cannot replace a print or licensed reproduction.
