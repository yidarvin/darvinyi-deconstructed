# Lillian Bassman — source shortlist

Lillian Bassman's fashion work makes the subject dissolve into a designed field of light, fabric, gesture, and reflection. This compact set uses four intact, publicly served gallery display JPEGs from Joseph Bellows Gallery's 2007 exhibition checklist. Each file is a 535px-long-edge rendition. That is below the usual 640px floor but above the permitted 480px exception: the figures, tonal construction, and full-frame relations remain clearly legible, and each is unusually useful for the chapter's specific lessons in silhouette, reflection, cropped light, and figure grouping. They are not upscaled and are too small to replace a print or licensed reproduction.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. Lillian Bassman's work is copyrighted; ABC News's contemporaneous public exhibition gallery credits every displayed Bassman photograph to “Lillian Bassman Estate, Courtesy Edwynn Houk Gallery,” while the Met collection record for *[Beauty Study]* gives “© Estate of Lillian Bassman.” The copies here are used only for nonprofit, transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching. Each entry makes the per-image four-factor assessment explicit.

## Acquisition and research notes

Joseph Bellows Gallery's public exhibition page identifies a much broader sequence of Bassman works, including *Across the Restaurant*, *Betty Threat*, *The Little Furs*, *In This Year of Lace*, *More Fashion Mileage Per Dress*, *The V-Back Evenings*, and *It's a Cinch*. Its publicly served display renditions for those vertical works resolve to only 420px on the long edge, below even the project's exceptional 480px minimum, so they were not acquired. The page supplies no larger public rendition.

The Met's public API currently returns 54 Bassman object records (for example, object 933648, *[Beauty Study]*, ca. 1950) but supplies no primary image and the public record states that its image cannot be enlarged, viewed full screen, or downloaded: https://www.metmuseum.org/art/collection/search/933648. ABC News's public Edwynn Houk Gallery slideshow visibly credits the Estate and exposes 1600px responsive image URLs, but its image CDN returned an ABC logo to an unauthenticated direct request; those files were deliberately rejected rather than attempting to evade the CDN's hotlink control. The four Bellows files above are therefore the complete usable direct-public set found in this pass: each opens as an intact JPEG, remains legible at its permitted exception size, and satisfies the registry's four-image readiness threshold.
