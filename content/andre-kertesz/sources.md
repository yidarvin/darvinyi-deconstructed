# André Kertész — source shortlist

Kertész made ordinary Paris material behave like a set of visual instruments: a fork becomes a diagonal and shadow; a musician's hands become a cropped rhythm; a café chair or a tower becomes a structure for chance. This selection moves from the early Hungarian picture through the concentrated Paris work and the *Distortions*, emphasizing his unusually direct way of finding geometry in everyday encounters.

## Research context

The Art Institute's artist record lists 255 Kertész works and its *Postcards from Paris* exhibition describes the 1925–28 Paris period as the period in which he developed his modern photographic practice through portraits, street views, studio scenes, and still lifes: https://www.artic.edu/artists/31706/andre-kertesz and https://www.artic.edu/exhibitions/2931. The subset above is deliberately broad enough to show Kertész's recurring compositional moves—oblique shadow, abrupt crop, small-object balance, repeated forms, elevated view, and mirrored deformation—rather than treating the *Distortions* as an isolated novelty.
