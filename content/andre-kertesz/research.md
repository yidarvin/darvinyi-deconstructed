# André Kertész — research

Scope: documented era, print information, and camera information for the
twelve selected works. This note does not infer capture facts from their visual
appearance.

## Era and approach

The source dossier places the selection between June 1919 and 1932/33, with a
concentration in Kertész's Paris work of 1925–31. The Art Institute of Chicago's
*Postcards from Paris* exhibition describes 1925–28 as the period in which he
developed a modern photographic practice through portraits, street views,
studio scenes, and still lifes. The selection tests that breadth: an airborne
figure, interiors, musicians, small tabletop arrangements, Paris streets, and
the mirror-made *Distortion #102*. [Source notes](sources.md)

## Technique and printing

The documented objects are photographic prints in the cited Art Institute
collection records, but the local source dossier does not give a consistent
print process or negative process for all twelve. It records later print dates
only where the collection caption supplies them: *Shadows, Paris* (made 1931)
and *Paris, The Eiffel Tower* (made 1925) are identified as prints from the
1970s. The other listed dates are the work dates supplied by the collection
records; no gelatin-silver, printing-out-paper, or other process is assigned
here without a specific record. [Source notes](sources.md)

## Camera information

No source in the local dossier establishes a camera model, lens, focal length,
aperture, shutter speed, film or plate stock, sensitivity, or exposure for any
selected work. The normalized image manifest contains no historical EXIF.
Specific settings are therefore not inferred from the sharpness, blur, mirror
distortion, high viewpoint, or strong shadow in these photographs.
