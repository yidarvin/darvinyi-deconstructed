# Raghu Rai — source shortlist

## Rights and selection basis

Rai's photographs remain copyrighted.  The Museum of Modern Art's collection
records identify the first three works and carry the notice `Copyright © 2026
Raghu Rai`; Magnum's public portfolio identifies Rai as represented by the
Estate and states `© 2014 Magnum Photos - All rights reserved`.  The National
Gallery of Modern Art, New Delhi, and the Smithsonian's National Museum of
Asian Art publicly display the additional selected works but do not grant an
open licence for their underlying photographs.  This set is therefore
**fair-use** throughout, not an assertion of open access.

For every image below, this book uses a complete, credited, source-linked,
web-sized display rendition solely for transformative criticism, scholarship,
and composition teaching.  Rai's frames are the particular subject of the
analysis, so a detail crop would remove the spatial relations being taught.
The copies do not replace a museum record, original print, or licensed
reproduction.  This is an individual four-factor assessment: the purpose is
transformative scholarship; the source work is creative; the whole frame is
used only because its complete composition is the object of criticism; and the
limited web copy is attributed and non-substitutive in the relevant licensing
market.

## Research notes

The broader canonical review included Rai's Bhopal gas-tragedy pictures, his
Bangladesh liberation-war work, and his Delhi, Varanasi, Kolkata, Mother Teresa,
and Indira Gandhi essays.  The official [Magnum portfolio](https://pro.magnumphotos.com/C.aspx?ERID=24KL535PGF&VF=MAGO31_10_VForm&VP3=CMS3) indexes Bhopal, Trees, Bangladesh, Mumbai, and *Raghu Rai's Delhi*; the [2008 NGMA exhibition page](https://www.ngmaindia.gov.in/pe_raghurai.asp) also identifies *Bazigars from Rajasthan, Delhi* (1986), *Mother consoling a child* (1970), *Indira Gandhi being escorted by security guards* (1967), *At Rajnish Ashram, Pune* (1996), and *My Father and My Son* (1969).  Its public files are 184–374px thumbnails, below even the project's rare-image exception, so they were not acquired.  The five retained institutional renditions are all at least 1095px on the long edge and exceed the four-image readiness threshold without pretending that low-resolution thumbnails are usable sources.
