# Raghu Rai — research notes

## Scope and record

This chapter is based on five credited institutional display renditions: three
Museum of Modern Art collection records dated 1976, 1977, and 1978; a 1998
Jamuna photograph documented by the Smithsonian's National Museum of Asian Art
and the Gaur Collection; and an undated National Gallery of Modern Art,
Bengaluru record. The per-image links, copyright notices, and fair-use
rationales are in [sources.md](sources.md). They place this small selection in
Rai's long documentary work in India, but they do not establish a common
assignment, camera, or production method for all five photographs.

## What the supplied records support

The sources identify Rai as the maker and document the titles or collection
records, dates where available, and the public display renditions used here.
The 1998 seagull image is identified by the Gaur Collection as a pigment print;
that record does not license a conclusion about the original negative, camera,
or printing process. The MoMA and NGMA records in the source set likewise do
not document camera body, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, film
stock, negative format, exposure method, paper, developer, or darkroom
procedure for these pictures.

## Working method for this chapter

The ingested JPEGs contain no camera EXIF. Tonal statistics, palette samples,
and measured edges therefore describe the web renditions, not the original
prints or capture settings. The chapter makes claims only about visible frame
relations: walls and wires; a roadway, portraits, and shadows; a bathroom
window and exterior stair; a boat and birds; and a lateral group of visitors.
Where a measured horizontal is a tonal or architectural boundary rather than a
literal horizon, it is described as such.
