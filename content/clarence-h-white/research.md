# Clarence H. White — research

Clarence H. White (1871–1925) was an American Pictorialist; the twelve selected works date from 1897 to 1909 and their Library of Congress records, public-domain status, and source links are listed in `sources.md`.

## Technique and era

White posed family and friends in intimate interiors, at windows, and outdoors at dawn or dusk. The Cleveland Museum of Art identifies him as a pioneer of American Pictorialism and describes this practice. The Met describes his compositions as expressive rather than documentary, carefully constructed, and allied to Tonalist, Impressionist, and Japanese-print design. [Cleveland Museum of Art](https://www.clevelandart.org/art/1980.159), [Metropolitan Museum of Art](https://www.metmuseum.org/art/collection/search/267729)

## Printing and settings

Platinum printing was central to White's work: the Met catalogs *Morning* (1905) and MoMA catalogs *Spring, A Triptych* (1898) as platinum prints. The Cleveland Museum notes platinum paper's sensitivity to middle tones. Those records do not establish a process for every exact LOC impression in this chapter. No per-frame aperture, shutter speed, focal length, or film stock appears in the source records or normalized-file manifest, whose EXIF entries are empty; none is invented.
