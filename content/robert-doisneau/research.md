# Robert Doisneau — research note

## Scope of the record

The source set documents twelve photographs made between 1934 and 1967 and served through the official Atelier Robert Doisneau portfolios. The portfolio grouping itself places the selection across children, bistros, Paris and the Seine, Les Halles, fairgrounds, and artists. The pictures therefore support a chapter about a long-running practice of finding staged-looking relations in ordinary public and working life, rather than a claim that one place, commission, or technical system explains the whole oeuvre.

## Technique and printing

The supplied official display records identify titles, dates, portfolio context, credits, and web-size reproductions. They do not document the original camera, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, film stock, negative format, paper, developer, or edition-specific print process for these individual photographs. The ingested files contain no capture EXIF. It would be inaccurate to infer historical exposure settings or original printing methods from modern web JPEGs.

What is available to analyze is the visible black-and-white reproduction: broad tonal separation, dense local texture in some plates, and highly legible figure-to-ground arrangements. Those are characteristics of the supplied reproductions, not evidence for an unstated negative or printing recipe. The source notes preserve the official links, rights basis, credits, and per-image fair-use rationale for the complete-frame reproductions used here.

## Historical framing

The dated sequence moves from *L'aéroplane de Papa* (1934) through children and street scenes of the 1940s and 1950s to the Halles and fairground scenes of the 1960s. Across it, a pedestrian, a child, a café table, a shop window, an urban structure, or a performer becomes legible because the surrounding space supplies a measured stage. This is a compositional observation grounded in the selected plates, not a substitute for a complete technical catalogue.
