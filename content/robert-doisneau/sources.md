# Robert Doisneau — source shortlist

Doisneau's humanist photographs make a social situation legible as a piece of framing: bodies form small alliances, architecture supplies a stage, and a telling gesture turns ordinary Paris and its suburbs into visual theater. This twelve-image working set draws from the official Atelier Robert Doisneau portfolios and moves between children, café life, the Seine, the Halles, a fairground, and the artist portrait. It preserves web-sized official display JPEGs whose long edges run from 650 to 1000px.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. Robert Doisneau's photographs remain copyrighted; the public display pages and image files are served by the official Atelier Robert Doisneau, which states that it conserves and represents his photographic collection and carries the notice “© Atelier Robert Doisneau.” Each individual assessment below is for nonprofit criticism, scholarship, and transformative composition teaching. The complete frame is retained only where it is needed to examine composition, at a modest web size, with source link and credit; it does not replace the Atelier page, an original print, or a licensed reproduction.

## Research context

The Atelier Robert Doisneau describes itself as the entity responsible for conserving and representing the photographer's collection. Its [Rome retrospective page](https://www.robert-doisneau.com/fr/expositions/robert-doisneau-rome-museo-dell-ara-pacis.htm) identifies *Le Baiser de l'Hôtel de Ville*, *La Dernière Valse du 14 juillet*, children, bistros, artists, and Parisian street scenes as central to the corpus. The present selection places the sustained teaching value on official, publicly served complete-frame examples that can be retained at web scale: childhood gesture, social grouping, visual joke, architectural diagonal, suspended action, and crowded working or fairground space.
