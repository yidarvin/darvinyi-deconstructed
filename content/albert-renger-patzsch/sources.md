# Sources — Albert Renger-Patzsch

## Acquisition note

Ten public, web-sized collection images were acquired from the Art Institute of Chicago (AIC) IIIF service on 2026-07-17. They are 843 px on the long edge or greater (actual dimensions are recorded below) and are retained in `raw/albert-renger-patzsch/`. Renger-Patzsch died in 1966; none of the AIC API records supplied an open licence or an explicit rights statement. Each file is therefore treated as a separate, documented fair-use reproduction for this critical and educational chapter, not as open content. The AIC collection record and its public IIIF display endpoint are linked for every work.

## Further researched canonical works

The AIC artist record also identifies *Blast Furnace. Good Hope Mill in Duisburg* (1928) and additional 1927–39 untitled studies. The Metropolitan Museum of Art records closely related landmarks including *Snake Head* (1927), *The Sapling* (1929), *Göhnender Pavian* (c. 1928), and *Stairwell, View from Below* (c. 1929). Together they establish the historical range—botanical close-up, animal, architecture, landscape, and industry—represented by this ten-image selection.
