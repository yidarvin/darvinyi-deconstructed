# John Vachon — research notes

## Record and era

This twelve-image selection is drawn from John Vachon's work for the Farm Security Administration/Office of War Information, dated 1938–41 in the linked Library of Congress item records in [sources.md](sources.md). It moves among planned housing in Wisconsin, industrial Maryland, Virginia and West Virginia small towns, Chicago streets, North Carolina, Iowa, and rail sites. The Library of Congress describes the wider FSA/OWI collection as a government photography project recording American life from 1935 to 1944, and identifies Vachon as one of its photographers. The supplied files are normalized, uncropped JPEG derivatives of the listed LOC downloads.

## Materials, printing, and technique

The source list identifies every selected plate as an FSA/OWI black-and-white negative. The LOC collection description says the project office developed, printed, numbered, reviewed, captioned, and filed most field negatives. That documents an institutional negative-to-print workflow for the collection, but not a particular surviving print, paper, developer, enlarger, or darkroom choice for these twelve exposures. This chapter therefore describes the reproduced image's measured tones and visible structure, rather than inventing printing history.

## Camera and exposure settings

The item records supplied for this set do not document a camera body, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, film stock, or exposure for individual photographs. The manifest's `Stokes`, `Leaf`, and `Phase One` EXIF values are digitization/scanning metadata on LOC derivatives, not Vachon's capture settings; two derivative files also retain a 2019 digitization date. No historical camera or exposure setting is assigned in this chapter.

## Rights

The Library of Congress FSA/OWI black-and-white-negative collection is public domain and free to use and reuse; the individual item pages linked in [sources.md](sources.md) carry the collection's no-known-restrictions advisory.
