# John Vachon — source shortlist

John Vachon's FSA/OWI photographs find graphic order in everyday American space: storefront lettering, rails, industrial structure, queues, and the hard boundaries of segregation. This set emphasizes the quiet Midwestern geometry named in the registry while keeping several close social-documentary pictures that show how he uses a threshold, line, or architectural frame to organize people.

**Rights basis:** Every selected photograph is a John Vachon image in the Library of Congress Farm Security Administration/Office of War Information (FSA/OWI) collection. LOC states that its [FSA/OWI black-and-white negatives](https://www.loc.gov/collections/fsa-owi-black-and-white-negatives/about-this-collection/rights-and-access/) are public domain and free to use and reuse; the linked item records identify Vachon and carry the collection's no-known-restrictions advisory. The local JPEGs are full-frame derivatives of the LOC preservation TIFFs.

## Shortlist

- **Why it matters:** The planned-community view turns sidewalks, façades, and the open foreground into a calm arrangement of receding planes—a concise example of Vachon's measured civic geometry.
