# Marion Post Wolcott — research notes

## Record and era

The supplied twelve-picture set is Marion Post Wolcott’s work for the Farm Security Administration, dated from 1938 to 1941. Its item records, linked individually in [sources.md](sources.md), range across Alabama, Mississippi, Louisiana, Kentucky, Tennessee, Maryland, Vermont, and Virginia. The sequence therefore joins Southern social spaces—juke joints, a roadside store, work, and a portrait—with civic, commercial, and rural landscapes rather than reducing Wolcott’s practice to a single subject.

## Materials and technique

The source records identify eleven selections as Library of Congress FSA/OWI black-and-white-negative material and one, *A cross roads store, bar, juke joint, and gas station*, as an FSA/OWI colour photograph. Those collection designations establish the archival source, but do not identify the original paper, darkroom process, or print state of every supplied file. The local JPEGs are normalized, full-frame derivatives of LOC masters or a LOC-derived open rendition; they are not presented as Wolcott’s vintage prints.

What is visible in the pictures is not a substitute for missing metadata. Wolcott repeatedly works with an actual threshold, road, table, ferry cable, wall, sign, roofline, or shore: physical boundaries make a social gathering, a task, or a public place legible in the frame. The black-and-white plates make those relations through value and texture; the one colour record makes painted signs, warm earth, pale sky, and dark openings part of the same organizing structure.

## Camera and exposure settings

No supplied item record documents a camera body, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, film stock, or exposure for these particular images. The normalized manifest preserves later scanner or derivative-file metadata where it exists, not Wolcott’s capture settings. This chapter therefore assigns no camera settings and does not infer an unrecorded photographic process from the appearance of a file.

## Rights

The source shortlist cites the Library of Congress rights statement for the FSA/OWI black-and-white-negative collection and records the same public-domain/free-to-reuse basis for the listed FSA/OWI colour photograph. The item records remain the source for the photographer attribution, titles, dates, and collection context.
