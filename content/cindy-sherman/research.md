# Cindy Sherman — research notes

## Verified working method and chronology

This source set follows Cindy Sherman from the black-and-white *Untitled Film Stills* of 1977–80 through the color *Centerfolds* of 1981 and later color photographs from 1983, 1985, and 1987. MoMA describes the *Film Stills* as a three-year project in which Sherman posed as generic female film characters, drawing on Hollywood, film noir, B-movie, and European art-house publicity stills. The Art Institute records in this selection document individual titles, dates, and the credited collection images; the 1983 photograph explicitly leaves camera equipment and a wig stand in view. [Source shortlist](sources.md)

The photographs are staged rather than candid: costume, pose, props, setting, viewpoint, and—in the studio image—the apparatus itself organize an identity for the camera. That does not make each image a still from an actual film. The source description’s emphasis on generic roles is important: these pictures borrow the visual pressure of a familiar screen narrative without providing its plot.

## Printing, camera, and settings

The available Art Institute collection pages and their 843px-long-edge display JPEGs do not identify a camera body, lens, focal length, film stock, aperture, shutter speed, ISO/sensitivity, lighting setup, darkroom process, print process, edition, or original print dimensions for these twelve works. The source record gives one date distinction—*Untitled Film Still #50* was made in 1979 and printed in 1983—but does not specify a printing technique. No camera or printing claim is inferred from the web files or from their modern metadata.

## Source and rights context

All twelve plates are credited as © Cindy Sherman, courtesy Metro Pictures, New York, on Art Institute records. They are retained as credited web-sized complete-frame copies under the per-image fair-use analyses in [sources.md](sources.md): the complete frame is needed to study staged composition, and the display derivatives are not substitutes for an exhibition print or licensed reproduction.
