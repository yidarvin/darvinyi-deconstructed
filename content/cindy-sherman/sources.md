# Cindy Sherman — source shortlist

This set traces Sherman’s staged identities from the black-and-white *Untitled Film Stills* (1977–80), through the wide *Centerfolds* (1981), to the later color work. All twelve JPEGs were retrieved directly from the Art Institute of Chicago’s public IIIF display service and verified as opening images. The service returns deliberately web-sized 843px-long-edge derivatives: above this project’s 640px acquisition floor, but unsuitable as substitutes for exhibition prints or licensed files.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. The Art Institute of Chicago records identify the notice “© Cindy Sherman. Courtesy Metro Pictures, New York.” Each use below is a separate assessment: this nonprofit book uses a credited, record-linked, web-sized copy for transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching; the complete frame is necessary to analyze the staged photographic construction; and the derivative cannot replace the original work, museum record, or a licensed reproduction.

## Research context

MoMA describes the *Untitled Film Stills* as photographs made over three years in which Sherman posed as generic female film characters, with the images styled after Hollywood, film noir, B-movie, and European art-house publicity stills: https://www.moma.org/collection/works/56618. The Art Institute’s Sherman collection record makes it possible to retain that suite’s black-and-white cinematic grammar while also studying its later color expansion into low angles, saturated props, extreme panoramas, and deliberately exposed studio construction: https://www.artic.edu/artists/23759/cindy-sherman.
