# Filip Dujardin — source shortlist

This ten-image core samples the canonical architectural photomontages of Dujardin’s ongoing *Fictions* series: discrete, plausible-looking structures assembled from buildings photographed in and around Ghent. The public editorial presentation identifies the images as courtesy of Filip Dujardin, but grants no reuse licence. The Met’s collection record for *Untitled* (2009, printed 2011) explicitly gives the reproduction notice “© Filip Dujardin” and describes his construction from virtual models and hundreds of photographs. Gessato’s origin display URLs now return 404, but on 2026-07-24 each corresponding public WordPress image-CDN route below returned `200 image/jpeg` and byte-matched the ingested raw file. The files measure 700–1,005px on their long edge; none was upscaled, generated, cropped, or materially altered.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. Creator/rightsholder: © Filip Dujardin. The Gessato presentation is the publicly accessible source and labels the images “courtesy of Filip Dujardin”; its *Fictions* overview says the series began in 2008, but does not assign an individual date or distinct title to these display images. The Met records a related *Untitled* from the series as 2009 and confirms © Dujardin. ## Research context

- [The Metropolitan Museum of Art’s *Untitled* record](https://www.metmuseum.org/art/collection/search/299411) identifies a 2009 work by Dujardin, gives the rights notice © Filip Dujardin, and explains the use of SketchUp and Photoshop to layer surfaces sampled from photographs of Ghent.
- [The Chicago Architecture Biennial profile](https://chicagoarchitecturebiennial.org/people/filip-dujardin/) dates the first presentation of *Fictions* to 2008 and describes Dujardin’s image-making as transfers among urban form, architectural type, construction detail, and alternative histories.
- [Gessato’s public presentation](https://www.gessato.com/imaginary-architecture-by-filip-dujardin/) states that the images are courtesy of Filip Dujardin and that he photographed buildings in and around Ghent before using digital techniques to construct the scenes. Its origin display files are now unavailable; the verified WordPress image-CDN routes recorded above are the current traceable public routes for this unit.
