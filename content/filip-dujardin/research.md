# Filip Dujardin — research note

## Scope and evidence

This chapter treats the ten supplied web-display images as *Untitled* works from
Filip Dujardin’s ongoing *Fictions* series. The series began in 2008, but the
Gessato presentation that supplied these particular files does not give an
individual title or date for any of them. The Metropolitan Museum of Art records
a related *Untitled* as 2009 (printed 2011). It would be inaccurate to transfer
that date or its print details to every plate here. See [sources.md](sources.md).

## Method and era

*Fictions* belongs to the digital-photomontage turn in architectural
photography of the late 2000s. The Met describes Dujardin's process as using
SketchUp and Photoshop to combine virtual models with surfaces sampled from
hundreds of photographs. Gessato likewise says that he photographed buildings
in and around Ghent before using digital techniques to construct the scenes.
The result is not a record of a building that stood before the camera: it is a
carefully matched construction made from photographic material, where scale,
perspective, weather, seams, and building skins have been made to agree.

That distinction matters compositionally. A horizon, tree line, curb, or small
vehicle may behave as a credible scale cue even when the building it measures is
an invented assembly. Repeated window modules make the fabrication feel
architecturally plausible; irregular cantilevers, mismatched cladding, and
impossible joints let the fiction emerge gradually rather than through a single
spectacular effect.

## Printing and camera settings

The available record gives a printing date only for the Met's related *Untitled*
(printed 2011); it does not establish a print process, edition, or paper for the
ten plates in this chapter. The source pages and the ingested display JPEGs do
not identify camera body, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, ISO,
sensor, capture settings, or the individual source photographs used in each
montage. The empty EXIF fields in the ingested manifest are not evidence of a
particular camera workflow. Assigning settings or printing details would be
invention.

## Reading the reproductions

The files are credited, web-sized fair-use reproductions for criticism and
composition teaching, not substitutes for Dujardin's prints or licensed
reproductions. Their analyzer measurements describe the supplied files only:
they help identify tonal range, palette, and edge density, but do not establish
the colour, scale, or material surface of an original print.
