Filip Dujardin's *Fictions* makes a peculiar promise: the buildings look as if a camera found them, but the camera's evidence has been recomposed. The source record describes photographs of buildings in and around Ghent combined through SketchUp and Photoshop; a wall, window bank, or concrete slab may therefore be locally credible while the whole refuses construction logic. The photographs work because their edits are restrained. Dujardin keeps skies plain, grounds level, and scale witnesses small, so an invented mass has to be read before it can be doubted. The overlays identify those stabilizing structures rather than pretending the sites are documentary. [Source notes](../../content/filip-dujardin/sources.md)

## credibility is built from measures

Its near-center axis does not make the construction truly symmetrical; the tree at right matters precisely because it breaks that reading. ## systems, interruptions, and manufactured weather

<Plate slug="filip-dujardin" id="04-fictions-05" label="Untitled, from Fictions (series began 2008)" caption="A nearly square façade holds scattered windows in balance, while enclosing side walls make a frame." />

The fourth plate turns a wall into a slow constellation. Two blunt gray side walls contain the central façade and prevent the dispersed windows from becoming merely decorative. A slight courtyard edge at y0.858 slopes through the base, while the small green, blue, rust, and black openings act as unequal counters across one field. Its #d9d8cd, #6b6961, and #403a32 palette stays narrow enough that colour accents are events, not a separate system.

<Plate slug="filip-dujardin" id="05-fictions-05a" label="Untitled, from Fictions (series began 2008)" caption="Paired domestic façades make the rough central boulder a deliberately monumental interruption." />

The boulder in plate five would be absurd if the two houses did not agree so calmly. Their roofline fixes a horizontal at y0.255, and the narrow gap between the two façades feels prepared for an intrusion. The traced boulder crest identifies the rock as one irregular mass rather than mistaking its many creases for several subjects. Its dark #2b2724 and brown #5d584f texture interrupts high-key #f4f5f3 sky and pale façades, a tonal contrast that makes the compositing premise instantly useful rather than merely surprising.

<Plate slug="filip-dujardin" id="06-fictions-06" label="Untitled, from Fictions (series began 2008)" caption="Projected blocks and dark triangular recesses turn a close façade into a jagged rhythm of mass and void." />

In the close crop, Dujardin removes landscape evidence and asks the joints to carry the fiction. A run of notches avoids any single perspectival receding edge; every pale block seems to project farther than the one beneath it, while the marked triangular recess makes the gaps equally solid. The analyzer records the set's lowest edge density here, 0.020, which is useful: large planes and shadows, rather than surface detail, organize the frame. Gray-beige #e6e6e0 and near-black #2e2d27 make the geometry feel almost diagrammatic.

<Plate slug="filip-dujardin" id="07-fictions-09" label="Untitled, from Fictions (series began 2008)" caption="A jagged roof silhouette and quiet central core make a small house hold together against open sky." />

This house is less a tower than a stack of unstable decisions. The roof-silhouette polyline follows the actual upper contour, whose steep rise and collapse hold the dark cladding against a very pale sky. A compact cluster of overlapping roofs keeps the assembly from becoming a random pile, while the tilted walls give it energy. A tree line at y0.896 and the tiny person at the entry enforce a human measure. The wide tonal span, p05 0.192 to p95 0.996, makes those small dark and green details unusually decisive.

## repetition as camouflage

<Plate slug="filip-dujardin" id="08-fictions-10" label="Untitled, from Fictions (series began 2008)" caption="Repeated balcony bands establish a long horizontal cadence that tiny shifts and a dark notch disrupt." />

The long slab nearly persuades because it repeats a familiar residential grammar. Its upper slab band sits at y0.321 and balcony bands carry the eye horizontally across the frame, leaving a central notch to interrupt the cadence. The marked balcony line is a visible compositional relay even though the analyzer treats it as detector-unconfirmed; it follows a real band rather than claiming a vanishing route. At edge density 0.162, the highest in this group, railing, bays, awnings, and altered window modules create just enough information for the larger assembly to pass as an elevation.

<Plate slug="filip-dujardin" id="09-fictions-11" label="Untitled, from Fictions (series began 2008)" caption="A tall panelled tower and lower attached blocks turn mismatched cladding into measured colour fields." />

Plate nine builds credibility by making its seams countable. The tall left tower rises beside a lower central tower and an attached right block; their surfaces are tiled into blue, rust, green, gray, and brown registers rather than blended into one material. The lower ground edge gives this mismatch a stable coordinate system. The palette measured from the supplied image, pale #eef6f7, green-brown #6a6248, and dark #3b3723, supports the effect: colour appears as construction material, not atmosphere.

<Plate slug="filip-dujardin" id="10-fictions-14" label="Untitled, from Fictions (series began 2008)" caption="A narrow glazed spine and deep cantilever make a restrained slab appear structurally impossible over the lawn." />

The last plate is almost austere. A narrow glazed spine, a deep cantilever, and a near-level tree line supply the discipline that lets the top slab cantilever so far beyond its support. The anchor deliberately names the small tree at left as a scale marker: it is not a building feature, but without it the volume could drift into model-like abstraction. The palette moves from near-white #f5f8f8 sky to #182113 foliage and gray-green concrete; the broad, relatively low-detail tonal field lets the unsupported underside remain the decisive dark fact.

## Exercises

<ExerciseCard n="01" title="Make the impossible earn its scale">
Photograph a familiar building or object under plain light, then make one speculative collage in which you change only its massing. Retain a ground line and one modest scale witness: a tree, person, vehicle, or door. If the fiction becomes obvious before the eye can read its scale, simplify the edit.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="02" title="Use repetition as a promise">
Make a frontal study of a façade, shelf, or repeated set of objects. Establish a strong interval first, then introduce one interruption: a missing unit, a shifted opening, or a different material. Compare whether the interruption reads as information or as an accident.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="03" title="Let shadows describe the join">
Work with a simple paper or digital montage. Instead of adding detail everywhere, decide which two seams need shadow, overlap, or a shared edge direction to become believable. Keep the surrounding field quiet enough that those joins can do the work.
</ExerciseCard>
