# Nicéphore Niépce — sources

Niépce's photographic legacy is not a body of composed pictures in the way every later photographer in this book built one — it is the residue of roughly a decade of chemical experiment, and by any strict count only a small handful of objects survive that qualify as photographs at all, bracketed by an even smaller number of reproduction plates. The two pictures ingested here mark the two poles of that practice. *View from the Window at Le Gras* is an accidental composition: an exposure lasting hours or days let the sun cross the sky and light both faces of the courtyard buildings at once, collapsing directional shadow into an ambiguous, near-shadowless field read from a raised, oblique window viewpoint — the roofline cuts a hard diagonal across the frame, one of the first instances in the medium of a horizon functioning as compositional armature rather than given fact. *La Table Servie*, by contrast, is a deliberately built picture — a table dressed and shot frontally in the manner of seventeenth-century still-life painting, objects arranged along a shallow depth axis toward the lens — the earliest attempt to import painterly composition directly into a camera image. Between them: a found view and a built one, which is close to the whole of what "composition" could mean before anyone had made more than a few dozen photographs.

**Rights basis:** Niépce died in 1833, so his entire body of work is in the public domain outright, everywhere. *View from the Window at Le Gras* is sourced from the Harry Ransom Center's own 2020 high-resolution rescan of the pewter plate (Wikimedia Commons mirror, dual CC0/PD-old). *La Table Servie* survives only as a photomechanical reproduction print made by the Société française de photographie in 1893 — the original glass plate disappeared early in the twentieth century — reproduced here from a public-domain scan credited to Zeno.org's "5,000 Masterpieces of Photography" collection. Two other works that belong in any account of Niépce's surviving corpus — *Cardinal d'Amboise* and the "Man with a Horse" engraving reproduction the Bibliothèque nationale de France bought as a national treasure — are genuinely public domain but not available at usable resolution from any open, cleanly downloadable source at time of writing; see NEEDED.md. Two further zinc heliograph plates at the Musée Nicéphore Niépce (*Paysage*, 1823, and *Paysage avec figures*, 1825) were also examined for this chapter; both are legitimate museum-catalogued artifacts, but the only available photographs of them are museum-display shots in which the actual plate image is largely illegible under glass and frame — they were dropped rather than padding the shortlist with content that cannot support compositional analysis.

## Shortlist

### 01 — View from the Window at Le Gras (1826–1827)
- **Why it matters:** The first surviving photograph made from nature — a multi-hour exposure that let sunlight cross the whole sky, lighting both sides of the courtyard buildings simultaneously and flattening the scene into a soft, near-shadowless field; the pitched roofline reads as a single hard diagonal cutting the frame, photography's first horizon-as-device.
- **Source:** https://www.hrc.utexas.edu/niepce-heliograph/
- **Rights:** Public domain (Niépce d. 1833; Harry Ransom Center, University of Texas at Austin, Gernsheim Collection; 2020 official digitization)
- **File:** raw/nicephore-niepce/01-view-from-window-le-gras.jpg (4530px)

### 02 — La Table Servie / The Set Table (c. 1832–1833)
- **Why it matters:** Niépce's only known still-life attempt and, on some accounts, the earlier of his two "point de vue" experiments — a dressed table shot frontally with a wine bottle, covered bowl, glass, and bouquet arranged along a shallow depth axis, deliberately quoting seventeenth-century still-life painting rather than found composition.
- **Source:** https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/La_Table_servie
- **Rights:** Public domain (Niépce d. 1833; original glass plate lost early 20th c.; surviving image is an 1893 photomechanical reproduction print held via the Société française de photographie collection, itself long out of copyright)
- **File:** raw/nicephore-niepce/02-table-servie.jpg (1280px)
