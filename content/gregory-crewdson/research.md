# Gregory Crewdson — research note

## What the supplied sources establish

The source set identifies all twelve plates as works from *Cathedral of the Pines* (2013–14), made during three productions in and around Becket, Massachusetts. Gagosian describes the interior pictures as studies of connection, separation, intimacy, and isolation; its exhibition record identifies the medium as digital pigment print and supplies each title, date, credit, copyright notice, and public display derivative used here. [Source notes](sources.md)

The selection is a deliberately constructed sequence rather than a record of unstaged moments. The supplied exhibition context supports discussion of directed interiors, domestic thresholds, woods, and the pressure between figures and settings. It does not establish a camera body, lens, film stock, aperture, shutter speed, sensitivity, lighting plan, retouching workflow, or color-management process for any individual image.

## Method for this chapter

The chapter treats staging, figure placement, architecture, light, and color as visible claims about the complete web-display frames. Its print language is limited to the documented "digital pigment print" designation. The JPEGs retain no camera EXIF, and neither their pixels nor their carefully controlled appearance can establish capture settings. The full-frame uses and image-specific fair-use rationales remain in the [source notes](sources.md).
