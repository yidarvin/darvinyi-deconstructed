# Gregory Crewdson — source shortlist

This set takes twelve complete frames from *Cathedral of the Pines* (2013–14), Crewdson’s rural Massachusetts sequence. The files were downloaded from Gagosian’s publicly accessible exhibition display service and each opens successfully at 900px or 1200px on the long edge. Gagosian’s exhibition record names and dates every work, identifies the medium as digital pigment print, and carries the notice “© Gregory Crewdson.” No open-license or public-domain rendition was located for these copyrighted photographs.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. Each entry below is a separate, limited assessment: this book uses a credited, record-linked, web-sized copy for transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching; it needs the complete published frame to analyze Crewdson’s constructed spatial and lighting relationships; and the copy neither substitutes for the artist’s print, Gagosian’s exhibition record, nor a licensed reproduction. The publicly accessible source page and display-file route are retained below.

## Research context

Gagosian states that *Cathedral of the Pines* was made during three productions in and around Becket, Massachusetts, and describes its interior scenes as probing connection, separation, intimacy, and isolation: https://gagosian.com/exhibitions/2016/gregory-crewdson-cathedral-of-the-pines/. The same exhibition record identifies the series as 2013–14 and supplies the work titles, dates, medium, edition information, copyright notice, and public display files used above.
