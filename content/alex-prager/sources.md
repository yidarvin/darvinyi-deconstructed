# Alex Prager — source record

## Selection and rights note

This source set focuses on ten of the large-format 2013 *Face In The Crowd*
photographs. Together they make the series' central compositional idea legible:
an elevated, theatrical view lets many independently staged figures function as a
single field, while one isolated gaze or bright costume can reorganize the whole
frame. The [artist's selected-work page](https://www.alexprager.com/part-ii-view)
identifies each title, date, and print dimensions, and exposes the public web
rendition linked below. The related [MoMA exhibition text](https://www.moma.org/interactives/exhibitions/2010/newphotography/alex-prager/)
describes Prager's boldly colored, dramatically lit, cinematic photographic practice.

Alex Prager retains copyright; no open licence or public-domain statement was
located. Every item is therefore a separate **fair-use** assessment. This book
uses a credited, linked, web-sized copy for nonprofit criticism, scholarship, and
transformative composition teaching. The photographs are highly creative works,
but each lesson requires the complete frame—not an isolated detail—to analyze the
field of staged figures, its focal hierarchy, and its paths of attention. The
public display copies below are 2,358–2,500px on their long edge, are retained
only as analytical web copies, and do not replace a print, the artist's record, or
a licensed reproduction.
