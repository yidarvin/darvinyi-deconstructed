# Horst P. Horst — source shortlist

Horst’s most durable pictures turn editorial commissions into exacting studies of pose, surface, and theatrical illumination. This selection begins with the 1930s Paris and Vogue work that made his name, then follows the studio vocabulary through celebrity portraiture, fashion, cover design, and the male nude. Eleven unaltered public collection display files were retrieved and verified as readable JPEGs; ten are 960–1280px on the short edge and the remaining *Marlene Dietrich* file is 640 × 803px, meeting the project’s 640px long-edge floor.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. ## Research context

The V&A characterizes Horst as a master of light, composition, and atmospheric illusion, and identifies its collection as spanning Vogue fashion, celebrity portraiture, nudes, travel, and botanical studies: https://www.vam.ac.uk/collections/horst. Its object record for *Barefoot Beauty* also documents Horst’s Vogue publication practice and the staged construction of beauty photography: https://collections.vam.ac.uk/item/O1293713/. The retrieved set concentrates on the compositional range most useful to this chapter—classical figure construction, fashion silhouette, staged groupings, close portrait light, and cover-scale graphic design—while retaining only authoritative public display renditions.
