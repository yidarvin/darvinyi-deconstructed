# Frederick H. Evans — research notes

The selected photographs span c. 1894–1903 and belong to turn-of-the-century British Pictorialism, especially Evans’s cathedral work. Getty describes his straight approach as not altering negatives; the Met identifies him as a major British Pictorialist and notes his exceptional platinum-printing and tonal gradation. Describe the cathedral sequence as platinum prints where the object record supports it, not as a uniform process: *On a French River* is gelatin silver, and *Aubrey Beardsley* is catalogued as a paired platinum print and photogravure object. No consulted record supplies camera body, lens model, focal length, aperture, shutter, or exposure time; none is inferred.
