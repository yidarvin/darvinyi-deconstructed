# Sebastião Salgado — source shortlist

This deliberately compact source set concentrates on the formal range named in
the registry: *Workers*' immense, collectively organized labour; a close,
direct group portrait; and social landscapes where a small figure arrests a
large field. Six prints are publicly served by the Art Institute of Chicago
(AIC) through its IIIF image service; the seventh is a public Getty IIIF
rendition. All local JPEGs were opened after download. The AIC service supplies
843px-long-edge display files despite a larger requested size; each clears the
current 640px floor. Getty's Ecuador file is 1600 × 1068px. The records make a
coherent seven-image teaching set without treating availability as a licence.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. AIC marks its six records
`is_public_domain: false` and gives no separate copyright notice. Getty's
Ecuador record explicitly states “© Sebastião Salgado / Amazonas Images”; that
is the known rightsholder notice used for the whole set, with AIC credited as
the holding institution for its six records. Each individual assessment below
addresses all four factors: nonprofit, transformative criticism and scholarship
about composition; the creative character of the photograph; the pedagogical
need for its complete frame; and a small, credited, record-linked web copy that
does not replace an original print, collection record, or licensed
reproduction.

## Source investigation note

The research pass checked the full six-work AIC artist record and the Getty
artist record, which catalogs seventeen Salgado objects. AIC provides the six
public display images used here; Getty provides a public IIIF image for
*Ecuador* while the other Getty artist-record entries are marked “Image not
available.” The set exceeds the registry minimum of four with seven real,
verified photographs. No limited-source fallback is used.
