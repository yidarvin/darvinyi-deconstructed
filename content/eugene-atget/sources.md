# Eugène Atget — sources

Atget photographed "Vieux Paris" as empty stage sets: look for deep one-point perspective plunging down cobbled streets, doorway and arch framing, shop-window vitrines that layer real goods against ghostly street reflections, and park studies where statuary and water build near-symmetrical geometry. The camera is almost always low and frontal, the light flat, the frame emptied of incident so structure reads clean.

**Rights basis:** Public domain worldwide. Atget died in 1927, so all works are out of copyright. Downloaded scans come from open-access institutional programs — the J. Paul Getty Museum (Getty Open Content) and the Metropolitan Museum of Art (Open Access / CC0) — which place their reproductions in the public domain.

## Shortlist

### 01 — Storefront, avenue des Gobelins (1925)
- **Why it matters:** Suited mannequins in a vitrine, faces staring out while the street and sky reflect across the glass — the defining Atget layering of real object, reflection, and void.
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Eug%C3%A8ne_Atget_(French_-_Storefront,_avenue_des_Gobelins_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg
- **Rights:** Public domain (J. Paul Getty Museum, Getty Open Content)
- **File:** raw/eugene-atget/01-storefront-avenue-des-gobelins.jpg (4385px)

### 02 — Corner of the rue de Seine and the rue de l'Échaudé (1924)
- **Why it matters:** A wedge of building splits two receding streets, a textbook two-point perspective fork with the empty morning square as stage floor.
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Eug%C3%A8ne_Atget,_Corner_of_the_rue_de_Seine_and_the_rue_de_l%27Echaud%C3%A9_-_Getty_Museum.jpg
- **Rights:** Public domain (J. Paul Getty Museum, Getty Open Content)
- **File:** raw/eugene-atget/02-corner-rue-de-seine-echaude.jpg (4191px)

### 03 — Boulevard de Strasbourg (1912)
- **Why it matters:** Shopfronts and awnings march to a single distant vanishing point — deep one-point perspective down a commercial street, corset signage stacking the left wall.
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Eug%C3%A8ne_Atget,_Boulevard_de_Strasbourg_-_Getty_Museum.jpg
- **Rights:** Public domain (J. Paul Getty Museum, Getty Open Content)
- **File:** raw/eugene-atget/03-boulevard-de-strasbourg.jpg (5763px)

### 04 — The Pont Neuf (1902–03)
- **Why it matters:** The bridge parapet and lamp posts drive a long diagonal across the frame, the river and quay opening a receding one-point recession.
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Eug%C3%A8ne_Atget,_The_Pont_Neuf_-_Getty_Museum.jpg
- **Rights:** Public domain (J. Paul Getty Museum, Getty Open Content)
- **File:** raw/eugene-atget/04-pont-neuf.jpg (5625px)

### 05 — Place Saint-Médard (1898–1900)
- **Why it matters:** A cobbled square funnels toward the church, converging pavement lines and flanking buildings forming a deep perspectival throat.
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Eug%C3%A8ne_Atget,_Place_Saint-M%C3%A9dard_-_Getty_Museum.jpg
- **Rights:** Public domain (J. Paul Getty Museum, Getty Open Content)
- **File:** raw/eugene-atget/05-place-saint-medard.jpg (6242px)

### 06 — Cour de Rohan (1915)
- **Why it matters:** An enclosed courtyard read as empty stage — arch and wellhead anchor a boxed, near-frontal geometry of walls and pavement.
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Eug%C3%A8ne_Atget,_Cour_de_Rohan_-_Getty_Museum.jpg
- **Rights:** Public domain (J. Paul Getty Museum, Getty Open Content)
- **File:** raw/eugene-atget/06-cour-de-rohan.jpg (4156px)

### 07 — Staircase, Hôtel de Brinvilliers, rue Charles V (1900)
- **Why it matters:** A wrought-iron spiral staircase coils into a logarithmic curve — pure rotational geometry filling the frame.
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Eug%C3%A8ne_Atget,_Staircase,_H%C3%B4tel_de_Brinvilliers,_rue_Charles_V_-_Getty_Museum.jpg

### 08 — Tree Roots, Saint-Cloud (1919–21)
- **Why it matters:** Exposed roots sprawl into a nervous branching lattice — organic composition where line and negative space replace built architecture.
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Eug%C3%A8ne_Atget_(French_-_Tree_Roots,_Saint-Cloud_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg

### 09 — Saint-Cloud Park (1924)
- **Why it matters:** A still basin mirrors sky and trees into near-vertical symmetry, the horizon of the water splitting the frame — the misty park reflection study.
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Eug%C3%A8ne_Atget,_Saint-Cloud_Park,_Paris,_1924.jpg

### 10 — Fête du Trône (1925)
- **Why it matters:** A fairground stall front flattens into frontal signage and figures — carnival vernacular arranged as a shallow, planar frieze.
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Eug%C3%A8ne_Atget,_F%C3%AAte_du_Tr%C3%B4ne_-_Getty_Museum.jpg

### 11 — Three Prostitutes, rue Asselin (1924–25)
- **Why it matters:** Figures posed frontally against a doorway and blank wall — the human subject pinned into Atget's architectural, stage-flat framing.
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Eug%C3%A8ne_Atget,_Three_Prostitutes,_rue_Asselin,_1924%E2%80%9325.jpg
- **Rights:** Public domain (J. Paul Getty Museum, Getty Open Content)
- **File:** raw/eugene-atget/11-three-prostitutes-rue-asselin.jpg (4354px)

### 12 — L'Éclipse, avril 1912 (1912)
- **Why it matters:** A crowd on the place de la Bastille tilts every face upward at the solar eclipse — a dense diagonal of gazes reading as a single vector (Berenice Abbott's favorite Atget).
- **Source:** https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Eclipse_MET_DP112699.jpg
