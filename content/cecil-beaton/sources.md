# Cecil Beaton — source notes

**Source pass:** 17 July 2026.  The first-choice late fashion and society
portraits (including the 1956 Marilyn Monroe sitting) remain rights-managed by
the Cecil Beaton Archive / Condé Nast at the National Portrait Gallery.  The
accessible, compositionally substantial core of the historical record is
Beaton's Second World War work for the Imperial War Museums (IWM).  Wikimedia
Commons serves the ten files below as **Public domain**, with an IWM object
record linked from each file page.  They were downloaded from the public Commons
originals without alteration; all have a long edge of at least 800px (four are
1772–3652px).  Credit: **Cecil Beaton / Imperial War Museums**.

This is an open-source chapter set, not a claim that every Beaton photograph is
out of copyright.  The individual Commons public-domain statements and IWM
provenance are the use basis for each selected wartime photograph.

## Selected photographs

### 01. *St Paul's Cathedral through an archway after the raid*, 1940–41 (MH 2718)

- **Why it matters:** A dark enclosing arch turns the ruined city view into a nested-frame study, with the cathedral towers held in the distant opening.
- **Best source:** [IWM MH 2718](https://www.iwm.org.uk/collections/item/object/205193603); [public original / licence statement](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Cecil_Beaton_Photographs-_General_MH2718.jpg)
- **Use basis — open:** Wikimedia Commons marks this IWM-held Beaton photograph **Public domain**. Credit Cecil Beaton / Imperial War Museums.

### 02. *Passengers through the bars of a Calcutta tram*, 1944 (IB 1974)

- **Why it matters:** The tram bars make a hard repeated grid, slicing a crowded documentary scene into compressed portrait cells.
- **Best source:** [IWM IB 1974](https://www.iwm.org.uk/collections/item/object/205089402); [public original / licence statement](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:CECIL_BEATON_PHOTOGRAPHS-_GENERAL_IB1974.jpg)
- **Use basis — open:** Wikimedia Commons marks this IWM-held Beaton photograph **Public domain**. Credit Cecil Beaton / Imperial War Museums.

### 03. *Mother and sick child, Canadian Mission Hospital, Chengtu*, 1944 (IB 2569C)

- **Why it matters:** The paired heads and pillow create an intimate triangular grouping, while the bed supplies a quiet horizontal ground.
- **Best source:** [IWM IB 2569C](https://www.iwm.org.uk/collections/item/object/205193639); [public original / licence statement](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Cecil_Beaton_Photographs-_General%3B_China_1944,_Canadian_Mission_Hospital_in_Chengtu_IB2569C.jpg)
- **Use basis — open:** Wikimedia Commons marks this IWM-held Beaton photograph **Public domain**. Credit Cecil Beaton / Imperial War Museums.

### 04. *Chow Chung-yuch beside a studded door*, 1944 (IB 1365C)

- **Why it matters:** A seated portrait is energized by the door's regular studs: an architectural counter-rhythm that makes the figure's stillness legible.
- **Best source:** [IWM IB 1365C](https://www.iwm.org.uk/collections/item/object/205193634); [public original / licence statement](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Cecil_Beaton_Photographs-_General%3B_Chung-yuch,_Chow_IB1365C.jpg)
- **Use basis — open:** Wikimedia Commons marks this IWM-held Beaton photograph **Public domain**. Credit Cecil Beaton / Imperial War Museums.

### 05. *Man between sunlight and shadow, Chengtu*, 1944 (IB 2482A)

- **Why it matters:** Beaton lets a sharp light boundary divide the standing figure, making tonal contrast rather than gesture carry the portrait.
- **Best source:** [IWM IB 2482A](https://www.iwm.org.uk/collections/item/object/205193636); [public original / licence statement](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Cecil_Beaton_Photographs-_General_IB2482A.jpg)
- **Use basis — open:** Wikimedia Commons marks this IWM-held Beaton photograph **Public domain**. Credit Cecil Beaton / Imperial War Museums.

### 06. *Long Range Desert Group member at a jeep wheel*, 1942 (CBM 1212)

- **Why it matters:** The steering wheel, cap, and face make overlapping circles and diagonals in a tightly cropped environmental portrait.
- **Best source:** [IWM CBM 1212](https://www.iwm.org.uk/collections/item/object/205193614); [public original / licence statement](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Cecil_Beaton_Photographs-_General;_British_Army,_Long_Range_Desert_Group_CBM1212.jpg)
- **Use basis — open:** Wikimedia Commons marks this IWM-held Beaton photograph **Public domain**. Credit Cecil Beaton / Imperial War Museums.

### 07. *Long Range Desert Group marching through an archway*, 1942 (CBM 2220)

- **Why it matters:** A large group is disciplined by a single monumental arch, giving the march a processional silhouette and a deep threshold.
- **Best source:** [IWM CBM 2220](https://www.iwm.org.uk/collections/item/object/205193647); [public original / licence statement](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Cecil_Beaton_Photographs-_General;_British_Army,_Long_Range_Desert_Group_CBM2220.jpg)
- **Use basis — open:** Wikimedia Commons marks this IWM-held Beaton photograph **Public domain**. Credit Cecil Beaton / Imperial War Museums.

### 08. *Wellington navigator at his chart table*, 1939–45 (D 4738)

- **Why it matters:** The cockpit's converging equipment and the chart table form a compact geometric stage around a concentrated working figure.
- **Best source:** [IWM D 4738](https://www.iwm.org.uk/collections/item/object/205193605); [public original / licence statement](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:CECIL_BEATON_PHOTOGRAPHS-_GENERAL_D4738.jpg)
- **Use basis — open:** Wikimedia Commons marks this IWM-held Beaton photograph **Public domain**. Credit Cecil Beaton / Imperial War Museums.

### 09. *Wellington rear gunner in position*, 1939–45 (D 4736)

- **Why it matters:** The gunner is surrounded by a circular, mechanical enclosure that pushes the portrait toward graphic abstraction.
- **Best source:** [IWM D 4736](https://www.iwm.org.uk/collections/item/object/205193604); [public original / licence statement](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Cecil_Beaton_Photographs-_General;_Royal_Air_Force_D4736.jpg)
- **Use basis — open:** Wikimedia Commons marks this IWM-held Beaton photograph **Public domain**. Credit Cecil Beaton / Imperial War Museums.

### 10. *Bengali boy using a spinning wheel*, 1944 (IB 1793)

- **Why it matters:** The wheel's radial spokes and the thread's lit diagonal turn a seated figure into a study of circular movement and line.
- **Best source:** [IWM IB 1793](https://www.iwm.org.uk/collections/item/object/205193629); [public original / licence statement](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Cecil_Beaton_Photographs_-_General,_IB1793_-_Bengali_boy_using_a_spinning_wheel_(India,_1944).jpg)
- **Use basis — open:** Wikimedia Commons marks this IWM-held Beaton photograph **Public domain**. Credit Cecil Beaton / Imperial War Museums.

## Scope note

The famous studio-fashion and society portraits were researched as canonical
context but were not selected because the available NPG display records identify
them as © Cecil Beaton Archive / Condé Nast and route reuse to rights holders.
This pass therefore uses the independently strong IWM wartime corpus rather
than substituting a poor reproduction or claiming an unsupported open licence.
