# Loretta Lux — source record

## Selection and rights basis

This twelve-image working set is taken directly from Loretta Lux's official
[Selected Works](https://lorettalux.de/selected-works/) portfolio. The artist's
site publicly serves the intact display JPEGs used below; every downloaded file
opened successfully and has a 900px long edge. Lux's official site identifies
her as the artist, while collection records for *Marianne* at
[MoMA](https://www.moma.org/collection/works/199240) and *The Drummer* at the
[Hood Museum](https://hoodmuseum.dartmouth.edu/objects/2006.34) confirm the
title/date and retain her copyright. The official portfolio makes no open licence
grant, so none of these files is treated as open access.

Each entry is a separate **fair-use** assessment: (1) this book uses a credited,
linked, web-sized copy for transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition
teaching; (2) the underlying work is a creative copyrighted photograph; (3) the
complete frame is necessary because the relation of figure, painted/digitally
constructed setting, and edge is the teaching subject; and (4) this 900px
reference copy neither replaces an original print nor a licensed reproduction,
and the chapter links back to the artist's portfolio and applicable collection
record. Credit for all works: Loretta Lux; copyright © Loretta Lux (MoMA's
record gives this notice for *Marianne*).

## Acquired shortlist

1. **The Red Ball** (2000) — `raw/loretta-lux/01-the-red-ball.jpg` (900 × 900px).
   The red sphere becomes a compact color counterweight within a square, reducing
   the child and empty surrounding space to a controlled balance. [Official public
   display JPEG](https://lorettalux.de/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Loretta-Lux-The-Red-Ball-1_-2000.jpg).
   **Use basis: fair-use.** The complete square is needed to analyze the sphere's
   scale, placement, and the deliberate silence around it.

2. **Girl with Crossed Arms** (2001) — `raw/loretta-lux/02-girl-with-crossed-arms.jpg`
   (900 × 900px). The closed arm gesture gives the centrally placed sitter a rigid
   internal triangle against an unnaturally calm, shallow setting. [Official public
   display JPEG](https://lorettalux.de/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Loretta-Lux-GirlwithcrossedArms-2001.jpg).
   **Use basis: fair-use.** Full-frame access is necessary for the figure/ground
   tension and its centered symmetry.

3. **The Rosegarden** (2001) — `raw/loretta-lux/03-the-rosegarden.jpg` (900 ×
   900px). The child and orderly floral field turn a garden into a flattened,
   slightly theatrical color plane. [Official public display JPEG](https://lorettalux.de/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Loretta-Lux-The-Rosegarden-2001.jpg).
   **Use basis: fair-use.** The whole square is needed to read the patterned field,
   figure scale, and uncanny perspective together.

4. **Study of a Boy 2** (2002) — `raw/loretta-lux/04-study-of-a-boy-2.jpg` (900 ×
   900px). A direct, isolated portrait makes minute proportions of face, torso,
   and blank surround carry the image's uneasy authority. [Official public display
   JPEG](https://lorettalux.de/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Loretta-Lux-Study-of-a-Boy-2-2002.jpg).
   **Use basis: fair-use.** The complete frame preserves the small shifts of body
   scale against negative space that the analysis addresses.

5. **The Book** (2003) — `raw/loretta-lux/05-the-book.jpg` (900 × 590px). The
   book anchors a horizontal tableau and turns a child-reader into a staged
   exchange between a small object and a broad setting. [Official public display
   JPEG](https://lorettalux.de/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Loretta-Lux-The-Book-2003.jpg).
   **Use basis: fair-use.** Its lateral spacing and the book's positional role
   require the unaltered full frame.

6. **The Wanderer** (2003) — `raw/loretta-lux/06-the-wanderer.jpg` (900 ×
   590px). A slight child figure is held in an unusually wide, manufactured-looking
   environment, making distance and horizon the subject. [Official public display
   JPEG](https://lorettalux.de/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Loretta-Lux-The-Wanderer-2003.jpg).
   **Use basis: fair-use.** The complete horizontal is indispensable for studying
   isolation, directional space, and scale.

7. **Marianne** (2004) — `raw/loretta-lux/07-marianne.jpg` (900 × 1255px). The
   vertical portrait presses the sitter through a narrow, cool-toned field, a key
   example of Lux making stillness feel constructed rather than candid. [Official
   public display JPEG](https://lorettalux.de/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Loretta-Lux-Marianne-2004.jpg);
   [MoMA collection record](https://www.moma.org/collection/works/199240).
   **Use basis: fair-use.** The whole vertical is necessary to teach the balance of
   body, surrounding void, and edge pressure.

8. **At the Window** (2004) — `raw/loretta-lux/08-at-the-window.jpg` (900 ×
   900px). The window becomes a nested rectangular device that separates the child
   from a simplified exterior and makes looking itself the compositional action.
   [Official public display JPEG](https://lorettalux.de/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Loretta-Lux_At-the-Window.jpg).
   **Use basis: fair-use.** The intact square is required to analyze the window,
   figure, and surrounding planes as one spatial construction.

9. **The Drummer** (2004) — `raw/loretta-lux/09-the-drummer.jpg` (900 × 1066px).
   The drum supplies a circular interruption in a high vertical portrait, turning
   prop, torso, and blank field into a measured hierarchy. [Official public display
   JPEG](https://lorettalux.de/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Loretta-Lux-The-Drummer-2004.jpg);
   [Hood Museum record](https://hoodmuseum.dartmouth.edu/objects/2006.34).
   **Use basis: fair-use.** The complete portrait is necessary to explain the
   circular counterpoint and vertical spacing.

10. **The Walk** (2004) — `raw/loretta-lux/10-the-walk.jpg` (900 × 712px). The
    walking child is staged in a broad horizontal field, where the long lateral
    interval makes movement read as isolation rather than action. [Official public
    display JPEG](https://lorettalux.de/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Loretta-Lux-The-Walk-2004.jpg).
    **Use basis: fair-use.** Cropping would defeat the teaching point: the figure's
    movement depends on the full field and its empty run-up.

11. **Sasha and Ruby** (2005) — `raw/loretta-lux/11-sasha-and-ruby.jpg` (900 ×
    645px). The double portrait creates a subtle relational imbalance: paired
    children occupy the same frame but resist becoming a warm pair. [Official public
    display JPEG](https://lorettalux.de/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Loretta-Lux-Sasha-and-Ruby-2005.jpg).
    **Use basis: fair-use.** The full two-figure arrangement is essential to
    analyze spacing, gaze, and detached symmetry.

12. **The Waiting Girl** (2006) — `raw/loretta-lux/12-the-waiting-girl.jpg`
    (900 × 645px). The figure's fixed pose and the deliberately sparse surrounding
    space make waiting a geometry of suspended weight. [Official public display
    JPEG](https://lorettalux.de/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Loretta-Lux-The-Waiting-Girl-2006.jpg);
    [Art Gallery of NSW record](https://www.artgallery.nsw.gov.au/collection/works/220.2007/).
    **Use basis: fair-use.** The complete frame is needed to teach negative space,
    posture, and the slightly estranged figure/ground relation.

## Acquisition record

portfolio, retained without alteration, and verified to open locally. The smallest
long edge is 900px, above the 640px normal floor. This source pass exceeds the
chapter's four-image minimum; no limited-source fallback is used.
