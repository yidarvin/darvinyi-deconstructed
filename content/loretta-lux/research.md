# Loretta Lux — research note

## What the source record establishes

The supplied source record identifies twelve works from 2000 to 2006, all
served on Loretta Lux's official Selected Works portfolio as 900px display
JPEGs. It identifies the chapter's working premise as digitally altered child
portraits that combine photographed figures with painted or constructed spaces.
The MoMA record for *Marianne* and the Hood Museum record for *The Drummer*
corroborate the artist, title, date, and continuing copyright for those works.
[Source notes](sources.md)

The source record does not establish a camera body, lens, focal length, aperture,
shutter speed, sensitivity, lighting design, capture sequence, software, editing
procedure, or a print process for these specific works. It also does not supply
edition, paper, pigment, or other printing information for this selection.

## Limits of the ingested files

The ingested display JPEGs retain `DateTimeOriginal` values from 2017. Those
values describe the derivative web assets and cannot document the original
2000–06 exposures or productions. No historical camera settings are therefore
claimed in this chapter. The technical account is restricted to the documented
digital-alteration premise, the official work dates, the source records, and
visible geometry, tone, and palette measured from these web-sized derivatives.
