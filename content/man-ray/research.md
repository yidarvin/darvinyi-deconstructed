# Man Ray — research note

## Scope of the source record

The supplied source packet spans ten works dated 1917–27: early New York portraits, the 1922 domestic portrait of Alice B. Toklas and Gertrude Stein, the Rrose Sélavy portrait of Marcel Duchamp, Paris studio portraits, and one untitled rayograph from *Champs délicieux* (1922). The source records identify the titles, dates, holding institutions or publication routes, credits, and access basis for every image; those per-image details remain in [sources.md](sources.md).

## Technique and printing

The source record supports one definite process statement for this selection: the *Champs délicieux* work is a rayograph, a camera-less photogram made by placing objects between light and photographic paper. The Art Institute record cited in the source packet identifies that object as a gelatin silver print. The other nine reproductions are photographs, but the supplied records do not establish a common negative process, paper, printing date, or original print process for each portrait. This chapter therefore treats their lighting, crops, and tonal structure as visible evidence, not as proof of a studio method.

## Camera settings and digitization metadata

No supplied record identifies a camera model, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, film stock, sensitivity, or exposure setting for any selected work. The normalized manifest retains only a few modern scan-side EXIF values (including a scanner/camera model and recent digitization dates); they describe the web-file production, not Man Ray's equipment. No historic setting is inferred from them.

## Terms and context

The titles *Kiki with African Mask* and *Noire et blanche* document the pairing of Kiki de Montparnasse with an African mask. The available source note calls attention to the cultural framing of that pairing but does not identify the mask's community, maker, or collecting history. The chapter limits itself to the photographs' visible formal rhyme and names the modernist appropriation question without supplying an undocumented provenance.

See [sources.md](sources.md) for object links, credits, rights notices, and the image-by-image open or fair-use rationale.
