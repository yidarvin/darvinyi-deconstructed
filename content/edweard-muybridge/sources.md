# Eadweard Muybridge — sources

Muybridge's *Animal Locomotion* (1887) is a photographic atlas in which composition is inseparable from measurement. Every action is broken into a grid of small, identically framed exposures against a ruled backdrop; a moving body becomes a line, arc, or repeated silhouette that can be compared across time and viewpoint. The ten plates below were downloaded from high-resolution Wikimedia Commons mirrors of Library of Congress scans. The 1887 plates are public domain in the United States (published before 1929); the LOC records are the underlying institutional source.

## Shortlist

### 01 — *Animal Locomotion*, plate 44: man walking while removing hat (1887)
- **Why it matters:** The repeated side-profile figure turns a casual gesture into a measured diagonal progression; the two-row sequence makes the hat's arc as legible as the stride.
- **Source:** https://www.loc.gov/item/92502807/ (high-resolution mirror: https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Animal_locomotion_LCCN92502807.jpg)
- **Rights:** Public domain; *Animal Locomotion* published 1887, Library of Congress scan.
