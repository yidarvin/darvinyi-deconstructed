# Thomas Demand — source notes

## Rights and acquisition basis

This eleven-image working set was downloaded directly from
[Thomas Demand’s official selected-work site](https://thomasdemand.net/selected-work),
where each work is publicly displayed at a web-sized rendition.  The site is the
artist’s portfolio, not an open-licence repository.  Accordingly, every entry below
is a separate **fair-use** assessment; none is represented as open access.  The
relevant collection records identify the applicable notice as © Thomas Demand /
Artists Rights Society (ARS), New York / VG Bild-Kunst, Bonn (for example,
[LACMA’s *Control Room* record](https://collections.lacma.org/object/182977) and
[the Met’s *Vault* record](https://www.metmuseum.org/art/collection/search/302440)).

Each file is a 800px-long-edge JPEG supplied publicly by the artist’s site and was
opened successfully after download.  The chapter’s use is limited to transformative
criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching.  It credits and links to the
artist’s public page and does not replace the artist’s print, museum record, or a
licensed reproduction.  Demand’s paper reconstructions are the subject of the
analysis, so the complete frame—not a decorative crop—is necessary in each case.

## Acquired shortlist

1. **Studio** (1997) — `raw/thomas-demand/01-studio-1997.jpg` (800 × 405px).
   The striped television-set field and empty furniture make a lesson in shallow
   theatrical space, repeated bands, and the productive unease of a room without
   people.  Source: [official work page](https://thomasdemand.net/selected-work/studio);
   [public display JPEG](https://thomasdemand.net/media/pages/selected-work/studio/4f79fb667a-1652963557/original-st-800x.jpg).
   **Use basis: fair-use.** Creator/rightsholder notice: Thomas Demand; © Thomas
   Demand / ARS / VG Bild-Kunst.  This transformative criticism studies the complete
   striped room; it is a creative work, but the web-sized, credited, record-linked
   copy is non-substitutive of a print or licensed image.

2. **Terrasse / Terrace** (1998) — `raw/thomas-demand/02-terrace-1998.jpg`
   (800 × 543px).  The empty patio’s rail, hedge, steps, and foreground plane teach
   how an apparently ordinary view is stabilized by nested horizontals and a held
   central void.  Source: [official work page](https://thomasdemand.net/selected-work/terrasse);
   [public display JPEG](https://thomasdemand.net/media/pages/selected-work/terrasse/9caac9be9f-1646609589/terrasse-800x.jpg).
   **Use basis: fair-use.** Creator/rightsholder notice: Thomas Demand; © Thomas
   Demand / ARS / VG Bild-Kunst.  The whole creative frame is needed to analyze its
   planes and sightline; this small credited teaching copy is transformative and
   does not substitute for the original, collection record, or licensed reproduction.

3. **Grotte / Grotto** (2006) — `raw/thomas-demand/03-grotto-2006.jpg`
   (800 × 364px).  A wide, apparently geological chamber turns paper strata,
   stalactite rhythm, and an uncertain light source into an unusually direct lesson
   in texture, scale, and immersive lateral sweep.  Source: [official work page](https://thomasdemand.net/selected-work/grotte-grotto);
   [public display JPEG](https://thomasdemand.net/media/pages/selected-work/grotte-grotto/7e929dee1a-1646989374/grotte-800x.jpg);
   supporting [Castello di Rivoli collection record](https://www.castellodirivoli.org/en/opera/grotto/).
   **Use basis: fair-use.** Creator/rightsholder notice: Thomas Demand; © Thomas
   Demand / ARS / VG Bild-Kunst.  Criticism needs the complete panoramic creative
   frame to compare its repeated formations; the credited 800px web copy is
   transformative, source-linked, and non-substitutive.

4. **Landing** (2006) — `raw/thomas-demand/04-landing-2006.jpg` (800 × 509px).
   The stair landing makes a nearly blank architectural diagram in which the rail,
   doorway, and floor edges assign visual weight to an otherwise vacant interior.
   Source: [official work page](https://thomasdemand.net/selected-work/landing);
   [public display JPEG](https://thomasdemand.net/media/pages/selected-work/landing/d0efd61ffb-1655457342/landing-800x.jpg).
   **Use basis: fair-use.** Creator/rightsholder notice: Thomas Demand; © Thomas
   Demand / ARS / VG Bild-Kunst.  The full creative frame is indispensable to the
   analysis of edge pressure and recession; this credited, linked web-sized copy is
   used transformatively and cannot replace a print or licensed reproduction.

5. **Presidency** (2008) — `raw/thomas-demand/05-presidency-2008.jpg`
   (800 × 1,113px).  This vertical work makes an empty official-looking interior
   read through a rigid central axis, doorframe repetition, and a high proportion
   of unoccupied wall.  Source: [official work page](https://thomasdemand.net/selected-work/presidency);
   [public display JPEG](https://thomasdemand.net/media/pages/selected-work/presidency/81a5344d85-1648633645/presidency-1-800x.jpg).
   **Use basis: fair-use.** Creator/rightsholder notice: Thomas Demand; © Thomas
   Demand / ARS / VG Bild-Kunst.  The complete creative image is required to assess
   its axial control and negative space; the credited 800px critical-use copy is
   transformative, record-linked, and non-substitutive.

6. **Kontrollraum / Control Room** (2011) —
   `raw/thomas-demand/06-control-room-2011.jpg` (800 × 533px).  The Fukushima
   control-room reconstruction uses scattered consoles, a low horizon, and dense
   cable/desk rhythms to make disaster legible through evacuated infrastructure.
   Source: [official work page](https://thomasdemand.net/selected-work/kontrollraum-controlroom);
   [public display JPEG](https://thomasdemand.net/media/pages/selected-work/kontrollraum-controlroom/7c06b236c8-1648645491/kontrollraum-800x.jpg);
   supporting [LACMA collection record](https://collections.lacma.org/object/182977).
   **Use basis: fair-use.** Creator/rightsholder notice: Thomas Demand; © Thomas
   Demand / ARS / VG Bild-Kunst.  Transformative criticism needs the complete
   creative frame to read the broken control-grid and recession; this small,
   credited, source-linked rendition does not substitute for the original or a
   licensed reproduction.

7. **Vault** (2012) — `raw/thomas-demand/07-vault-2012.jpg` (800 × 636px).
   Frames turned toward the walls, cross-beams, and stacked storage planes turn
   concealment into the picture’s organizing geometry.  Source: [official work page](https://thomasdemand.net/selected-work/vault);
   [public display JPEG](https://thomasdemand.net/media/pages/selected-work/vault/9c9fec47fb-1655716879/vault-800x.jpg);
   supporting [Met collection record](https://www.metmuseum.org/art/collection/search/302440).
   **Use basis: fair-use.** Creator/rightsholder notice: Thomas Demand; © Thomas
   Demand / ARS / VG Bild-Kunst.  The full creative view is needed to analyze the
   facing-away paintings and structural frames; the credited 800px scholarly copy
   is transformative, linked, and non-substitutive.

8. **Atelier** (2014) — `raw/thomas-demand/08-atelier-2014.jpg` (800 × 562px).
   The studio’s cut-paper objects and long work surface teach how a room can be
   ordered by a single directional table and irregular clusters at its margins.
   Source: [official work page](https://thomasdemand.net/selected-work/atelier);
   [public display JPEG](https://thomasdemand.net/media/pages/selected-work/atelier/8e083a9a76-1695213850/atelier-800x.jpg).
   **Use basis: fair-use.** Creator/rightsholder notice: Thomas Demand; © Thomas
   Demand / ARS / VG Bild-Kunst.  The intact creative frame is necessary to compare

   transformative and does not supplant a print or licensed file.

9. **Parkett** (2014) — `raw/thomas-demand/09-parkett-2014.jpg` (800 × 584px).
   The parquet’s repeated directional units convert a domestic floor into a dense
   all-over field whose seams and tonal shifts regulate the frame.  Source:
   [official work page](https://thomasdemand.net/selected-work/parkett);
   [public display JPEG](https://thomasdemand.net/media/pages/selected-work/parkett/14e9bbb89b-1648551681/parkett-800x.jpg).
   **Use basis: fair-use.** Creator/rightsholder notice: Thomas Demand; © Thomas
   Demand / ARS / VG Bild-Kunst.  Critical analysis requires the complete creative
   field to study its repetitions and cropped borders; this 800px credited display

10. **Werkstatt / Workshop** (2017) —
    `raw/thomas-demand/10-workshop-2017.jpg` (800 × 465px).  Tools, shelves, and

    [official work page](https://thomasdemand.net/selected-work/werkstatt);

11. **Pond** (2020) — `raw/thomas-demand/11-pond-2020.jpg` (800 × 400px).  The
    shallow panorama uses bands of water, vegetation, and reflection to make a

    Source: [official work page](https://thomasdemand.net/selected-work/pond);
