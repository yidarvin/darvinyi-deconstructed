# Thomas Demand — research note

## What the supplied sources establish

The supplied source set identifies eleven works made between 1997 and 2020 and
documents their public display on Thomas Demand’s official selected-work site.
The project record describes Demand’s method as life-size paper reconstructions
photographed before the models are destroyed. That construction process makes
the images appropriate for a chapter about staged photographic space, but it
does not license claims about how any individual exposure was made. The source
notes retain the title, year, direct public-display source, copyright notice,
and image-specific fair-use rationale for every plate. [Source notes](sources.md)

## Technical limits for this chapter

The supplied pages do not establish a camera body, lens, focal length, aperture,
shutter speed, sensitivity, film or sensor format, lighting scheme, color
workflow, or edition-specific printing process for these individual works. The
ingested display JPEGs retain no camera EXIF. Accordingly, this chapter treats
paper construction, photographed scale, visible geometry, measured tonal range,
and palette as evidence; it does not turn the images’ precise appearance into
invented capture settings or undocumented print facts.
