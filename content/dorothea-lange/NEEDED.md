# Dorothea Lange — needed / flagged (not cleanly public domain)

Canonical Lange photographs that fit the pyramid / gesture / leading-line signature but are **not** available as U.S.-government public domain, because Lange made them independently — before her 1935 federal hire or after her FSA years. Copyright in this work is held by the **Dorothea Lange Collection, Oakland Museum of California** (gift of Paul S. Taylor; Lange d. 1965, life+70 runs to 2035). Listed here for reference; do not ingest without a clean rights basis.

## White Angel Breadline, San Francisco (1933)
- **Why it matters:** Lange's breakthrough and single most canonical non-government image — a lone destitute man at a soup kitchen rail, arms folded, hat down, back to the crowd, isolated by the diagonal rail against a compressed mass of anonymous backs. The picture that made her documentary reputation and led to the 1935 federal commission.
- **Rights:** NOT public domain. Made in 1933 on Lange's own account, **before** her SERA / Resettlement Administration / FSA work, so it is not a U.S. government work. Rights held by the Dorothea Lange Collection, Oakland Museum of California.
- **Where a good scan lives:** Oakland Museum of California (A67.137.7511) — https://dorothealange.museumca.org/image/white-angel-bread-line-san-francisco/A67.137.7511/ ; SFMOMA (63.19.126) — https://www.sfmoma.org/artwork/63.19.126/ ; vintage prints also at MoMA, the Getty, and Princeton University Art Museum.

## Ditched, Stalled, and Stranded, San Joaquin Valley, California (1935)
- **Why it matters:** A migrant man leaning out of the window of his broken-down car, the long diagonal of the automobile slashing the frame — a canonical Lange road/machine composition often paired with *Toward Los Angeles*.
- **Rights:** Not cleanly PD. Dated 1935 and generally attributed to Lange's California State Emergency Relief Administration (SERA) / pre-federal period; the canonical print is held by the Oakland Museum of California, and it does not appear in the LOC FSA/OWI public-domain negative file. Skip until a verified U.S.-government PD scan surfaces.
- **Where a good scan lives:** Oakland Museum of California, Dorothea Lange Collection (gift of Paul S. Taylor); prints also at MoMA and SFMOMA.

## Migratory Cotton Picker, Eloy, Arizona (1940)
- **Why it matters:** A tight portrait of a cotton picker with his hand drawn across his lower face — the hand-as-keystone gesture that runs through *Migrant Mother* and *Woman of the High Plains*, here at its most reduced.
- **Rights:** NOT cleanly PD. Made in 1940, after Lange's FSA tenure; the canonical print is held by the Oakland Museum of California and is not in the LOC FSA/OWI collection. Flagged so it is not mistaken for FSA-era PD material.
- **Where a good scan lives:** Oakland Museum of California, Dorothea Lange Collection; print also at the J. Paul Getty Museum.

## San Francisco strike & May Day series (1934)
- **Why it matters:** Lange's first street documentary — the 1934 San Francisco general/longshoremen's strike and May Day rallies (e.g. *Street Demonstration, San Francisco*; *May Day Listener, San Francisco*) — the work that, with *White Angel Breadline*, established her before the FSA.
- **Rights:** NOT public domain. Made independently in 1934, pre-federal; rights held by the Oakland Museum of California, Dorothea Lange Collection. Secondary to *White Angel Breadline* — pursue only if a specific frame is wanted.
- **Where a good scan lives:** Oakland Museum of California (e.g. A67.137.7590) — https://dorothealange.museumca.org/ ; prints also at SFMOMA.
