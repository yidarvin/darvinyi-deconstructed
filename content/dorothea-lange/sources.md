# Dorothea Lange — sources

Lange composes for gravity and confrontation. Again and again she monumentalizes a single figure or family into a stable central triangle — the pyramid of *Migrant Mother* — and makes an expressive hand the keystone of that pyramid (the hand to the chin, to the brow, across the mouth). Where she opens the frame she runs a road or a plowed furrow as a hard leading line down to a low vanishing point, the empty distance doing the emotional work; where she closes it she pins the subject dead-center against a flat ground — a papered wall, a shack, an open sky — that denies depth and holds the viewer eye-to-eye. Her group pictures collapse into shallow friezes of figures ranged across a single plane, and her documents of power stage a bright, looming foreground against a low, shadowed background. It is documentary built from classical geometry, and a rich overlay test bed for triangles, leading lines, central-axis symmetry, and figure friezes.

**Rights basis:** Public domain. Images 01–10 are Lange's Farm Security Administration work: she made them in 1935–1938 as a photographer for the U.S. Resettlement Administration / Farm Security Administration (later merged into the Office of War Information), and as works of the U.S. federal government they carry no copyright. They come from the Library of Congress FSA/OWI Black-and-White Negatives collection (LC-USF / LC-DIG numbers), except *Ex-slave with a Long Memory* (09), a Lange print held in the LOC Prints & Photographs Division (PH – Lange (D.)). Images 11–12 (1942) were made for the **War Relocation Authority**, also a U.S. federal agency, and are likewise public domain — held by the U.S. National Archives and the Library of Congress. This PD basis does **not** extend to Lange's pre-federal independent work: *White Angel Breadline* (1933), her 1934 San Francisco general-strike and May Day series, and post-FSA images such as *Migratory Cotton Picker, Eloy* (1940). Copyright in those is held by the Dorothea Lange Collection at the Oakland Museum of California (gift of Paul S. Taylor; Lange d. 1965). See NEEDED.md. (LOC item URLs, dates, and call numbers below are the best-known LOC records, matched to each file's embedded FSA digital id; a few were confirmed from LOC search metadata and museum catalogs where the live LOC page blocked automated fetch.)

## Shortlist

### 01 — Migrant Mother, Nipomo, California (1936)
- **Why it matters:** The defining Lange pyramid — the mother's hand-to-chin apex descends through her shoulders and the two face-averted children into a broad seated base, a classical triangle that monumentalizes the group while her off-frame gaze and clenched hand hold the tension.
- **Source:** https://www.loc.gov/item/2017762891/ (LC-USF34-009058-C; digital id fsa 8b29516). LOC caption: "Destitute pea pickers in California. Mother of seven children. Age thirty-two. Nipomo, California."
- **Rights:** Public domain (U.S. government work; FSA/OWI Collection, Library of Congress)
- **File:** raw/dorothea-lange/01-migrant-mother.jpg (8539px)

### 02 — Damaged Child, Elm Grove, Oklahoma (1936)
- **Why it matters:** A dead-center frontal portrait — the child's shoulders make a base-heavy pyramid up to the apex of her head, while the horizontal seam of the corrugated cardboard cuts a false horizon at nose level just below her eyes, a flat and escape-proof frame pinned to her direct gaze.
- **Source:** https://www.loc.gov/item/2017763117/ (LC-USF347-009690-C; digital id fsa 8b38490). LOC caption: "Child living in Oklahoma City shacktown."
- **Rights:** Public domain (U.S. government work; FSA/OWI Collection, Library of Congress)
- **File:** raw/dorothea-lange/02-damaged-child.jpg (4320px)

### 03 — Toward Los Angeles, California (1937)
- **Why it matters:** Road-as-leading-line — the highway drives a hard diagonal to a low vanishing point, the two back-turned walkers anchored on the left third against the bright rectangle of the "NEXT TIME TRY THE TRAIN / RELAX" billboard, the irony built straight into the geometry.
- **Source:** https://www.loc.gov/item/2017769825/ (LC-USF34-016317-E; digital id fsa 8b31801)
- **Rights:** Public domain (U.S. government work; FSA/OWI Collection, Library of Congress)
- **File:** raw/dorothea-lange/03-toward-los-angeles.jpg (4849px)

### 04 — Tractored Out, Childress County, Texas (1938)
- **Why it matters:** Concentric contour-plow furrows sweep as curved leading lines that wrap around and isolate the lone farmhouse perched high on the horizon — the machine's own geometry encircling the displaced tenant against a near-empty sky.
- **Source:** https://www.loc.gov/item/2017770664/ (LC-USF34-018281-C; reproduction LC-DIG-ppmsc-00232). LOC caption: "Power farming displaces tenants from the land in the western dry cotton area. Childress County, Texas Panhandle."
- **Rights:** Public domain (U.S. government work; FSA/OWI Collection, Library of Congress)
- **File:** raw/dorothea-lange/04-tractored-out.jpg (5783px)

### 05 — Woman of the High Plains, Texas Panhandle (1938)
- **Why it matters:** A low-horizon monumental portrait — the raised bent arm and the crossed lower arm interlock into paired triangles that lock the head as apex, making the hand-to-brow gesture the compositional keystone against an almost empty sky.
- **Source:** https://www.loc.gov/item/2017770680/ (LC-USF34-018294-C; digital id fsa 8b32434). LOC caption: "Wife of a migratory laborer with three children. Near Childress, Texas" (Nettie Featherston).
- **Rights:** Public domain (U.S. government work; FSA/OWI Collection, Library of Congress)
- **File:** raw/dorothea-lange/05-woman-high-plains.jpg (9088px)

### 06 — Filipinos Cutting Lettuce, Salinas, California (1935)
- **Why it matters:** A receding diagonal — the cracked-earth furrow drives in from the lower left while the bent workers repeat as rhythmic, diminishing forms toward a high horizon, turning stoop labor into pure perspective.
- **Source:** https://www.loc.gov/item/2017759127/ (LC-USF347-000826-D; digital id fsa 8e07356)
- **Rights:** Public domain (U.S. government work; FSA/OWI Collection, Library of Congress)
- **File:** raw/dorothea-lange/06-filipinos-cutting-lettuce.jpg (9408px)

### 07 — Drought Refugees, California (1936)
- **Why it matters:** The family compressed into a tight human pyramid wedged between two dark framing masses — the diagonal slope of the tent on the left, the hard curve of the loaded car's fender on the right — the two small children anchoring the triangle's base.
- **Source:** https://www.loc.gov/item/2017759653/ (LC-USF34-001823-C; digital id fsa 8b27087). LOC caption: "Drought refugees. California."
- **Rights:** Public domain (U.S. government work; FSA/OWI Collection, Library of Congress)
- **File:** raw/dorothea-lange/07-drought-refugees.jpg (9456px)

### 08 — Plantation Overseer and His Field Hands, Mississippi Delta (1936)
- **Why it matters:** Power as geometry — the overseer a looming vertical wedge, his propped leg a bracing triangle filling the bright right foreground, while the Black field hands are pressed into a low, shadowed horizontal frieze behind; the raised knee is the diagonal hinge that subordinates them.
- **Source:** https://www.loc.gov/item/2017763069/ (LC-USF34-009596-C; digital id fsa 8b29711). LOC caption: "Plantation overseer. Mississippi Delta, near Clarksdale, Mississippi."
- **Rights:** Public domain (U.S. government work; FSA/OWI Collection, Library of Congress)
- **File:** raw/dorothea-lange/08-plantation-overseer.jpg (9448px)

### 09 — Ex-slave with a Long Memory, Alabama (1938)
- **Why it matters:** A lone frontal standing figure planted on the central vertical axis, her diagonal cane and the road's converging ruts steadying the body against a chaotic overgrown ground and a collapsing cabin — Lange's dignified, monumentalized isolation of a single subject.
- **Source:** https://www.loc.gov/item/2002706484/ (PH – Lange (D.), no. 9; reproduction LC-USZC4-9823; digital id cph 3g09823). Full-resolution scan via Wikimedia Commons.
- **Rights:** Public domain (U.S. government work; Dorothea Lange, FSA era; Library of Congress Prints & Photographs Division)
- **File:** raw/dorothea-lange/09-ex-slave-long-memory.jpg (3856px)

### 10 — Family Walking on Highway, Pittsburg County, Oklahoma (1938)
- **Why it matters:** The dirt highway is a hard receding leading line to a vanishing point on the tree-lined horizon; the family is strung out as a broken diagonal frieze — the large near father anchoring the lower right, the figures diminishing back into the distance — and the cart's cast shadow doubles the forward diagonal of departure.
- **Source:** https://www.loc.gov/item/2017770606/ (LC-USF34-018227-C; digital id fsa 8b38702). LOC caption: "Family walking on highway, five children. Started from Idabel, Oklahoma. Bound for Krebs, Oklahoma. Pittsburg County, Oklahoma."
- **Rights:** Public domain (U.S. government work; FSA/OWI Collection, Library of Congress)
- **File:** raw/dorothea-lange/10-family-walking-highway.jpg (9833px)

### 11 — Members of the Mochida Family Awaiting the Evacuation Bus, Hayward, California (1942)
- **Why it matters:** A nine-person frieze compressed into a shallow human wall — two staggered rows spanning the full width with almost no depth, the stair-step of heights descending to the two "Mochida"-labeled duffels on the foreground baseline, the paper identification tags repeating as bright accents across the mass.
- **Source:** https://catalog.archives.gov/id/537505 (NARA Local Identifier 210-GC-153; National Archives ID 537505; RG 210, Records of the War Relocation Authority). Original NARA TIFF via Wikimedia Commons.
- **Rights:** Public domain (U.S. government work; War Relocation Authority; U.S. National Archives)
- **File:** raw/dorothea-lange/11-mochida-family-hayward-evacuation.jpg (3000px)

### 12 — Pledge of Allegiance, Raphael Weill Elementary School, San Francisco (1942)
- **Why it matters:** A tight frieze of frontal faces on a shallow plane — the winter-coated girls a rhythmic foreground row along the lower-third line, each right hand a repeated diagonal across the chest, while the crowd of upturned heads stacks into a dense wall behind, converging on the unseen flag.
- **Source:** https://www.loc.gov/item/2001705926/ (repro LC-DIG-ppmsca-98857). LOC caption: "Children of the Weill public school … shown in a flag pledge ceremony. Some of them are evacuees of Japanese ancestry." Full-resolution frame via Wikimedia Commons.
- **Rights:** Public domain (U.S. government work; War Relocation Authority; Library of Congress)
- **File:** raw/dorothea-lange/12-pledge-of-allegiance.jpg (1516px)
