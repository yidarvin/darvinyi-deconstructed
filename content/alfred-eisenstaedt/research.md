# Alfred Eisenstaedt — research

## Scope and sources

This chapter uses ten web-sized display reproductions credited to Alfred Eisenstaedt by the LIFE Photo Collection through Google Arts & Culture. The item titles, dates, credits, and rights route are documented image by image in [sources.md](sources.md). The selection ranges from 1933 to 1972 and includes public ritual, political portraiture, work, family, photographic equipment, and postwar aftermath. It is a teaching selection, not a survey of Eisenstaedt's full career.

## Technique and visual method

The records support a description of visual method rather than a technical recipe. Across the supplied photographs, Eisenstaedt makes a readable event from strong relations between bodies, built or found frames, and surrounding witnesses: an embrace interrupts a street; nurses make a tilted stairwell into a ring; a raised hand makes a portrait active; baskets, scaffolds, trees, and equipment turn context into structure. The visual evidence supports careful claims about cropping, placement, tone, repetition, and scale. It does not establish that every apparent line was deliberately planned in advance.

## Process, camera, and printing

The supplied LIFE/Google Arts & Culture records do not provide a camera model, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, film stock, negative format, darkroom process, or original print process for these ten particular reproductions. `manifest.json` records no capture EXIF after normalization. The JPEGs are contemporary web reproductions; their pixels and any later scan characteristics cannot establish the settings or printing method of the historical exposure. No camera setting, film stock, or print finish is assigned in this chapter.

## Rights and provenance
