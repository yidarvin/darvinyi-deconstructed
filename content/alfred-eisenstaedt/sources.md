# Alfred Eisenstaedt — source notes

## Acquisition and rights approach

All ten files below are public, web-sized display renditions supplied on the
[LIFE Photo Collection’s Alfred Eisenstaedt story](https://artsandculture.google.com/story/13-unforgettable-photos-by-alfred-eisenstaedt/OwVxHMDOPffkLQ?hl=en)
at Google Arts & Culture.  The collection identifies Eisenstaedt as photographer
and LIFE as provider.  They are not offered as open content; Google’s individual
LIFE records identify the collection as © Time Inc. and restrict use to personal,
non-commercial use.  Accordingly, every reproduction here is a deliberately
limited **fair-use** selection, not an assertion that the files are free to reuse.

Each item below records the same four-factor conclusion, applied to that specific
photograph: (1) this nonprofit chapter uses the image transformatively for
criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching; (2) the historical/documentary
work is published, but the expressive photograph is respected by using only the
complete frame needed for the analysis; (3) the web-sized display copy is no
larger than needed for legibility; and (4) the chapter credits and links to LIFE’s
record and is not a substitute for the original, the record, or a licensed print.
The complete frame is pedagogically necessary in each case because the lesson
depends on the photographer’s placement of subject, setting, and edge space.

   Source: [LIFE Photo Collection record](https://artsandculture.google.com/asset/vj-day/3wE6XAoA27pQuw?hl=en).  **Use basis: fair-use.** LIFE Photo Collection;
