# Bill Brandt — research notes

## Scope and chronology

The twelve supplied Art Institute of Chicago records span the catalogue dates 1928/36–1957: northern industrial work from 1937, *Stonehenge* (1935/49), wartime London from c. 1940 and 1940, and nudes from 1953–57. The slash dates are retained as the museum’s catalogue notation rather than treated as reconstructed exposure ranges. The 1937 northern photographs sit in Brandt’s response to industrial Britain and unemployment; the V&A relates the Jarrow work to the 1936 Jarrow Crusade and identifies coal-searching as retrieving coal from spoil heaps. [V&A, “Setting the Scene”](https://www.vam.ac.uk/articles/bill-brandt-setting-the-scene)

Brandt (1904–1983) assisted Man Ray in Paris in 1929 before establishing his English practice in the 1930s. The V&A identifies his early books as *The English at Home* (1936) and *A Night in London* (1938); it also cautions against taking his scenes as unmediated reportage, documenting the use of family and friends and darkroom alteration in his practice. These are practice-level facts, not evidence that a particular supplied plate was staged or altered. [V&A introduction](https://www.vam.ac.uk/articles/bill-brandt-an-introduction)

## Materials and method

Every selected Art Institute object is catalogued as a gelatin silver print. The records supplied for this chapter do not establish a vintage-print status, negative, paper, developer, or per-image darkroom process. *East Sussex Coast* (1957) is explicitly catalogued as from *Perspective of Nudes*; the other series attributions are not inferred here.

For the later nude work, a MoMA catalogue describes Brandt’s series-level use of a large, wide-angle, fixed-focus mahogany-and-brass Kodak camera originally made for estate and crime-scene inventory. It says he moved progressively closer, amplifying distortion, and later used it on English and French beaches. That supports the chapter’s account of proximity and scale in the nudes, but not a camera, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, film stock, or exposure claim for any individual image. [MoMA catalogue](https://www.moma.org/d/pdfs/W1siZiIsIjIwMTgvMDYvMTMvMWM0eWhlcDZiMV9CcmFuZHRfUFJFVklFVy5wZGYiXV0/Brandt_PREVIEW.pdf?sha=fe75fa6cdef114fc)

The Institute of Contemporary Photography records Brandt’s air-raid-shelter photographs as Ministry of Information work during the war. That context supports the chapter’s treatment of *London During the Blitz* as shelter imagery, while the date and title remain tied to its Art Institute record. [ICP biography](https://www.icp.org/browse/archive/constituents/bill-brandt)

## Settings honesty

No selected image has a documented per-frame camera body, lens focal length, aperture, shutter speed, film stock, exposure setting, or paper/developer in the supplied record. The web JPEGs cannot establish those facts. The chapter therefore treats the overlays and image analysis as visible formal evidence and confines historical process claims to the documented series and practice level above.
