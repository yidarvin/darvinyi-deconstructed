# Bill Brandt — source shortlist

Bill Brandt used the same hard, graphic black-and-white language to describe social class, industrial Britain, bombed London, ancient landscape, and the radically foreshortened nude. This twelve-image working set follows that range: northern-industrial photographs, war work, then the later landscape and nude studies. Every file is a verified JPEG served publicly from the Art Institute of Chicago’s collection IIIF service at 843px wide. That modest display size clears the project’s 640px floor while remaining unsuitable as a substitute for a print or licensed reproduction.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. The Art Institute API identifies the works as not public domain and credits Bill Brandt (English, 1904–1983); its individual records list the acquisition credit below but do not supply a separate copyright notice or named rightsholder. The applicable rightsholder is therefore recorded conservatively as “Bill Brandt / rightsholder not specified by the Art Institute record.” Each assessment below applies all four factors: this nonprofit book uses the whole web-sized frame for transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching; the underlying photograph is a creative work; the whole frame is necessary to analyze the stated spatial and tonal construction; and the credited, record-linked display copy does not replace an original, museum record, print, or licensed reproduction.

## Research context

The Art Institute’s [Bill Brandt artist page](https://www.artic.edu/artists/5647/bill-brandt) identifies him as English (1904–1983) and its collection records show the body of work above moving from 1930s British social and industrial subjects to wartime London, landscape, and 1950s nudes. This selection keeps those bodies in dialogue so the eventual chapter can treat Brandt’s high contrast and altered perspective as a continuous compositional method rather than as separate genres.
