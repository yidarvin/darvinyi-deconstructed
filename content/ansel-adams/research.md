# Ansel Adams: research note

## Record-bound facts

The source set is made from twelve Art Institute of Chicago collection records, all credited to Ansel Adams and supplied here as 843px public display renditions under the documented fair-use rationale in [sources.md](sources.md). The documented picture dates run from *Forest, Winter, Yosemite Valley* (1922/52) and *Monolith, The Face of Half Dome* (1927; printed 1959), through 1960's *Moon and Half Dome, Yosemite Valley*. Several records distinguish the exposure or negative date from a later print date: *Clearing Winter Storm* is c. 1937 and printed 1959; *Moon and Half Dome* is 1960 and printed 1975/80; *Moon and Clouds* is 1959 and printed 1970; and *El Capitan, Sunrise* is 1956 and printed 1959. The records list *White House Ruin* as no. 4 from *Portfolio Two: The National Parks and Monuments*.

## Technique, printing, and settings

These AIC record pages, as summarized in the source notes, identify photographs and their dates or later print dates, but they do not document a camera model, lens, negative format, film stock, aperture, shutter speed, filtration, development, paper, or printing process for these individual objects. The ingest manifest confirms that all twelve acquired display JPEGs have empty EXIF objects. No camera or exposure setting is inferred from the display files, their contrast, or their grain.

The chapter can therefore make claims about visible framing and the analyzer's measured tonal data, and it can distinguish the recorded dates from the print dates where supplied. It does not use the broad label "Zone System" as evidence for an unrecorded setting or printing decision in any individual photograph.

## Scope and limits

The present analyses are readings of web-sized museum display renditions, not examinations of original negatives or prints. Their numerical palette, luminance, and edge-density observations describe the normalized JPEGs in this repository. Titles, dates, collection credit lines, and the fair-use basis remain in [sources.md](sources.md).
