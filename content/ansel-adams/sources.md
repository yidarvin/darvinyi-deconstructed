# Ansel Adams — source shortlist

This twelve-image set follows Adams from his early, sharply simplified Yosemite pictures through the tonal theatre of his mature landscapes and the social geography of *Mount Williamson*. Every file is an unaltered, publicly served Art Institute of Chicago (AIC) IIIF display rendition, downloaded directly from the museum and individually opened after download. The 843px long-edge files clear the project’s 640px acquisition floor while remaining materially smaller than print-production or licensed master files.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. AIC marks all twelve object records `is_public_domain: false`; the records identify Ansel Adams as maker but do not supply a separate copyright field. The registry’s rights holder is The Ansel Adams Publishing Rights Trust. Each assessment below therefore uses the museum’s public display image for nonprofit, transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching: the underlying photographs are creative; their complete frames are needed to analyze their particular formal structures; and the credited, record-linked 843px copy is web-sized and cannot substitute for a museum record, original print, or licensed reproduction.

## Research context

The AIC’s collection offers a coherent, authoritative acquisition route while keeping the chapter’s files deliberately small. The selection centers the works named in the registry—*Moonrise* and *Clearing Winter Storm*—then uses closely related Yosemite, desert, and sky pictures to show Adams’s recurring compositional tools: extreme tonal separation, large silhouette, foreground scale anchors, vertical pressure, and measured intervals of light.
