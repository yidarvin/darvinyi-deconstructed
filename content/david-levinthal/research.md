# David Levinthal — research note

## Scope and evidence

The selected works span *Modern Romance* (1984–85), *Wild West* (1988–98), *American Beauties* (1990), *Barbie* (1998), *Baseball* (2004), and *History* (2013). The Smithsonian American Art Museum describes these as part of Levinthal's best-known bodies of work, made with toy cowboys, Barbie dolls, baseball players, and American historical imagery. The artist's biography describes a longer practice concerned with fantasies, myths, events, and characters. The individual object records and rights information are collected in [sources.md](sources.md).

## Method and look

The available institutional material establishes staged, toy-scale subject matter and the series titles, but it does not establish a camera model, lens, film stock, exposure settings, darkroom process, paper, edition printing process, or other technical specification for this set. It would be inaccurate to infer any of those details from the web derivatives. The photographs themselves make selective focus, blur, compressed scale, color fields, and staged gesture visibly central; this chapter discusses those observable effects rather than assigning undocumented equipment or process to them.

## Image files

The 12 supplied JPEGs are Smithsonian web-display derivatives. They preserve the full framed work—including its visible paper edge and signature in many cases—and are useful for studying the image's composition, but they are not evidence about the original print's materials or dimensions.
