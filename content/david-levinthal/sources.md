# David Levinthal — source shortlist

This twelve-image set follows David Levinthal's toy-scale tableaux across *Modern Romance*, *American Beauties*, *Barbie*, *Wild West*, *Baseball*, and *History*. Each JPEG was retrieved directly from the Smithsonian American Art Museum's public image-delivery service, linked to its collection record below, and verified as an opening JPEG. The files are web-sized 1200px-long-edge derivatives, not substitutes for prints or licensed reproductions.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. Each SAAM object record identifies David Levinthal and a © David Levinthal notice (with the work's year); the museum's image-download interface labels its supplied media public domain, but that statement does not clear the underlying copyrighted photograph. Each assessment below therefore treats the use as a limited, record-linked reproduction for transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching. The complete frame is retained only where needed to analyze the tableau; the copies remain web-sized, credited, and non-substitutive of the artist's work, the museum record, or licensed reproductions.

## Research context

The Smithsonian American Art Museum's 2019 exhibition describes the series represented here as six of Levinthal's best-known bodies of work, made between 1984 and 2018, and notes their use of toy cowboys, Barbie dolls, baseball players, and American historical imagery: https://americanart.si.edu/exhibitions/levinthal. The artist's own biography identifies his longer investigation of fantasies, myths, events, and characters through major series including *Hitler Moves East*, *Modern Romance*, *Wild West*, *Desire*, *Blackface*, *Barbie*, *Baseball*, and *History*: https://davidlevinthal.com/biography/.
