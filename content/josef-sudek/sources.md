# Josef Sudek — source shortlist

This eleven-image working set draws on the Art Institute of Chicago's public collection API and IIIF image service. It pairs Sudek's Prague and cathedral studies with the rigorously arranged Sutnar commercial commissions and later, more inward-looking landscapes and still life. Every direct public display rendition was retrieved as a JPEG and checked as opening successfully. The served files are 843px on the short or long dimension and 626–1371px on the other; each meets the project's 640px long-edge floor while remaining too small to substitute for a print or licensed reproduction.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. The Art Institute API identifies Josef Sudek as the artist and marks every listed object `is_public_domain: false`; the individual records supply collection-credit lines but no copyright notice or named rightsholder. This chapter therefore credits the museum record and does not invent a more specific rights notice. For each image, the use is nonprofit, transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching; the photograph is a creative work; the complete frame is required for the stated spatial analysis; and the credited, record-linked web display copy is not a substitute for the original, collection record, or a licensed reproduction.

## Research context

The canonical studio-window and late still-life groups were separately checked against the Getty and MoMA collection records: Getty identifies *The Window of My Studio* (1940–1954) and *Late Roses* (1959), while MoMA lists *The Window of My Studio*, *A Walk in the Magic Garden*, *My Studio (Muj Atelier)*, and *Still Life by Window*. Getty's served image for these copyrighted works was only a 300px thumbnail at this pass, below the project floor, so it was not acquired or used. The acquired Art Institute set instead offers a sufficient, compositionally varied and documented route into Sudek's Prague, architectural, commercial, landscape, and late interior practices without substituting an image or lowering the quality threshold.
