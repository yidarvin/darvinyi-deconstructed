# Josef Sudek — research note

## Scope and sources

This chapter studies the eleven Art Institute of Chicago records documented in [the source notes](sources.md), rather than treating the selection as a proxy for every part of Sudek's career. The group includes Prague and cathedral studies, four porcelain commissions for Ladislav Sutnar, a 1953 figure study, and a 1975 tree study. The well-known *Window of My Studio* group is discussed only as context: the Getty dates one such work to 1940–1954 and identifies it as a gelatin silver print, but that work was not acquired for this chapter.

The Museum of Decorative Arts in Prague records that Sudek began as an amateur photographer in 1911 while apprenticed as a bookbinder; he entered the State Graphic School's photography department in 1922 and completed it in 1924. It also describes commercial commissions from 1928, including porcelain and industrial work, and his later turn toward subjective work during the Second World War. These facts help explain the range visible here, but they do not license a claim that all of the photographs use one method or one series.

## Technique and printing

The object records supply print information only selectively. *Blossoming Tree*, *Prague at Night*, and the untitled cathedral view (09) are described as gelatin silver prints on postcard stock. *Study of a Blasted Tree* is a gelatin silver contact print. *Untitled* (1953) is a pigment print. The available source records do not establish a process for every plate, so the chapter does not generalize these descriptions across the group.

The Museum of Decorative Arts notes Sudek's use around 1940 of contact prints, pigment processes, and cumbersome large-format plate cameras. That is useful career context, not an attribution of a camera or process to any individual image unless the object record says so. Neither the Art Institute records nor the ingested JPEGs provide reliable individual camera, lens, film-stock, aperture, shutter-speed, filter, or development data. The normalized files have no usable EXIF. No settings are inferred from grain, contrast, or tonal range.

## Historical cautions

The Art Institute dates this set as follows: 1928 for *St. Vitus Cathedral*; 1930–32 for the Prague and Sutnar works; 1953 for the later *Untitled*; and 1975 for *Study of a Blasted Tree*. The record for the forest *Untitled* gives 1915/76, a broad collection date rather than evidence that it was made in 1915. The source notes retain the collection links, credits, copyright status, and per-image fair-use rationale for every reproduced work.
