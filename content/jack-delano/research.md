# Jack Delano — research notes

## Record and era

This twelve-image selection is drawn from Jack Delano's Farm Security Administration/Office of War Information work, dated 1940–43 in the linked Library of Congress records in [sources.md](sources.md). It begins with Connecticut and Vermont fair and coastal scenes, then follows Delano to railroad work in Illinois, Iowa, Chicago, and New Mexico. The Library of Congress identifies the collection as FSA/OWI colour photographs and describes its colour material as Kodachrome; the supplied files are normalized, uncropped JPEG derivatives of the listed LOC downloads.

## Materials and technique

The item and collection records support calling these original colour transparencies/Kodachrome material. They do not establish a particular exhibition print, paper, or darkroom workflow for these individual photographs, so this chapter treats the measured colours as qualities of the reproduced images rather than claims about historical printing. Across the set, colour makes distinct spatial masses legible: red wood and wheels at the auction, painted fair banners, blue-grey locomotive steel and steam, the saturated caboose, and the pale sky over rails.

## Camera and exposure settings

Neither the supplied item records nor the normalized manifest records a camera body, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, or exposure for these photographs. The manifest has no capture EXIF. No camera or exposure settings are assigned in this chapter.

## Rights

The source shortlist records each plate as a Library of Congress FSA/OWI colour transparency. The Library's collection page states that the collection material is public domain and free to use and reuse; the individual item pages carry “No known restrictions on publication.”
