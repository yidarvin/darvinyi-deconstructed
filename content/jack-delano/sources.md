# Jack Delano — source shortlist

This set centers Delano's unusually rich FSA/OWI color work from 1940–43. The Vermont fair pictures establish his use of color as theatrical structure; the later railroad series turns industrial work, steam, and yard geometry into a sustained study of scale, rhythm, and human placement.

**Rights basis:** Every image below is a Library of Congress Farm Security Administration/Office of War Information color transparency. LOC states that the collection's contents are public domain and free to use and reuse; the item records carry the Rights Advisory “No known restrictions on publication.” Master TIFFs from the LOC download endpoints were converted without cropping to the listed JPEGs.

## Source notes

- LOC's [FSA/OWI color photographs rights statement](https://www.loc.gov/pictures/collection/fsac/) is the collection-level rights basis for this set. The collection record identifies its Kodachrome material and Delano's 1940–43 work.
- The [*Bound for Glory: America in Color* exhibition checklist](https://www.loc.gov/exhibits/bound-for-glory/exhibititems.html) independently identifies the Delano fair and railroad pictures above as FSA/OWI collection works.
- All twelve files were opened after conversion and have a long edge from 4,637px to 8,585px; no source remains outstanding, so `NEEDED.md` is not required for this photographer.
