Maggie Taylor's early digital photomontages do not ask their fragments to agree on a single world. A field can be a stage, a dress can become a swarm, a tree can hold an impossible ladder, and a house can be outscaled by flying pages. Across this selection, the square frame is the stabilizer: it gives collisions of photographs, scans, historical-looking figures, animals, and objects a compact place to negotiate scale. The overlays name the visible routes rather than pretending the collages offer ordinary perspective. [Source notes](../../content/maggie-taylor/sources.md)

## the square as a shallow stage

<Plate slug="maggie-taylor" id="01-garden" label="Garden" caption="Three inverted figures turn a field boundary into a shallow, unstable stage." />

In *Garden*, the field boundary at y0.329 is a flat band, not a natural horizon: it lets three inverted figures read as an uneven left–center–right rhythm. The teal anchors land on the raised boots, and the thirds grid makes the larger outer figures bracket the smaller middle one. Mean luminance is 0.301, with dark green #0f220f and #25472e held against muted sky #757e96 and #a3abc7; that restrained range makes the bodies feel inserted into the field rather than absorbed by it.

<Plate slug="maggie-taylor" id="02-three-trees-two-rabbits" label="Three trees, two rabbits" caption="A floating turf platform and a dark oval opening set two rabbits at deliberately unequal scales." />

Two ellipses organize *Three trees, two rabbits*: the raised turf platform holds the merged tree crown, while the black opening below releases a smaller rabbit against the oversized foreground animal. At edge density 0.208, the chapter's most articulated early field, brown #603b13 and gold #9b7330 keep those ovals materially separate from the blue-green surround. The image works by making counting and scale more persuasive than depth.

<Plate slug="maggie-taylor" id="03-woman-with-a-stone-skirt" label="Woman with a stone skirt" caption="A headless torso and a mound of stones turn costume into a heavy lower-frame architecture." />

The stone silhouette in *Woman with a stone skirt* expands downward until the torso becomes an apex rather than a complete figure. The dog-to-torso diagonal is an invitation across the empty left field, but the dog remains a small counterweight, not an explanation. The wide p05–p95 range, 0.016–0.882, lets near-black #1b1407, pink-gray #bc9996, and pale #e2d5d7 hold the improbable assembly together without making the low background seam into a horizon.

## figures held by interruption

<Plate slug="maggie-taylor" id="04-girl-with-a-bee-dress" label="Girl with a beedress" caption="A frontal figure and flower are crossed by a tapering mass of bees." />

*Girl with a beedress* begins with a near-central frontal axis, then lets the flower at the sternum interrupt that calm. The bee mass tapers down through the body rather than functioning as a conventional garment; it turns silhouette into surface and surface into movement. A comparatively low 0.056 edge density keeps the #e4b474 flower and the dark #2a160b / brown #60422a bee field readable as one concentrated event rather than visual noise.

<Plate slug="maggie-taylor" id="05-night-garden" label="Night garden" caption="Tree shadow, ladder, and opening create two linked descents in a blue nocturne." />

The tree in *Night garden* acts as the first vertical anchor, its shadow carrying the eye into the lower left. The ladder provides a second descent into the dark opening at right; the overlay names these object paths instead of forcing the stylized fence into a precise vanishing system. Its mean luminance is 0.199, with #0b0d10 and #1a2331 offset by blue #1b4291, so the sparse fruit-like accents and white fence marks have to work as discoveries against darkness.

<Plate slug="maggie-taylor" id="06-cloud-sisters" label="Cloud sisters" caption="Two unequal seated figures are paired, then broken apart by scale and drifting clouds." />

In *Cloud sisters*, a line between the two heads is a tension line rather than proof of exact symmetry. The smaller figure at left and larger figure at right have similar seated poses, but cloud crossings obscure them differently and the right body carries more weight. The pale palette of #c6d9dd, #99afb5, and #d0af6e, plus a very low 0.028 edge density, gives the rain-streaked composite a broad, airy field in which those differences can register.

<Plate slug="maggie-taylor" id="07-the-alchemists" label="The alchemists" caption="A rock edge holds the group at right while a curved rod reaches to a suspended pod." />

*The alchemists* turns a large rock into a diagonal platform. Four crowned figures mass at its upper edge; the left figure's curved rod reaches across the empty field to the hanging pod, making a circuit that is concrete rather than merely symbolic. Black #15120b, pale #cfdcc5, green #48635b, and water-blue at the base spread a p05–p95 range of 0.004–0.898, so the sparse scene retains a theatrical range without claiming natural space.

<Plate slug="maggie-taylor" id="08-moth-dancer" label="Moth dancer" caption="A centered dancer is held in place by a winged, uneven peripheral balance." />

The dancer in *Moth dancer* is deliberately still: the face and vertical axis hold the frame while two feather-like head forms and flanking moths construct a winged perimeter. The paired polyline paths trace that lateral balance without inventing upward motion. Mean luminance 0.300 and the palette from #21100a and #40342d to #859ba0 and #cbc598 preserve the centered body against a dim, worn field.

## paper, apparatus, and the improbable field

<Plate slug="maggie-taylor" id="09-poets-house" label="Poet's house" caption="A low house anchors an enormous spiral of manuscript pages." />

The house in *Poet's house* is small but structurally decisive: it pins the bottom of a page spiral that occupies most of the square. The ground–sky boundary at y0.800 is a local compositional seam, not a claim about real distance. The warm paper colors #a2713b and #e1c599 lift from blue #27316b and red-brown #6d5659; the 0.069 edge density leaves enough open air for the loop of pages to read as one enlarged gesture.

<Plate slug="maggie-taylor" id="10-the-reader" label="The reader" caption="A seated man, gramophone horn, and large page arc make the empty left side active." />

Despite its title, *The reader* does not show a person reading. A seated man is weighted to the right, while the horn points toward his head and the loose pages describe a broad counterclockwise arc through the vacant left. The low floor boundary grounds that relay without converting the check pattern into a reliable perspective diagram. Dark #201b14, gray #726c77, and gold #dbbe71 make the pages the picture's brightest moving punctuation at an edge density of 0.057.

<Plate slug="maggie-taylor" id="11-water-folly" label="Water folly" caption="A ladder scaffolds a flat theatrical field between a blue basin and a small butterfly." />

*Water folly* is less an image of reflection than of a backdrop: the low line is the roll of a stage cloth, and the ladder is the central drawn structure. A blue basin at lower left and one small blue butterfly at upper right make a diagonal counterpoint without filling the broad blank field. Its 0.010 edge density is the set's quietest; pale #c1cbcd and #919f9b allow the brown ladder and blue details to carry almost all the event.

<Plate slug="maggie-taylor" id="12-the-patient-gardener" label="The patient gardener" caption="A monumental vine-covered figure spreads its arms across a dense field of butterflies and foliage." />

The title of *The patient gardener* understates its figure: it is monumental, center-left, and crossed by a broad arm span. Blue butterflies and the small glowing house act as counters against the vine-covered body, not as empty-space markers. With edge density 0.156, dark green #0a140e, #29402a, and yellow-green #6f7023 make the foliage tactile enough that the blue accents feel precisely placed rather than decorative.

## Exercises

<ExerciseCard n="01" title="Make a seam into a stage">
Photograph or assemble a field with one clear horizontal boundary. Place three figures or objects at unequal scales across it. Check whether the boundary makes their relation feel intentionally shallow rather than like an accidental background.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="02" title="Give an impossible object a job">
Choose one implausible prop: a ladder, a floating platform, a large stone, or a paper trail. Give it one formal task: bridge, anchor, diagonal, or internal frame, and remove any other object that duplicates that task.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="03" title="Balance a centered figure without symmetry">
Start with a frontal figure on the vertical axis. Use two unequal peripheral motifs, such as clouds, insects, pages, or light shapes, to make the two sides feel related without becoming mirror images.
</ExerciseCard>
