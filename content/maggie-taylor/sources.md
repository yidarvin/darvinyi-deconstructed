## Research context

Taylor's portfolio describes the early series as digital photomontages combining photographs, scanned objects, and historical imagery, often nineteenth-century photographs. The Harn Museum's *Dreaming Alice* exhibition further describes her contemporary photographs as combining nineteenth-century photographic materials with scans of insects, dolls, period etchings, and Victorian flora and fauna: https://harn.ufl.edu/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/2019%E2%80%932020-Annual-Report-Harn-Museum-of-Art.pdf. These twelve selections establish the visual grammar—square fields, clipped historical figures, botanical and animal motifs, and deliberately implausible scale—from which a later build can develop the chapter's compositional argument.
