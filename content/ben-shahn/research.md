# Ben Shahn — research

## Scope and record

These ten photographs belong to the Library of Congress's *Cotton Pickers*
portfolio: Pulaski County, Arkansas, October 1935, Resettlement Administration
Lot 1657.  The Library identifies Shahn as photographer and the Resettlement
Administration as the related agency; its portfolio places the work in the
newly formed Historical Section in 1935.  Each selected item is catalogued as a
black-and-white **35 mm nitrate negative**, not as a surviving camera print.
Sources: [LOC portfolio](https://www.loc.gov/pictures/collection/fsa/docchap1.html),
[LOC example item record](https://www.loc.gov/pictures/item/2017730842/), and
[LOC collection background](https://www.loc.gov/collections/fsa-owi-black-and-white-negatives/about-this-collection/).

## Technique and material

The physical claim the records support is modest but specific: Shahn made these
on 35 mm nitrate black-and-white negative film.  Their untrimmed negative-edge
marks are retained in the supplied scans, so the chapter treats the black border
as archival frame evidence rather than part of the depicted Arkansas scene.
The negative cataloguing does not establish a camera model, lens, film stock,
developer, or lighting method for any frame.  Those are therefore not inferred
from the small format or from apparent depth of field.

## Printing and later access

The selected files are modern digital files from original negatives.  The
Library says exposed FSA/OWI negatives were usually mailed to its Washington
photographic unit for developing, numbering, and printing, and that prints were
usually made by laboratory staff from negatives selected for printing.  That
describes the collection workflow; it does not document the exact paper or
printing process for every image here, so this chapter does not call these
particular historical prints gelatin silver, contact prints, or photographer-made
prints.  [LOC collection overview](https://www.loc.gov/collections/fsa-owi-black-and-white-negatives/about-this-collection/)
and [LOC print workflow](https://www.loc.gov/collections/fsa-owi-black-and-white-negatives/articles-and-essays/fsa-owi-photographic-prints/).

## Camera settings

No aperture, shutter speed, focal length, sensitivity, or exposure log appears
in the local source set or in the surviving item records for these ten negatives.
The ingest manifest records no original-camera EXIF, as expected for archival
scans.  The chapter consequently gives no numerical settings and distinguishes
the image analysis measurements from historical camera metadata.
