# August Sander — source shortlist

Sander's *People of the Twentieth Century* turns portraiture into a deliberately ordered social atlas: occupational tools, dress, stance, and the space around a sitter all operate as visual evidence. This selection moves across farmer, worker, professional, politician, artist, and urban woman while retaining the direct, full-frame portraits that make the series useful for composition teaching. All ten files are public, web-sized 2000px-long-edge JPEG renditions served from the Museum of Modern Art collection records; each record identifies the work, object number, portfolio, and credit.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. The works are credited by MoMA to Die Photographische Sammlung / SK Stiftung Kultur – August Sander Archiv, Cologne / ARS, NY (copyright notice shown on the individual records). Each use is a small, complete-frame copy used for nonprofit criticism, scholarship, and transformative composition teaching; the complete frame is necessary to discuss Sander's spatial and typological decisions; the web-sized copy does not replace a museum record, original, or licensed reproduction; and this chapter links to and credits the authoritative collection record. Those factors are assessed below for each work rather than presumed from the project's educational purpose.

## Research context

MoMA's artist record describes *People of the Twentieth Century* as a more-than-600-image project organized into seven groups, including *The Farmer*, *The Skilled Tradesman*, *The Woman*, *Classes and Professions*, *The Artists*, *The City*, and *The Last People*: https://www.moma.org/artists/5145. It acquired the 619 photographs that make up the project in 2015. The selection above deliberately uses the complete photograph in each case because cropping would remove the visual evidence needed to teach Sander's balance of individual likeness and social type.
