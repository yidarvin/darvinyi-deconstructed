# August Sander — research note

## Scope of the source record

The supplied source packet identifies ten August Sander works held in the Museum of Modern Art collection and dates them from 1914 to 1931. MoMA's artist record describes *People of the Twentieth Century* as a project of more than 600 photographs arranged in seven social groups. The packet's captions and object links are the authority for the work titles, dates, collection information, and the selected social range.

## Technique, printing, and settings

The local source record does not document a camera model, lens, aperture, shutter speed, film stock, negative format, or exposure setting for any of these photographs. It also does not identify the print process or a printing date for these particular web renditions. The ingested files contain no retained capture EXIF. This chapter therefore makes no technical reconstruction beyond calling the works photographs and locating them in the documented 1914–31 period.

## Working visual evidence

The photographs establish their typological force through visible, rather than presumed, means: full figures and occupational props in *Bricklayer* and *Police Officer*; the relation of several sitters in *Proletarian Intellectuals* and *The Painter Otto Dix and his Wife Martha*; and a sparse studio field around the portraits of the secretary, Dix, and the young woman. The overlay analysis is deliberately limited to features visible in the supplied images: field edge, cane, brick load, body axis, arm, face, uniform silhouette, step, umbrella, chair, and the contrast between a frontal face and a profile.
