# Germaine Krull — source shortlist

Krull's 1928 *Métal* portfolio is a compact argument for treating industrial infrastructure as a photographic subject rather than as background: cranes, iron lattice, wheels, and bridges are cropped and tilted until scale becomes uncertain and functional geometry becomes abstraction. The National Gallery of Victoria identifies *Métal* as a 64-plate portfolio of structures photographed in Amsterdam, Rotterdam, Marseille, Saint-Malo, and Paris; Jeu de Paume places the portfolio and Krull's 1929 *Film und Foto* appearance at the center of her modernist reputation. This unit also keeps two adjacent works—the carnival image and the Peugeot commission—because they show the same high/low and dynamic framing being carried from steel architecture into popular spectacle and commercial photography.

**Rights basis:** Krull died in 1985 and the selected works remain copyrighted. The National Gallery of Art identifies the rightsholder notice for its Krull works as **© Estate Germaine Krull, Museum Folkwang, Essen**. These are therefore not represented as open content.
