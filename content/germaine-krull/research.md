# Germaine Krull — research

Scope: technique, era, printing, and documented camera information for the
five selected works. This note keeps collection-record facts separate from the
composition readings in the chapter.

## Era and approach

The selection runs from 1925 to 1930: *Untitled (Abstract Industrial
Building)* (1925), *Untitled (from Métal)* (1925–28), *Carnival Ride* (1928),
*The Eiffel Tower* (1928), and *Advertisement for the Six Peugeot 12-cylinder*
(1930). The National Gallery of Victoria describes *Métal* (1928) as a
64-plate portfolio of industrial structures photographed in Amsterdam,
Rotterdam, Marseille, Saint-Malo, and Paris. Jeu de Paume places the portfolio
and Krull's participation in the 1929 *Film und Foto* exhibition at the center
of her modernist reputation. The two adjacent works in this group show the
same steep framing and graphic treatment of metal moving into public spectacle
and commercial imagery. [Source notes](sources.md)

## Technique and printing

Bruce Silverstein's individual work records document the following print
objects: *Untitled (Abstract Industrial Building)* is a gelatin silver print
mounted to board, printed c. 1925; *Untitled (from Métal)* is a gelatin silver
print mounted to card, printed c. 1925–28; and *Carnival Ride* is a gelatin
silver print, printed c. 1928. The record for *The Eiffel Tower* identifies a
ferrotype gelatin silver print, printed c. 1928; *Advertisement for the Six
Peugeot 12-cylinder* is a gelatin silver print mounted on original blue
cardboard, printed c. 1930. [Source notes](sources.md)

These records establish the listed print processes and supports, but not the
negative process or a camera/lens/exposure record. The chapter can therefore
name the documented gelatin-silver and ferrotype objects while keeping its
description of the photographs' visible effects—cropped steel, silhouette,
lattice, and reflected lettering—separate from unrecorded capture details.

## Camera information

No source in the recorded set gives a verified camera model, lens, focal length,
aperture, shutter speed, film or plate stock, sensitivity, or exposure for these
five pictures. The local manifest contains only normalized derivative-file
dimensions and no historical EXIF. Specific settings are not inferred from
their sharpness, blur, or viewpoint.
