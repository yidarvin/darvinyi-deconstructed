# Irving Penn — source shortlist

Irving Penn made the controlled studio a versatile compositional instrument: a fashion body can become a sharply legible silhouette, a sitter can be pressed into a corner or isolated in open ground, and an everyday object can be arranged as exact geometry. This set follows those recurring decisions across fashion, portraiture, still life, and the travel photographs. All twelve files are public, web-sized Art Institute of Chicago IIIF display renditions downloaded directly from the museum's public IIIF service and checked as opening JPEGs. Their 843px long edge clears the project's 640px floor while remaining far below a print-production master.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. The Art Institute records mark these works `is_public_domain: false`; their copyright notices identify either Condé Nast Publications, Inc. or The Irving Penn Foundation. The records and public display images are used here for nonprofit, transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching. Each assessment below treats the creative nature of the photograph, the need for its complete frame, and the small, credited, record-linked web copy that does not replace an original, museum record, or licensed reproduction.

## Research context

The Art Institute's Penn collection makes the breadth of his practice available through one authoritative public collection endpoint: the selected records retain their original copyright notices while exposing enough of the full photograph for scholarly viewing. This particular sequence is intentionally organized around Penn's recurring compositional argument—precise control of the studio field—rather than treating his fashion, portrait, still-life, and travel photographs as separate visual languages.
