# Erik Johansson — research note

The supplied source record identifies a twelve-work selection made between 2008 and 2018, during the contemporary digital-composite period, and describes the pictures as photographic composites in which photographed elements are made to share an impossible scene. The selection moves among road, house, shoreline, water, lighthouse, and figure motifs, but its consistent method is to preserve recognisable photographic light, texture, horizon, and scale while reorganising their physical relations. The source record characterises *The Architect* through an oversized hand and drawn house, *Impact* through raised reflective panels, and *Go Your Own Road* through a road becoming a carried landscape; those are compositional descriptions of the supplied finished works, not evidence of a particular software or capture workflow. [Source record](sources.md)

The artist's public gallery copies are 1,500px-long-edge web renditions, and the source record identifies signed, numbered limited-edition prints in the artist's shop. It does not document the print process, paper, edition details for each selected work, camera body, lens, focal length, exposure, ISO, lighting setup, or post-production sequence. The ingested manifest likewise contains no capture EXIF. This chapter therefore treats the exact technical settings as unreported rather than inferring them from the finished composites.
