Erik Johansson's pictures begin by making the ordinary photographic world reliable: weather has weight, horizons hold their place, and small people still give landscapes scale. Then a single relation becomes impossible. A moon is handled like equipment, a house rises on an island, a road folds into itself, or water turns into a cut surface. The seams are persuasive because the underlying compositions are legible before the paradox is. These overlays name that legible structure: field edge, reflection seam, curve, anchor, or repeated silhouette, rather than treating a composite as a trick to be solved. ## scale needs an ordinary measure

<Plate slug="erik-johansson" id="01-full-moon-service-2017" label="Full Moon Service, 2017" caption="A slanting field edge and small moon make the scale reversal feel grounded before it becomes impossible." />

In *Full Moon Service*, the field edge plotted at y=0.528, angled -5°, gives the night scene a physical floor. The moon anchor at x=0.231, y=0.341 sits just above it, while the measured road convergence at x=0.289, y=0.531 pulls the eye back to the service vehicle and figures. That triangle makes the luminous object feel handled rather than pasted into the sky. The analyzer's dark #0e1d1d and #22404b, with moonlit #cdc49c, create the restrained palette that lets the several bright spheres become a hierarchy instead of a scatter.

<Plate slug="erik-johansson" id="02-the-architect-2015" label="The Architect, 2015" caption="Roof planes turn a tabletop drawing into a house-sized proposition." />

*The Architect* keeps the central structure believable by letting its roof planes run outward as real diagonals. The overlay follows the upper plane from x=0.317, y=0.151 to x=0.953, y=0.346 and the lower one from x=0.359, y=0.201 to x=0.999, y=0.430. They carry the gaze from the roof toward the drawing hand, while the distant field edge at y=0.759 preserves a familiar outside world below. A p05 of 0.110 and p95 of 0.690 make the wood and gray weather read as a compressed, moderate tonal field: the white paper and roof markings can do the implausible work without excessive contrast.

<Plate slug="erik-johansson" id="03-the-cover-up-2013" label="The Cover-Up, 2013" caption="A road becomes a directional relay into one lifted edge." />

The road in *The Cover-Up* is less a perspective demonstration than an invitation into the reveal. Its traced route rises from the lower left toward the lifted grass edge at x=0.680, y=0.477; the horizontal roadside horizon at y=0.528 lets that rupture read against a settled landscape. Dark grass #2c3524 and #54593b collect below pale #f1e2c5 light, a wide p05-to-p95 span of 0.141–0.871 that turns the elevated turf into a bright, tactile threshold rather than a graphic cutout.

## an object can borrow a landscape's authority

<Plate slug="erik-johansson" id="04-soundscapes-2015" label="Soundscapes, 2015" caption="Nested circles and a low waterline make a headland read as an object without losing its landscape scale." />

*Soundscapes* makes its conceptual conversion through silhouette. The waterline at y=0.578 runs through the center of the image, while the anchored turntable center at x=0.289, y=0.572 and the broad record ellipse keep the circular claim precise. The work has the set's very low measured edge density, 0.011: pale #d7d7d4 sky, dark #34322c shore, and muted #899084 water leave plenty of uninterrupted area for the circle to be read both as terrain and as a disc.

<Plate slug="erik-johansson" id="05-demand-and-supply-2017" label="Demand & Supply, 2017" caption="A repeated house silhouette gathers into one improbable load over a calm waterline." />

In *Demand & Supply*, repetition first makes the stacked house shapes legible as a collective mass. The house-load anchor at x=0.570, y=0.177 hangs far above the distant water edge at y=0.608, so the large empty interval becomes the visual pressure of the picture. The palette moves from #6d8893 blue through #575f5a to #e1ded1 light, and the low 0.017 edge density keeps the open water and sky quiet enough for the dense architecture to read as one loaded silhouette.

<Plate slug="erik-johansson" id="06-impact-2016" label="Impact, 2016" caption="A small boat pins a radial break in an otherwise level reflecting surface." />

*Impact* gives its disruption an anchor: the boat and impact point at x=0.585, y=0.634. Everything above it is steadied by the far shore at y=0.362, a calm horizontal that makes the broken reflection below feel consequential across the full frame. The analyzer records #cfd9de, #9faeb1, and dark #454236, with p95 at 0.902; the high-key water lets the wood-toned fragments and boat remain readable as a patterned rupture rather than visual noise.

<Plate slug="erik-johansson" id="07-the-light-keeper-2018" label="The Light Keeper, 2018" caption="A cable to a giant bulb carries the eye diagonally away from the lighthouse and across the sea." />

The lighthouse in *The Light Keeper* matters because it establishes a vertical before the image sends us away from it. The sea horizon at y=0.490 is level, then the cable runs from x=0.166, y=0.905 to x=0.865, y=0.389 into the bulb convergence at x=0.744, y=0.604. The narrow dark-to-blue palette of #0d121b, #213342, #527993, and #81aabf keeps that diagonal unusually visible; at mean luminance 0.252, light is not atmosphere but a structural object.

## continuity makes a paradox credible

<Plate slug="erik-johansson" id="08-imminent-2016" label="Imminent, 2016" caption="A suspended boulder gains enormous scale from the tiny settlement beneath it." />

*Imminent* places a large suspended boulder inside an ellipse centered at x=0.200, y=0.225, then supplies a village-scale witness at x=0.787, y=0.767. The ground line at y=0.725 holds the lower valley steady, so the boulder's unsupported state is read as a specific violation of a coherent place. Pale #d9d3c0 and gray #a7a799 dominate the open atmosphere, while darker #2c2f1e land remains sparse; the 0.029 edge density is low enough that the silhouette and gap carry the tension.

<Plate slug="erik-johansson" id="09-endless-reflections-2015" label="Endless Reflections, 2015" caption="A reflection seam repeats one house into a quiet corridor of doubled space." />

In *Endless Reflections*, the sloping reflection seam at y=0.520, angled 2°, makes the upper and lower worlds touch without becoming a strict mirror. The first house at x=0.156, y=0.358 begins the relay toward the measured receding axis at x=0.209, y=0.369. That modest offset preserves the unsettling sense of repetition. The pale blue-gray palette of #b5c5ce, #d5dcdf, and #90a4b0, with an edge density of only 0.008, leaves the trees, boat, and repeated house as a few deliberate interruptions in a broad luminous field.

<Plate slug="erik-johansson" id="10-cut-and-fold-2012" label="Cut & Fold, 2012" caption="A raised crease redirects an otherwise continuous field and road." />

*Cut & Fold* depends on continuity: the distant field edge at y=0.348, angled 0.9°, and the fold convergence at x=0.516, y=0.577 make the raised lip look like a changed condition of the same land, not a separate prop. The raised fold anchor at x=0.539, y=0.252 pinpoints the moment the road becomes surface. Green #5e5f38 and #3a3c22 hold against pale #e3e3dd sky; p95 reaches 0.910, giving the crease enough tonal separation to be read before its impossible material logic registers.

<Plate slug="erik-johansson" id="11-fishy-island-2010" label="Fishy Island, 2010" caption="A fishing-line gesture completes the island's transformation into a fish." />

In *Fishy Island*, the distant waterline at y=0.362 separates the small island-fish body, anchored at x=0.542, y=0.300, from the larger fish below. The traced fishing-line loop connects x=0.100, y=0.260 through the island to x=0.910, y=0.210, making a visual sentence between the two scales. The extreme tonal range, from p05 0.004 to p95 0.875, reinforces the split: #c9d7df sky and water sit above the near-black #0d0d0a underwater field, where the giant fish becomes an answering shadow.

<Plate slug="erik-johansson" id="12-go-your-own-road-2008" label="Go Your Own Road, 2008" caption="A road curve works both as a route into the landscape and as a carried, folded object." />

*Go Your Own Road* finishes by making a familiar lead-in line behave like a physical sheet. The far field edge at y=0.154 gives the open scene a stable top register, while the road curve runs from x=0.610, y=0.443 to x=0.999, y=0.899 through the measured convergence at x=0.638, y=0.489. At edge density 0.181, the highest in this group, the road markings, grasses, folds, and figure produce enough local information for the radical bend to feel photographed rather than diagrammed. Warm #64592f and #908252 ground against pale #f2efec light help the roadway retain its ordinary material identity.

## Exercises

<ExerciseCard n="01" title="Anchor the impossible">
Make a small composite with one implausible scale change. Before changing the central object, photograph or draw a stable ground line and include one ordinary size witness: a doorway, person, vehicle, or tree. Ask whether the scale shift remains readable when you remove every decorative element.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="02" title="Let one continuity survive">
Build a two-image montage in which a road, waterline, shadow, or repeating texture crosses the join. Keep the connection simple enough to trace with one line. If the join is invisible but the picture has no directional structure, simplify the construction until the continuity also guides the eye.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="03" title="Use a quiet field">
Photograph a busy subject against an open sky, wall, or water surface. Make two frames: one with background detail competing everywhere, and one with a broad tonal reserve. Compare which version gives the altered object a clearer hierarchy and a more believable scale.
</ExerciseCard>
