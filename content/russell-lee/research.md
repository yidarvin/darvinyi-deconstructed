# Russell Lee — research notes

## Record and era

The supplied source set identifies all twelve photographs as work by Russell Lee for the Farm Security Administration, dated from 1938 to 1941. It combines Texas, Louisiana, Chicago, Washington, and the Pie Town, New Mexico sequence rather than treating Pie Town as an isolated project. The item-level dates and titles are recorded in [sources.md](sources.md); the two colour photographs are the 1940 Pie Town portraits and school image.

## Materials and technique

The source records identify the black-and-white files as entries in the Library of Congress FSA/OWI black-and-white-negative collection, and the two colour files as FSA/OWI colour photographs. That establishes the collection and original image category, not the physical process or print material of each supplied reproduction. The local JPEGs are uncropped derivatives of LOC master TIFFs, normalized for this site; they are not evidence of the original paper, negative size, or darkroom treatment.

Lee's recurring visual method in this selected set is directly observable rather than inferred as technical metadata: frontal rooms, doorways, boards, tables, fences, and repeated figures make social relationships spatially legible. The colour frames use a restrained earth-and-sky palette, while the black-and-white frames depend on the contrast between walls, clothing, furniture, and daylight.

## Camera and exposure settings

No supplied LOC item record or local EXIF field documents a camera body, lens, film stock, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, or exposure for these individual pictures. The normalized image manifest contains only processing metadata from this project, not historical capture settings. This chapter therefore does not assign camera settings or an unrecorded photographic process to any plate.

## Rights

The source shortlist records that the images derive from the Library of Congress FSA/OWI collections, whose black-and-white-negative and colour-photograph collection pages state that the material is public domain and free to use and reuse. The individual LOC records are linked per image in [sources.md](sources.md).
