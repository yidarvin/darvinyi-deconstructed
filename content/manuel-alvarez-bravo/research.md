# Research — Manuel Álvarez Bravo

## What the supplied records establish

This chapter follows twelve photographs made between 1928/29 and 1964/65, as
dated in [the source notes](sources.md). The group moves between street and
shop-window observations, portraits, a political photograph, staged or
symbolic scenes, and a late cemetery image. Several AIC records distinguish an
image's making date from a later print: *Two Pairs of Legs* (1928/29, printed
1977), *Optical Parable* and *The Daughter of the Dancers* (made early in the
1930s, printed later), and *Striking Worker Murdered* (1934, printed 1974).
Those distinctions matter: a modern web reproduction is evidence of the
catalogued print, not a direct record of the original negative or its first
printing.

## Technique and material limits

The supplied AIC and Commons records identify the author, titles, dates, and
holding institutions; the Commons rendition of *La buena fama durmiendo* is
credited to Casa MAB under CC BY-SA 4.0. The supplied records do not document a
camera model, lens, film stock, aperture, shutter speed, exposure method,
development, paper, or print process consistently enough to make per-image
technical claims. This chapter therefore does not infer settings from look or
era. It discusses the visible tonal and spatial effects of the supplied
reproductions, and identifies later-print dates only where the source notes do.

## Scope

The images were acquired as public web-sized collection renditions: eleven are
documented fair-use references from AIC's public display service and one is an
open CC BY-SA rendition, with the per-image rationale and credits retained in
[sources.md](sources.md). Their JPEG scans are useful for composition analysis
but do not establish darkroom intent or camera settings.
