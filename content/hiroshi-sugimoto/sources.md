# Hiroshi Sugimoto — source shortlist

This working set joins eight *Seascapes* with four *Theaters*: the two series make Sugimoto's long-exposure treatment of time legible through its most economical compositions — a horizon split and a glowing screen. The artist's official *Seascapes* presentation identifies the first three works; Fraenkel Gallery's public *Seascapes* and *Theaters* portfolios identify the remaining titles, dates, media, and editions. All twelve files were retrieved directly from the public display-image responses linked below and successfully opened as JPEGs. They range from 847px to 2000px on the long edge, exceeding the 640px floor without enlargement.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. Hiroshi Sugimoto is the creator; the National Museum of Modern Art, Tokyo's official exhibition presentation credits a canonical *Seascape* “© Hiroshi Sugimoto / Courtesy of Gallery Koyanagi,” while the artist site and Fraenkel identify the works as Sugimoto photographs/gelatin-silver prints. No open licence or public-domain statement was found. Each assessment below is individual: this nonprofit book makes transformative criticism and scholarship of the composition; it needs the complete frame to teach the work's structure; it uses only a web-sized rendition; and it credits and links to the public record without replacing the original, a print, or a licensed reproduction.

## Research context

Sugimoto's official *Seascapes* text calls water and air the materials of life's origins and calls viewing the sea “a voyage of seeing”: https://www.sugimotohiroshi.com/seascapes-1/. Princeton University Art Museum describes *Theaters* and *Seascapes* as iconic series balancing focused pictures with near-geometric abstraction, made with large-format exposures over the duration of a film or a voyage: https://artmuseum.princeton.edu/art/collections/objects/16815. The National Museum of Modern Art's official exhibition identifies *Dioramas*, *Theaters*, and *Seascapes* as the series that established Sugimoto's reputation and gives the above artist/courtesy credit: https://art.nikkei.com/sugimoto/en/exhibition/.
