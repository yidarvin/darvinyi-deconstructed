# Guy Bourdin — source shortlist

This working set uses twelve 1000px-long-edge JPEG renditions publicly displayed by the Guy Bourdin Estate on its official artwork page. The Estate's page states that modern prints from the Estate are numbered, stamped, and signed by Samuel Bourdin; it also identifies the Estate as the copyright holder and its exclusive representation. These are deliberately web-sized display copies, not print files.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. The Guy Bourdin Estate's artwork page says “Copyright The Guy Bourdin Estate 2021 - All rights reserved” and names Louise Alexander Gallery as the Estate's representative. Each selection is used here for nonprofit criticism, scholarship, and transformative composition teaching. The work is creative, so that factor weighs against fair use; however, the intact web-sized frame is necessary to analyze Bourdin's deliberate cropping, colour blocks, repeated images, and product/figure relations. Each use is credited and links to the Estate's display page and public rendition; it neither replaces an Estate print nor a licensed reproduction. The entries below record the image-specific compositional reason for the complete-frame use.

## Research context

MoMA's artist record lists five works by Bourdin online, including *French Vogue* (May 1977), *20 Years* (c. 1977), and *Charles Jourdan* (Spring 1978), corroborating the period and principal fashion series represented here: https://www.moma.org/artists/8189-guy-bourdin. The Estate's own public artwork display was used for acquisition because it supplies authoritative, directly viewable web renditions while identifying the estate that controls the works.
