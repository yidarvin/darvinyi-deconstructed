# Jeff Wall — source shortlist

This set follows Wall’s constructed photographic tableaux from the early light-box works through later digitally assembled scenes. Nine files were downloaded directly from Glenstone’s public artwork-display service as 1600px-long-edge JPEG derivatives; the tenth is Tate’s public 600px collection display. Each file opens successfully and preserves the whole published image rather than a detail crop. Glenstone’s records identify the artist notice as “© Jeff Wall”; Tate identifies the work as Jeff Wall’s 1993 *A Sudden Gust of Wind (after Hokusai)*. No open-license or public-domain reproduction was located for these copyrighted works.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. Each is a separate, limited assessment: this nonprofit book uses a credited, record-linked, web-sized copy for transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching; the complete published frame is needed to analyze Wall’s staged spatial construction; and the derivative neither substitutes for the artwork, museum record, nor a licensed reproduction. The source pages remain linked below.

## Shortlist

### 01 — *The Destroyed Room* (1978)
- **Why it matters:** The shattered red interior transforms a single room into a tightly orchestrated field of diagonals, debris, and withheld narrative.
- **Source:** https://www.glenstone.org/artworks/the-destroyed-room (Glenstone; public display derivative: https://www.glenstone.org/medias/azure-43938.jpg?format=jpg&quality=80&width=1600)
- **Rights / fair-use assessment:** Jeff Wall; © Jeff Wall; collection photo credit Tim Nighswander / Imaging4Art.com. **Factor 1:** transformative criticism studies the frame’s arranged disorder. **Factor 2:** a highly creative staged photograph. **Factor 3:** the full room is essential to the relation of mattress, torn walls, dresser, and floor. **Factor 4:** this credited 1600 × 1107px display derivative is record-linked and cannot replace a light box, print, or licensed reproduction.
- **File:** `raw/jeff-wall/01-the-destroyed-room.jpg` (1600 × 1107px)

### 02 — *Picture for Women* (1979)
- **Why it matters:** Mirror, camera, artist, and model form a self-conscious system of gazes, with the center seam making the picture’s construction visible.
- **Source:** https://www.glenstone.org/artworks/picture-for-women (Glenstone; public display derivative: https://www.glenstone.org/medias/azure-93702.jpg?format=jpg&quality=80&width=1600)
- **Rights / fair-use assessment:** Jeff Wall; © Jeff Wall. **Factor 1:** transformative composition teaching examines the mirror axis and reciprocal gaze. **Factor 2:** a creative staged photograph. **Factor 3:** the complete frame is necessary to retain camera, artist, model, and reflection together. **Factor 4:** the credited 1600 × 1115px display derivative links to the collection record and does not substitute for the work or licensed imagery.
- **File:** `raw/jeff-wall/02-picture-for-women.jpg` (1600 × 1115px)

### 03 — *Mimic* (1982)
- **Why it matters:** A seemingly casual street encounter is carefully staged so the walking trio, sidewalk, and receding industrial corridor carry racialized social tension.
- **Source:** https://www.glenstone.org/artworks/mimic (Glenstone; public display derivative: https://www.glenstone.org/medias/azure-101825.jpg?format=jpg&quality=80&width=1600)
- **Rights / fair-use assessment:** Jeff Wall; © Jeff Wall. **Factor 1:** transformative criticism analyzes spatial blocking and the directed gesture. **Factor 2:** a creative staged photograph. **Factor 3:** the full scene is required to read the bodies against the street and distant corridor. **Factor 4:** this credited 1600 × 1397px display file is a non-substitutive, record-linked derivative.
- **File:** `raw/jeff-wall/03-mimic.jpg` (1600 × 1397px)

### 04 — *Diatribe* (1985)

### 06 — *A Sudden Gust of Wind (after Hokusai)* (1993)
- **Why it matters:** Wall translates Hokusai’s wind-driven design into a contemporary, digitally assembled tableau in which papers and bodies establish motion across a bleak landscape.
- **Source:** https://www.tate.org.uk/art/artworks/wall-a-sudden-gust-of-wind-after-hokusai-t06951 (Tate, T06951; public display derivative: https://media.tate.org.uk/aztate-prd-ew-dg-wgtail-st1-ctr-data/images/.width-600_DYyrtIC.jpg)
- **Rights / fair-use assessment:** Jeff Wall; © Jeff Wall / Tate collection record. **Factor 1:** transformative scholarship analyzes the photographic reworking of Hokusai’s directional structure. **Factor 2:** a highly creative staged photograph. **Factor 3:** the intact panorama is required for the wind’s path across figures, papers, canal, and tree. **Factor 4:** the credited 600 × 364px Tate display image is a deliberately small public derivative, record-linked, and plainly non-substitutive. Its 600px long edge is a documented compositionally essential exception to the normal 640px floor: papers, bodies, and landscape direction remain legible.
- **File:** `raw/jeff-wall/06-a-sudden-gust-of-wind.jpg` (600 × 364px)
