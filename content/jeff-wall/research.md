# Jeff Wall — research note

## What the supplied sources establish

The source set dates these selected photographs from 1978 to 2016. Its MoMA checklist context places Wall's early light-box works, *A Sudden Gust of Wind (after Hokusai)*, and *Restoration* within a practice that moves among art-historical quotation, staged narrative, and photographic description. The individual Glenstone records are the stated sources for title, date, medium, copyright notice, and the public display derivatives used here. [Source notes](sources.md)

The supplied notes explicitly describe the works as constructed or staged tableaux. They identify *A Sudden Gust of Wind (after Hokusai)* as a digitally assembled contemporary reworking of Hokusai's wind-driven design. They also refer to the original light-box presentation of *The Destroyed Room* and *The Well*, the original lightjet print of *Knife Throw*, and the framed inkjet print of *Mother of pearl*. These are source-specific presentation or print facts, not grounds for generalizing one process to every work in the chapter.

The notes do not supply a camera model, lens, film stock, aperture, shutter speed, sensitivity, darkroom process, or capture workflow for this selection. The ingested JPEGs contain no retained EXIF camera settings and are web-display derivatives, not evidence of an original capture or print workflow.

## Method for this chapter

The chapter therefore treats staging, sequencing of figures, architecture, props, and the described digital assembly as documented context, while treating overlay claims as visible claims about the supplied whole-frame reproductions. It does not infer exposure settings or a particular printing technology from the JPEGs. The rights basis and image-specific fair-use rationales remain in the [source notes](sources.md).
