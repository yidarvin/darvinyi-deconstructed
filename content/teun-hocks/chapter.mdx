Teun Hocks treats the self-portrait as a compact piece of stagecraft. In this selection, the recurring suited protagonist is never granted the authority of a hero: he waters a duck, fishes a tiny pond, holds a toast while submerged, or waits beside a smoking ship. The painterly field around him is not neutral scenery. It sets the scale of the predicament, then leaves enough room for the joke to turn quiet and uneasy. The overlays name actual horizons, props, body contours, and intervals rather than imposing a universal geometry on these deliberately made worlds.

## a person made small by the stage

<Plate slug="teun-hocks" id="01-untitled-2006" label="Untitled [watering duck], 2006" caption="A measured field edge at y=0.640 keeps the watering can, tiny duck, and suited figure low in a pale expanse; the falling water makes their improbable exchange literal." />

In *Untitled* [watering duck], the level field edge is the image's quiet structural fact. It permits sky to occupy most of the frame, while the can, water stream, duck, and bent body must make their own small circuit near the lower right. The analyzer records a bright mean luminance of 0.663, p05/p95 of 0.267/0.886, and only 0.0075 edge density: this is an image of broad, nearly unbroken passages, not one that hides its pressure in detail.

<Plate slug="teun-hocks" id="02-untitled-moon-2007" label="Untitled (Moon), 2007" caption="Raised hands, a trailing cord, and the arc of the lunar rim make the blindfolded figure a fragile vertical interruption in a starred black field." />

The moon picture reverses that spacious daylight logic. The raised hands and trailing cord give the blindfolded party-hatted figure a task, but the lunar rim cuts below him like a tiny proscenium. The dark field is genuinely dark: mean luminance 0.112, p05 0.035, with an unusually high edge density of 0.1234 concentrated in stars, cord, silhouette, and cratered rim. Hocks makes a theatrical sky from almost nothing.

<Plate slug="teun-hocks" id="03-untitled-261-2015" label="Untitled [two men smoking], 2015" caption="The floor-to-wall seam and near-central symmetry make two pipe smokers answer each other across a column of smoke." />

In the two-smoker picture, the composition is more frontal but not more stable. A wall-to-floor seam at y=0.754 clears the upper field for two facing bodies; the vertical axis falls through the smoke rather than through either person. The modest tonal range, p05 0.098 to p95 0.482, and edge density of 0.0083 keep the wall quiet enough that the smoke can become the picture's unstable third participant.

<Plate slug="teun-hocks" id="04-untitled-259-2015" label="Untitled [birdhouse], 2015" caption="A ground-to-sky break and the small birdhouse give the bending figure an awkward partner across a broad empty field." />

Here the small birdhouse, not the man, receives the anchor because it makes his bent posture legible as a response. A low ground-to-sky break at y=0.537 places both forms beneath a large, thinly worked sky. The frame's edge density is only 0.0029, the lowest in this group, so the shape of the body and the little pipe-like prop must do almost all the narrative work.

## props that pull the body off balance

<Plate slug="teun-hocks" id="05-untitled-253-2014" label="Untitled [fishing], 2014" caption="A level water-and-sky break and the bent fishing rod connect the waiting fisherman at right to the tiny pond at left." />

The fishing rod is not a decorative line. It gives the picture its long delayed reach: from the seated man at right, it bends over the small pond at left before the horizon-like water break restores the frame's stillness. The pale blue-grey field and dark suit produce a p05/p95 span of 0.133/0.749, while the 0.0086 edge density confirms how little surface event is needed once the rod supplies the route.

<Plate slug="teun-hocks" id="06-untitled-255-2015" label="Untitled [drowning toast], 2015" caption="The waterline and central axis make the two raised hands read as a formal toast even as the submerged bodies turn it absurd." />

In the submerged toast, Hocks removes almost every normal marker of a social occasion. The waterline at y=0.544 and centered pairing leave only a glass, cigar, hands, and concentric disturbances. The tonal statistics are correspondingly compressed: p05 0.396, p95 0.655, edge density 0.0039. The small gestures feel improbably ceremonial against an uninterrupted water field.

<Plate slug="teun-hocks" id="07-untitled-260-2015" label="Untitled [snow and basket], 2015" caption="A frontal axis, low feet, and the snowfield break hold the basket-bearing figure in a patterned but nearly empty white expanse." />

The snow picture makes the body frontal but not monumental. It stands low beneath a break at y=0.455, while flakes and footprints keep the snow from reading as a blank studio sweep. The overlay's axis follows the body rather than claiming the whole scene is perfectly mirrored. The image remains light (mean 0.619, p95 0.784), but the p05 of 0.176 preserves the suit, shoes, and basket as firm interruptions.

<Plate slug="teun-hocks" id="08-untitled-227-2008" label="Untitled [cardboard shelter], 2008" caption="The cardboard shelter forms a literal low frame for the sleeping figure, set across a measured terrain break from the distant repeated mass of housing blocks." />

The shelter picture is built from a strong near/far contradiction. The low cardboard frame contains the sleeping figure and a few intimate objects; the horizon-level housing blocks are visible but distant, almost indifferent. Its p05 of 0.047 and p95 of 0.851 make this the chapter's widest tonal spread, while the 0.0196 edge density belongs to box edges, body, and repeated apartment windows rather than to a busy scene.

## distant things answer back

<Plate slug="teun-hocks" id="09-untitled-240-2010" label="Untitled [hunter and fox], 2010" caption="A low landscape break and the visible interval from walking hunter to fox make the apparently broad landscape a delayed encounter." />

The hunter is moving left while the fox waits at right; the drawn interval is an interpretive path, not a claim that a literal line crosses the land. A landscape break at y=0.620 keeps both figures close to the lower half while shrubs and bare branches block an easy view between them. At edge density 0.0201, this is more textured than the open-field images, yet the wide pale sky still holds the encounter at a distance.

<Plate slug="teun-hocks" id="10-untitled-215-2007" label="Untitled [night reader], 2007" caption="The crescent moon, curved night ridge, and diagonal sleeping body make a compressed nocturnal stage from books, ground, and silhouette." />

The night reader makes its black upper field feel less empty than the moon scene because the crescent, ridge, books, and reclining body form a chained set of stops. The overlay follows the literal ridge and moon rather than forcing a line onto the body's irregular contour. Its mean luminance is just 0.088 and p95 0.263, but edge density rises to 0.1750: a large quantity of small contour information is pressed into the darkness.

<Plate slug="teun-hocks" id="11-untitled-208-2004" label="Untitled [carrying branches], 2004" caption="A snowfield edge and dark branch bundle make the labouring body cut diagonally across an almost blank field." />

The carried branches exaggerate the figure into a lopsided moving mass. A loose branch lies at lower left, but the overlay confines its claim to the supported snowfield edge and branch-bundle anchor; the far house remains a small scale marker rather than an overlay claim. The bright field reaches mean 0.765 and p95 0.937; its p05 of 0.176 remains reserved for the branch bundle and suit, which must carry the contrast.

<Plate slug="teun-hocks" id="12-untitled-242-2011" label="Untitled [ship and suitcase], 2011" caption="The traveller's hand, sea horizon, and vertical plume of ship smoke organize a foreground wait against a small event far offshore." />

In the beach picture, the suitcase and back-turned traveller make the foreground heavy, but the ship's smoke rises high enough to compete with him. The measured sea horizon at y=0.593 separates sand from an active grey-blue field, and the smoke supplies a vertical answer to the figure's upright posture. With p05/p95 of 0.184/0.804 and edge density 0.0133, the image leaves most of its work to broad tonal zones and the exact placement of three dark interruptions: suitcase, figure, ship.

## Exercises

<ExerciseCard n="01" title="Give a small action a large field">
Stage one ordinary action, a pour, wait, carry, or search, in a place with very little visual detail. Make two frames: one in which the action is large enough to explain itself, and one in which it must be discovered across a much larger field.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="02" title="Make the prop set the route">
Choose a prop that can create a physical path: a cord, rod, branch, plume, or reflected line. Compose the body so it responds to that path. Check that the prop changes where the eye travels rather than simply adding narrative information.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="03" title="Let a distant event compete">
Place a near figure or object against a small distant interruption. Use horizon height, empty space, and tonal contrast to decide whether the distant event feels like a destination, a threat, or an unanswered thought.
</ExerciseCard>
