# Teun Hocks — source shortlist

Teun Hocks made staged, hand-coloured photographs in which he generally appears as a deadpan, vulnerable protagonist. This set draws from the publicly accessible full-frame files on the artist/estate website's **Analogue Work** page. The site identifies the medium as oil on toned gelatin-silver print; no open licence or public-domain declaration is supplied. All twelve downloaded JPEGs opened successfully and measure 800–1,828px on the long edge.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. The website is an artist/estate presentation and gives no reuse licence; the copyright holder is therefore recorded conservatively as Teun Hocks / estate (© Teun Hocks where applicable). Each item below is a separate, limited four-factor assessment: (1) this nonprofit book makes transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching; (2) the source is a highly creative constructed photograph; (3) the complete published frame is needed to analyze its figure, prop, painted backdrop, and spatial design; and (4) the retained web-sized copy is credited and linked to the public artist record, so it does not replace an original print, the record, or a licensed reproduction.

## Research context

The artist/estate page describes the selected body as oil on sepia-toned gelatin-silver prints. Groninger Museum's memorial exhibition notes that Hocks staged and photographed himself with a self-timer, built and painted his backdrops, then enlarged and carefully hand-coloured the photographs: https://www.groningermuseum.nl/en/art/exhibitions/teun-hocks. This confirms why full-frame copies are necessary for the chapter's compositional analysis rather than interchangeable illustrative details.
