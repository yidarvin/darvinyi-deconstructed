# Teun Hocks — research notes

## Verified process and period

The twelve selected works date from 2004 to 2015. The artist/estate's **Analogue Work** page identifies this body as **oil on toned gelatin-silver print**. Groninger Museum's memorial exhibition states that Hocks staged and photographed himself with a self-timer, built and painted the backdrops, then enlarged and carefully hand-coloured the photographs. Those records support describing these pictures as constructed, self-performed, hand-coloured photographic tableaux rather than as found scenes.

The source records do not give a camera body, lens, film stock, aperture, shutter speed, lighting diagram, development recipe, paper brand, edition, or print date for these particular works. The ingested web JPEGs contain no useful camera-setting EXIF. This chapter therefore does not infer technical settings from their painted-looking surfaces.

## Visual working method supported by the selection

Across the 2004–15 group, Hocks places the recurring suited protagonist in deliberately sparse, painted-looking fields: a farm, moon, water, snow, beach, or low theatrical landscape. The figure often undertakes an impractical action—watering a duck, fishing a small pond, raising a toast while submerged, carrying branches, or waiting beside a smoking ship. The full frame matters because the joke and the melancholy depend on the interval between the protagonist and the minor prop, animal, or distant interruption.
