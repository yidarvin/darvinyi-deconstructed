# Yousuf Karsh — source shortlist

Yousuf Karsh made the studio portrait into a stage for character: a dark or plainly controlled setting, theatrical key and rim light, and a sitter arranged so that face, hands, clothing, and silhouette carry the psychological claim. This set follows that method across political power, scientific concentration, writers, artists, and performers. The ten files are unaltered, publicly displayed JPEG renditions supplied by the official Yousuf Karsh Estate archive; each opened successfully and has a long edge of at least 1,122px.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. The Estate’s [archive](https://karsh.org/photographs/) says “All images © Yousuf Karsh,” and its [use guidance](https://karsh.org/sections/the-work/) says the images are copyrighted and available for licensed editorial and commercial use. Each image assessment below is specific to that copyrighted work: this nonprofit book uses the complete frame for transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching; its full frame is necessary to analyze the placement of a sitter, lighting, hand gesture, and surrounding negative space; the retained copy is web-sized; and the chapter credits and links to the Estate record rather than replacing the original print, Estate record, or a licensed reproduction.

## Research context

The Estate’s [portrait overview](https://karsh.org/overview/portraits/) identifies Churchill, Picasso, Hemingway, Sibelius, and Einstein among its representative portraits. Its [archive overview](https://karsh.org/photographs/) reports 15,312 sittings and more than 370,000 negatives; the present selection deliberately narrows that breadth to works where light, gesture, and negative space provide distinct composition lessons.
