# James Casebere — research note

## What the source set establishes

The artist's 2000–05 portfolio identifies the eleven selected works by title and date: *Pink Hallway #3* (2000); *Nevisian Underground #1*, *Monticello #1*, *Monticello #3*, and *Yellow Hallway #2* (2001); *Green Staircase #1* (2002); and *Green Staircase #4*, *Dorm Room*, *Turning Hallway*, *Tunnel #2*, and *Spanish Bath (Vertical)* (2003). The source notes identify this body of work as photographs of fabricated architectural models, and the BOMB interview cited there discusses the flood imagery and the Monticello works in relation to architecture, temporality, and emotion. [Source notes](sources.md)

The complete-frame analyses in this chapter are therefore about photographed constructions rather than documentary interiors. That distinction matters: water, built apertures, stairs, arches, and light are deliberately arranged materials, but the chapter does not infer an undocumented lighting recipe or construction process from their appearance.

## Technique, printing, and settings

The supplied portfolio pages establish titles, dates, authorship, and public display files. They do **not** establish a camera body, lens, focal length, film or sensor, aperture, shutter speed, sensitivity, exposure method, lighting equipment, model scale, retouching workflow, print process, or edition information for these individual works. The ingested web JPEGs contain no capture EXIF. No such setting or process is invented in the chapter.

The photographs are discussed as web-display reproductions of Casebere's constructed images. Tonal statistics and palette values come from those normalized display files; they describe this edition's pixels, not the original objects' materials or printing.
