# James Casebere — source shortlist

This eleven-image working set follows Casebere’s 2000–03 photographs of built architectural models: flooded halls and historical interiors, then modernist rooms and tunnels. The files were retrieved directly from the artist’s publicly accessible portfolio page and its public Squarespace image responses, without authentication or access-control circumvention. Each JPEG opens successfully. Ten exceed the normal 640px long-edge floor; *Green Staircase #1* (600px) and *Turning Hallway* (756px) are compositionally essential exceptions: their stair/landing and bending-corridor structures remain plainly legible.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. Casebere is a living artist; no open-licence or public-domain rendition was found for these photographs. The portfolio identifies the works as James Casebere’s, and the source page is credited below. For each separate assessment: (1) this nonprofit book makes a transformative use for criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching; (2) the work is a highly creative photograph; (3) the complete frame is needed to analyze its constructed space; and (4) the linked, web-sized copy is credited and does not replace the artist’s work, portfolio record, or a licensed reproduction.

## Research context

Casebere’s portfolio establishes titles and dates for the selected series. The Metropolitan Museum of Art’s collection records for *Library I* (1980), *Arches* (1985), *Hospital* (1997), and *Landscape with Houses (Dutchess County, NY) #1* (2009) establish the broader career trajectory of photographed tabletop models; its curatorial text describes the works as fabricated institutional and architectural spaces. BOMB Magazine’s 2001 interview identifies the flood imagery as a model-based exploration of temporality, emotion, and architecture, and discusses the Monticello works: https://bombmagazine.org/articles/2001/10/01/james-casebere/.
