# Thomas Ruff — source shortlist

Thomas Ruff’s practice repeatedly turns photography’s framing systems and technical conditions into the subject: the standardized portrait, the architectural elevation, the scientific archive, the compression artifact, and the virtual darkroom. The twelve full-frame files below were downloaded from the public **Thomas Ruff: Works** archive, where each work is identified by title and date. The artist’s site supplies no public-domain or reuse licence. MoMA’s collection record for Ruff’s *Portrait* identifies the copyright as © Thomas Ruff / Artists Rights Society (ARS), New York / VG Bild-Kunst, Germany; the use below therefore treats Thomas Ruff (with ARS/VG Bild-Kunst representation where applicable) as the known rightsholder.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. Each assessment below applies separately: (1) this nonprofit book uses the work for transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching; (2) each is a highly creative photographic/artistic work; (3) the complete, uncropped frame is needed to teach its particular treatment of scale, grid, point of view, or photographic mediation; and (4) the retained copy is a credited, linked, web-sized display image and does not replace the artist’s record, an original print, or a licensed reproduction. The direct display files were publicly served by the artist’s archive without authentication or access-control bypass.

## Shortlist

### 01 — *Porträt (P. Lappat)* (1987)

- **Why it matters:** The near life-scale, frontal bust makes a neutral background and even light feel both objective and intensely confrontational; it is Ruff’s canonical test of portraiture as surface.
- **Source:** [Portraits — official works archive](https://www.thomasruff.com/en/works/portraits/) (display file: https://www.thomasruff.com/site/assets/files/1130/026_lappat_druck.jpg)
- **Rights / fair-use assessment:** © Thomas Ruff; ARS/VG Bild-Kunst representation recorded by MoMA; no licence stated. Transformative analysis concerns standardized frontal framing; the complete portrait and colored ground are necessary; the credited 900 × 1199px artist-site display copy is web-sized and non-substitutive.
- **File:** `raw/thomas-ruff/01-portrait-p-lappat-1987.jpg` (900 × 1199px)

### 02 — *House Nr. 1 I* (1987)

- **Why it matters:** Ruff turns an ordinary façade into a large, level field, making the photograph’s straight-on geometry and muted gray sky more assertive than the building’s anecdote.
- **Source:** [Houses — official works archive](https://www.thomasruff.com/en/works/houses/) (display file: https://www.thomasruff.com/site/assets/files/1131/haus_nr_1_i.jpg)
- **Rights / fair-use assessment:** © Thomas Ruff; no licence stated. The chapter critiques frontal architectural framing and tonal normalization; the complete façade, ground, and sky must remain together; the credited 1980 × 1061px display copy is web-sized and non-substitutive.
- **File:** `raw/thomas-ruff/02-house-nr-1-i-1987.jpg` (1980 × 1061px)

### 03 — *House Nr. 7 I* (1988)

- **Why it matters:** The tall house is pinned to the vertical frame with a deliberately blank sky above it, a concise lesson in alignment, deadpan scale, and the pressure of a centered subject.
- **Source:** [Houses — official works archive](https://www.thomasruff.com/en/works/houses/) (display file: https://www.thomasruff.com/site/assets/files/1131/haus_nr_7_i.jpg)
- **Rights / fair-use assessment:** © Thomas Ruff; no licence stated. The complete image is needed to analyze its vertical proportion and surrounding void; the creative work is used only for transformative criticism in a credited 903 × 1200px web copy that does not substitute for a print.
- **File:** `raw/thomas-ruff/03-house-nr-7-i-1988.jpg` (903 × 1200px)

### 04 — *00h 00m / −20°* (1990), from *Sterne*

- **Why it matters:** A scientific sky survey becomes a composition of dispersed points against an almost total black field, asking how scale and authorship survive when the artist selects an astronomical image.
- **Source:** [Stars — official works archive](https://www.thomasruff.com/en/works/stars/) (display file: https://www.thomasruff.com/site/assets/files/1132/00h_00m_-20.jpg)
- **Rights / fair-use assessment:** © Thomas Ruff; no licence stated. Transformative analysis addresses the selected cosmic field and point distribution; the whole field is essential; the credited 797 × 1199px display copy is web-sized and non-substitutive. The archive explains that Ruff worked from European Southern Observatory survey negatives.
- **File:** `raw/thomas-ruff/04-00h-00m-minus-20-1990.jpg` (797 × 1199px)

### 05 — *Nacht 2 I* (1992)

- **Why it matters:** Green night-vision makes a suburban scene readable as a dense threshold of foliage, illumination, and uncertain depth rather than as ordinary documentary space.
- **Source:** [Nights — official works archive](https://www.thomasruff.com/en/works/nights/) (display file: https://www.thomasruff.com/site/assets/files/1149/nac02_i_kopie.jpg)
- **Rights / fair-use assessment:** © Thomas Ruff; no licence stated. The full square scene is needed to teach how the night-vision field organizes visibility and occlusion; this is transformative criticism using a credited 1269 × 1200px display copy, not a substitute for the original or licensed reproduction.
- **File:** `raw/thomas-ruff/05-nacht-2-i-1992.jpg` (1269 × 1200px)

### 06 — *nudes ez14* (1999)

- **Why it matters:** Ruff enlarges a low-resolution found web image until blur becomes a positive compositional material, making a cropped figure and color blocks compete for recognition.
- **Source:** [Nudes — official works archive](https://www.thomasruff.com/en/works/nudes/) (display file: https://www.thomasruff.com/site/assets/files/1153/ez14.jpg)
- **Rights / fair-use assessment:** © Thomas Ruff; no licence stated. The chapter requires the intact soft-edged frame to analyze enlargement, crop, and pixel blur; the creative work is credited and linked, with only a 1826 × 1200px web display copy retained for criticism rather than substitution.
- **File:** `raw/thomas-ruff/06-nudes-ez14-1999.jpg` (1826 × 1200px)

### 07 — *jpeg ny01* (2004)

- **Why it matters:** The enlarged file makes compression blocks visibly govern a city image, so the image oscillates between a grid of color and a distant photographic scene.
- **Source:** [jpeg — official works archive](https://www.thomasruff.com/en/works/jpeg/) (display file: https://www.thomasruff.com/site/assets/files/1139/ny01.jpg)
- **Rights / fair-use assessment:** © Thomas Ruff; no licence stated. The complete frame is required to teach the relation between 8 × 8 compression blocks and represented space; it is used transformatively as a credited 850 × 1199px artist-site copy, not as a market replacement.
- **File:** `raw/thomas-ruff/07-jpeg-ny01-2004.jpg` (850 × 1199px)

### 08 — *jpeg nt01* (2004)

- **Why it matters:** Its large, torn-looking pixel regions demonstrate that the JPEG artifact can become the main directional and color structure before the source scene resolves.
- **Source:** [jpeg — official works archive](https://www.thomasruff.com/en/works/jpeg/) (display file: https://www.thomasruff.com/site/assets/files/1139/nt01_kopie.jpg)

- **File:** `raw/thomas-ruff/08-jpeg-nt01-2004.jpg` (1500 × 1200px)

### 09 — *ma.r.s.01* (2010)

- **Why it matters:** Ruff recasts a vertical NASA Mars observation as a navigable, colored landscape, converting a top-down scientific record into an oblique cinematic space.
- **Source:** [ma.r.s. — official works archive](https://www.thomasruff.com/en/works/ma.r.s/) (display file: https://www.thomasruff.com/site/assets/files/1145/ma_r_s_01_druck.jpg)
- **Rights / fair-use assessment:** © Thomas Ruff; no licence stated for the transformed artwork. The full frame is essential to analyze the shift in viewpoint and color; the credited 841 × 1199px artist-site display copy serves only transformative teaching and is non-substitutive. The archive identifies the underlying 2006-onward HiRISE Mars imagery and Ruff’s processing.
- **File:** `raw/thomas-ruff/09-ma-r-s-01-2010.jpg` (841 × 1199px)

## Research context

The official archive supplies the core historical framing for the selected bodies of work: its *Houses* essay describes Ruff’s use of overcast skies and matter-of-fact architectural photography; its *Stars* essay identifies the ESO source archive; *Nights* describes his use of light-intensifier imagery; and the later series pages explain the compression, Mars-imagery, virtual-photogram, and press-archive transformations. MoMA’s [*Portrait* record](https://www.moma.org/collection/works/50870) independently confirms Ruff’s portrait series and the Thomas Ruff / ARS / VG Bild-Kunst copyright notice used above.
