# Thomas Ruff — research note

## What the source record establishes

The supplied works run from 1987 to 2015. They move from the deliberately
neutral colour portrait and architectural view associated with Ruff's Düsseldorf
period to selections from scientific archives, light-intensifier imagery, Internet
files, Mars observations, a virtual darkroom, and press-photo material. The useful
through-line is not documentary continuity but Ruff's repeated attention to how an
image is framed, enlarged, processed, and read. The official series pages linked in
[sources.md](sources.md) identify every selected title and date.

The official archive describes the *Portraits* (1981–91), *Houses* (1987–91),
*Sterne* (1989–92), *Nights* (1992–96), *nudes* (1999–2012), *jpeg* (2004–22),
*ma.r.s.* (2010–14), *Photograms* (2012–15), and *press++* (2015–18) series.
It records that the *Houses* pictures were made around Düsseldorf under grey skies;
that *Sterne* selects from European Southern Observatory negatives; that *Nights*
uses a light-intensifier device; that *nudes* derives from altered low-resolution
web gallery images; that *jpeg* enlarges and intensifies JPEG block structure; that
*ma.r.s.* transforms HiRISE Mars material into coloured, oblique-looking views;
that *Photograms* are digitally controlled virtual-darkroom simulations; and that
*press++* combines scanned fronts and backs of press photographs. These are
documented series methods, not evidence that Ruff made every source exposure himself.

## Printing and technical limits

The source pages identify the selected series as chromogenic colour prints; listed
large formats commonly use Diasec face mounting and wooden frames. That is a
series-level record, so this chapter does not assign an exact process, dimensions,
or edition to each displayed work. The ingested display JPEGs contain no usable
camera EXIF (the scanner metadata on *House Nr. 1 I* is not capture metadata).
Neither the supplied records nor these web copies establish camera body, lens,
focal length, aperture, shutter speed, ISO, film or sensor format, lighting plan,
capture workflow, or work-specific printing settings. The analysis therefore stays
with documented methods and visible, measured structure.
