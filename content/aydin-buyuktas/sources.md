# Aydın Büyüktaş — source shortlist

This set follows Büyüktaş’s 2015 *Flatland* views of Istanbul, each built by joining drone photographs into a continuous urban surface that folds upward through the frame. Bant Mag’s 2016 editorial presentation names the works below and describes the series as geometric reconstructions of Istanbul; its publicly accessible display JPEGs supplied the complete-frame files. Every downloaded JPEG opens successfully and has a 1024px long edge, above the project’s 640px normal floor. The publisher credits the work to Aydın Büyüktaş; no open licence or public-domain rendition was located.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. These are copyrighted contemporary photographs by Aydın Büyüktaş. Each assessment is limited and transformative: this nonprofit book uses a credited, page-linked, web-sized copy for criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching; the complete frame is necessary to analyze how the horizon curve, aerial plan, and upright city are joined; and the display copy does not replace the artist’s original, a print, the publisher’s page, or a licensed reproduction.

## Research context

Bant Mag’s 2016 presentation identifies these as *Flatland* images made from Istanbul scenes and characterizes the project’s geometric construction. The Marmara Urban Forum’s bilingual report independently describes Büyüktaş’s drone photographs of locations including the Grand Bazaar, Sultanahmet, Eminönü/New Mosque, and Fenerbahçe Stadium, combined from differing angles and heights into one image: https://www.marmara.gov.tr/UserFiles/Attachments/2017/06/19/75e9ffeb-30d4-4c24-b66d-dcceae6b6572.pdf. The deliberately compact source set holds the same city-scale problem in eleven variations: aerial plan, landmark, and street texture are all made to share a single folded surface.
