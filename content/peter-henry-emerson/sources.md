# Peter Henry Emerson — source shortlist

The first eight files are original JPEGs from The Metropolitan Museum of Art, marked Public Domain and available through its CC0 Open Access program. The final four are British Library scans on Wikimedia Commons, each marked Public domain.

1. **Poling the Marsh Hay** — 1886. Worker and pole create an assertive diagonal, deepened by a second figure and storm-dark sky. [Met record](https://www.metmuseum.org/art/collection/search/283227). **Rights:** Met Open Access, CC0/Public Domain.
2. **Setting the Bownet** — 1886. Boat, paired figures, circular nets, and reflection create a calm near-symmetry on water. [Met record](https://www.metmuseum.org/art/collection/search/286469). **Rights:** Met Open Access, CC0/Public Domain.
3. **Rowing Home the Schoof-Stuff** — 1886. Boat bundle and rower sit against broad reflected water while oars extend the gesture horizontally. [Met record](https://www.metmuseum.org/art/collection/search/286418). **Rights:** Met Open Access, CC0/Public Domain.
4. **Cantley: Wherries Waiting for the Turn of the Tide** — 1886. Low bank and water establish horizontal bands; wherries and rigging punctuate them rhythmically. [Met record](https://www.metmuseum.org/art/collection/search/286676). **Rights:** Met Open Access, CC0/Public Domain.
5. **Gunner Working Up To Fowl** — 1886. Marsh reeds frame the open water that encloses the small boat and figure. [Met record](https://www.metmuseum.org/art/collection/search/286673). **Rights:** Met Open Access, CC0/Public Domain.
6. **Gathering Water-Lilies** — 1886. Figures and boat supply focal mass while floating lilies create a richly textured, shallow surface. [Met record](https://www.metmuseum.org/art/collection/search/286671). **Rights:** Met Open Access, CC0/Public Domain.
7. **Water-Lilies** — 1886. Sparse panoramic layers and lily clusters balance detail against blank water. [Met record](https://www.metmuseum.org/art/collection/search/286677). **Rights:** Met Open Access, CC0/Public Domain.
8. **A Stiff Pull** — 1880s; printed 1887. A horse team, plough mechanism, and linkage make a compact diagonal across the worked field. [Met record](https://www.metmuseum.org/art/collection/search/269197). **Rights:** Met Open Access, CC0/Public Domain.
9. **Coming Home from the Marshes** — 1886. A small procession crosses expansive marsh, using a low horizon to emphasize distance and labour. [Commons/British Library record](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Coming_Home_from_the_Marshes,_Peter_Henry_Emerson,_1886.jpg). **Rights:** Public domain.
10. **At Plough, The End of the Furrow** — 1887. Furrows and plough team establish the frame’s strongest directional structure. [Commons/British Library record](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:At_Plough,_The_End_Of_The_Furrow,_Peter_Henry_Emerson,_1887.jpg). **Rights:** Public domain.
11. **A Dame’s School** — 1887. Figures group in layers inside a cramped cobbled yard contained by architecture. [Commons/British Library record](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:A_Dame%27s_School,_Peter_Henry_Emerson,_1887.jpg). **Rights:** Public domain.
12. **A Fisherman at Home** — 1887. A close vertical portrait is organized by the fisherman’s pose and telescope, with doorway and yard as an environmental frame. [Commons/British Library record](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:A_Fisherman_At_Home,_Peter_Henry_Emerson,_1887.jpg). **Rights:** Public domain.
