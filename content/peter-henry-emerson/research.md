# Peter Henry Emerson — research notes

## Era and place

These plates date principally from 1886–87 and picture work, transport, and domestic life in the Norfolk Broads and East Anglia. *Poling the Marsh Hay* belongs to Emerson’s first sustained naturalist sequence, *Life and Landscape on the Norfolk Broads* (1885–86). The photographs are interpretations of rural labour, not transparent ethnography; the Met describes Emerson’s vision as personal as well as anthropological.

## Technique and print process

Emerson opposed the composite-picture method associated with H. P. Robinson and argued for selective focus and graduated tonal values. That polemic makes the photographs’ broad water, sky, and reed planes especially important, but the chapter’s individual compositional readings are our analysis, not quotations of Emerson’s intention.

The Met identifies the 1886 Norfolk plates including *Poling the Marsh Hay* and *Setting the Bownet* as platinum prints from glass negatives. *A Stiff Pull* is an exception: it was printed in 1887 as a photogravure. The British Library scans used for the final four plates establish title/date/source but do not support a uniform process claim for every image.
