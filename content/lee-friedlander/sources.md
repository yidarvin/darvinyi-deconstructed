# Lee Friedlander — source shortlist

This twelve-image set concentrates on Friedlander’s social landscape: shop-window recursion, the photographer’s shadow or reflection, signs, monuments, and the camera’s interruption of ordinary street space. Every file was retrieved from a publicly reachable Art Institute of Chicago IIIF response, opened successfully as a JPEG, and measures 843px on its long edge. That is above the project’s 640px floor but deliberately web-sized.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. The Art Institute API marks each record `is_public_domain: false`; the artist is Lee Friedlander (born 1934), and the returned records do not name a separate rightsholder. Each assessment below is individual: this nonprofit book makes a transformative use for criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching; these are highly creative photographs; the complete frame is necessary for the stated spatial argument; and the credited, linked 843px copy is web-sized and cannot substitute for the museum record, original print, or licensed reproduction.

## Research context

The Art Institute records provide the titles, dates, artist attribution, collection credits, and public IIIF display files used here. The set is intentionally weighted toward the 1960s–70s material in which Friedlander makes framing visible: mirrors and shop windows turn public space into layered surfaces; his shadow or reflection makes the photographer a compositional actor; roadside and civic motifs become competing sign systems. The direct IIIF routes were tested as public JPEG responses and retain the whole photographed frame without cropping or retouching.
