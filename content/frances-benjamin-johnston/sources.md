# Frances Benjamin Johnston — source shortlist

This shortlist centers Johnston's 1899–1900 Hampton Normal and Agricultural Institute assignment (often called the *Hampton Album*). The Library of Congress group record identifies 173 prints from the assignment and says **"No known restrictions on publication"**; every item below is an LOC record with the same no-known-restrictions rights advisory. The assignment's institutional setting is historically charged: its images should be interpreted as Johnston's authored compositions as well as documents made within Hampton's assimilationist educational program. Official catalog wording is retained in titles where necessary for traceability.

1. **[Hampton Institute, Va., 1899 — Classroom scenes — Bible history](https://www.loc.gov/pictures/item/2001703744/)** — 1899 or 1900. The receding desks, upright teacher, and ordered wall diagrams turn a lesson into a tightly rectilinear grid. **Rights:** LOC: no known restrictions on reproduction. Downloaded as `01-classroom-bible-history.jpg` from the LOC TIFF master (1536 × 1207 px).
2. **[Hampton Institute, Hampton, Va., 1899 — Geography class, “studying the cathedral towns”](https://www.loc.gov/pictures/item/2001703809/)** — 1899 or 1900. Rows of seated students, maps, and framed pictures make the classroom a layered, centered study in repetition. **Rights:** LOC: no known restrictions on reproduction. Downloaded as `02-geography-cathedral-towns.jpg` from the LOC TIFF master (1536 × 1207 px).
3. **[Hampton Institute, Hampton, Va., 1899 — Ancient History class studying Egypt](https://www.loc.gov/pictures/item/2001703810/)** — 1899 or 1900. A large Egyptian image and a classroom of alternating heads create a strong picture-within-picture hierarchy. **Rights:** LOC: no known restrictions on reproduction. Downloaded as `03-ancient-history-egypt.jpg` from the LOC TIFF master (5796 × 4612 px).
4. **[Hampton Institute, Va. — Indian orchestra](https://www.loc.gov/pictures/item/2001703741/)** — 1899 or 1900. The seated ensemble makes a shallow horizontal frieze, punctuated by instruments and the tonal rhythm of coats and faces. **Rights:** LOC: no known restrictions on reproduction. Downloaded as `04-indian-orchestra.jpg` from the LOC TIFF master (1536 × 1199 px).
5. **[Ten Afro-American women in cooking class at Hampton Institute, Hampton, Va.](https://www.loc.gov/pictures/item/92500048/)** — 1899 or 1900. A long working counter, bright utensils, and a measured row of figures produce an exacting frontal plane. **Rights:** LOC: no known restrictions on publication. Downloaded as `05-cooking-class.jpg` from the LOC TIFF master (1536 × 1136 px).
6. **[School assembly in Hampton Institute, Hampton, Va.](https://www.loc.gov/pictures/item/92500524/)** — 1899–1900. The massed seated audience creates an unusually dense field; architectural edges organize it without dissolving individual faces. **Rights:** LOC: no known restrictions on publication. Downloaded as `06-school-assembly.jpg` from the LOC TIFF master (1536 × 908 px).
7. **[American Indian student, half-length portrait, facing right, taken at Hampton Institute](https://www.loc.gov/pictures/item/92500527/)** — 1899–1900. A reserved profile is isolated against a pale ground, making pose, negative space, and tonal separation unusually legible. **Rights:** LOC: no known restrictions on publication. Downloaded as `07-student-portrait-right.jpg` from the LOC TIFF master (1025 × 1536 px).
8. **[Indian student at Hampton Institute, Hampton, Va.](https://www.loc.gov/pictures/item/2001703890/)** — 1899 or 1900. The opposing left-facing half-length portrait makes a useful counterpart: figure scale and empty ground carry the whole frame. **Rights:** LOC: no known restrictions on reproduction. Downloaded as `08-indian-student-left.jpg` from the LOC TIFF master (1099 × 1536 px).
9. **[A class in dressmaking, Hampton Institute, Hampton, Virginia](https://www.loc.gov/pictures/item/98502966/)** — 1899. Parallel tables, garments, and figures arrange vocational work as a controlled diagonal progression. **Rights:** LOC: no known restrictions on publication. Highest master retrieval is logged in `NEEDED.md`.
10. **[Formal class in liberal arts and sciences — “clay modeling”](https://www.loc.gov/pictures/item/2001703864/)** — 1899 or 1900. A blackboard, cast forms, and the instructor establish several nested rectangles around the students. **Rights:** LOC: no known restrictions on reproduction. Highest master retrieval is logged in `NEEDED.md`.
11. **[Before entering school — seven Indian children of uneducated parents](https://www.loc.gov/pictures/item/2001703887/)** — 1899 or 1900. The spare family-group arrangement, building edge, and compressed spacing create a severe documentary tableau; later treatment must provide historical context for the catalog language and the school’s assimilationist program. **Rights:** LOC: no known restrictions on reproduction. Highest master retrieval is logged in `NEEDED.md`.
12. **[Hampton Normal and Agricultural Institute — Memorial Church and Academic Hall](https://www.loc.gov/pictures/item/2008677098/)** — 1899 or 1900. Water foreground, rooflines, and the church tower make a carefully tiered architectural landscape. **Rights:** LOC: no known restrictions on reproduction. Highest master retrieval is logged in `NEEDED.md`.
