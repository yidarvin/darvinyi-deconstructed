# Frances Benjamin Johnston — research

## Record and process

The selected photographs belong to Johnston's Hampton Normal and Agricultural Institute assignment, made in 1899–1900. The Library of Congress group record identifies 173 photoprints: 164 gelatin silver prints and 9 cyanotypes. It describes classroom, vocational, agricultural, and home-economics scenes, and lists Johnston as photographer. A related Library of Congress catalog record says the photographs were originally made for the Paris Exposition of 1900.

The source records do not establish the physical process of each individual image in this chapter. The chapter therefore treats the displayed files as digital reproductions of the Library's records, not as proof that any particular plate is gelatin silver rather than cyanotype.

## Camera and settings

No source consulted for this chapter records a camera body, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, plate sensitivity, or exposure for an individual Hampton photograph. The ingestion manifest contains no capture EXIF. Specific settings would be invented and are not given.

## Historical caution

The assignment is a constructed institutional view of Hampton. The Library of Congress group record describes the work through the school’s curricular categories; its period catalog language is retained in `sources.md` only for traceability. Formal analysis here makes claims about visible framing, placement, tone, and repetition. It does not treat those formal choices as neutral evidence about the students or the institution.
