# Eadweard Muybridge — research notes

## Historical record

The four selected plates come from Eadweard Muybridge's *Animal Locomotion* (1887), a late-nineteenth-century motion-study publication. The Library of Congress catalogs them as "electro-photographic investigation[s]" made of consecutive images: plate 636 records twenty phases of a man riding a horse; the other records identify a woman with children, a man walking on an incline, and a man jumping. [Plate 636](https://www.loc.gov/item/91727224/), [plate 52](https://www.loc.gov/item/92502815/), [plate 489](https://www.loc.gov/item/92502812/), and [plate 161](https://www.loc.gov/item/92502817/).

## Process and printing

The LOC catalog medium for each selected object is "1 photomechanical print : collotype." They are therefore best described as collotype-printed plates reproducing sequential photographic studies, not as surviving direct camera prints. The records cite *Animal Locomotion*, published in Philadelphia by the Photogravure Company of New York in 1887.

## Camera settings

The supplied catalog records do not give a camera model, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter mechanism, exposure duration, frame interval, negative process, lighting arrangement, or camera count for these four plates. No numerical setting or production setup is inferred here. The empty EXIF fields in the local manifest are likewise properties of the delivered scan files, not evidence about Muybridge's equipment.
