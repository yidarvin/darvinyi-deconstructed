# Garry Winogrand — source shortlist

This working set contains twelve Art Institute of Chicago public-display IIIF renditions. They were retrieved directly from the museum's public image service and verified as opening JPEGs. Each has an 843px long edge, clearing the project's 640px quality floor without upscaling. The selection moves from Winogrand's New York street and zoo pictures through public spectacle, protest, and his late West Coast work, so the chapter can teach his tilted, crowded, and deliberately unstable frames.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. Garry Winogrand died in 1984 and these photographs remain copyrighted; the Art Institute records identify him as artist and give collection-credit lines, but do not state a copyright notice or name a rightsholder. The present attribution therefore identifies Garry Winogrand and the Art Institute collection credit without inventing an estate notice. Each assessment below applies all four factors: this nonprofit book uses a web-sized copy for transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching; the work is a creative photograph; the complete frame is necessary to analyze its compositional relationships; and the credited, record-linked display copy does not replace a museum record, original print, or licensed reproduction.

## Research context

SFMOMA's 2013–15 retrospective describes Winogrand as a major voice of 1960s New York who also photographed widely across the United States, from California and Texas to Miami and Chicago: https://www.sfmoma.org/exhibition/garry-winogrand-2/. Its collection listing confirms the range of his subjects—streets, demonstrations, airports, zoos, and late California scenes: https://www.sfmoma.org/artist/Garry_Winogrand/. The Art Institute set preserves that breadth through publicly served, modest-resolution collection-display files chosen for composition study rather than reproduction.
