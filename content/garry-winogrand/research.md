# Garry Winogrand — research notes

## Scope and evidence

The supplied source set documents twelve Garry Winogrand photographs made from 1963 to 1980: New York street and zoo scenes, public spectacle and protest, then Texas and California. SFMOMA characterizes him as a major voice of 1960s New York who also worked widely across the United States; its cited collection context lists streets, demonstrations, airports, zoos, and late California scenes. The Art Institute of Chicago records are the per-work authority for the titles, dates, collection credits, and the publicly displayed renditions used here. See [sources.md](sources.md).

## Technique and record limits

The source notes support a visual account of crowded, tilted, and deliberately unstable framing: competing depth zones in *American Legion Convention*; a canted booth in *New York City*; hard crowd density in *Hard Hat Rally*; an obstructing foreground body in *Venice*; and bicycle, shadow, and sidewalk diagonals in *Beverly Hills*. These are observations about the pictured frames, not claims about a camera system.

The supplied records do **not** identify a camera body, lens, focal length, film stock, aperture, shutter speed, exposure, negative process, print process, print dimensions, or edition for any selected photograph. No such technical settings are inferred here. The usable files are 843px-long-edge Art Institute public-display IIIF JPEG renditions, retained as web-sized fair-use copies for criticism and composition teaching; they are not evidence about the original prints.

## Working chronology

- **1963–67, New York:** cropped gestures, bench friezes, and people/animal pairings complicate ordinary public encounters.
- **1969–72, spectacle and crowd:** Apollo spectators, a political rally, a demonstration, and a tilted phone booth make the frame carry clashing directions of attention.
- **1973–80, Texas and California:** distance through trees, bicycle-and-shadow diagonals, and foreground occlusion test how much a social scene can withstand being blocked or pitched.
