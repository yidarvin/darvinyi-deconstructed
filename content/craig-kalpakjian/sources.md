# Craig Kalpakjian — source shortlist

This set follows Kalpakjian’s photoreal computer-generated institutional interiors from 1996 through 2002: corridors, ventilation, stair, and a cropped organizational void. The ten JPEGs were downloaded from the artist’s publicly accessible Works index and each opens successfully. The artist’s site states titles, dates, media, and dimensions; its works index is the authoritative record for these display images: https://kalpakjian.com/#works. Whitney’s collection record independently confirms *Lobby* (1996) and identifies the rights as “© artist or artist’s estate”; MoMA’s record identifies *Room* (1996) as © Craig Kalpakjian; and SFMOMA confirms that the related *Corridor* animation was computer-generated. No open licence or public-domain statement was found for this series.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. This is a separate, limited assessment for each public artist-site display image: this nonprofit book uses a credited web-sized copy for transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching; each complete frame is needed to analyze its spatial construction; and the linked display copy does not replace the artist’s work, the collection record, or a licensed reproduction. Creator/rightsholder notice: © Craig Kalpakjian (the artist site supplies no open reuse licence; the MoMA collection page explicitly gives © Craig Kalpakjian for a 1996 work, and the Whitney record gives © artist or artist’s estate for *Lobby*).

## Research context

SFMOMA describes Kalpakjian’s digital modeling as making familiar office-like spaces that are artificial constructions, and explains that every visible detail of its 1995 *Corridor* was programmed: https://www.sfmoma.org/artwork/2000.15/. The selection concentrates on still works whose built-in corridors, grids, thresholds, reflections, and containment systems let the chapter distinguish photorealistic rendering from photographic depiction.
