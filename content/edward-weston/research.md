# Edward Weston — research

## Scope and sources

This selected group spans 1923–1937: *Tina [nude on the azotea]* (1923), *Washbowl* (1925), vegetable studies and *“E” Town, New Mexico* (1929–31), and the nude and landscape works of 1936–37. The Art Institute of Chicago records identify the set primarily as later prints, not necessarily prints made at the moment of exposure: eleven selected works are documented as gelatin silver prints, largely printed in 1952–54, and the records attribute ten of those later printings to Brett Weston. *Washbowl* is separately catalogued as a platinum print. See the linked object records in [sources.md](sources.md).

## Technique and printing

The local source record supports a careful distinction between photographed date and surviving print. It does not support a single-process description across the chapter: gelatin silver applies to the later Weston/Brett Weston prints, while *Washbowl* is platinum. The files retain thin print borders in several plates; these are characteristics of the supplied reproduction and are not treated as camera-frame evidence.

## Camera settings

No selected source record or ingested file documents a camera body, lens, aperture, shutter speed, film stock, or exposure for these individual photographs. The normalized manifest contains no original-camera EXIF. This chapter therefore makes visual claims from the photographed and printed forms, not invented technical-setting claims.
