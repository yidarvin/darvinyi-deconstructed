# Edward Weston — source shortlist

This working set contains twelve web-sized JPEG renditions retrieved directly from the Art Institute of Chicago's public IIIF service and verified as opening image files. Weston’s photographs remain rights-managed: the Art Institute API marks every selected object `is_public_domain: false`. The records list collection credit lines but no copyright notice. The Cleveland Museum of Art’s [*Church at “E” Town, New Mexico* record](https://www.clevelandart.org/art/1984.169) for a comparable Weston print identifies the notice as “© Center for Creative Photography, Arizona Board of Regents / Artists Rights Society (ARS), New York”; that notice is recorded here as the known rightsholder attribution, rather than inferred from the Art Institute's accession credits.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. Each is an authoritative, publicly served 843px-long-edge collection rendition used for nonprofit, transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching. The complete photograph is needed to analyze its framing and internal spatial relationships; every chapter item will credit and link to the source record; and this modest web copy cannot substitute for an original print, museum record, or licensed reproduction. The four factors are applied to each selection below.

## Research context

The Art Institute’s Weston collection identifies the artist’s sustained studies of vegetables, nudes, architecture, and western landscape. Its record for *Pepper* describes how close observation transforms a familiar object into a sculptural form: https://www.artic.edu/artworks/120846/pepper. This set preserves that central concern while giving the later builder enough variation to teach closed organic form, figure-ground fusion, tonal architecture, and landscape scale.
