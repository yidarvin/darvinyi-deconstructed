# Bernard Faucon — research note

## What the supplied sources establish

The supplied source set identifies ten works from Bernard Faucon's *Les Grandes
Vacances* (1976–1981), a series in which the artist staged shop-window
mannequins in real childhood locations. The official series page names the works
and describes locations including Lioux cemetery, Saint-Saturnin pool, and the
Camargue; the Maison Européenne de la Photographie places the series at the start
of Faucon's 1976–1995 photographic work. The source notes preserve the official
display URL, copyright credit, and a work-specific fair-use rationale for each
web-sized plate. [Source notes](sources.md)

## Process and technical limits

The sources support calling these constructed or staged photographs, not treating
the mannequins as documentary subjects. They do not document a camera body, lens,
focal length, aperture, shutter speed, sensitivity, film stock, original negative
format, or lighting plan for these individual works. Scanner-like metadata in the
supplied 1200px JPEGs describes the display files, not Faucon's capture process.

Two later print records are specific enough to retain but not generalize:
*L'enfant qui vole* (1979) is documented by Holden Luntz as a 2013 Fresson
photograph, while *Diabolo menthe* (1980) is documented by Gutknecht Gallery as a
2005 charcoal Fresson print. Neither record establishes an edition process for the
whole series. The chapter therefore uses the visible arrangement, the documented
staging, and measured tonal and palette results rather than invented settings or
print claims.
