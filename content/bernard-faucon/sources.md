# Bernard Faucon — source shortlist

Bernard Faucon's *Les Grandes Vacances* (1976–1981) is the foundational series of his staged childhood worlds: shop-window mannequins, real locations in Provence, bright summer light, ritual, play, and sudden violence. The artist's official series page supplies the titles below and publicly serves each complete 1200 × 1200px JPEG. The page explains that Faucon staged mannequins in childhood locations including Lioux cemetery, Saint-Saturnin pool, and the Camargue. Its footer says “© 2019 BERNARD FAUCON - TOUS DROITS RÉSERVÉS”; the image metadata also carries `© Bernard Faucon`. No public-domain or open-licence rendition was found.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. This is a separate, limited assessment for each copyrighted photograph: the book uses a credited, linked, web-sized complete-frame copy for transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching. A complete frame is necessary because Faucon's meaning is constructed by the relation of mannequins, props, setting, and empty space. The copies are 1200px public display files and neither replace the artist's record, an original Fresson print, nor a licensed reproduction.

## Research context

The Maison Européenne de la Photographie identifies Faucon as an early practitioner of photographic staging and lists *Les Grandes Vacances* as the first phase of the chronological retrospective: https://www.mep-fr.org/event/bernard-faucon/. The official series page records the project as 1976–1981 and identifies the selected titles. The set deliberately samples processions, group tableaux, aerial illusion, ritual, play, isolated address, and fire so a later chapter can analyze the range of Faucon’s constructed realities rather than repeat one motif.
