# Fan Ho — research notes

## Scope and record

This chapter studies twelve Hong Kong photographs represented by authorised Blue Lotus Gallery display files. The dated selection runs from *Approaching Shadow* (1954) through *Waterfront* (1965), with six further works identified by the gallery only as c. 1950s–60s. *Controversy* is dated 1955 by the page caption even though its display-file path says 1956; this chapter follows the caption and records the discrepancy rather than resolving it by inference.

The selection repeatedly gives ordinary street movement an architectural scale: a lone pedestrian, cart, market vendor, child, or walking group meets a field of shadow, rails, signs, light, scaffold, or skyline. That is a visual through-line in these twelve supplied frames, not a claim that every work made by Ho has the same method.

## Medium and production limits

The supplied gallery page identifies the artist's estate rights and provides web display renditions, but its records for these twelve works do not identify the original print process, paper, print date, negative format, camera body, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, film stock, exposure method, lighting setup, or darkroom process. The ingested JPEGs have no capture EXIF. Accordingly, no camera setting or undocumented production fact is inferred in this chapter.

The display files are black-and-white renditions whose measured luminance, edge density, palette, and geometric signals support discussion of the files' visible structures. They are not a substitute for inspecting original prints. The source set ranges from 1,600 to 3,000 pixels on the long edge before normalisation; the 1,600px ingested copies preserve the complete supplied frames for study.
