# Fan Ho — source shortlist

Source investigation completed 18 July 2026. All twelve acquisitions are direct, publicly accessible display files from [Blue Lotus Gallery’s authorised Fan Ho page](https://bluelotus-gallery.com/work/ho-fan-1-9zwhp), which identifies Blue Lotus as the artist’s exclusive representative and states that all rights are reserved by © Fan Ho Estate. The page is retained as the record page and each entry below links to its exact public display rendition.

Every selection uses **fair-use**. Rights notice: © Fan Ho Estate; Blue Lotus Gallery, authorised representative. This nonprofit scholarly book uses each web-sized copy transformatively for criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching. The complete, uncropped frame is necessary to examine its spatial structure; the small display copy is linked to and credited to the authorised gallery and does not substitute for the original, museum record, estate edition, or licensed reproduction.

1. **Approaching Shadow** (Hong Kong, 1954) — The advancing diagonal of shadow turns the street into an architectural stage and makes scale legible. [Public display file](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/54e9ba8ae4b0165bdcf59868/1536842320191-UGOR7GWV12TE6MSPMSR6/Fan+Ho_approaching+shadow.jpg). **Use basis: fair-use.** The complete vertical frame is essential for teaching the shadow’s diagonal progression; the shared four-factor rationale above applies.
2. **As Evening Hurries By** (Hong Kong, 1955) — A sharply divided light field and small moving figures demonstrate timing against a geometric façade. [Public display file](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/54e9ba8ae4b0165bdcf59868/1536842381407-5KYYX93XYGJVNYTKRERM/Fan+Ho_As+evening+hurries+by.jpg). **Use basis: fair-use.** The full frame is required to analyse the figure-to-shadow scale; the shared four-factor rationale above applies.
3. **Construction** (Hong Kong, 1957) — Scaffold-like linear rhythm and a compressed human scene make a strong lesson in layered depth. [Public display file](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/54e9ba8ae4b0165bdcf59868/1536842552349-YDOLVVXFXJ4USISCFQXS/Fan+Ho_Construction+2.jpg). **Use basis: fair-use.** The full horizontal frame is needed to trace the repeated structural intervals; the shared four-factor rationale above applies.
4. **In a Chinese Street** (Hong Kong, 1959) — Architecture frames a dense street corridor, balancing human activity across receding planes. [Public display file](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/54e9ba8ae4b0165bdcf59868/1536842852154-KLIUTSTVAPS34MB83OZ2/Fan+Ho_In+a+chinese+street.jpg). **Use basis: fair-use.** The complete frame is necessary to teach enclosure and recession; the shared four-factor rationale above applies.
5. **Waterfront** (1965) — Its unusually wide panoramic strip makes negative space and a low horizon the primary compositional problem. [Public display file](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/54e9ba8ae4b0165bdcf59868/1536843595724-8UFCYHLN41ZPFS4UT114/Fan+Ho_Waterfront.jpg). **Use basis: fair-use.** Cropping would defeat the lesson in panoramic proportion; the shared four-factor rationale above applies.
6. **Her Study** (c. 1950s–60s; undated in the gallery) — Interior planes, a seated figure, and window light show how tonal masses organise a quiet scene. [Public display file](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/54e9ba8ae4b0165bdcf59868/1536842752441-X2XMQEMZ9N7KCLKN6KPP/Fan+Ho_Her+Study.jpg). **Use basis: fair-use.** The complete vertical frame is needed to compare the interior’s stacked planes; the shared four-factor rationale above applies.
7. **A Day Is Done** (c. 1950s–60s; undated in the gallery) — A figure’s placement inside a deep alley demonstrates a controlled exit path through darkness. [Public display file](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/54e9ba8ae4b0165bdcf59868/1536842204091-Y4SRWNO893T10KNVTWFE/Fan+Ho_A+day+is+done.jpg). **Use basis: fair-use.** The whole frame is required to teach the figure’s relation to the enclosing tonal field; the shared four-factor rationale above applies.
8. **Afternoon Chat** (c. 1950s–60s; undated in the gallery) — Multiple people and repeating building bands make an instructive counterpoint between social grouping and architectural rhythm. [Public display file](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/54e9ba8ae4b0165bdcf59868/1536842252211-DQC89YLWPEO0SQANAB5Y/Fan+Ho_Afternoon+chat.jpg). **Use basis: fair-use.** The complete breadth is necessary to study repetitions across the scene; the shared four-factor rationale above applies.
9. **Arrow** (c. 1950s–60s; undated in the gallery) — A directional sign and crossing lines make visual vectors explicit rather than merely decorative. [Public display file](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/54e9ba8ae4b0165bdcf59868/1536842319449-H2FYT149FXXN2VJINXH2/Fan+Ho_Arrow.jpg). **Use basis: fair-use.** The complete frame is necessary to follow the competing directional cues; the shared four-factor rationale above applies.
10. **Childhood** (c. 1950s–60s; undated in the gallery) — A child’s small silhouette activates a large built environment and teaches scale through contrast. [Public display file](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/54e9ba8ae4b0165bdcf59868/1536842527198-DWUX2LECNJ0W7L0EO16D/Fan+Ho_ChildHood.jpg). **Use basis: fair-use.** The complete image is needed to retain the scale relationship; the shared four-factor rationale above applies.
11. **Sun Rays** (c. 1950s–60s; undated in the gallery) — Raking light divides the street into luminous wedges, ideal for analysing a light-led hierarchy. [Public display file](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/54e9ba8ae4b0165bdcf59868/1536843491802-XFCCFQQ2A4ZPP92R9EFM/Fan+Ho_Sun+Rays+1.jpg). **Use basis: fair-use.** The full frame is needed to map the rays against the figures and buildings; the shared four-factor rationale above applies.
12. **Controversy** (Hong Kong, 1955; gallery file path labels 1956, but the page caption gives 1955) — A high-contrast vertical scene makes the pressure between figures, walls, and a narrow aperture visible. [Public display file](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/54e9ba8ae4b0165bdcf59868/1536851746778-5WKI1I8LHPLAAQC26NPR/Fan+Ho%2C+Controversy%2C+Hong+Kong+1956). **Use basis: fair-use.** The full vertical image is required to analyse the compression of the scene; the shared four-factor rationale above applies.
