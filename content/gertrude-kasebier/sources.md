# Gertrude Käsebier — source shortlist

All ten selected files were downloaded to `raw/gertrude-kasebier/` as original JPEG renditions. Each opens successfully and has a long edge of at least 1,550 px (the smallest is *Yoked and Muzzled* at 2,160 × 1,550 px).

1. **The Manger (Ideal Motherhood), c. 1900** — A near-white mother-and-child grouping; it is Käsebier’s clearest exercise in tonal compression and a key expression of her motherhood theme. [National Gallery of Art, object 150206](https://www.nga.gov/artworks/150206), [open 2085 × 3000 px file](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Gertrude_K%C3%A4sebier,_The_Manger,_c.1900,_NGA_150206.jpg). **Rights:** NGA’s open-collection image is marked CC0 on Wikimedia Commons.
2. **Blessed Art Thou Among Women, c. 1900** — The low viewpoint, dense foreground, and stepped mother/child silhouettes make a compact, almost icon-like pyramid. [National Gallery of Art, object 150204](https://www.nga.gov/artworks/150204), [open 1766 × 3000 px file](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Gertrude_K%C3%A4sebier,_Blessed_Art_Thou_Among_Women,_c.1900,_NGA_150204.jpg). **Rights:** NGA open-collection image, CC0 on Wikimedia Commons.
3. **Family Group (Mrs. White, Maynard & Lewis), c. 1899** — The figures lock into a shallow, balanced domestic tableau, showing Käsebier’s pictorial staging rather than snapshot space. [National Gallery of Art, object 139521](https://www.nga.gov/artworks/139521), [open 3000 × 2270 px file](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Gertrude_K%C3%A4sebier,_Family_Group_(Mrs._White,_Maynard_%26_Lewis),_c._1899,_NGA_139521.jpg). **Rights:** NGA open-collection image, CC0 on Wikimedia Commons.
4. **Florentine Boy, 1899** — A single figure is isolated against a pale field, making pose, edge light, and negative space do the compositional work. [National Gallery of Art, object 127435](https://www.nga.gov/artworks/127435), [open 1997 × 3000 px file](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Gertrude_K%C3%A4sebier,_Florentine_Boy,_1899,_NGA_127435.jpg). **Rights:** NGA open-collection image, CC0 on Wikimedia Commons.
5. **Portrait of a Boy, 1899** — The head and dark garment are massed off-center against a luminous ground, a concise example of Käsebier’s tonal portrait design. [National Gallery of Art, object 127441](https://www.nga.gov/artworks/127441), [open 2354 × 3000 px file](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Gertrude_K%C3%A4sebier,_Portrait_of_a_Boy,_1899,_NGA_127441.jpg). **Rights:** NGA open-collection image, CC0 on Wikimedia Commons.
6. **The Bat, 1902** — The costumed child becomes a dark, wing-shaped silhouette; the image is unusually direct evidence of her theatrical framing and value control. [National Gallery of Art, object 139520](https://www.nga.gov/artworks/139520), [open 2976 × 4000 px file](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Gertrude_K%C3%A4sebier,_The_Bat,_1902,_NGA_139520.jpg). **Rights:** NGA open-collection image, CC0 on Wikimedia Commons.
7. **Little Good Harbor, Maine, c. 1913** — A late landscape in which rocks, water, and horizon are organized as broad, quiet tonal bands rather than descriptive detail. [National Gallery of Art, object 137853](https://www.nga.gov/artworks/137853), [open 2826 × 4000 px file](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Gertrude_K%C3%A4sebier,_Little_Good_Harbor,_Maine,_c._1913,_NGA_137853.jpg). **Rights:** NGA open-collection image, CC0 on Wikimedia Commons.
8. **Sunshine in the House, 1908–1912** — The window, figure, and bright interior form a layered rectangular composition that extends Käsebier’s soft-focus pictorialism into domestic architecture. [Museum of Fine Arts, Houston / Google Art Project open file](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Gertrude_K%C3%A4sebier_-_Sunshine_in_the_House_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg). **Rights:** Wikimedia Commons marks the historic work public domain in the United States.
9. **The Bride, 1902** — A frontal head-and-shoulders portrait reduced to veil, face, and pale surround; a useful counterpoint to the more elaborate narrative photographs. [George Eastman Museum / Google Art Project open file](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Gertrude_K%C3%A4sebier_-_The_Bride_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg). **Rights:** Wikimedia Commons marks the historic work public domain in the United States.
10. **Yoked and Muzzled — Marriage (also known as *Find the Parallel*), c. 1915** — The paired figures and a binding horizontal accent make the social allegory readable through its geometry before its narrative. [Library of Congress collection mirror / open 2160 × 1550 px file](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Gertrude_Kasebier-Yoked_and_Muzzled.jpg). **Rights:** Library of Congress source; Wikimedia Commons marks the work Public Domain Mark / public domain.

## Source notes

- The seven NGA records are the preferred collection provenance; Wikimedia Commons supplies the openly downloadable full-resolution copies used here and preserves the CC0 designation.
- The three non-NGA files are openly downloadable Commons copies with stated museum or Library of Congress provenance. All were made before 1928 and are marked public domain by their file records.
- No source remains outstanding; `NEEDED.md` is therefore not required for this pass.
