# William Henry Jackson — research notes

## Record-backed context

The supplied Yellowstone photographs date from 1871, 1872, and 1878 and were made during F. V. Hayden's U.S. Geological Survey of the Territories for the Department of the Interior. The Library of Congress records classify the objects as expedition and landscape photographs, and, crucially, as albumen prints. The Great Falls record identifies a 324 × 248 mm albumen print mounted at 508 × 406 mm; the Columnar Basalts record identifies a 242 × 328 mm albumen print mounted at 406 × 508 mm. Those catalog records establish the print process and object scale, rather than a speculative camera workflow.

The archive metadata does **not** record aperture, shutter speed, focal length, exposure duration, negative format, lens, or a per-image camera model for this selection. The scanner's `Metis Systems` EXIF in `manifest.json` belongs to the modern digitization, not Jackson's camera, and is not treated as historical camera information.

## Sources

- Library of Congress, [*Great Falls of the Yellowstone* (1871)](https://www.loc.gov/item/2020630255/): Hayden survey context; albumen-print format and dimensions; no known publication restrictions.
- Library of Congress, [*Columnar Basalts on Yellowstone River* (1878)](https://www.loc.gov/item/2020630263/): Hayden survey context; albumen-print format and dimensions; no known publication restrictions.
- The remaining item records cited in `sources.md` identify the individual 1871–78 Yellowstone survey views. They do not supply camera settings, so none are asserted here.
