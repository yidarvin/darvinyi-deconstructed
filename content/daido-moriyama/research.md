# Daido Moriyama — research note

## Scope and documented context

The Smithsonian National Museum of Asian Art's *Japan Modern* gallery identifies this ten-work selection, with dates from 1965 to 2001, and its biography places Moriyama's early work in the Provoke-era vocabulary of high contrast, grain, and blur. The source packet also identifies the later color, cropped-body, and street photographs. Those records establish dates, titles, and a broad artistic context; they do not supply a uniform technical history for every negative or print.

## Technique and printing

The files are unaltered Smithsonian-hosted display JPEGs, not scans whose printing history is documented in the source packet. The visual discussion can therefore describe pictured grain, blur, severe contrast, cropping, and the later color palette, but it cannot identify a negative format, film stock, developer, paper, print date, or original printing process for any individual work. The display files' lack of capture EXIF is consistent with that limit.

## Camera settings

No source record here identifies a camera body, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, sensitivity, lighting setup, or exposure setting for these ten photographs. The chapter does not infer settings from their grain, blur, darkness, or apparent depth. Its numerical observations are limited to the analyzer's measurements of the supplied display renditions.
