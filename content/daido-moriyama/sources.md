# Daido Moriyama — source shortlist

This ten-image working set is drawn from the Smithsonian National Museum of Asian
Art's public *Japan Modern* gallery for Moriyama. The gallery supplies the object
captions and states: “Images © Daido Moriyama Photo Foundation.” Each public JPEG
was downloaded directly from that gallery's Smithsonian-hosted display rendition,
opened successfully, and retained without alteration. Their long edges are
640–768px, meeting the project's normal 640px acquisition floor.

All entries are **fair-use** assessments, not claims of open access. The works are
creative, copyrighted photographs; the stated notice is © Daido Moriyama Photo
Foundation. For each entry, this book uses a complete, web-sized frame only for
transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching; the full frame is
needed for the particular relationship described below. The credited, linked image
does not replace the Smithsonian collection record, an original print, or a
licensed reproduction.

## Acquired shortlist

  attention in the same shallow plane.

- **Use basis — fair-use:** Daido Moriyama; © Daido Moriyama Photo Foundation.
  The complete sign–adult–child arrangement is necessary to analyze its flattened
  social geometry. This 472 × 640px credited, linked teaching copy is
  transformative criticism and scholarship, not a substitute for the print,
  record, or licensed image.
- **File:** `raw/daido-moriyama/01-yokosuka-club-crown.jpg` (472 × 640px)

### 05 — *Stray Dog, Misawa, Aomori* (1971)

- **Why it matters:** Moriyama's signature near-frame dog makes the road and
  horizon buckle around a gritty, off-center animal silhouette; it is the clearest
  lesson in his are-bure-boke compression and instability.
- **Source:** [Smithsonian object page](https://asia-archive.si.edu/exhibition/moriyama-daido/s2018-2-183/); [public display JPEG](https://asia-archive.si.edu/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/S2018.2.183.jpg).
- **Use basis — fair-use:** Daido Moriyama; © Daido Moriyama Photo Foundation.

  asymmetry, and blur. This 707 × 548px credited, linked public display copy is
  used transformatively for criticism and scholarship, not as a market substitute.
- **File:** `raw/daido-moriyama/05-stray-dog-misawa-aomori.jpg` (707 × 548px)

## Research context

The [Smithsonian Moriyama gallery](https://asia-archive.si.edu/exhibition/moriyama-daido/)
identifies these ten works, their dates, media, and accession numbers, and records
the copyright notice used above. Its biography describes Moriyama's Provoke-era
high-contrast, grainy, blurry practice; the selected set deliberately spans its
1965 Yokosuka beginnings, the 1970–72 road/rail photographs and *Stray Dog*, and
later color, cropped-body, and street work.
