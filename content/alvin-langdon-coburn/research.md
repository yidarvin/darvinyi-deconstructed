# Alvin Langdon Coburn — research

## Record and process

The selected source records date from 1900 to 1913. They include Coburn's London and New York city views and portraits of H. G. Wells, George Bernard Shaw, G. K. Chesterton, Alfred Stieglitz, and Max Weber. The Library of Congress records identify the items as photographs or photographic prints, and the local files are its digital reproductions of those objects. Several reproductions visibly retain a print border, mount, or color target; those features describe the institutional digitization as well as the original work.

The sources consulted for this chapter do not establish the print process for every individual image. *Vortograph* is discussed in `sources.md` but is not included: no qualifying open, reusable high-resolution rendition was verified for this build.

## Camera and settings

No chapter source establishes a camera body, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, or exposure for these frames. The ingest manifest contains no historical capture EXIF. Specific settings are not given.
