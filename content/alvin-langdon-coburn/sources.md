# Alvin Langdon Coburn — sources

Coburn's early city and bridge photographs compress London and New York into graphic silhouettes, steep viewpoints, and strong perspectival corridors; his portraits bring the same pictorial discipline to face, gesture, and dark ground. The shortlist deliberately ends with the 1917 *Vortograph*: the decisive break from those observed scenes into camera-made abstraction.

**Rights basis:** The eleven downloaded files are highest-resolution TIFFs from the Library of Congress Prints & Photographs Division. They are pre-1929 works by Coburn and are public domain in the United States; LOC supplies the catalog records and unrestricted master files. The title-level record and the direct TIFF URL are retained below for provenance.

## Canonical shortlist

### 01 — *A Corner of 25 Mortimer Street* (June 1900)
- **Why it matters:** The digitized mounted print contains an interior with a seated figure, wall pictures, and a fireplace; its nested frames make the reproduction's physical border distinct from Coburn's composed room.
- **Source:** [LOC record](https://www.loc.gov/item/96512197/) · [master TIFF](https://cdn.loc.gov/master/pnp/cph/3g00000/3g04000/3g04600/3g04676u.tif)
- **Rights:** Public domain in the US (published 1900).
- **File:** `raw/alvin-langdon-coburn/01-corner-of-25-mortimer-street.jpg` (1492px long edge)

### 02 — *H. G. Wells* (1905; published 1913)
- **Why it matters:** Wells's hand, angled face, and the surrounding dark mass turn a literary portrait into a compressed triangular study.
- **Source:** [LOC record](https://www.loc.gov/item/97515265/) · [master TIFF](https://cdn.loc.gov/master/pnp/cph/3c10000/3c19000/3c19800/3c19873u.tif)
- **Rights:** Public domain in the US (the 1913 *Men of Mark* plate).
- **File:** `raw/alvin-langdon-coburn/02-h-g-wells.jpg` (4096px long edge)

### 03 — *Portrait of George Bernard Shaw* (1905)
- **Why it matters:** Shaw's close head and beard move diagonally through a deep dark field, making the portrait's tonal mass and tilt do the structural work.
- **Source:** [LOC record](https://www.loc.gov/item/2004674432/) · [master TIFF](https://cdn.loc.gov/master/pnp/cph/3a10000/3a10000/3a10500/3a10537u.tif)
- **Rights:** Public domain in the US (published 1905).
- **File:** `raw/alvin-langdon-coburn/03-portrait-of-george-bernard-shaw.jpg` (5592px long edge)

### 04 — *Portrait of Gilbert Keith Chesterton* (1904; published 1905)
- **Why it matters:** The large, close portrait lets Chesterton's pale face and coat emerge from a dark tonal field—an economical example of figure/ground control.
- **Source:** [LOC record](https://www.loc.gov/item/2004674426/) · [master TIFF](https://cdn.loc.gov/master/pnp/cph/3b00000/3b00000/3b00400/3b00436u.tif)
- **Rights:** Public domain in the US (published 1905).
- **File:** `raw/alvin-langdon-coburn/04-portrait-of-g-k-chesterton.jpg` (1536px long edge)

### 05 — *St. Paul's Cathedral* (1905)
- **Why it matters:** Coburn renders the dome as a pale central mass, its monumental curve countered by dark, framing riverside structures.
- **Source:** [LOC record](https://www.loc.gov/item/2004674429/) · [master TIFF](https://cdn.loc.gov/master/pnp/cph/3a40000/3a41000/3a41900/3a41982u.tif)
- **Rights:** Public domain in the US (published 1905).
- **File:** `raw/alvin-langdon-coburn/05-st-pauls-cathedral.jpg` (1536px long edge)

### 06 — *The Thames* (1905)
- **Why it matters:** Smoke, water, and low industrial silhouettes are reduced to layered tonal bands, an early template for Coburn's atmospheric urban abstraction.
- **Source:** [LOC record](https://www.loc.gov/item/2004674428/) · [master TIFF](https://cdn.loc.gov/master/pnp/cph/3b10000/3b11000/3b11400/3b11415u.tif)
- **Rights:** Public domain in the US (published 1905).
- **File:** `raw/alvin-langdon-coburn/06-the-thames.jpg` (5538px long edge)

### 07 — *Weir's Close, Edinburgh* (1906)
- **Why it matters:** The narrow lane forms a dense perspective funnel, with the bright opening at its end pulling the eye through a nearly black frame.
- **Source:** [LOC record](https://www.loc.gov/item/2003675020/) · [master TIFF](https://cdn.loc.gov/master/pnp/cph/3b40000/3b42000/3b42500/3b42544u.tif)
- **Rights:** Public domain in the US (published 1906).
- **File:** `raw/alvin-langdon-coburn/07-weirs-close-edinburgh.jpg` (1536px long edge)

### 08 — *The Bridge, Venice* (1908)
- **Why it matters:** Bridge arch, canal edge, and suspended reflection make a compact chain of curves and diagonals rather than a conventional travel view.
- **Source:** [LOC record](https://www.loc.gov/item/89712351/) · [master TIFF](https://cdn.loc.gov/master/pnp/cph/3b40000/3b43000/3b43600/3b43620u.tif)
- **Rights:** Public domain in the US (published 1908).
- **File:** `raw/alvin-langdon-coburn/08-the-bridge-venice.jpg` (1536px long edge)

### 09 — *Spider-Webs* (1908)
- **Why it matters:** Boat rigging and its reflected verticals make a loose lattice across the water. The documented title *Spider-Webs* aptly suggests that analogy without changing the visible subject.
- **Source:** [LOC record](https://www.loc.gov/item/89712352/) · [master TIFF](https://cdn.loc.gov/master/pnp/cph/3b40000/3b43000/3b43600/3b43621u.tif)
- **Rights:** Public domain in the US (published 1908).
- **File:** `raw/alvin-langdon-coburn/09-spider-webs.jpg` (1536px long edge)

### 10 — *Williamsburg Bridge* (1911)
- **Why it matters:** The bridge's cables and trusses turn the view into a hard diagonal web, balancing industrial structure against the city haze beyond.
- **Source:** [LOC record](https://www.loc.gov/item/90715092/) · [master TIFF](https://cdn.loc.gov/master/pnp/cph/3c00000/3c01000/3c01700/3c01751u.tif)
- **Rights:** Public domain in the US (published 1911).
- **File:** `raw/alvin-langdon-coburn/10-williamsburg-bridge.jpg` (4096px long edge)

### 11 — *Alfred Stieglitz* (1908)
- **Why it matters:** Coburn frames his fellow Photo-Secessionist with a broad dark surround and a small illuminated face, making tonal hierarchy—not background detail—the portrait's subject.
- **Source:** [LOC record](https://www.loc.gov/item/2002715574/) · [master TIFF](https://cdn.loc.gov/master/pnp/cph/3b30000/3b33000/3b33300/3b33385u.tif)
- **Rights:** Public domain in the US (published 1908).
- **File:** `raw/alvin-langdon-coburn/11-alfred-stieglitz.jpg` (1536px long edge)

### 12 — *Vortograph* (1917)
- **Why it matters:** Coburn's kaleidoscopic mirror attachment fractures an observed scene into crystalline planes—the canonical early abstract photograph and the chapter's crucial formal pivot.
- **Best source:** [Art Institute of Chicago object 1987.379](https://www.artic.edu/artworks/70745/vortograph) (also documented by [MoMA](https://www.moma.org/collection/works/83725)).
- **Rights:** The Art Institute record does not mark this 1917 print as public domain; no qualifying open, downloadable rendition was verified. It is not in the raw set; see `NEEDED.md`.
