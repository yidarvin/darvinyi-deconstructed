# Minor White — source shortlist

This eleven-work set follows White from his early Portland documentary photographs through the San Francisco work and the later, more abstract pictures that develop his idea of photographic equivalence. Every file is a complete-frame 1600px-long-edge JPEG served publicly from the Princeton University Art Museum's IIIF image service, retrieved from the linked object record and verified as opening.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. Princeton’s object API marks each image “Restricted Download - Under Copyright” and gives the reproduction credit “© Trustees of Princeton University.” White’s archive is held by the Princeton University Art Museum; the archive page identifies it as the principal collection of White’s photographs, proof cards, negatives, and related material. The page does not name a different rightsholder for these particular digital reproductions, so the credited notice is retained without conjecture. Each use below is individually limited to nonprofit, transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching; needs the complete photograph to make the stated formal claim; uses a web-sized credited copy; and links to the collection record rather than replacing the record, original print, or licensed reproduction.

## Research context

Princeton describes the Minor White Archive as the artist’s most significant primary-source collection and notes that its proof cards are a key guide to his creative process: https://artmuseum.princeton.edu/art/collections/themes/minor-white-archive. The Museum’s discussion of *The Sound of One Hand Clapping* identifies equivalence as White’s search for formal relationships in nature that reflect inner experience and describes a sequence as a “cinema of stills”: https://www.metmuseum.org/art/collection/search/267297. The selected works give a builder a usable arc from documentary street structure through landscape and the later window/wall images, where equivalence is most directly teachable through the complete frame.
