# Lisette Model — research

Scope: technique, dates, printing, and camera information for the six Art Institute of Chicago collection derivatives documented in `sources.md`. This note keeps catalog facts separate from the visual evidence of the normalized JPEGs.

## Era and selection

The source records date *Man with Newspaper, Paris* to 1938, the three selected *Running Legs* views to 1940 or 1940–41, *We Mourn Our Loss* to 1945, and *Circus, The Wallendas, New York City* to 1950. They therefore give this chapter a compact view across Model's prewar Paris work and her New York photographs of the 1940s and 1950. The chapter does not treat the six works as a complete survey of her practice.

## Technique and printing

All six supplied files are monochrome, museum-delivered JPEG renditions. The records linked in `sources.md` establish their titles, dates, and collecting institution, but the source packet does not provide a consistent per-work account of negative format, film stock, paper, developer, enlarger, or original print date. The chapter accordingly describes the tonal range and visible framing of these present derivatives, not an inferred historical darkroom process.

## Camera settings

No supplied collection record documents a camera body, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, filter, or exposure for these individual photographs. `manifest.json` contains no original-camera EXIF. The low angle in the *Running Legs* photographs, the close framing of *Man with Newspaper*, and the long view in *Circus* are visible choices, but they are not evidence for numerical camera settings. No setting is invented here.

## Rights and source route

The selection is used under the per-image fair-use rationales recorded in `sources.md`. The listed rights holder is © The Lisette Model Foundation, Inc.; every plate is credited and linked to its Art Institute of Chicago collection record. The files are web-sized analytical references, not claims to replace a collection record, original print, or licensed reproduction.
