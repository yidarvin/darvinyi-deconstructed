# Lisette Model — source record

## Selection and acquisition note

Lisette Model's photographs remain under copyright; the Metropolitan Museum of
Art identifies the rights holder on its Model records as **© The Lisette Model
Foundation, Inc.** The Art Institute of Chicago makes the six collection
renditions below publicly reachable through its collection pages and IIIF image
service. Each downloaded direct rendition opened successfully and measures at
least 843px on its long edge (the public service's delivered size); none has
been upscaled or altered.

All six uses below are **fair-use** assessments made separately for this
criticism and composition-teaching book. Each complete frame is needed to
explain the stated compositional device, rather than to decorate the chapter;
the copy is a web-sized reference image. The chapter credits and links back to
the collecting museum, so it does not replace the object record, original
print, or a licensed reproduction. This is transformative scholarship and
criticism, uses only the frame required for analysis, and has no substitute
effect on the market for Model Foundation or museum image licensing.

## Acquired shortlist

1. **Running Legs** (1940–41) — `raw/lisette-model/01-running-legs-1940-41.jpg`
   - **Why it matters:** The ground-level crop turns pedestrian movement into a
     compressed pattern of diagonals, showing how an assertive edge can make
     motion feel immediate.
   - **Collection/source:** [Art Institute of Chicago, 100939](https://www.artic.edu/artworks/100939/running-legs); direct public rendition: `https://www.artic.edu/iiif/2/6863125b-eff1-5912-29cc-2e4056212309/full/full/0/default.jpg`.
   - **Use basis:** **fair-use**. Creator: Lisette Model; rightsholder notice
     where identified: © The Lisette Model Foundation, Inc. Complete frame is
     essential to teach the low viewpoint, crowding, and diagonal rhythm; this
     web-sized analytical copy is credited and linked, not a replacement for a
     museum or licensed image.

2. **We Mourn Our Loss** (1945) — `raw/lisette-model/02-we-mourn-our-loss-1945.jpg`
   - **Why it matters:** The tall, hard-edged frame converts a public sign and
     grouped figures into a severe vertical stack, useful for reading emotional
     pressure through scale and tonal separation.
   - **Collection/source:** [Art Institute of Chicago, 66076](https://www.artic.edu/artworks/66076/we-mourn-our-loss); direct public rendition: `https://www.artic.edu/iiif/2/3919cb94-83c0-69f8-f972-80642e969a53/full/full/0/default.jpg`.
   - **Use basis:** **fair-use**. Creator: Lisette Model; rightsholder notice
     where identified: © The Lisette Model Foundation, Inc. The complete frame
     is necessary for criticism of the sign/figure hierarchy; the credited,
     linked web copy supports transformative teaching and does not substitute
     for the collection record or licensed print.

3. **Running Legs, New York Public Library** (1940) — `raw/lisette-model/03-running-legs-nypl-1940.jpg`
   - **Why it matters:** Another radical low crop makes an architectural
     threshold and crossing legs compete for the frame, clarifying Model's use
     of occlusion and off-center momentum.
   - **Collection/source:** [Art Institute of Chicago, 125770](https://www.artic.edu/artworks/125770/running-legs-new-york-public-library); direct public rendition: `https://www.artic.edu/iiif/2/3ccbdd2d-9995-d987-abf8-eb8119c25bd1/full/full/0/default.jpg`.
   - **Use basis:** **fair-use**. Creator: Lisette Model; rightsholder notice
     where identified: © The Lisette Model Foundation, Inc. Full-frame access
     is required to analyze the cut-off bodies and threshold geometry; the
     small credited teaching reproduction is transformative and non-substitutive.

4. **Circus, The Wallendas, New York City** (1950) — `raw/lisette-model/04-circus-wallendas-1950.jpg`
   - **Why it matters:** Its near-vertical framing organizes performers,
     apparatus, and dark voids into a precarious ladder, a strong case for
     rhythm built from repeated shapes.
   - **Collection/source:** [Art Institute of Chicago, 125769](https://www.artic.edu/artworks/125769/circus-the-wallendas-new-york-city); direct public rendition: `https://www.artic.edu/iiif/2/8994dc24-f127-ebab-5fd4-49ad87a058d5/full/full/0/default.jpg`.
   - **Use basis:** **fair-use**. Creator: Lisette Model; rightsholder notice
     where identified: © The Lisette Model Foundation, Inc. The complete
     vertical is needed to teach the precarious figure-to-space sequence; this
     linked, web-sized critique is transformative and cannot replace a licensed
     reproduction.

5. **Running Legs** (1940/41) — `raw/lisette-model/05-running-legs-1940-41.jpg`
   - **Why it matters:** A distinct print/crop of the walking series makes the

## Additional canonical works checked

other key works—*Woman at Coney Island*, *Reflections, New York*, *Lower East
Side*, *Singer, Café Metropole*, and the *Promenade des Anglais* photographs—
but their records state that media are not available for download or direct the
reader to image-licensing channels. Because the six direct, public museum
renditions above already exceed this chapter's four-image readiness threshold,
this pass retains that coherent source set rather than bypassing any access
restriction. Their catalogue records remain useful research leads for the
builder: [NGA artist works](https://www.nga.gov/artists/6774-lisette-model/artworks),
