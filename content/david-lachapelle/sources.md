# David LaChapelle — source shortlist

This twelve-image working set follows LaChapelle from the pop-religious tableaux of *Jesus Is My Homeboy* through the baroque still lifes and model-built petro-landscapes of the later work. Every file was retrieved as a publicly served, non-authenticated JPEG from the artist's official website and opens as a complete web-display image. The official site credits the works to David LaChapelle but supplies no separate rightsholder notice or open licence; therefore every entry below is used on a carefully limited **fair-use** basis, not as open content.

Each assessment is specific to the image. **Factor 1:** this nonprofit book uses the credited copy transformatively for criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching. **Factor 2:** each is a highly creative staged photograph. **Factor 3:** the complete published frame is needed to analyze the work's visual organization. **Factor 4:** the linked web-display JPEG is modest in scale, credits and links to the artist's record, and does not replace an original, a gallery sale, or a licensed reproduction.

## Research context

LaChapelle's [official biography](https://www.davidlachapelle.com/davidlachapelle-about) describes staged tableaux, portraits, and still lifes developed through his distinctive color and imaginative narratives. The official [*Land Scape* exhibition record](https://www.davidlachapelle.com/exhibition-paul-kasmin-2014) confirms that the Gas Stations and Refineries were handcrafted scale models made from recycled materials, with the former photographed in Maui rainforest. The official [*Jesus Is My Homeboy* series record](https://www.davidlachapelle.com/series-jesus-is-my-homeboy) and [*Earth Laughs in Flowers* series record](https://www.davidlachapelle.com/series-earth-laughs-in-flowers) supply the titles and dates for the selected series works.
