# David LaChapelle — research notes

## Scope and sources

This chapter is built from the twelve artist-site display files documented individually in [sources.md](sources.md): five works from *Jesus Is My Homeboy* (2003), *Deluge* (2006), three works in *Earth Laughs in Flowers* (2008–2011), and three 2013 *Land Scape* works plus *Gas Shell* (2012). The artist's official biography describes a practice of staged tableaux, portraits, and still lifes developed through distinctive color and imaginative narrative. The official *Land Scape* exhibition record specifically states that the Gas Stations and Refineries are handcrafted scale models made from recycled materials; it identifies *Gas Shell* as photographed in Maui rainforest.

## Method and visual language

The supplied source record supports discussion of deliberately staged, highly saturated scenes, not an inference that these works are unmediated documentary moments. The 2003 pictures restage Christian iconography in contemporary urban and domestic spaces; their wide groups, direct gestures, fluorescent ceilings, street signs, and emphatic props operate as constructed tableaux. The later flower pictures use still-life accumulation and vanitas-like collision: blossoms coexist with consumer packaging, food, mannequins, and body fragments. The *Land Scape* works turn recycled miniature infrastructure into landscape-scale scenes, so their relation of broad sky or water to dense construction is part of their material premise rather than merely an optical trick.

## Technique, printing, and settings

The official records in this source set do not establish a print process, edition, paper, lighting plan, post-production sequence, camera body, lens, focal length, exposure, aperture, or ISO for every selected work. It would be inappropriate to generalize technical specifications across the series.

The ingested files preserve a small amount of embedded metadata. It records *Deluge* as a Phase One P 45 file dated 2006-11-09, 50 mm, 1/8 s, f/5.6, ISO 100; *Late Summer* as a Phase One P 30+ file dated 2008-11-26, 35 mm, 3 s, f/32, ISO 200; and *Deathless Winter* and *The Lovers* as Phase One P 40+ files dated 2009, 28 mm, about 15 s, f/14 and f/22 respectively, ISO 50. This is file-level EXIF, not an independently published technical record, so it is reported as embedded metadata rather than proof of every capture condition. The four other *Jesus Is My Homeboy* files identify a Heidelberg Prepress Scanner DC3000 rather than a camera, and the four model-landscape files contain no camera EXIF.

## Evidence limits

Titles, dates or date ranges, series membership, artist credit, the public display source, and the *Land Scape* model construction claim are documented in [sources.md](sources.md). Historical claims beyond those records—such as a particular printing method, exact degree of digital alteration, or studio lighting setup—are not made in the chapter.
