# Bernd & Hilla Becher — research note

## Scope and method

The supplied collection records identify Bernd and Hilla Becher's industrial
typologies as serial photographs made from 1963 to 1997. Their method in this
selection is comparison: closely matched, full-frame views let changes in
silhouette, bracing, pipes, openings, supports, contour, and perspective become
legible across a group rather than elevating one industrial site as a scenic
exception. The selected groups range from *Coal Mine Tipples, Pennsylvania*
(1974–78) and *Coal Tipple, Goodspring, Pa.* (1975) to *Pitheads—Perspective
Views* (1981) and *Blast Furnaces, Heads, Frontal Views* (1985).

## Materials and technical record

The ten supplied files are publicly displayed museum collection reproductions:
three Art Institute of Chicago IIIF display renditions and seven SFMOMA
collection-display renditions. They are copyright works of the Estate Bernd &
Hilla Becher and are documented in [the source record](sources.md) as
image-specific fair use for criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching.
The ingested files are monochrome display JPEGs and carry no camera EXIF.

The supplied records do **not** identify a camera body, lens, negative format,
film stock, aperture, shutter speed, lighting setup, development process,
printing process, paper, print dimensions, or edition for these individual
works. Those facts are therefore not inferred from the images. This chapter
discusses visible framing and the displayed typological arrangements only.

## Displayed groups

The sequence includes fifteen perspective pitheads, twelve frontal blast-furnace
heads, a four-view coal tipple, thirty water towers, nine winding towers, and
five nine-image groups: telescoping gas holders, spherical gas holders,
cylindrical gas holders, cooling towers, and coal-mine tipples. The supplied
files skip a filename number after `04`; this is a file-label convention, not a
missing work: `06-winding-towers-belgium-france.jpg` is the fifth source-record
entry.
