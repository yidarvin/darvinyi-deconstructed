# Saul Leiter — source shortlist

This set draws from the Saul Leiter Foundation's own public [Color gallery](https://www.saulleiterfoundation.org/color). The gallery identifies each work by title and date and serves its display JPEG directly from the Foundation's public Squarespace CDN; no login, paywall, technical restriction, or screen capture was involved. The twelve downloaded files were opened with `sips` and range from 776 to 2277px on their long edge. They are deliberately web-display copies, not print-production files.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. The photographer's work is copyrighted; the public gallery is an authoritative display route, not an open licence. Credit: Saul Leiter / Saul Leiter Foundation (the Foundation's gallery is the source; related Foundation and gallery materials identify the rights as © Saul Leiter Foundation / Estate of Saul Leiter). Each assessment below applies the four factors: this nonprofit book uses the complete web-sized image for transformative criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching; the underlying work is creative; the complete frame is needed to analyze its spatial construction; and the credited, linked display copy does not replace the Foundation record, an original print, or a licensed reproduction.

## Research context

The Foundation's public gallery records a coherent early-color vocabulary—windows, reflections, weather, partial figures, and occlusion—rather than treating Leiter's famous red as a standalone motif. The National Gallery of Art's [Saul Leiter artist page](https://www.nga.gov/artists/30157-saul-leiter) independently catalogs *Snow* (1960), *Through Boards* (1957), and early black-and-white work, corroborating the centrality of the selected color period and the latter title. The selected twelve display the chapter's argument across obscuring surfaces, nested frames, color fields, and the deliberately incomplete street view.
