# Beate Gütschow — research note

## What the supplied sources establish

The documented selection spans the *LS* series (1999–2003), the *S* series
(2004–2008), and the later *HC* series (2018–2020). The source notes cite the
Metropolitan Museum of Art’s record for *LS #3*, which describes an LS work made
from up to one hundred photographic images and invoking seventeenth- and
eighteenth-century landscape painting. The cited Museum of Contemporary
Photography essay describes the LS series’ foreground/middle/background structure
and the S series’ rearrangement of international architectural fragments. A Saint
Louis Art Museum record for *LS #17* similarly documents construction from dozens
of negatives. These are historical statements about the series’ montage method;
the descriptions of individual plates here remain visual readings. [Source
notes](sources.md)

## Technical limits for this chapter

The gallery pages document author, titles, dates, copyright credit, and public
display images, but do not establish camera body, lens, focal length, aperture,
shutter speed, sensitivity, film or sensor format, or an edition-specific printing
process for these works. The ingested display JPEGs retain no camera EXIF. This
chapter therefore does not infer a capture workflow or print material from their
appearance. It discusses documented composite construction, visible geometry, and
the analyzer’s tonal and palette measurements instead.
