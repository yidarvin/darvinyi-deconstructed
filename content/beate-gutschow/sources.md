# Beate Gütschow — source shortlist

Beate Gütschow constructs photographs from many separately made fragments, testing the inherited visual languages of ideal landscape, postwar architectural photography, and digital collage. The twelve full-frame display JPEGs below were downloaded from the publicly accessible [Produzentengalerie Hamburg artist archive](https://www.produzentengalerie.com/artists/beate-guetschow/). Its work listings identify the author, titles/dates, and the copyright credit © Beate Gütschow / VG Bildkunst; no open licence is stated.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for each selected work. Each assessment is individual but follows the same four-factor analysis: (1) this nonprofit book uses the image transformatively for criticism, scholarship, and composition teaching; (2) each work is a highly creative constructed photograph; (3) the complete, uncropped frame is needed to teach the particular arrangement of landscape, architecture, scale, or montage; and (4) this is a credited, linked, web-sized display copy that does not replace the gallery page, original edition, or a licensed reproduction. The gallery served each direct file publicly, without authentication or bypass of a technical control.

## Research context

The [Met’s record for *LS #3*](https://www.metmuseum.org/art/collection/search/302432) explains that LS combines up to one hundred photographic images to invoke seventeenth- and eighteenth-century landscape painting, and records the rightsholder as © Beate Gütschow, VG–Bildkunst Bonn. The [Museum of Contemporary Photography’s LS/S exhibition essay](https://www.mocp.org/exhibition/beate-gutschow-lss/) independently describes LS’s foreground/middle/background landscape structure and S’s rearrangement of international architectural fragments. The [Saint Louis Art Museum’s *LS #17* record](https://www.slam.org/collection/objects/56043/) further documents the series’ construction from dozens of negatives and identifies © Artists Rights Society, New York for that collection object.
