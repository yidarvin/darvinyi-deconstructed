Beate Gütschow’s composites are most persuasive when they behave less like a
collection of photographs than like a picture with a prior history. The documented
*LS* works borrow the foreground, middle distance, and horizon of ideal landscape;
the *S* and *HC* works carry that staged coherence into architectural space. The
overlays stay with visible structures: a ridge, a ramp, a wall cap, a stair, because
they are the means by which fragments become a single available-looking world.

## landscape after the ideal view

<Plate slug="beate-gutschow" id="01-ls-5-2000" label="LS #5, 2000" caption="A distant ridge locks an unusually wide sky to a shallow valley and a bright interruption at the right edge." />

In *LS #5*, the ridge at y0.442 is less a natural horizon than a hinge: above it,
almost white #f0eef4 sky; below it, dark #1a1e1e and green #394235 vegetation.
The image’s substantial tonal spread (p05 0.086, p95 0.969) lets the meadow feel
both lit and constructed. The shallow valley does not drive toward a dramatic
destination; it preserves the panorama’s promise of continuous, inhabitable depth.

<Plate slug="beate-gutschow" id="02-ls-6-1999" label="LS #6, 1999" caption="A fallen trunk crosses the lower field beneath a tilted hill crest, while a tree mass closes the image at right." />

The fallen trunk in *LS #6* supplies a more insistent entrance. Its curve moves
from the lower left into the grass, while the hill crest tilts gently through
y0.417. Pale #efefec sky, dark #21251e foliage, and olive #50563a hold a broad
range from p05 0.090 to p95 0.980. That generous range makes the landscape
formula credible even as the two trees read as deliberately placed brackets.

<Plate slug="beate-gutschow" id="03-ls-10-2001" label="LS #10, 2001" caption="A pale water edge crosses the low field and makes the tall right-hand tree answer a broad expanse of sky." />

*LS #10* makes emptiness an active mass. The water edge and low shoreline at
y0.744 leave nearly three quarters of the frame to sky, while the right-hand tree
stops that expanse from dissolving. Its mean luminance is 0.717, the highest among
these early landscapes; pale blue #e9f8fd and #cde7f5 press against dark green
#32352a and olive #595d42. The visual calm is therefore carefully weighted, not
merely scenic.

<Plate slug="beate-gutschow" id="04-ls-11-2001" label="LS #11, 2001" caption="A portrait field of empty sky is countered by an embankment, a covered object, and an isolated tree at right." />

The vertical *LS #11* compresses the landscape formula. A distant tree line at
y0.629 makes the sky a large, pale register, while the embankment rises into the
small covered object at lower right. The relatively low edge density, 0.008, and
the light palette (#eaf1f0, #d4c0be, #a39578) makes the interruptions unusually
legible. Gütschow lets the frame resemble an old landscape before allowing its
awkwardly specific pieces to disturb that expectation.

<Plate slug="beate-gutschow" id="05-ls-18-2003" label="LS #18, 2003" caption="A left bank leads into a tree-bound clearing, where distant woods and a dark right edge keep water from becoming a neutral view." />

*LS #18* uses trees as an internal frame rather than a picturesque accessory. The
left bank descends toward the pond while the woods form a level band at y0.649.
Dark #262318, white #f3f3f3, and brown #574c31 span p05 0.082 to p95 0.980, a
range that makes the clearing read in sharply separated light zones. The route is
open, but the enclosing trunks prevent it from becoming a simple vista.

## constructed cities

<Plate slug="beate-gutschow" id="06-s-1-2004" label="S #1, 2004" caption="A concrete bay, low break, and machinery boom turn an urban fragment into an imposing stage." />

In *S #1*, the machinery boom begins near the lower-left cluster and travels across
the foreground, while the concrete bay blocks an easy way into the dark
right side. The grayscale palette moves from #161616 and #363636 to #e1e1e1;
mean luminance is only 0.364. Rather than offering a stable architectural view,
the tonal split turns different building fragments into competing planes.

<Plate slug="beate-gutschow" id="07-s-2-2005" label="S #2, 2005" caption="A stair/ramp and a rectangular opening arrange a small figure group inside an otherwise severe architectural field." />

The white ramp in *S #2* gives the eye a clear diagonal, but the broad opening
above it is too shallow to become a conventional passage. The small group beneath
the ramp establishes scale against a smooth gray range: edge density is only 0.020,
with #212121, #616161, #949494, and #d8d8d8 carrying the changes in depth. The
image feels both photographed and diagrammed because its strongest elements are
large, simple planes.

<Plate slug="beate-gutschow" id="08-s-24-2007" label="S #24 (M), 2007" caption="A cylindrical tank, its exposed stair, and a distant city band convert one building into an isolated vertical field." />

*S #24 (M)* places the building against an almost featureless sky, making the
cylindrical tank and right-hand platform behave as cut-out components. The exposed
stair breaks the vertical mass without offering a full route through it. The
analyzer finds the chapter’s smallest tonal spread here (p05 0.388, p95 0.808) and
edge density 0.013; #303030 through #c8c8c8 keep the scene nearly monochrome. Its
coherence is therefore a matter of silhouette, not atmospheric detail.

<Plate slug="beate-gutschow" id="09-s-30-2008" label="S #30, 2008" caption="A waterline and a foreground route hold a civic complex in place while displaced towers undo its apparent order." />

The waterline at y0.609 in *S #30* makes the frontal complex look momentarily
anchored, yet the foreground route curls toward an entrance that is not quite the
frame’s center. The gray palette (#333333, #5f5f5f, #8b8b8b, #d6d6d6) keeps the
view industrial and flat, while edge density 0.041 gives the rubble enough detail
to resist a clean civic axis. It is a panoramic landscape logic translated into a
more alien public space.

## later fragments, shallow ground

<Plate slug="beate-gutschow" id="10-hc-1-2018" label="HC #1, 2018" caption="A circular fountain and dark ramp organize people and graffiti inside a shallow, sunken enclosure." />

The overhead position in *HC #1* denies the distant horizon that structured the
LS works. Instead, the fountain at x0.586, y0.697 and a dark ramp bind the scene
to a sunken enclosure. Its restricted palette of #3d3e25, #555536, #6a654a, and
#a7a7a7 and low standard deviation (0.121) make the separate fragments share a
muted field, even while the people keep the construction from becoming abstract.

<Plate slug="beate-gutschow" id="11-hc-4-2018" label="HC #4, 2018" caption="A level wall cap and a planter-edge zigzag order a courtyard whose trees interrupt every stable route." />

*HC #4* is built from flat registers: sky, wall, gravel, planting, and paving. The
wall cap at y0.304 is the image’s single reliable horizontal; the planter edges
break the lower field into turns. Midtone #9d9785 and #b5b2aa meet green #484b2c,
with a modest edge density of 0.051. The result is calm at first glance, then
strange because every route has to negotiate an abrupt boundary or tree.

<Plate slug="beate-gutschow" id="12-hc-12-2020" label="HC #12, 2020" caption="A large arch, a diagonal ramp wall, and a left-hand shrub keep an offset courtyard from resolving into symmetry." />

In *HC #12*, the arch is a frame but not an invitation to a deep space; a ramp
wall cuts across it and the lone figure holds a separate lower zone. Dark #2b2c29
walls, pale #bebcbb paving, and gray #8b8c7f create a compressed tonal scene with
mean luminance 0.497. The horizontal break at y0.295 and the diagonal make
construction visible as a sequence of fitted surfaces rather than a seamless city.

## Exercises

<ExerciseCard n="01" title="Make a landscape in three registers">
Photograph a foreground, middle distance, and horizon in separate views. Assemble
only enough of each to create a plausible route through the frame, then keep one
edge or tonal shift that lets the joins remain perceptible.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="02" title="Let a ramp interrupt a building">
Find a stair, ramp, shadow edge, or planter boundary that crosses an architectural
view. Frame it so it directs the eye but does not settle at a conventional
vanishing point; compare the version with and without the crossing structure.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="03" title="Use a shallow field as a collage test">
Photograph a courtyard or park from above. Keep one circular, rectangular, or
arched form and one figure-scale detail, then arrange the frame so that several
separate surfaces have to read as one plausible place.
</ExerciseCard>
