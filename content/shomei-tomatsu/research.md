# Shomei Tomatsu — research note

## Scope of the supplied records

The twelve source records are Art Institute of Chicago collection records for photographs by Shomei Tomatsu. They establish a compact postwar sequence made in Nagoya, Nagasaki, Tokyo, Shinjuku, Kawasaki, Iwakuni, and Yokosuka between 1959 and 1969. Several records also distinguish the negative date from a later print date: for example, *Untitled (Nagoya)* was made in 1959 and printed in 1966; *Tsuyo Kataoka, Motohara Town, Nagasaki* was made in 1961 and printed in 1974; and the Tokyo, Shinjuku, and Nagasaki records include prints from the 1970s, 1980, 2003, or 2005. The chapter preserves those distinctions rather than treating a museum print date as the date of exposure.

## Technique and settings

The supplied records identify these objects as photographs and give titles, dates, collection credits, and public display images. They do not document camera body, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, film stock, negative format, filtration, development, lighting setup, or original printing process for this selection. The ingested display JPEGs also contain no capture settings that can responsibly be used as historical evidence. Consequently, the chapter discusses only visible framing, blur, grain, tonal range, color, reflection, and surface, and does not infer technical settings from the scans.

## What the sequence supports

Across the source shortlist, Tomatsu's photographs move between aftermath, close portraiture, reflective surfaces, street encounters, signs, and dense technological or urban fields. That is a curatorial observation grounded in the supplied works and locations, not a claim about a single camera method or uniform print process. Individual title, date, credit, source route, and fair-use rationale remain in the [source shortlist](sources.md).
