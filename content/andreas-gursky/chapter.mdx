Andreas Gursky makes information legible at a scale where it threatens to become
abstract. In these photographs, a supermarket, trading floor, stadium, factory,
concert, river, and transit station are not simply places. They are systems of
repetition, lateral bands, crowd density, and controlled interruptions. The source
record documents a practice of digital refinement, and The Broad describes an
ordered, indexical approach in which Gursky adjusts photographic structure; its
account of *99 Cent* specifically identifies rearranged aisles and a mirrored roof.
The overlays therefore name visible structure, rather than treating every detected
line as a literal horizon. [Source notes](../../content/andreas-gursky/sources.md)

## fields that overpower the individual

<Plate slug="andreas-gursky" id="01-99-cent" label="99 Cent, 1999" caption="A lower shelf band and near-central aisle balance hold a dense retail field whose many price labels and packages resist being read one at a time." />

In *99 Cent*, the lower shelf band at y0.854 and the aisle balance at x0.463 keep
the picture from dissolving into product noise. The small anchor on the left names a
dense price field, not a single protagonist: the visual proposition is that the
whole store can be scanned as one frame. The range from p05 0.180 to p95 0.890 and
edge density 0.209 support its high-information surface. Pale #d0d1cf and
#9ba3a4 are repeatedly interrupted by blue-gray #556c78 and saturated packaging.
The mirrored ceiling makes the shelf logic rebound upward, so abundance reads as a
constructed totality rather than an ordinary aisle.

<Plate slug="andreas-gursky" id="02-chicago-board-of-trade-i" label="Chicago Board of Trade I, 1997" caption="An upper trading rail and a curving floor edge hold the floor's crowd as a large, layered event rather than a collection of separate transactions." />

*Chicago Board of Trade I* starts with a dark register of electronic boards and
then drops into human density. The upper trading rail at y0.292 separates those
two registers, while the curving floor edge runs into the lower crowd. A detected
anchor at the right names one local trader cluster, but its purpose is to show how
little one person matters against the field. Mean luminance is 0.219, with p05
0.020, p95 0.882, and #110b0f, #472d2d, and #7b635c carrying the darkness and
warm flashes. The high contrast turns activity into an all-over pattern.

<Plate slug="andreas-gursky" id="03-hong-kong-stock-exchange-ii" label="Hong Kong Stock Exchange II, 1995" caption="An upper display band, a central exchange axis, and the desk field below turn a working room into a sharply organized information surface." />

In *Hong Kong Stock Exchange II*, the upper display band slopes slightly at
y0.182, then the exchange center at x0.525 fixes the suspended board, podium, and
opening in the floor. The tightly repeated desks occupy both sides without losing
the central gap. Its p05–p95 span of 0.012–0.859 and moderate edge density 0.124
keep dark #13080b, pale #d6cbbd, and tan #ad9a7a legible as separate strata. Order
here is not calm; it is what lets a large operational system remain readable.

<Plate slug="andreas-gursky" id="04-greeley" label="Greeley, 2002" caption="A feedlot horizon, repeated pen field, and a foreground herd make the cattle operation visible as an industrial expanse." />

*Greeley* works by refusing the sentimental scale of an animal picture. The
feedlot horizon at y0.312 holds a vast pen field below the open sky; fences divide
the near herd into repeating cells. The overlay's left foreground anchor makes one
group available only as an entry point into a much larger count. Mean luminance
0.478, p05 0.098, p95 0.886, and edge density 0.159 describe a scene where
blue-gray #c3d9e7 has to share the frame with brown #5f4933 and earth #846c53.

## systems arranged as pictures

<Plate slug="andreas-gursky" id="05-f1-boxenstopp-i" label="F1 Boxenstopp I, 2007" caption="A pit-wall band crosses the panorama while separate red and white pit-lane actions become parallel units inside a single strip." />

The extreme width of *F1 Boxenstopp I* makes sequencing its subject. The pit-wall
band at y0.625 carries the eye from one coordinated stop to the next, and the
right-side anchor identifies an action cluster without suggesting it is the whole
story. With mean luminance 0.101, p05 0.004, p95 0.357, and a palette led by
#040202, #1b1918, and #3c3232, the near-black garage turns the red and white teams
into clipped signals. Repetition gives speed a measured, almost diagrammatic form.

<Plate slug="andreas-gursky" id="06-em-arena-amsterdam-ii" label="EM, Arena, Amsterdam II, 2000" caption="The centre line and circle, aligned to the pitch’s vertical axis, turn play into small deviations across a striped green field." />

In *EM, Arena, Amsterdam II*, the vertical pitch axis at x0.500 holds the centre
circle, players, and both goal areas in register. The horizontal at y0.311 is the
halfway line through the circle; the lower detected line is a pitch marking, not a
stadium tier. Mown bands and uneven player positions keep the symmetry from
becoming a diagram. This is the smoothest plate in the set (edge density 0.014);
its narrow p05–p95 range of 0.278–0.639 and greens #799834, #8fad4c, and #5f7c23
turn play into an arrangement of marks on a luminous plane.

<Plate slug="andreas-gursky" id="07-bangkok-ii" label="Bangkok II, 2011" caption="An upper reflection band and two surface breaks cross a dark water field whose bright fragments gather around a measured convergence." />

*Bangkok II* almost withholds scene recognition. The overlay marks an upper
reflection band at y0.249 and two visible surface sweeps that meet near x0.412,
y0.436; these are routes through reflections and debris, not perspective in a
stable room. Mean luminance is only 0.159, p05 is 0.031, and #12080c and #1f1b18
surround small silver-gray #878379 events. The moderate edge density 0.128 lets
the river behave like a dark abstract field without becoming blank.

<Plate slug="andreas-gursky" id="08-pyongyang-iv" label="Pyongyang IV, 2007" caption="A centered globe and platform bridge the upper card mosaic and a dense lower field of performers." />

*Pyongyang IV* is organized around a central vertical axis: the globe and platform
near x0.49, y0.43 bridge an upper red card mosaic and mountain band with the much
larger ranks of performers below. The break at y0.438 is a stage-to-performer
threshold, not a perspective horizon; the repeated bodies become a human raster
while the centered globe holds the two fields together. Its p05–p95 span reaches
0.031–1.000; red #65151d, black #140a0c, and white #f9f6f6 produce the chapter's
strongest tonal range.

## labor, spectacle, and circulation

<Plate slug="andreas-gursky" id="09-amazon" label="Amazon, 2016" caption="An upper rack edge and warehouse balance keep the immense book-and-package field from becoming visually limitless." />

*Amazon* makes inventory look both monumental and precarious. The upper rack edge
at y0.189 gives the frame a firm shelf, while the loose axis at x0.625 and a bright
inventory cluster let the eye test the field for local order. Its edge density
0.288 is the highest in this selection, matching the relentless upright parcels.
Yet brown #7d6d5e, beige #9f9488, and light #bfbbb5 temper that density: this is a
system of storage presented as a color-and-edge distribution, not a readable list.

<Plate slug="andreas-gursky" id="10-nha-trang" label="Nha Trang, 2004" caption="Raking work-row and bamboo-rack diagonals cross a densely repeated factory floor, drawing attention to labor as a distributed pattern." />

The image of *Nha Trang* does not isolate one worker. Its workroom convergence
near x0.512, y0.445 and the raking rows turn individual gestures, chairs, and
bamboo material into an engineered density. The wide tonal range from p05 0.027 to
p95 0.859, edge density 0.271, and palette of #1d1211, #4c403a, and #7d6a60 keep
the orange shirts visible as a repeated counter-rhythm. The composition makes the
factory feel like an expansive, gridded labor system rather than a background for a
single anecdote.

<Plate slug="andreas-gursky" id="11-madonna-i" label="Madonna I, 2001" caption="A lit stage at left and the packed audience at right organize a concert image where spectacle is carried as much by audience mass as by the stage." />

In *Madonna I*, the lit stage holds the left edge while the audience becomes a
steep, glittering expanse at right. The overlay anchors the bright stage rather
than inventing a directional convergence in the crowd. Mean luminance 0.124 and
p05 0.008 leave the image largely dark, while edge density 0.257 lets #0a0507,
#2a2221, and #574d48 break into innumerable lights, heads, and gestures.

<Plate slug="andreas-gursky" id="12-sao-paulo-se" label="Sao Paulo, Sé, 2002" caption="A station axis, skylight opening, and upper concourse rail organize layered commuters and curved concrete levels into a symmetrical social field." />

*Sao Paulo, Sé* ends the sequence with a public interior built from repeating
curves. The station axis at x0.500 runs from the skylight opening through the
stacked concourse levels, while the upper rail at y0.421 introduces a decisive
horizontal interruption. The commuters gather in bands rather than a single
crowd. Its p05–p95 range of 0.008–0.792, edge density 0.112, and dark
#110a0b, brown #443725, and tan #7e6548 make the concrete's tonal weight as
important as the people it carries.

## Exercises

<ExerciseCard n="01" title="Give a large field one rule">
Photograph a place with many similar units: shelves, seats, windows, desks, or
people. Find one horizontal, axis, or edge that makes the field readable, then make
a second frame without it. Keep the image in which the viewer can traverse the
density without needing a singled-out subject.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="02" title="Make repetition carry scale">
Choose a repeated activity: packing, waiting, shelving, playing, or cleaning.
Stand far enough back that one action becomes a unit in a larger pattern. Include a
small interruption that lets the eye judge the scale of the whole system.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="03" title="Turn a crowd into a structure">
Photograph a performance, market, station, or sporting event from a position where
the crowd has visible bands, diagonals, or a central opening. Do not use a face as
the subject; use the route through the mass as the subject, and compare how the
image changes when the route disappears.
</ExerciseCard>
