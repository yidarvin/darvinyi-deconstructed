# Andreas Gursky — research note

## What the source record establishes

The chapter's twelve supplied collection renditions document works made from 1995
to 2016: *Hong Kong Stock Exchange II* (1995), *Chicago Board of Trade I*
(1997), *99 Cent* (1999), *EM, Arena, Amsterdam II* (2000), *Madonna I* (2001),
*Greeley* and *Sao Paulo, Sé* (2002), *Nha Trang* (2004), *F1 Boxenstopp I* and
*Pyongyang IV* (2007), *Bangkok II* (2011), and *Amazon* (2016). The Broad
identifies Gursky as a student of Bernd and Hilla Becher and describes an ordered,
indexical approach to scenes carrying enormous amounts of visual information. It
also states that he subtly enhances and adjusts photographic structure; its account
of *99 Cent* specifically names the altered store-aisle arrangement and added
mirrored roof. [The Broad, *99 Cent*](https://www.thebroad.org/art/andreas-gursky/99-cent)
and the chapter's [source record](sources.md) support those claims.

## Printing and technical limits

The Broad records work-specific media rather than one uniform process: *99 Cent*,
*Chicago Board of Trade I*, *Hong Kong Stock Exchange II*, *Greeley*, *F1
Boxenstopp I*, *EM, Arena, Amsterdam II*, *Nha Trang*, and *Sao Paulo, Sé* are
listed there as chromogenic prints (most mounted on Plexiglas); *Pyongyang IV* is
listed as a Cibachrome print; *Bangkok II* as an inkjet print; and *Amazon* as a
C-print. These are collection-record facts, not licenses to assign the same medium
to every work or edition. [The Broad, *99 Cent* collection page](https://www.thebroad.org/art/andreas-gursky/99-cent)
lists the related collection works and their media.

Neither the supplied records nor the ingested display JPEGs establish a camera
body, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, ISO/sensitivity, film or sensor
format, lighting plan, capture workflow, or edition-specific postproduction for
each work. The files have no EXIF. This chapter therefore discusses only documented
digital refinement where the collection explicitly identifies it, visible structure,
and the analyzer's measured tonal and palette evidence; it does not invent settings.
