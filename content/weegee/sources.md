# Weegee (Arthur Fellig) — source shortlist

Weegee's New York photographs make the flash a compositional instrument: it isolates a face, body, or piece of evidence from a dark city while leaving enough street, crowd, doorway, or threshold to make the social scene readable. This ten-image working set moves from crime and disaster to Bowery nightlife, Harlem, and spectacle. Every retained file is a public, unaltered web-sized IIIF delivery from an Art Institute of Chicago collection record, checked as an opening JPEG. The service supplies these derivatives at roughly 843px on the long edge—above the 640px floor, but deliberately too modest to substitute for a print or licensed reproduction.
