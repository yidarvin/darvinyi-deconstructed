# Joan Fontcuberta — research note

## What the supplied records establish

The supplied Art Institute of Chicago records identify the eleven selected works
as Joan Fontcuberta's works from 1984 to 1999. Together they span the botanical
and zoological fictions of *Herbarium* and *Fauna*, photogram-based images, a
painted photographic body, and later all-over fields. Banco de España identifies
*Herbarium* (1982), *Fauna* (1987), *Frottogrames* (1987–90), *Palimpsests*
(1989–93), *Sputnik* (1997), and *Googlegrams* (2005) as parts of a wider
practice concerned with photography's documentary, illusory, and contextual
claims. MACBA similarly describes institution-shaped presentations that question
photographic truthfulness. The chapter uses those accounts as context, while its
formal claims remain tied to the supplied complete frames. [Source notes](sources.md)

## Medium and technical record

The supplied entries identify *Autumn* as a gelatin-silver photogram on vinyl
wallpaper, and *Cebus nigittatus* and *The Garden of the Dragon* as gelatin-silver
photograms. The source record for *Pirofagus Catalanae* describes a photographic
work reproducing an ink-and-wash drawing. Other selected records identify a
photographic work or object but do not provide a uniform print process, camera,
or capture account for the whole set.

None of the supplied museum-display JPEGs retains camera EXIF. The sources do
not establish a camera body, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed,
sensitivity, film or sensor format, lighting, or processing workflow for these
individual works. This chapter therefore discusses documented series and medium
where available, plus visible crop, surface, tonal grouping, palette, and
measured overlays; it does not infer camera settings from the web derivatives.

## Source and rights context

Every plate is a credited, publicly served Art Institute IIIF display JPEG used
under its individual fair-use assessment in [sources.md](sources.md). The
complete, web-sized frames are retained here for nonprofit criticism,
scholarship, and composition teaching; they do not replace prints or licensed
reproductions.
