# Richard Avedon — source shortlist

This working set traces Avedon’s defining moves across fashion, celebrity, political group portraiture, and the psychologically charged white-ground portrait. All seven JPEGs are unaltered, publicly served Art Institute of Chicago IIIF display renditions, individually opened after download. They are deliberately web-sized (843px on the long edge), above this project’s 640px floor but materially below a print or licensed master.

**Rights basis:** **fair-use** for every selection. The Art Institute API marks each object `is_public_domain: false` and gives the copyright notice **Copyright © The Richard Avedon Foundation.** Each assessment below considers the four factors: this nonprofit book makes a transformative criticism-and-scholarship use for composition teaching; the underlying photograph is creative; the full frame is necessary to analyze the specific spatial and expressive relation; and the small credited, record-linked display rendition does not replace a collection record, original print, or licensed reproduction.

## Shortlist

### 01 — *Ronald Fisher, Beekeeper, Davis, California* (May 9, 1981; printed 1985)

- **Why it matters:** The shirtless beekeeper fills the tall white field from edge to edge; the swarm turns portrait texture into a startling, all-over surface while the direct gaze locks the scattered detail to a single vertical axis.
- **Source:** [Art Institute of Chicago object 120869](https://www.artic.edu/artworks/120869/ronald-fisher-beekeeper-davis-california) (collection credit: Gift of Boardroom, Inc.; direct public display rendition: https://www.artic.edu/iiif/2/41f9a984-5c5b-bc7d-09d7-9b6fe0f348c9/full/843,/0/default.jpg)
- **Rights / fair-use assessment:** Richard Avedon; **Copyright © The Richard Avedon Foundation.** **Factor 1:** transformative nonprofit criticism teaches the relation between the frontal body, bees, and blank ground. **Factor 2:** creative photographic portrait. **Factor 3:** the complete frame is required to see its bodily scale and field of white. **Factor 4:** this 843 × 1053px credited display copy links to the collection record and cannot substitute for an original or licensed reproduction.
- **File:** `raw/richard-avedon/01-ronald-fischer-beekeeper.jpg` (843 × 1053px; filename retains the commonly used “Fischer,” while the Art Institute catalog spells the surname “Fisher”)

### 02 — *Dovima with Elephants, Evening Dress by Dior, Cirque d’Hiver, Paris* (August 1955; printed 1979)

- **Why it matters:** A fashion figure is held between the massive, wrinkled flanks of elephants. Her long dark dress and outstretched arms make a fragile X-like countergesture to the animals’ vertical mass.
- **Source:** [Art Institute of Chicago object 221681](https://www.artic.edu/artworks/221681/dovima-with-elephants-evening-dress-by-dior-cirque-dhiver-paris) (collection credit: Gift of the David C. and Sarajean Ruttenberg Arts Foundation; direct public display rendition: https://www.artic.edu/iiif/2/b7a41ddb-bf31-fea5-0869-911d2d8776c3/full/843,/0/default.jpg)
- **Rights / fair-use assessment:** Richard Avedon; **Copyright © The Richard Avedon Foundation.** **Factor 1:** transformative criticism examines the human/animal scale reversal and armature of arms, dress, and trunks. **Factor 2:** creative fashion photograph. **Factor 3:** the whole figure and both elephants are essential to the compositional argument. **Factor 4:** the 843 × 1051px credited display copy is web-sized, record-linked, and not a substitute for a print or licensed image.
- **File:** `raw/richard-avedon/02-dovima-with-elephants.jpg` (843 × 1051px)

### 03 — *The Duke and Duchess of Windsor, New York City* (1957)

- **Why it matters:** The couple’s posed elegance is punctured by the small dogs at their feet. The low, active animal cluster shifts the image’s attention away from the central social pair and gives the formal portrait an unstable base.
- **Source:** [Art Institute of Chicago object 156003](https://www.artic.edu/artworks/156003/the-duke-and-duchess-of-windsor-new-york-city) (collection credit: The Mary and Leigh Block Endowment Fund; direct public display rendition: https://www.artic.edu/iiif/2/9a9ac573-a7ff-c783-5275-b19e0f4247b9/full/843,/0/default.jpg)
- **Rights / fair-use assessment:** Richard Avedon; **Copyright © The Richard Avedon Foundation.** **Factor 1:** transformative scholarship analyzes the tension between courtly pose and the dogs’ disruptive lower register. **Factor 2:** creative portrait photograph. **Factor 3:** complete-frame use keeps both the pair and the dogs in their intended hierarchy. **Factor 4:** this 843 × 870px, credited, linked display copy does not replace an original or licensed reproduction.
- **File:** `raw/richard-avedon/03-duke-and-duchess-of-windsor.jpg` (843 × 870px)

## Research context

The Art Institute’s seven online records supply a compact, directly verifiable cross-section of Avedon’s work. The set is intentionally not limited to the famous fashion image: it pairs *Dovima* with white-background celebrity and artist portraits, a serial political group, and the bodily intensity of *In the American West*. The Museum of Modern Art’s artist page documents a substantially wider online corpus, including *Marilyn Monroe, actress, New York* (1957), *Jacob Israel Avedon* (1969–73), and *Allen Ginsberg and Peter Orlovsky* (1963), but its reproduction licensing page directs image users to licensing agencies. Those records inform the canonical research but were not treated as downloadable acquisition routes here: https://www.moma.org/artists/248-richard-avedon.
