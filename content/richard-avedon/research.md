# Richard Avedon — build research

## Scope and evidence

This chapter uses the seven Art Institute of Chicago collection records and public display renditions documented in `sources.md`. The selection runs from *Dovima with Elephants* (made August 1955; printed 1979) and *The Duke and Duchess of Windsor* (1957) through *The Chicago Seven* and *Andy Warhol* (both 1969), the white-ground portraits of June Leaf and James Galanos (1975), and *Ronald Fisher, Beekeeper* (made 1981; printed 1985). The dates and later print dates are catalog facts from those records, not inferred production history.

## Technique and printing

The records establish a range of fashion, celebrity, political-group, and white-ground portrait images. Across this supplied selection, the visible method is a deliberate reduction of setting: a body, a face, a gesture, or a repeated panel is made to carry the frame. This is a visual description of the selected images, not a claim that a single camera, negative process, lighting arrangement, paper, or darkroom method produced every work. `sources.md` records image dates and, for three selections, later print dates; it does not identify a negative process, paper, developer, or lighting setup for individual photographs.

## Camera and exposure information

The ingested manifest records no EXIF data. The cited Art Institute records in `sources.md` do not supply a camera body, lens, focal length, aperture, shutter speed, film stock, or exposure for these photographs. No such settings are asserted in the chapter.

## Analyzer observations for the supplied web reproductions

The deterministic analysis describes the normalized 843px public display files, not Avedon's negatives or prints. The selected reproductions range from the spare *James Galanos* (edge density 0.0134) and *Ronald Fisher* (0.0284) to the panelled *Chicago Seven* (0.0803), textured *Dovima* (0.0915), and high-contrast *Andy Warhol* (0.1289). Representative extracted palettes make the tonal strategy tangible: near-white `#f3f3f1` against near-black `#181917` in *James Galanos*; cream-gray `#e8e8e7` and dark brown-black `#201c19` in *Ronald Fisher*; and black `#0d0b0c` against pale gray `#b9bab8` in *Andy Warhol*.
