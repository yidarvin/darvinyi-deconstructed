# Felice Beato — source shortlist

All downloads are from Wikimedia Commons’ open collection records (Google Art Project / institutional scans). The Commons records label each file **Public domain**; Beato died in 1909. Titles retain the collection’s historical wording, including terms that require contextual treatment in the chapter.

1. **Exterior of the Secundra Bagh** — April 1858. The breached architecture makes a severe frame-within-frame, with the ruin’s dark opening pulling the eye through the scene. [Wikimedia Commons record](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Felice_Beato_(British,_born_Italy_-_(Exterior_of_The_Secundra_Bagh)_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg). **Rights:** Public domain (Commons record; Beato, 1832–1909).
2. **Interior of the Angle of Taku North Fort Immediately After Its Capture by Storm** — 21 August 1860. Rubble, wall planes, and the ragged breach create a dense recession from foreground debris to the captured fort. [Wikimedia Commons record](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Felice_Beato_-_Interior_of_the_Angle_of_Taku_North_Fort_Immediately_After_Its_Capture_by_Storm_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg). **Rights:** Public domain (Commons record).
3. **Prince Kung** — 2 November 1860. A vertical formal portrait whose costume, stance, and restrained backdrop isolate the figure as a precise compositional anchor. [Wikimedia Commons record](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Felice_Beato_(British),_born_Italy_-_Prince_Kung_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg). **Rights:** Public domain (Commons record).
4. **Entrance to the Kaiserbagh** — 1858. The gateway establishes a strong central threshold, balanced by small figures and damaged court surfaces. [Wikimedia Commons record](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Felice_Beato_(British,_born_Italy_-_(Entrance_to_the_Kaiserbagh)_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg). **Rights:** Public domain (Commons record).
5. **Entrance to the Large Mosque of Jumma Musjid in Delhi** — 1858. Monumental stairs and portal geometry convert a documentary view into a layered, ascending composition. [Wikimedia Commons record](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Felice_Beato_(British,_born_Italy_-_(Entrance_to_the_Large_Mosque_of_Jumma_Musjid_in_Delhi)_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg). **Rights:** Public domain (Commons record).
6. **Execution of Rebels, Lucknow** — 1858. The line of bodies and ruined architecture form a disturbing but historically important study of staged narrative and visual power. [Wikimedia Commons record](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Felice_Beato_(British,_born_Italy_-_(Execution_of_Rebels,_Lucknow)_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg). **Rights:** Public domain (Commons record).
7. **Exterior of Asaf-ud-Daula’s Imambara, or Tomb** — 1858–1862. A broad façade anchors the frame while foreground paths and tiny figures establish scale and depth. [Wikimedia Commons record](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Felice_Beato_(British,_born_Italy_-_(Exterior_of_Asaf-ud-Daula%27s_Imambara,_or_Tomb)_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg). **Rights:** Public domain (Commons record).
8. **Exterior of the Hindu Temple in Kootub** — 1858. The temple’s dark openings and stepped mass produce alternating tonal blocks and a measured progression inward. [Wikimedia Commons record](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Felice_Beato_(British,_born_Italy_-_(Exterior_of_the_Hindu_Temple_in_Kootub)_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg). **Rights:** Public domain (Commons record).
9. **Exterior of the North Fort, Showing the English Entrance** — 21 August 1860. A diagonal breach and scattered masonry choreograph a clear path from foreground aftermath to the military entry point. [Wikimedia Commons record](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Felice_Beato_(British,_born_Italy_-_(Exterior_of_the_North_Fort,_Showing_the_English_Entrance)_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg). **Rights:** Public domain (Commons record).
10. **Gateway and Banqueting Hall, Lucknow** — April 1858. Repeated arches and a low foreground establish rhythm, then lead to the ruined hall’s deeper layers. [Wikimedia Commons record](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Felice_Beato_(British,_born_Italy_-_(Gateway_and_Banqueting_Hall,_Lucknow)_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg). **Rights:** Public domain (Commons record).
11. **Panorama of Lucknow, Taken from the Kaiserbagh Palace** — 1858–1862. Its extreme horizontal sweep makes a valuable study in segmented panorama, skyline rhythm, and cumulative depth. [Wikimedia Commons record](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Felice_Beato_(British,_born_Italy_-_(Panorama_of_Lucknow,_Taken_from_the_Kaiserbagh_Palace)_-_Google_Art_Project.jpg). **Rights:** Public domain (Commons record).
12. **Entrance of the Golden Temple of Amritsar** — c. 1858–1860 (possibly October 1859). The monumental entrance, reflected water, and tiny human scale produce a strongly tiered architectural view. [Wikimedia Commons record](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Felice_Beato_photograph_of_the_Golden_Temple_from_ca.1858%E2%80%931860.jpg). **Rights:** Public domain (Commons record; record links the Rijksmuseum scan).

Context source: [Getty, *Felice Beato: A Photographer on the Eastern Road*](https://www.getty.edu/art/exhibitions/beato/) documents the 1858–60 India and 1860 China campaigns and cautions that some aftermath images, notably Secundrabagh, may have been deliberately arranged.
