# Vibeke Tandberg — research note

## What the supplied sources establish

Vibeke Tandberg (b. 1967) uses serial performance, portrait conventions, and digital montage to test how a photographed self becomes a role. Kode dates *Brud* to 1993, *Living Together* to 1996, and *The Lost Ones* to 2019; OSL identifies the 1996 *Living Together* works as C-print digital montages and the 2003 *Old Man Going Up and Down a Staircase* works as unique black-and-white RC prints. Kode's current exhibition material also documents *Old Man Cowboy #05* (2022–23). [Source notes](sources.md)

Those records support discussion of staged personae, montage, titles, dates, and the stated media. They do not establish camera body, lens, exposure, film or sensor, editing software, lighting plan, or a universal printing method. The empty EXIF fields in the ingest manifest are properties of the supplied web files, not evidence about original capture.

## Method for this chapter

The overlays name only visible frame relationships: paired figures, portrait axes, stair rails, and room divisions. Analyzer tonal and palette results describe the normalized display JPEGs rather than original objects. The source notes retain the individual credit, link, and fair-use rationale for every complete-frame reproduction.
