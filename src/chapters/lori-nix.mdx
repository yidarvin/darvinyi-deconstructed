Lori Nix and Kathleen Gerber make the empty room feel less like a stage after the
action than a system whose rules have become visible. *The City* (2005–15) is
made from hand-built miniature interiors, photographed from a carefully selected
viewpoint; the final photographs turn civic order into a question of openings,
repetition, enclosure, and interruption. The overlays stay with visible facts in
the supplied reproductions rather than treating every detected line as a claim.
[Source notes](../../content/lori-nix/sources.md)

## order, then weather

<Plate slug="lori-nix" id="01-museum-of-art" label="Museum of Art, 2005" caption="A gallery cornice, near-symmetrical arcade, and suspended bee swarm turn a civic interior into a measured ruin." />

In *Museum of Art*, the line at y0.517 names the gallery cornice rather than an
invented horizon. The museum axis holds the central arcade while the bees occupy
the upper center as an unruly dark punctuation. Mean luminance 0.433 runs from
p05 0.090 to p95 0.808; charcoal #252019 against cream #cdc4b3 lets the ruined
plaster read as a bright enclosure rather than neutral backdrop.

<Plate slug="lori-nix" id="02-natural-history" label="Natural History, 2005" caption="A damaged display hall becomes a lateral rhythm of cases, frames, debris, and a foreground plinth." />

*Natural History* refuses the usual central destination. The vitrine rhythm moves
across a 1500-by-476 panorama, with the central debris cluster acting as a pause
instead of a vanishing point. Its edge density is 0.174, high enough to keep the
cases distinct; near-black #1b1c19, gray-green #736d5e, and pale #c3cac8 hold a
sideways sequence of damaged displays.

<Plate slug="lori-nix" id="03-majestic" label="Majestic, 2006" caption="A proscenium axis, chandelier, balcony front, and seating aisle retain theatrical order inside the ruined cinema." />

*Majestic* is a viewing machine that still works after its story has disappeared.
The proscenium and vertical axis lock the chandelier to the center; the left aisle
then releases that order toward the floor. The dark mean of 0.238 and p95 of
0.686 preserve the stage's red-brown #3f312b and the pale upholstery as separate
planes, not decorative noise.

<Plate slug="lori-nix" id="04-library" label="Library, 2007" caption="A central birch interrupts stacked shelving and paired gallery rails, making knowledge storage read as a room reclaimed by growth." />

The central birch in *Library* is not an incidental prop: its tall oval interrupts
the rear shelving bay and turns bilateral architecture into an obstruction. The
two gallery rails make the interruption legible as depth. This is the densest
plate in the selection at edge density 0.219; black #1a1815, olive-brown #595142,
and pale #b8ad9a keep books, branches, and windows separated.

## containers overturned

<Plate slug="lori-nix" id="05-aquarium" label="Aquarium, 2007" caption="A centered room, ceiling medallion, wall frieze, arch, and glowing tank convert containment into an unstable subject." />

*Aquarium* makes the tank, not the room, the brightest internal enclosure. The
room axis and ceiling medallion establish a ceremonial order that the tank's
yellow-green light displaces to the right. Blue #38597b and slate #1d2732 govern
the surrounding water-filled floor, while the p05–p95 range of 0.090–0.741 gives
the tank enough luminance to become a second room inside the first.

<Plate slug="lori-nix" id="06-botanic-garden" label="Botanic Garden, 2008" caption="Greenhouse crossbars and roof ribs set a precise enclosure around a dense planting bed." />

The conservatory in *Botanic Garden* is visibly constructed as a shell: crossbar,
axis, and paired roof-rib paths remain readable above the planting bed. Their
discipline makes the foliage feel more excessive, not less. Edge density reaches
0.227, the chapter's highest, and the range from p05 0.012 to p95 0.824 moves
from near-black #120f0b through green-brown #756740 to glassy #c9ced1.

<Plate slug="lori-nix" id="07-laundromat" label="Laundromat, 2008" caption="Washer rows, a central storefront opening, and a checkered floor direct the eye through an ordinary service room made strange by abandonment." />

In *Laundromat*, the important route is the floor recession into the bright
storefront, not a fictional flood line. Machines on both walls and the central
axis turn routine repetition into a corridor. The restrained mean of 0.334 and
palette of brown #443f33, charcoal #1b1713, and pale pink-gray #ded0cb leave the
light outside as the image's most forceful invitation.

<Plate slug="lori-nix" id="08-bar" label="Bar, 2009" caption="A long back bar and pool table make an emptied social room read as a layered set." />

*Bar* layers one threshold behind another: the long back bar is a stable top band,
the pool table anchors the foreground, and the counter and stools form a short
rightward rhythm. Its edge density of 0.166 makes the clutter active without
overwhelming the large dark intervals. Near-black #211915, brown #695c55, and
pale #c3bdba make familiar hospitality feel materially deserted.

## instruments and apertures

<Plate slug="lori-nix" id="09-circulation-desk" label="Circulation Desk, 2012" caption="A cornice and stack bay steady the room while the curved desk rim and open roof interrupt its promise of orderly circulation." />

*Circulation Desk* puts its rupture above the shelves. The cornice divides the
space from the torn roof, while the desk curve makes a foreground route that ends
in rubble rather than service. Mean luminance 0.404 and the blue accent #3f5088
against gray-brown #5f554f allow the sky opening to register as a material break.

<Plate slug="lori-nix" id="10-violin-repair-shop" label="Violin Repair Shop, 2011" caption="Arched windows, hanging instruments, a workbench edge, and floorboard recession organize a room whose tools wait without workers." />

The workshop's arched windows are a bright internal frame, answered by the small
cluster of hanging instruments. The workbench edge holds the middle while the
floorboards release depth below it. At edge density 0.082 this is comparatively
quiet; warm brown #504337 and pale #c8bbb1 keep the labor of the room legible
without making its objects into a catalogue.

<Plate slug="lori-nix" id="11-subway" label="Subway, 2012" caption="Ceiling edge, carriage wall, seats, sanded floor, and the carriage-end opening reduce a transit interior to a narrowing route." />

*Subway* makes a train car into a tube of obsolete infrastructure. The measured
opening at the right and the ceiling edge are real directional facts; the overlay
names the seat-and-floor recession rather than claiming nonexistent tracks. Mean
luminance is 0.302, with #1c1718, rusty #4d3936, and pale #d3cdc5 separating the
corroded shell from the light beyond it.

<Plate slug="lori-nix" id="12-observatory" label="Observatory, 2015" caption="A telescope points toward a broken oculus, while dome ribs and a low gallery rail keep the damaged observatory geometrically legible." />

In *Observatory*, the telescope gives the broken oculus a purpose even as the
opening admits vines and sky. The dome ribs curve outward from that damaged frame;
the low rail restores a horizontal counterweight. The image has p95 0.863 against
p05 0.055, with dark #1d1a13 and pale #d8dbc6 making the aperture read as a real
source of light rather than a symbolic backdrop.

## Exercises

<ExerciseCard n="01" title="Turn a room into a system">
Photograph an empty interior. Identify one repeatable order: tiles, shelves, seats,
or windows; then add or retain one organic interruption. Make the interruption
large enough to alter the order without becoming a simple portrait subject.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="02" title="Use an aperture as an event">
Frame a doorway, roof opening, or broken window. Let the opening change the
picture's balance through light or depth, rather than treating it as background.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="03" title="Name only visible structure">
Draft an overlay with three to five claims. Delete any line that does not sit on a
real edge, rail, frame, or object path. Keep the version in which each label can
be defended by the photographed surface.
</ExerciseCard>
