Bernard Faucon's *Les Grandes Vacances* (1976–1981) makes mannequins perform
childhood in real places. That difference matters: these bodies look available to
the camera, but their fixed faces and poses make every outing, meal, speech, and
danger read as a set. The overlays name the visible structures that make the
fiction persuasive without mistaking the figures for living children. [Source
notes](../../content/bernard-faucon/sources.md)

## a landscape made into a stage

<Plate slug="bernard-faucon" id="01-le-chemin" label="Le chemin, 1976–1981" caption="A road and an overhanging oak turn a line of mannequins into a processional entrance." />

In *Le chemin*, the road is less a natural route than a cue for arrival. It runs
from the close lead mannequin toward the smaller figures behind, while the oak
canopy makes an uneven proscenium around the bright opening. The analyzer finds a
broad range from p05 0.118 to p95 0.976 and an edge density of 0.193: bark,
branches, gravel, and clothing keep the staged procession from becoming a clean
theatrical silhouette. Dark blue-black #2a282e and pale #f4f0e9 make the opening
behind the group feel lit from within.

<Plate slug="bernard-faucon" id="02-ceremonie" label="Cérémonie, 1976–1981" caption="A posed cluster interrupts the large, calm bands of sky, sea, and shore." />

*Cérémonie* makes the shore into three broad, nearly level registers. The sea
horizon at y0.368 stabilizes the scene; the compact posed row and its flower stand
become conspicuous precisely because the surrounding sky, water, and sand leave
so much room. Its low edge density, 0.035, is the set's quietest. Pale blue
#d1d6e0, blue-gray #acb6cb, and sandy #cbb7ae support a ceremony whose calm is
too arranged to feel spontaneous.

<Plate slug="bernard-faucon" id="03-le-cimetiere-de-lioux" label="Le cimetière de Lioux, 1976–1981" caption="A dominant monument, central crossing, and divided cemetery ground make the graveyard a layered stage." />

At Lioux, the seated monument receives the frame's most emphatic anchor, while the
marked central crossing and vertical divide organize the figures, graves, and
monuments without treating the misty valley as a false horizon. This is a relatively open image, with edge density 0.058,
the p05–p95 span of 0.184–0.961 lets dark #38373f ironwork and pale #f1eef2
stone coexist as sharply different theatrical materials.

<Plate slug="bernard-faucon" id="04-l-enfant-qui-vole" label="L'enfant qui vole, 1979" caption="Foreground witnesses and a distant ring make the suspended mannequin read as a staged spectacle." />

*L'enfant qui vole* converts scale into an apparatus of belief. A close ring of
witnesses looks across the rock threshold, the blue figure is held in the middle
distance, and a second ring of tiny figures spreads through the clearing. The
overlay's flight path is interpretive rather than a claim about perspective: the
picture asks the eye to complete an impossible movement. Its mean luminance is
0.334 and its palette stays in blue-green #515e60, #6b808b, and deep #1c1b15, so
the blue shirt arrives as a designed interruption, not a flash of reportage.

## rituals that refuse to move

<Plate slug="bernard-faucon" id="05-le-banquet" label="Le banquet, 1976–1981" caption="A table edge carries the picnic tableau from a seated guest toward an implausible fire." />

The banquet's long white table supplies a practical route through a crowded field:
from the seated foreground mannequin, past bottles and food, toward the flare
beyond the stone wall. The marked guest-to-fire path makes the image's conflict
legible: feast and catastrophe share one carefully blocked stage. Mean luminance
is 0.438, with a p95 of 0.827; brown-black #382922, pale cloth #bebbc5, and flame
orange #f2c47c make light the picture's most conspicuous prop.

<Plate slug="bernard-faucon" id="06-diabolo-menthe" label="Diabolo menthe, 1980" caption="A fire band and a chain of green glasses turn four fixed figures into a frontal ritual." />

Here, the fire forms a level pressure behind the mannequins while their raised
glasses connect across the frame as a bright green chain. The right-hand jacket is
an anchor not because it tells a story, but because its black-and-white mass
closes the confrontation at the edge. Edge density is only 0.056, so the faces,
shirts, glasses, and fire read as large deliberate units. Charcoal #3a3232,
pink #b89f97, pale #dbd6d4, and orange #c98642 make the stillness feel gaudy and
unsettling.

<Plate slug="bernard-faucon" id="07-la-chasse-aux-papillons" label="La chasse aux papillons, 1976–1981" caption="Two colored nets and a stiffly posed figure turn a familiar game into an arranged field." />

In *La chasse aux papillons*, the yellow-net mannequin at right is held against a
grass skyline while blue and yellow net circles rhyme above the group. The dense
foreground weeds do not naturalize the scene; with edge density 0.188, they make
the smooth plastic faces and fabrics look even more placed. Olive #66604c,
sky-blue #5f7cae, dark #33332c, and off-white #cecfc4 sharpen the collision of
garden growth and manufactured childhood.

<Plate slug="bernard-faucon" id="08-la-comete" label="La comète, 1976–1981" caption="A burning arc, empty chairs, and paired figures hold a garden clearing in suspended expectation." />

The comet's bright curve is a photographic trace, not a literal celestial object
in the scene. It sweeps above the clearing and hands attention down to the paired
figures, while the chairs make the open ground feel like an audience space. This
is the darkest plate, with mean luminance 0.259 and p05 0.094. Black #1f1e20 and
brown #4a3b2f let the pale #efe5cc arc function as a single graphic event.

## address, witness, and aftermath

<Plate slug="bernard-faucon" id="09-le-jeune-orateur" label="Le jeune orateur, 1976–1981" caption="A looming speaker and a low microphone cluster turn a meadow into a public address." />

*Le jeune orateur* gains its odd authority from empty sky. The oversized speaker
occupies the right edge, the microphone cluster rises from the crowd, and the
wooded band at y0.687 keeps the lower field from dissolving. At edge density
0.011, the smoothest surface in the selection, blue #b8c1cf and #83a2ca give the
open air a studio-like blankness. The microphones do the work of scenery: they
make a fixed profile seem to address a public.

<Plate slug="bernard-faucon" id="10-la-neige-qui-brule" label="La neige qui brûle, 1976–1981" caption="A blurred foreground witness and a relay of fires draw the snowy field toward the distant ridge." />

*La neige qui brûle* makes the picture's nearest face deliberately hard to read,
then distributes smaller fires across snow and tables toward the ridge. The overlay
does not treat that relay as a literal road; it maps a sequence of attention from
foreground flame to distant flame. Its p05–p95 range, 0.153–0.902, holds a deep
#3f3233 against pink-white #e7c8cd and burnt #905c1c. The result is not a record
of an event but a staged contradiction: cold ground, warm fire, and an observer
too close to see clearly.

## Exercises

<ExerciseCard n="01" title="Make a route do theatrical work">
Photograph a path, corridor, or strip of floor with a person or object placed near
its front edge. Add two smaller figures or objects farther back. Keep the version
in which the route reads as an entrance rather than merely as depth.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="02" title="Stage an impossible action with witnesses">
Make one still image in which a figure appears to float, jump, or otherwise defy
the setting. Use foreground witnesses and a distant group to make the action feel
like a performance, then compare it with a version that has no spectators.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="03" title="Use a bright event as a structural line">
At dusk or with a controlled light source, arrange one bright arc, band, or flare
above darker figures. Make sure the light joins the composition rather than merely
decorating it: if you hide the light, the remaining placements should lose their
relation.
</ExerciseCard>
