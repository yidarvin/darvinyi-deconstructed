Shomei Tomatsu makes postwar space feel unstable: a flooded surface becomes evidence, a face becomes an encounter, a storefront becomes a screen, and a street becomes a hard collision of bodies and signs. This twelve-work selection moves from 1959 to 1969 and includes later prints where the collection record identifies them. Its overlays name only visible structures: edges, figures, tonal divisions, and pressure from the frame, rather than turning grain or ambiguous shadows into false perspective. [Source notes](../../content/shomei-tomatsu/sources.md)

## surfaces that retain pressure

<Plate slug="shomei-tomatsu" id="01-floods-and-japanese-nagoya" label="Untitled (Nagoya), from Floods and Japanese, 1959; printed 1966" caption="A boot sits inside a square water field, surrounded by fragments whose reflections keep the picture from resolving into a simple still life." />

In *Untitled (Nagoya)*, a boot anchors a field that has no stable horizon. The overlay encloses its swollen silhouette and follows two nearby debris contours, making the picture's central fact a small usable object marooned in an expansive, all-over surface. The phi grid is the analyzer's best fit; its edge density of 0.1652 and restrained brown-gray palette, from #1f1911 to #afa596, keep water, reflections, and scraps materially active around the boot.

<Plate slug="shomei-tomatsu" id="02-tsuyo-kataoka-nagasaki" label="Tsuyo Kataoka, Motohara Town, Nagasaki, 1961; printed 1974" caption="The face fills the close frame, while the brow line and surrounding hair make the sitter's direct look an intensely local event." />

Tomatsu's Nagasaki portrait refuses a neutral viewing distance. The face ellipse and brow-and-gaze contour stay close to what is visible: expression, wrinkled skin, shadowed hair, and a shallow, unfocused surround. Its tonal span runs from p05 0.125 to p95 0.871, while the modest 0.0563 edge density leaves expression rather than background texture to carry the image.

<Plate slug="shomei-tomatsu" id="03-eiko-oshima-prize-stock" label="Eiko Oshima, Actress in the Film Shiiku (Prize Stock), 1961; printed 2003" caption="Lowered eyelids and a cropped mouth turn a close portrait into a field of interrupted facial lines." />

The close portrait of Eiko Oshima is not organized around a conventional head-and-shoulders likeness. The overlay's large face ellipse and two short contours isolate the lowered lids and mouth, where the crop turns familiar features into long, soft intervals. The image has exceptionally low edge density, 0.0108, and a p05–p95 range of 0.051–0.839; broad gray tone, hair, and skin do more work than hard outlines.

<Plate slug="shomei-tomatsu" id="04-asphalt-tokyo" label="Asphalt, Tokyo, 1960; printed 1970s" caption="Tiny light fragments puncture a nearly black surface, making the asphalt read as a deliberately unreconciled field." />

*Asphalt, Tokyo* gives almost nothing the status of a main object. Two small ellipses and a short reflective-mark path identify isolated glints without claiming an invented scene. The analyzer measures a very low mean luminance of 0.0509 and a high edge density of 0.2719: the dark frame is alive with minute boundaries even when its subject remains resistant to naming.

## bodies, screens, and crowded light

<Plate slug="shomei-tomatsu" id="05-editor-nakahira-shinjuku" label="Editor, Takuma Nakahira, Shinjuku, Tokyo, 1964; printed 2003" caption="A doorway edge and a blurred figure make the room's open floor feel like an active zone of interruption." />

The body in *Editor, Takuma Nakahira* is caught at an oblique, blurred angle under a hanging fixture. The overlay keeps the argument spare: a right-hand doorway frame and a body contour show how the room's open center is tightened by hard vertical edges. Dark values dominate (mean luminance 0.2327, p05 0.031), and the brighter floor and shirt become brief releases inside that enclosure.

<Plate slug="shomei-tomatsu" id="06-screen-shinjuku" label="Screen, Shinjuku, Tokyo, 1969; printed 1980" caption="A reflective panel overlays a near silhouette with smaller reflected figures and lights, leaving no single plane fully secure." />

*Screen, Shinjuku* makes reflection its subject and its disruption. The panel frame, measured seam, and reflected-figure anchor show several depths crowded onto one surface: a body seen close, a luminous interior, and small figures behind or within it. The phi grid is the best fit, and #0b0a08 through #b3b2ad describes a narrow monochrome scale in which highlights arrive as scattered interruptions.

<Plate slug="shomei-tomatsu" id="07-blood-and-rose-tokyo" label="Blood and Rose, Tokyo, 1969; printed 2005" caption="A central figure is held in a bright lower screen while a blue-black upper band makes the frame feel vertically compressed." />

Here color is a structural event. The overlay frames the warm lower screen, anchors the central figure, and traces the blue upper band that arcs above it. With #026c7d and #04232d set against the warmer #c9a083 and #7c503c, the palette makes a small area of light feel crowded beneath a larger ceiling of dark color.

<Plate slug="shomei-tomatsu" id="08-young-power-shinjuku" label="Young Power, Shinjuku, 1969; printed 1974" caption="A bright walker, reflected pavement route, and drifting crowd line turn a wet plaza into dispersed but connected movement." />

*Young Power* is built from movement that does not cohere into a single crowd. One small bright walker is anchored at the left; a measured diagonal follows a long reflection toward the lower field; a separate polyline names the drift of figures across the upper half. Its edge density of 0.1274 and p05–p95 range of 0.031–0.812 let the lighted pavement act as both route and obstacle.

<Plate slug="shomei-tomatsu" id="09-computer-tokyo" label="Computer, Tokyo, 1968; printed 1980" caption="A dense wire field flattens the machine into a rectangle of connections, with a single foreground cable cutting across it." />

The computer image makes apparatus look almost biological. A frame encloses the densest cable field, while two paths identify one foreground cable and a route through the tangled middle rather than pretending the wiring leads cleanly to a vanishing point. It is the selection's busiest measured surface: edge density 0.2999, from near-black #0c0b0c to only #969596.

## signs, abstraction, and encounter

<Plate slug="shomei-tomatsu" id="10-protest-kawasaki" label="Protest, Kawasaki, 1969" caption="A canted roofline, hanging sign, and repeated windows turn a building face into an uneasy public graphic." />

The facade in *Protest, Kawasaki* is not treated as neutral background. The roofline tilts across the top, the sign interrupts the upper-left window rhythm, and broken or dark openings make the wall an uneven visual register. The pale p95 of 0.835 and dark #12100f establish the severe contrast that allows lettering and window voids to compete.

<Plate slug="shomei-tomatsu" id="11-iwakuni-yamaguchi" label="Iwakuni, Yamaguchi, 1960; printed 1980" caption="A broad diagonal tonal boundary and small bright angular mass hold a grainy field without forcing it into a descriptive scene." />

*Iwakuni, Yamaguchi* is the set's most withholding frame. The overlay follows the large diagonal tonal boundary, marks the bright angular mass, and traces a dark grain transition; it does not assign a false object to the ambiguous forms. The phi grid is the analyzer's strongest fit, with p05 0.098, p95 0.929, and edge density 0.1892 showing a high-contrast, highly articulated surface.

<Plate slug="shomei-tomatsu" id="12-yokosuka-kanagawa" label="Yokosuka, Kanagawa, 1966; printed 1978" caption="A near sailor's face, a curved canopy edge, and a second cropped figure compress the low-angle street encounter." />

The final picture gives the camera's low angle a social force. The near sailor's face is enclosed by the ellipse; the canopy curve crosses the frame in front of him, while a second sailor remains only partly visible at lower right. Although the analyzer finds a phi-grid preference, the force comes from the off-center face against a broad bright sky: low edge density, 0.0295, and a wide p05–p95 range of 0.075–0.859.

## Exercises

<ExerciseCard n="01" title="Make a surface carry the subject">
Photograph one ordinary object in water, pavement, reflection, or another active surface. Keep enough surrounding material that the object does not become a simple isolated still life.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="02" title="Work at uncomfortable distance">
Make a portrait close enough that one facial line, a crop, or a broad tonal interval matters as much as the sitter's identity. Do not use a false horizon to tidy the frame.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="03" title="Let a screen complicate depth">
Photograph through or against glass, a mirror, or a reflective panel. Make at least two planes legible, then keep one visual interruption that prevents them from settling into a clean scene.
</ExerciseCard>
