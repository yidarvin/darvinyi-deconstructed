Inez & Vinoodh make an image feel designed before it feels observed. This selection moves from the altered nude of 1993 to current fashion and celebrity pictures, but its common pressure is on the seam between a person and the frame that presents them: a body becomes a smooth object, a face is interrupted by jewelry, or a family group is joined by a deliberately theatrical handhold. The source record identifies the pair as pioneers of digital image-making; the overlays stay with visible, finished-frame relationships rather than claiming undocumented studio methods or edits. ## bodies as constructed surfaces

<Plate slug="inez-vinoodh" id="08-thank-you-thighmaster-britt" label="Thank You Thighmaster: Britt, 1993" caption="A large body ellipse and leaning axis make the altered nude read as a singular, designed form against white." />

*Thank You Thighmaster: Britt* starts with a nearly blank field and declines to make that field neutral. The broad ellipse gathers the body as one continuous silhouette, while the long polyline tracks its pronounced lean from head through ankle. That simple structure makes the work's alteration feel compositional: the body is presented less as a person located in a room than as an object set out for inspection. The analyzer records a very high mean luminance of 0.805 and an exceptionally low edge density of 0.013, with off-white #e4e1e1 surrounding warm skin tones #c8917c and #b16b54. The source record documents the 1993 digitally modified-nude series; it does not identify the camera or print process for this particular display file.

<Plate slug="inez-vinoodh" id="02-untitled-2-333" label="[Couple with red-painted figure], date not supplied" caption="Two profiles meet at a narrow hinge while painted red and black clothing divide the field into competing weights." />

The kiss in the second plate is not simply a central event. The subject anchor lands on the joined profiles, and the nearby contact hinge makes the exact point of touch the picture's sharpest compression. At right, the red-painted figure fills a tall ellipse; at left, the black jacket becomes a counter-mass. The directional shoulder edge pulls attention downward after the faces meet, so the image keeps alternating between intimacy and surface. Its palette makes that argument unusually spare: gray #8b8b8d surrounds black-purple #1a0d15 and reds #7d0d1c and #9b4354. The 0.075 edge density keeps the painted highlights articulate without breaking the two-body block into descriptive detail.

<Plate slug="inez-vinoodh" id="06-untitled-sh20034" label="[Vertical portrait, SH20034], date not supplied" caption="Taped edges, a centered jewel axis, and an internal portrait frame turn a face into a layered display surface." />

The vertical portrait makes intervention literal. The frame-in-frame follows the taped photograph while the vertical axis passes through the large central jewel; the jewel's right edge interrupts the face and projects downward into the lower half. Rather than pretending that the portrait is a seamless window, the image lets tape, collage, and expensive-looking ornament compete for equal visual authority. The analyzer's pale beige palette (#d7cfbe and #bfb7a7) gives the support a low-contrast surround, while burgundy #5e3b34 and warm #915c47 concentrate attention inside the mounted image. The sources do not supply a work title, date, materials, or production process, so the reading concerns the visible layered presentation only.

## editorial figures, made theatrical

<Plate slug="inez-vinoodh" id="03-untitled-d-01" label="[Cyclists with lipstick and launch], date not supplied" caption="Two cyclists exchange a lipstick across a central rocket, making a studio fantasy read as a four-part relay." />

The rocket in the third plate is neither background nor ordinary prop. Its ellipse establishes a tall central column, while the lipstick polyline crosses between the two riders and makes their gestures answer each other. The lower bicycle ellipse prevents the central vertical from becoming a lone monument: handlebars, wheels, and legs widen the base into a staged platform. A measured waterline at y0.828 pins that platform low in the frame. Blue #394774 and #556c9b dominate the open sky, with skin and pale cloud tones #9c8679 and #d9cec3 building a more theatrical foreground. At 0.092 edge density, the crisp bicycle and rocket boundaries give the collage its insistent, advertisement-like clarity.

<Plate slug="inez-vinoodh" id="04-rihanna-asap-rocky" label="Rihanna and A$AP Rocky, 2023" caption="A sloped shoreline and a hand-to-hand relay bind the separated adults and child into a shallow family tableau." />

In *Rihanna and A$AP Rocky*, the figures are purposely unequal in scale and stance, but the overlay identifies a precise relay between them. The anchor holds A$AP Rocky and the child at left; from there the polyline descends through the body group and rises again to the joined hands. The shallow shoreline is slightly canted at 4.9 degrees, a small instability that keeps the large figure at right from reading as a static fashion pose. The phi grid accommodates that off-center weight rather than forcing an artificial central balance. Low edge density (0.042) and a cool palette of #aab8d1, #818994, and #676466 leave the black clothing and faces as the few dense interruptions in an otherwise quiet beach field.

<Plate slug="inez-vinoodh" id="05-taylor-swift-time" label="Taylor Swift for Time, date not supplied" caption="A compact head-and-cat cluster and one raised arm turn a square editorial portrait into a tight, asymmetrical silhouette." />

The square portrait with Taylor Swift and a cat is structured by compactness rather than by depth. The head-and-cat ellipse deliberately takes in both faces, and the subject anchor sits on the cat rather than treating the human face as the only available focal point. The raised arm supplies the only strong diagonal and gives the upper cluster a hook-like silhouette. Behind it, the measured break at y0.166 is a tonal boundary in the gray backdrop, not a claim about a horizon. At 0.026, the image has the selection's second-lowest edge density; grays #989796, #7d7b7a, and #4f4b4a leave the white fur, red lipstick, and black garment to perform nearly all of the contrast work.

<Plate slug="inez-vinoodh" id="07-untitled-vmag" label="[V Magazine portrait, VLM13033], date not supplied" caption="A frontal face is held in an oval while jacket seams and a shoulder slope activate the lower half of the portrait." />

The black-and-white portrait establishes frontal address through the large face ellipse, then troubles that calm with the patterned jacket. A broad shoulder slope and a near-vertical jacket seam make the hand and chest a second, busier register beneath the face. The analyzer's vanishing-point measurement sits at the collar/hand hinge; it names the local convergence of those garment lines, not a literal deep perspective. The palette is a disciplined grayscale from #1c1c1c through #5f5f5f to #c4c4c4, while 0.078 edge density gives the jacket enough texture to argue with the composed, direct gaze.

## an image can contain another world

<Plate slug="inez-vinoodh" id="01-think-love" label="Think Love, 2025" caption="A central red veil interrupts a road's recession, keeping intimate bodies in tension with an open landscape." />

*Think Love* makes the central event both intimate and opaque. The big red-veil ellipse contains two bodies and several hands without resolving them into a single clean portrait. Beyond that mass, the road's distant point and the measured ridge line open a conventional landscape; the draped silhouette edge then sends the eye back down into the red enclosure. It is a useful reversal of the usual fashion-image hierarchy: the background's perspective is legible, but the couple's surface remains the picture's more complicated space. The 0.105 edge density is carried by gravel, cloth shimmer, and the road, while dark earth #40392e and deep red #670e19 are cut by a bright white highlight #f7f7f6. The source caption records that this 2025 work was shot on an Apple iPhone 17 Pro Max, but gives no further settings or print information.

## Exercises

<ExerciseCard n="01" title="Make a contact point carry a picture">
Photograph two people or two objects so that one point of contact has to organize the frame. Keep the surrounding areas visually unequal, one heavy and one open, and test whether the contact still reads as the hinge.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="02" title="Interrupt a portrait with a second surface">
Make a direct portrait, then introduce one visible surface between viewer and subject: fabric, reflection, paper, tape, or an object held close to the lens. Keep the intervention legible as an intervention rather than disguising it as an accidental obstruction.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="03" title="Give a fashion pose a spatial counterargument">

Place a posed figure in front of a road, shore, corridor, or other receding setting. Use one clear line in the background to provide depth, then give the figure a contour, garment, or gesture that competes with that recession.
</ExerciseCard>
