Loretta Lux makes a child portrait feel less like an encounter than a perfectly
placed object. Across this 2000–06 group, the figures are alert, frontal, and
improbably still; their worlds are broad, pale, and too settled to be casual.
The overlay diagrams name only the visible contracts: a level field break, an
axis, a window, a drum, a path, or the space around a body. The result is not
a claim that the pictures are simple. It is a way to see how their apparently
calm surfaces make looking feel rehearsed. [Source notes](../../content/loretta-lux/sources.md)

## A figure measured against a world

<Plate slug="loretta-lux" id="01-the-red-ball" label="The Red Ball, 2000" caption="A red sphere interrupts an almost bilateral child portrait, while the low tree line preserves a broad, unnaturally quiet sky." />

*The Red Ball* starts with a frontal child who seems to hold the whole square
in balance, then puts the balance under strain with the lacquered-looking sphere.
The overlay's pose axis runs close to the face and torso; the ball is the heavy
counterweight below it, while the distant tree line stays low enough to leave
the sky dominant. The analyzer measures a modest edge density of 0.020 and a
mean luminance of 0.670. Sea-green #a4bdb8 and #bdc6be fill the broad field;
the red ball and patterned shirt become concentrated interruptions rather than
incidental color.

<Plate slug="loretta-lux" id="02-girl-with-crossed-arms" label="Girl with Crossed Arms, 2001" caption="A frontal axis and locked arm gesture turn a pastoral field into a shallow, resistant stage." />

In *Girl with Crossed Arms*, the face offers the stillness that the arms refuse.
The crossed-arm path makes a compact bar across the torso, and the field break
arrives at the same height as the lower face rather than opening believable
depth. The phi grid is the analyzer's best fit, though the body actively pulls
against it. Mean luminance is 0.627, with a much wider p05–p95 range of 0.282
to 0.808 than the tranquil colors imply. Orange #cb641a is made almost
emblematic against muted green #7c874b and the low-detail sky.

<Plate slug="loretta-lux" id="03-the-rosegarden" label="The Rosegarden, 2001" caption="A centered child and a level garden wall divide the ornate rose field into an almost ceremonial frontal arrangement." />

*The Rosegarden* gives a garden the structure of a stage set. The figure is
enclosed as one tall central mass, while the wall and the frame's vertical axis
keep the rose beds from becoming a naturalistic view. The analyzer records the
selection's highest edge density, 0.070, but the active edges belong mostly to
the roses, walls, and garment detail. Pale #d8cfc1 and green #7c8464 keep those
many marks organized as a patterned field around the pink-and-green figure.

## Rooms and fields made deliberately spare

<Plate slug="loretta-lux" id="04-study-of-a-boy-2" label="Study of a Boy 2, 2002" caption="A small seated boy, folded leg, and hard floor line leave the blank wall to carry most of the picture's pressure." />

The pressure in *Study of a Boy 2* comes from how little is allowed to happen.
The floor line fixes the body low in the square; a head ellipse and folded-leg
contour keep the only complex mass local. The measured edge density is just
0.004, the lowest in the group, and mean luminance reaches 0.758. Wall colors
#d7ddd1 and #c6cbbc make the boy's checked shirt and brown shorts feel placed
against a field rather than simply photographed in a room.

<Plate slug="loretta-lux" id="05-the-book" label="The Book, 2003" caption="A prone reader, an upright companion, and a receding doorway make an empty room read as a charged lateral tableau." />

*The Book* replaces portrait symmetry with an uneasy exchange of positions.
The open book is held low and left, the standing figure is a vertical stop at
right, and the doorway beyond her makes the room continue without offering an
exit from the composition. The wall-relief seam at y0.249 is a real horizontal
register, not an invented horizon. Its mean luminance of 0.769 and p95 of
0.902 explain the washed, dry room; #d4cfc7, #c4beb5, and #e4e2db make the
blue vest and brown sweater carry the work's few emphatic tonal decisions.

<Plate slug="loretta-lux" id="06-the-wanderer" label="The Wanderer, 2003" caption="A reclining child and bag occupy a thin strip beneath the ridge, leaving the blank sky to overwhelm the human scale." />

In *The Wanderer*, the body lies across the bottom third rather than walking
through the landscape. The ridge line at y0.632 confirms how much of the frame
is given to the almost featureless sky; the bag holds the right edge of the
small human event. This is the brightest image in the chapter by mean
luminance, 0.816, with p95 at 0.933 and edge density only 0.008. Near-white
#e6e9e9 and quiet beige #c5bfb2 make the figure's brown vest and exposed limbs
read as fragile punctuation.

## Portraits that refuse to relax

<Plate slug="loretta-lux" id="07-marianne" label="Marianne, 2004" caption="A narrow frontal portrait uses the coat's center and hem to make stillness feel engineered rather than informal." />

*Marianne* makes a straight-on pose slightly uncanny by denying it a generous
surround. The measured figure axis follows the face and coat center; the slanted
hem at the lower edge interrupts what would otherwise be a perfectly settled
vertical. The image remains sparse at edge density 0.007. Its palette moves
from cream #d6d0bd and blue-green #98b9b9 to brown #6d523c, so the cool coat
appears as an exact, isolated plane. MoMA's record confirms the work's title,
date, artist, and copyright notice. [MoMA record](https://www.moma.org/collection/works/199240)

<Plate slug="loretta-lux" id="08-at-the-window" label="At the Window, 2004" caption="A turned child, a window rectangle, and a low exterior field line make looking outward the picture's primary action." />

The window in *At the Window* is both object and instruction. Its rectangular
opening encloses the pale distance, while the subject's head and shoulder turn
the viewer's attention through it. The exterior field line at y0.772 keeps that
view flat and available rather than scenic. With edge density only 0.003 and
mean luminance 0.720, the image avoids visual noise; pale #ced4d6, cream
#ccbfac, and curtain yellow do the quiet work of separating inside from out.

<Plate slug="loretta-lux" id="09-the-drummer" label="The Drummer, 2004" caption="A frontal axis, oval drum, and table edge stabilize a child whose mallets promise action without releasing the pose." />

*The Drummer* turns a prop into the portrait's dominant geometry. The drum's
oval cuts across the bottom of the frontal body, while the left mallet fixes a
small asymmetry within an otherwise centered pose. The table edge at y0.771
keeps the instrument from floating. The analyzer finds vertical symmetry of
0.960, a p05–p95 luminance range of 0.404–0.922, and edge density 0.008. Cream
#e7e2d8 and #d3cbbe keep the striped wall light, letting brown-orange #79462e
give the drum its dense visual beat. The Hood Museum record corroborates the
work's artist, title, and date. [Hood Museum record](https://hoodmuseum.dartmouth.edu/objects/2006.34)

## Pairing without ease

<Plate slug="loretta-lux" id="10-the-walk" label="The Walk, 2004" caption="A path edge and two closely aligned heads make a quiet outdoor encounter feel staged against an expansive field." />

In *The Walk*, the path does not lead into open movement; it stops at the
figures. The strong diagonal path edge makes a route from the lower left, then
the paired heads and the rightward subject anchor hold it at the field break.
Mean luminance is 0.743, p05–p95 spans 0.404 to 0.906, and edge density is
0.019. The pale blue #dae3e1 sky and sand #cebea2 path make the blue and yellow
coats read as precisely separated costume planes.

<Plate slug="loretta-lux" id="11-sasha-and-ruby" label="Sasha and Ruby, 2005" caption="Two nearly matched frontal figures are set on either side of a central axis, but their uneven expressions and shared arm prevent clean symmetry." />

*Sasha and Ruby* lets resemblance become a compositional problem. The pair axis
is real, and each body is held in its own ellipse, but the figures do not mirror
each other: the hair, mouths, eye-lines, and arm crossing create tiny refusals
of equality. The analyzer measures strong vertical symmetry of 0.896 while the
edge density remains 0.024. Cream #ebe0d4 and #dfcfc4 suppress the background;
the dotted dresses and warm hair make the small dissimilarities legible.

<Plate slug="loretta-lux" id="12-the-waiting-girl" label="The Waiting Girl, 2006" caption="An ornate settee, a sleeping cat, and a small seated child turn a level room into a formal arrangement of suspended attention." />

*The Waiting Girl* makes the settee a huge internal frame. Its ornate red field
holds the cat and child as unequal occupants, while the sofa axis exposes the
child's deliberate offset; the crossed shoes become the low point of her still
body. The floor-wall break at y0.771 gives the furniture real weight. Mean
luminance is 0.662 with p05–p95 of 0.412–0.824, and edge density is 0.017.
Muted cream #d4cdbe, pink-brown #9c7a63, and the patterned upholstery give
waiting a measured, material setting. [Art Gallery of NSW record](https://www.artgallery.nsw.gov.au/collection/works/220.2007/)

## Exercises

<ExerciseCard n="01" title="Make a field feel constructed">
Photograph a person against a broad wall, field, or sky. Keep one level break
low in the frame and remove every small object that would make the setting feel
casual. Then introduce one saturated object or garment and check whether it
becomes a counterweight rather than mere decoration.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="02" title="Build stillness with one interruption">
Make a frontal portrait on an axis. Add one visible contradiction to that
stability: crossed arms, a turned head, an object, or an uneven hand. Compare
the result with a fully symmetrical version and identify the smallest feature
that changes the portrait's emotional temperature.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="03" title="Let an interior frame direct attention">
Photograph a person beside a window, doorway, or large piece of furniture.
Keep the internal rectangle intact, then place the figure so their body either
blocks it or directs a look through it. Make a second frame without the
rectangle and compare how much visual direction the nested frame supplies.
</ExerciseCard>
