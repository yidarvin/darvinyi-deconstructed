Clarence H. White builds small theatres. A window, doorframe, tree trunk, or print edge establishes the stage; a pale body or gesture then emerges from soft middle tone. The analyzer confirms how little hard detail he needs: edge density is 0.003 in *The Kiss*, 0.006 in *Ring Toss*, and 0.007 in *Maine Marshes*.

## rooms made for gesture

*Ring Toss* makes a complete theatre from the dark rectangular room and long floorboards. *Blind Man's Bluff* frames a blindfolded central figure and the arc of her arms. *The Bashful Child*, *The Readers*, and *How Much Was That a Yard?* all use a doorway or window to make one gesture legible as a tableau.

## the figure held by a field

*Girl with Harp* has a broad tonal range, p05 0.227 to p95 0.910, but the lit face and poised hands at a zither are enough to activate its dark surround. *Little Lord Fauntleroy* uses the same economy: a pale child, bright hair, and collar stand against a deep garden field. The print edge is part of the object, so it is marked instead of treated as transparent.

*The Kiss* compresses two people into one small pale gesture inside a doorway. *Spring, A Triptych* opens that intimacy into decorative rhythm, repeated panels holding a white figure among blossoms.

## nature as a screen

In *Maine Marshes*, a far marsh band and water glint divide the landscape into dark and pale horizontals. Its p05-to-p95 range is 0.184 to 0.843 but edge clutter is minimal. *Morning* uses a leaning trunk as the separator between haze and a pale figure. *The Wrestlers* makes the woodland another stage, a small knot of bodies isolated in a lit opening.

## Exercises

<ExerciseCard n="01" title="Build a small stage">
Use a doorway or window as an internal frame, then arrange one gesture so it is legible from across the room.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="02" title="Let a figure emerge">
Photograph a pale figure against a darker field. Keep only one local bright detail, such as a collar, face, or hand, and see whether it can carry the whole frame.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="03" title="Use one natural divider">
In a landscape, place a trunk, shore band, or window edge between two tonal fields. Make that divider do the organizing before you look for detail.
</ExerciseCard>
