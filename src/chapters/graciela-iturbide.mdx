Graciela Iturbide's photographs make a relation before they make an anecdote. A
face meets an animal, a body bears another picture, a child sits inside a painted
or carved frame, a wall takes on the force of a portrait. The supplied SFMOMA
renditions move between the Sonoran desert, Juchitán, domestic rooms, and later
self-portrait work, but their common method is pressure: a figure must negotiate
another form already active in the frame. The overlays stay literal to that
pressure: an edge, a contour, a cluster, a centerline, not a story presumed
beyond the photographed moment.

## portraits with a second image inside them

*Our Lady of the Iguanas, Oaxaca* is an upright confrontation between a still
face and a crown that refuses symmetry. The overlay follows the irregular
iguana contour, its measured convergence, and the face held beneath it. The
reproduction is low-key (mean
luminance 0.293, p05 0.086, p95 0.612): texture in the animals and face matters
because the background remains comparatively reticent. The phi grid's off-center
intervals describe how the crown projects to both sides without becoming a
balanced emblem.

<Plate slug="graciela-iturbide" id="01-our-lady-of-the-iguanas" label="Our Lady of the Iguanas, Oaxaca, 1979" caption="An irregular iguana crown presses against a calm frontal face, making asymmetry carry a vertical portrait." />

In *La Frontera*, the back is not merely a surface but a broad image-field. A
shirt-collar line separates the head from the tattooed shoulders, and the overlay
uses the measured vertical center to make visible the near-frontal organization.
Its high vertical symmetry correlation (0.82) does not mean exact bilateral
symmetry; the landscape fragments at the edges and the body shifts it. The
reproduction's wide tonal span, from p05 0.063 to p95 0.843, allows the linework
to remain legible without reducing the person to the tattoo.

<Plate slug="graciela-iturbide" id="02-la-frontera" label="La Frontera, 1989" caption="A tattooed back becomes a near-frontal image field, with a narrow landscape held at the edges." />

*Mujer ángel* releases its figure into a much larger field. The ridge at y 0.511
divides pale air from stony ground, while the walking figure and boom box form a
dark, three-part contour at the left. The image's broad p05-to-p95 range
(0.078–0.812) makes that black clothing decisive against pale desert; the modest
edge density (0.0482) leaves the right side genuinely open. The overlay's rock
slope points into the figure but stops there, rather than claiming a false,
single-point landscape perspective.

<Plate slug="graciela-iturbide" id="03-mujer-angel" label="Mujer ángel, Desierto de Sonora, México, 1979; printed later" caption="A dark walking figure and boom box enter a long pale desert interval from the rocky left edge." />

The near-frontal face in *Autorretrato con los Seris* is more severe. A center
axis runs through nose, buttons, and collar, while the small line across the eyes
is enough to disturb that stability. Its bright rendition (mean 0.634; p95
0.906) leaves the wall as a large quiet surround, and the low edge density
(0.0110) makes the few interruptions, eyes, ornament, seams, and buttons, feel exact.

<Plate slug="graciela-iturbide" id="04-self-portrait-with-seris" label="Autorretrato con los Seris, desierto de Sonora, México, 1979; printed 2017" caption="A centered face and collar are unsettled by the small horizontal line crossing the eyes." />

## pictures, animals, and figures that answer one another

*Comadres* begins low, with birds crowded toward the lower left, then lets a
polka-dotted garment occupy the upper right. The overlay traces the bird group
and a striped hen instead of forcing the animals into a portrait of one subject.
At edge density 0.139, the highest in this set, the different feather, ground, and
cloth marks are all active. The restrained grayscale palette, from near-black
#292522 through light #ded7cf, lets pattern distribute attention rather than
turning into noise.

<Plate slug="graciela-iturbide" id="05-comadres" label="Comadres, Juchitán, Oaxaca, 1979" caption="A cluster of birds and a cropped, dotted garment make a pattern-rich but deliberately unequal field." />

*Tehuantepec* stages an answer across a mural. At left, the painted bird is a
large pale profile; at right, the child is enclosed by an oval, jaw-like form.
The overlay names the actual mural base and the enclosing oval, not an imaginary
horizon. With p05 0.075 and p95 0.792, the saturated blacks behind the bird and
the white outline around the child's face are the image's principal tonal events.

<Plate slug="graciela-iturbide" id="06-tehuantepec" label="Tehuantepec, 1980" caption="A painted bird and a child's face framed by a pale oval answer each other across a dark mural field." />

In *Después del rapto*, people work beneath a hard cast shadow that turns a wall
into a second, abstract action. The overlay follows the shadow's broken path,
which travels from the seated group through the hat and toward the small figure at
right; it does not mistake the light break for a conventional horizon. The median
darkness of the derivative (mean 0.321) and 0.0965 edge density make the wall,
basket, straw, bodies, and shadows compete as distinct textures.

<Plate slug="graciela-iturbide" id="07-despues-del-rapto" label="Después del rapto, Juchitán, Oaxaca, 1986" caption="Cast tree shadows transform a working group and wall into a shallow, theatrical field." />

*Marcelina* is a room built from recursive portraits. The large devotional image
at upper right, a smaller portrait at left, the clustered frames, and the seated
woman make a sequence of frontal presences. The overlay frames the upper-right
devotional portrait, then marks the measured central axis without claiming that
the whole room is symmetrical. The reproduction has a long tonal reach (p05 0.078, p95
0.835) and 0.0868 edge density: the patterned floor pushes forward, but the
wall's open right half lets the seated figure retain gravity.

<Plate slug="graciela-iturbide" id="08-marcelina" label="Marcelina, Juchitán, Oaxaca, 1984" caption="Devotional images and a seated woman turn a domestic wall into a layered field of portraits." />

## touch, shelter, and rough surfaces

*Jueves Santo* comes close enough that fabric becomes structure. The diagonal
cradle line crosses competing floral and striped textiles, while the child's face
is held in the small opening made by cloth and hands. This bright, high-contrast
rendition (mean 0.515; p05 0.106; p95 0.894) has an edge density of 0.0820, so
the patterns remain articulate rather than flattening into a single tone.

<Plate slug="graciela-iturbide" id="09-jueves-santo" label="Jueves Santo, Juchitán, Oaxaca, 1988" caption="Hands, a sleeping child's face, and clashing printed textiles make a close embrace into an all-over field." />

*Fiesta del lagarto* opens that closeness into a shaded grove. Hammocks make a
set of strong diagonal crossings, and the child at the far right is caught at the
edge of their weave. The overlay marks one real hammock edge and the crossing it
creates, while the low ground band prevents the foreground figure from floating
free. At 0.194 edge density, this is the busiest rendering in the group; bright
threads and leaf-shadow keep the scene moving across its surface.

<Plate slug="graciela-iturbide" id="10-fiesta-del-lagarto" label="Fiesta del lagarto, 1988" caption="Interlaced hammocks turn a shaded grove into diagonal structure, with figures held at opposite edges." />

*Lagarto* returns to a frontal pairing. Two women stand on either side of a pale
carved lizard; cropped hands at both frame edges interrupt the pair's apparent
order. The overlay's central axis is therefore a measured relation, not a claim
of calm symmetry. The light wall, with p95 0.812, gives the faces, object, and
hands room to register against the darker garments.

<Plate slug="graciela-iturbide" id="11-lagarto" label="Lagarto, 1986" caption="A pale carved lizard holds two women in an almost frontal pairing, unsettled by cropped hands at the edges." />

The last image pares the method down to surface. In *Autorretrato en la casa de
Trotsky*, small dark openings puncture a rough wall that shifts from a light left
field to deep right shadow. The overlay circles individual openings and follows
the curved light-to-shadow edge that gives the surface its major division. Its mean
luminance is 0.292, with p05 0.016 and p95
0.635: the tonal drama is not a spotlighted figure but the wall's gradual loss
of information into black.

<Plate slug="graciela-iturbide" id="12-self-portrait-trotskys-house" label="Autorretrato en la casa de Trotsky, Coyoacán, México, 2006; printed 2017" caption="Dark openings and an expanding shadow make a rough wall read as an austere, almost abstract portrait." />

## Exercises

<ExerciseCard n="01" title="Give a portrait a second image">
Photograph a person with a picture, sculpture, garment pattern, animal, or
reflection that can share the frame without becoming mere background. Decide
whether the second image should echo the face, oppose it, or pull the eye away
from it.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="02" title="Use a contour as a relation">
Make a close frame in which one contour joins two distinct things: a hand and
cloth, a hammock and tree, a body and shadow, or an object and a wall. Keep only
the line that actually changes how the two things read together.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="03" title="Let a surface become a portrait">
Photograph a wall, garment, floor, or other surface with a few dark interruptions.
Make one version that names the interruptions as objects and another that lets
them become rhythm. Compare which version better holds the frame without a
central figure.
</ExerciseCard>
