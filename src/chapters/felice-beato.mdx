Felice Beato's pictures make architecture do more than describe a place. Parapets, lintels, steps, and damaged walls become the means by which the eye moves through a scene made in the aftermath of imperial violence. The overlays stay with that visible construction: a roofline holds a battered facade, a trench edge enters a wrecked timber bay, and an arch makes a threshold inside a larger frame. They do not turn the photographs into neutral evidence. Getty's records establish that Beato sequenced images for a Western audience and that some scenes were deliberately arranged; the force of these prints lies partly in that act of arrangement.

The analyzer's tonal readings make the formal contrast clear. The quiet exterior at Secundrabagh has a mean luminance of 0.65 and an edge density of only 0.022; the much denser North Fort interior reaches 0.110. Across the albumen prints, a warm palette recurs: pale creams and browns such as #d1c498, #846f50, and #664d34, while the black-and-white North Fort record moves from #282828 to #e7e7e7. Beato turns those materials into a grammar of pale distance, dark openings, and a few sharply placed incidents.

## walls, breaches, and the measured aftermath

<Plate slug="felice-beato" id="01-exterior-sekundra-bagh" label="Exterior of the Secundra Bagh, 1858" caption="A damaged parapet, a large breach, and a small group of figures organize a broad, pale facade." />

In Exterior of the Secundra Bagh, the overlay's low roofline runs along the damaged parapet at y=0.493. A frame locates the large breach at the right, while an ellipse gathers the small figures in the lower left. These are modest claims, but together they explain the picture's scale: the facade runs laterally almost without interruption, the breach interrupts that rhythm, and the figures make the building's size legible. The analyzer's high-key mean of 0.65, p05 of 0.36, and p95 of 0.78 leave little tonal drama in the sky or ground. The image's pressure comes instead from the repeated arched openings and the particular wound at the edge.

<Plate slug="felice-beato" id="02-interior-taku-north-fort" label="Interior of the Angle of Taku North Fort, 1860" caption="A trench edge and a collapsed timber bay direct the eye through wreckage without making the casualties a visual spectacle." />

Interior of the Angle of Taku North Fort is structurally the opposite: it has a mean luminance of 0.56, a very wide p05–p95 range of 0.12–0.93, and the set's second-highest edge density, 0.110. The overlay follows the trench edge up into the middle of the frame, then marks the sagging roofline and a collapsed timber bay. The labels identify supports and terrain, not an invented vanishing point or a narrative of capture. Visible bodies remain part of the historical image, but the diagram does not isolate them as spectacle. Getty's object record and the contemporary account connected with this sequence make it especially important to distinguish the picture's controlled arrangement from unmediated evidence.

<Plate slug="felice-beato" id="06-execution-rebels-lucknow" label="Execution of Rebels, Lucknow, 1858" caption="The wooden gallows frame a sparse, staged arrangement of a central figure and separated witnesses." />

Execution of Rebels, Lucknow is even more reduced. Its edge density of 0.002 is nearly empty by comparison, and its mean of 0.70 is the lightest of the India views here. A frame names the gallows structure, an ellipse locates the central figure, and a polyline follows the witness line across the platform. The composition's spareness is its disturbing fact: the poles and crossbeam reduce the open field to a controlled internal rectangle. The overlay remains descriptive; it does not add claims about the event beyond what the source title and historical record establish.

## thresholds and facades

<Plate slug="felice-beato" id="04-entrance-kaiserbagh" label="Entrance to the Kaiserbagh, 1858" caption="Two rail and facade diagonals meet at a central archway, where a figure establishes the threshold's scale." />

Entrance to the Kaiserbagh works by a compact, highly articulated set of diagonals. The phi grid fits the analyzer's measured features better than thirds. Two labelled lines follow the stair rail and facade diagonal to the central archway; an ellipse catches the figure at its threshold. With an edge density of 0.075 and palette running from #493422 through #cfc097, the print has enough surface detail to make that convergence feel earned rather than diagrammatic. It is an architectural view whose invitation to enter is continually checked by damaged surfaces and by the smallness of the people beneath them.

<Plate slug="felice-beato" id="07-asaf-ud-daulas-imambara" label="Exterior of Asaf-ud-Daula's Imambara, 1858–62" caption="A principal entrance and a receding arcade turn a broad facade into a rightward movement above the garden." />

The exterior of Asaf-ud-Daula's Imambara begins frontally, then escapes to the right. The overlay frames a principal entrance, circles the front facade, and traces the receding arcade. The analyzer records a high edge density of 0.099 and a large tonal span, from p05 0.23 to p95 0.95; the pale #f5eccc sky and darker #4e3931 shadows help separate the long arcade from the irregular garden. The point is not perfect symmetry; the building is emphatically oblique, but the transition from stable facade to lateral extension.

<Plate slug="felice-beato" id="08-hindu-temple-kootub" label="Exterior of the Hindu Temple in Kootub, 1858" caption="A carved lintel, a dark central opening, and a seated figure make the colonnade read as rhythm and scale." />

In the Kootub temple view, a long polyline follows the carved lintel, a frame defines the central opening, and an ellipse locates the seated figure at its base. This is a better account than the analyzer's tempting but false horizon and symmetry suggestions: the print is a field of repeated columns, not a landscape. Its mean of 0.61 and edge density of 0.083 explain why the carved stone holds attention even at a distance; the palette moves between #715445 and #e6d8b2, keeping the dark opening legible within all that ornament.

<Plate slug="felice-beato" id="12-golden-temple-amritsar" label="Decorated frontal facade (supplied Golden Temple source file), c. 1858–60" caption="The supplied file is a close frontal decorated facade: its centered doorway, standing figure, and repeated panels make a different argument from a wide exterior view." />

The final architectural plate corrects its own source-list expectation. Although the supplied file is named for the Golden Temple source, it is not the broad entrance-and-water view described in the shortlist; it is a close frontal decorated facade. The overlay therefore reads what is actually in the file: a centerline through the middle panel, the deep central doorway, and the standing figure to its left. The analyzer's near-perfect vertical symmetry score of 0.96 supports that restrained reading, while its exceptionally high edge density of 0.151 comes from the repeated panels and carved borders rather than spatial recession.

## portrait and panorama

<Plate slug="felice-beato" id="03-prince-kung" label="Prince Kung, 1860" caption="Prince Kung's face, robe, and near-center axis make the portrait a controlled field of dark mass and bright embroidery." />

Prince Kung is an abrupt scale change from those buildings. The overlay uses an ellipse on the face, a polyline through the embroidered robe, and an axis that sits close to the sitter's vertical bearing. The analyzer missed the face as a saliency signal, which is why the overlay does not pretend that a detector found it. Visually, however, the face is the clear anchor: a dark hat and robe frame it, then the bright embroidered sleeves take the eye down and out. With an edge density of 0.020 and a warm palette from #6e5647 to #e9d4aa, this is a sparse tonal construction, not an architectural one.

<Plate slug="felice-beato" id="05-jumma-musjid-delhi" label="Entrance to the Large Mosque of Jumma Musjid in Delhi, 1858" caption="Terrace rooflines and a broad mosque skyline stage the central dome as a distant anchor over a dense foreground." />

Jumma Musjid makes its depth by stacking rather than converging. A leading line moves from the foreground terraces to the mosque, a large ellipse marks the central dome, and a skyline polyline retains the unequal rhythm of domes and minarets. The image is very light (mean 0.73, p95 0.85) and only moderately edged at 0.030. That low contrast lets the architectural silhouette, rather than any single dramatic incident, do the compositional work.

<Plate slug="felice-beato" id="09-taku-north-fort-exterior" label="Exterior of the North Fort, 1860" caption="A plank path reaches a broken fort wall across reeds and water; the frame identifies the damaged wall rather than claiming an abstract horizon." />

The exterior North Fort view turns a low plank path into a hesitant entry. The overlay follows it from the foreground, frames the broken wall, and circles the breach. The analyzer's suggested horizon was actually a tonal split across reeds, so it has been rejected. The print's mean of 0.59, p05 of 0.24, p95 of 0.91, and edge density of 0.088 give the reflection and tangled foreground enough weight to delay the eye before it reaches the fort.

<Plate slug="felice-beato" id="10-gateway-banqueting-hall" label="Gateway and Banqueting Hall, Lucknow, 1858" caption="A rubble slope rises toward a ruined gateway arch, while small figures make the damaged threshold a measure of scale." />

Gateway and Banqueting Hall uses the rubble slope as an oblique route to the arch. The overlay's line names that slope; a frame holds the ruined gateway and an ellipse gathers the small figures beneath it. Its edge density of 0.061 is lower than the other heavily damaged views, so the pale #d4c8a0 and #755b44 palette leaves room for the bare tree and open sky to counterweight the gateway. The picture is not a symmetrical ruin but a carefully unbalanced passage from debris to opening.

<Plate slug="felice-beato" id="11-lucknow-panorama" label="Panorama of Lucknow, 1858–62" caption="The visible joins of an assembled panorama support a distant skyline sweep ending in a tall tower at the right." />

The panorama changes the unit of composition from a single frame to a horizontal sequence. The overlay marks the distant skyline, circles the tall tower at the right, and follows the city sweep across the visibly joined panels. At 1600×235 pixels, its extreme aspect makes the analyzer's generic symmetry and saliency suggestions unreliable; the overlay uses only the claims that survive visual inspection. Its p05 of 0.12, p95 of 0.85, and edge density of 0.098 describe a city that is both tonally broad and optically dense, held together by the long skyline rather than a central subject.

## Exercises

<ExerciseCard n="01" title="Find the threshold">
Photograph an exterior with one opening, such as a doorway, arch, bridge, or passage, and make it the destination of a line from the foreground. Keep one small person near the threshold so the structure has a measurable scale. Before adding a compositional overlay, ask whether the line truly follows a visible edge or merely cuts across the scene.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="02" title="Build a skyline in layers">
Make a landscape or city view in three layers: a close, irregular foreground; a middle band of roofs, trees, or walls; and a distant silhouette. Hold the camera steady and keep the tonal contrast modest. Review the image in grayscale to see whether the silhouette can carry the frame without a strong central subject.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="03" title="Use repetition without false symmetry">
Photograph a facade or colonnade with repeated openings. Mark one real rhythm, such as the lintel, arcade, or panel sequence, and one deliberate break. Do not draw a symmetry axis unless the image actually mirrors across it; repetition and symmetry are different visual claims.
</ExerciseCard>
