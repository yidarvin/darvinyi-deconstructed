Thomas Demand photographs built scenes that feel uncannily available and strangely
vacant at the same time. The source record identifies this selection as photographs
of paper reconstructions; here, that fact matters less as a trick than as a way to
notice how a table edge, a doorway, a row of consoles, or an unoccupied channel of
water can hold an entire image together. The overlays name visible structural facts
rather than pretending to uncover a hidden original scene. [Source notes](../../content/thomas-demand/sources.md)

## rooms that behave like sets

<Plate slug="thomas-demand" id="01-studio-1997" label="Studio, 1997" caption="A level table crosses a wall of colored stripes, making place settings read as small interruptions in a repeatable field." />

*Studio* turns a room into a frontal sequence. The tabletop at y0.549 keeps the
bright striped wall from becoming a purely decorative backdrop, while the right-hand
place setting punctuates the long lateral span. Its p05 is 0.004 but p95 is 0.765,
so colored bands and pale plates arrive out of a near-black floor. Black-green
#0e1111, green #2b9c35, yellow #cbc316, and red #a2171c make repetition feel
cheerful and mechanically exact.

<Plate slug="thomas-demand" id="02-terrace-1998" label="Terrasse / Terrace, 1998" caption="Three colored lanterns and a central doorway turn an empty dining room into a sequence of lit thresholds." />

In *Terrace*, the blue lantern at the far right answers the orange and red lamps
across the room. The central doorway is an internal frame, but its darkness stops
depth rather than opening it. Mean luminance is only 0.117 and edge density 0.055;
black #09090b, red-brown #39221f, and isolated orange #db300e let the lamps
organize a room that otherwise withholds visual access.

<Plate slug="thomas-demand" id="03-grotto-2006" label="Grotte / Grotto, 2006" caption="A left cave ridge and several diagonal structures gather around a lit central formation." />

*Grotto* is a panorama of tactile accumulation. The measured convergence at
x0.438, y0.464 and the left ridge steer inward, but stalactites, shelves, and dark
cavities keep thickening that route. Its edge density of 0.263 is the chapter's
highest, while the palette moves from #0d0809 through #534f4e to #c3c1ba. The
bright central formation works because it has to be found amid many insistent
paper textures.

<Plate slug="thomas-demand" id="04-landing-2006" label="Landing, 2006" caption="Window-grid diagonals and a sill fragment turn a stair landing into a controlled field of breakage." />

*Landing* gives fragments an architectural counterweight. The window-grid
convergence sits high at x0.542, y0.082; below it, broken vessels scatter across
the floor and concentrate on the sill. This relatively smooth image has edge
density 0.051 and a p05–p95 range of 0.169–0.945. Stone #625c54 and #3f3732
hold pale window #f0ece6, making the shards feel deliberately distributed.

## institutions without occupants

<Plate slug="thomas-demand" id="05-presidency-2008" label="Presidency, 2008" caption="A desk horizon, near-central axis, and flanking flag make an official interior read as a carefully emptied emblem." />

The desk in *Presidency* is a low horizon at y0.620, not merely furniture. It
separates the repeated windows and curtains from the heavy carved mass below, while
the axis at x0.475 passes through chair, desk, and carpet emblem. The American
flag's small interruption prevents the symmetry from becoming inert. Mean luminance
0.445 spans from p05 0.078 to p95 0.973; #754924 curtains and #eaeef2 windows
make authority luminous but provisional.

<Plate slug="thomas-demand" id="06-control-room-2011" label="Kontrollraum / Control Room, 2011" caption="A continuous console bank and a foreground console make an evacuated control room legible through repeated operational surfaces." />

*Control Room* replaces the conventional horizon with the long console bank at
y0.570. Monitors, panels, switches, and the left foreground console establish an
image of work that has no worker. The tonal range is broad (p05 0.059, p95 0.890)
but the palette stays mostly gray-green: #4a4d4b, #6f706f, #a5a1a1, with white
#d7d5d8 overhead. At edge density 0.087, the information remains an uneasy,
operational pattern rather than noise.

<Plate slug="thomas-demand" id="07-vault-2012" label="Vault, 2012" caption="A storage corridor axis and a nested bay lead through stacked frames to the small rear frame." />

In *Vault*, pictures turned toward walls make looking itself the withheld act. The
vertical axis at x0.512 and the internal storage bay establish a corridor, but the
rear frame receives the pull of the floor and the piles at either side. Its low mean
luminance of 0.196 and restrained edge density of 0.056 make the lit back wall
decisive. Brown-black #100d0b, olive #4f4639, and pale #bab47f make the objects
read as barriers rather than a collection of individual works.

## material fields

<Plate slug="thomas-demand" id="08-atelier-2014" label="Atelier, 2014" caption="A broad parquet field leaves the colored paper offcuts at lower right as a concentrated interruption." />

*Atelier* withholds the expected activity of a workroom. The table, stool, and
rolled paper remain at the upper edge while colored offcuts become the anchor at
x0.726, y0.601. The muted field is confirmed by edge density 0.053, mean
luminance 0.358, and a palette that runs from #1d1414 and #51413c through #a38d7e
to #dad2d2. Color arrives as a cut-paper event inside a room whose larger argument
is empty floor.

<Plate slug="thomas-demand" id="09-parkett-2014" label="Parkett, 2014" caption="A middle parquet register and a lower seam make the floor a field of overlapping directional units." />

*Parkett* removes every conventional subject. The line at y0.363 identifies a
visible register in the repeated boards, while the lower seam preserves one
direction through a surface that otherwise lets orientation flip. It is the
smoothest plate here, at edge density 0.029, yet p05 0.153 and p95 0.808 keep the
brown field from flattening. Dark #402b1a, mid-brown #5e4530, and pale #d1c4b0
make light itself another layer of parquet.

<Plate slug="thomas-demand" id="10-workshop-2017" label="Werkstatt / Workshop, 2017" caption="A rail of hanging instruments separates crowded shadow from the bright right-hand window." />

*Workshop* is crowded without becoming chaotic because the hanging-instrument rail
at y0.218 gives the upper half a common measure. Right-hand window light then
answers the dense suspension with a clear source of pressure. The range from p05
0.024 to p95 0.745 is organized in warm values: #150a02, #4e2b17, #7c532d, and
#f4efe1. The workshop reads as a made environment, not a documentary inventory.

<Plate slug="thomas-demand" id="11-pond-2020" label="Pond, 2020" caption="A middle lily band and a narrow channel of open water organize the all-over pond surface." />

*Pond* tests Demand's constructed logic against a landscape motif. The middle lily
band gives the surface a pause at y0.318, while the channel of open water runs from
the foreground toward it and makes the pad field directional. Edge density returns
to 0.169, but the picture is held by dark #252325, blue #4a4965, olive #968738,
and yellow #c9b864. The repeated discs never become wallpaper because the water
keeps opening an unstable route through them.

## Exercises

<ExerciseCard n="01" title="Build an empty room around one line">
Choose a room with a long table, counter, desk, or rail. Remove people from the
frame and make that horizontal do the job of a horizon. Add one small object whose
placement interrupts the line, then compare whether it reads as subject or as
measured punctuation.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="02" title="Use repetition without flattening the frame">
Photograph tiles, books, leaves, panels, or tools. Find one open channel, seam, or
gap that gives the eye a direction through the repetitions. Keep the version where
the route is visible without a singled-out object.
</ExerciseCard>

<ExerciseCard n="03" title="Treat a threshold as a stop, not an exit">
Frame a doorway, window, corridor, or storage bay. Make a photograph in which the
opening blocks or holds the eye rather than simply promising depth. If covering the
opening makes the rest of the frame lose tension, the threshold is doing the work.
</ExerciseCard>
